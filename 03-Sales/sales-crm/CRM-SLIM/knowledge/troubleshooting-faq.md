## MORNING/TIMING ISSUES

### "I feel dizzy or shaky mid-morning"

**What this usually means:** Blood sugar drop, or fasting isn't right for you

**Response to customer:**
"Safety first—eat something. Then let's find what works. This might mean adjusting your timing earlier, or trying the non-fasting track instead. Your clinic will personalize this for you."

**What to suggest:**
1. Eat immediately (if dizzy, don't push)
2. If it keeps happening: switch timing or use non-fasting track
3. Refer to clinic for personalized adjustment

**Key insight:** Dizziness means stop fasting. Some people's bodies don't work with fasting, and that's fine. Non-fasting track has same results.

---

### "I'm hungry all morning and can't wait for lunch"

**What this usually means:** Either early days (normal), or fasting isn't right for your body

**Response to customer:**
"Mornings are hardest the first 7 days. Sparkling water + wait 10 minutes usually helps—hunger comes in waves. If it's still unbearable after a week, we switch to non-fasting. Structure matters more than fasting."

**What to suggest:**
1. Week 1? This is normal. Sparkling water is your best tool.
2. Days 4-7? Usually gets easier.
3. Beyond week 1? If still intense: adjust timing earlier or switch tracks
4. After 7 days still struggling? Non-fasting track is the answer

**Key insight:** Constantly fighting hunger means the plan won't stick. We adjust.

---

### "I ate breakfast when I wasn't supposed to"

**What this usually means:** Your body needed it, or week 1 is hard, or something triggered it

**Response to customer:**
"One breakfast doesn't reset the system. Continue tomorrow. Did something make you break—stress, sleep deprivation, actual hunger? That's information. Your clinic can help you adjust."

**What to suggest:**
1. Not a failure. Just continue.
2. What triggered it? Tiredness? Low blood sugar? Habit?
3. Tomorrow: Try again. Use more sparkling water.
4. If it happens repeatedly: Non-fasting track might be better
5. Don't spiral; information beats perfection

**Key insight:** One slip doesn't undo the method. Return is what matters.

---

### "I ate late last night (11pm) and now I'm panicking"

**What this usually means:** Maltese life happened (normal), or customer is catastrophizing

**Response to customer:**
"Late dinner? That's just real life. Return to your normal rhythm tomorrow. No punishment, no compensation. One meal doesn't break the system."

**What to suggest:**
1. No guilt. This happens.
2. Morning: Return to normal fasting/eating rhythm
3. No need to "make up for it" tomorrow
4. Just rhythm. Not perfection.

**Key insight:** Late dinners are cultural. We expect them. One late meal is fine.

---

### "I worked through lunch and missed my eating window"

**What this usually means:** Real life happened; customer might feel like they failed

**Response to customer:**
"Real work happens. Eat when you can, then return to normal tomorrow. One disrupted day isn't failure—returning is what counts."

**What to suggest:**
1. Eat when you can (not ideal, but fine)
2. Tomorrow: back to normal rhythm
3. If this happens often: work with clinic on adjusting timing to your schedule

**Key insight:** Structure isn't rigid. It's flexible enough for real life.

## HUNGER & APPETITE ISSUES

### "I'm constantly hungry, even after meals"

**What this usually means:** Protein too low, or lunch not structured enough, or portions small

**Response to customer:**
"Constant hunger means we adjust. Could be more protein at lunch, bigger portions, or including more vegetables for volume. We don't expect you to white-knuckle through hunger. Your clinic will fine-tune this."

**What to suggest:**
1. Check: Is lunch high-protein? (1-2 palm portions)
2. Check: Are vegetables 2 fists? (Volume matters for satiety)
3. Check: Are you staying hydrated? (Sometimes thirst feels like hunger)
4. Refer: Clinic adjusts protein targets and portions based on hunger

**Key insight:** If constantly hungry, the plan will fail. We fix this.

---

### "I get really hungry at 4pm every day"

**What this usually means:** Lunch wasn't high-protein enough, or timing needs adjustment

**Response to customer:**
"4pm hunger is usually about lunch. Let's check: Was your lunch high-protein? (That's what prevents the afternoon slide.) Your clinic can tweak protein or suggest a small protein snack."

**What to suggest:**
1. Check lunch composition (protein level?)
2. Suggest: Add more protein to lunch tomorrow
3. Or: Small protein snack at 4pm if needed (Greek yogurt, nuts)
4. Refer: Clinic adjusts based on feedback

**Key insight:** Afternoon crash = predictable lunch issue. Easy fix.

---

### "I get cravings for sweets at night"

**What this usually means:** Evening blood sugar patterns, or not eating enough at dinner, or stress/habit

**Response to customer:**
"Evening cravings are normal. Could be blood sugar, or just habit. Try: Protein with dinner, brush your teeth after eating, or have a small planned treat that fits your points. Your clinic can help if it persists."

**What to suggest:**
1. More protein at dinner (stabilizes blood sugar)
2. Plan a small treat within points (not forbidden, just intentional)
3. Brush teeth after dinner (habit break)
4. Consider: Is it actual hunger, or boredom/stress/habit?

**Key insight:** Cravings are information, not failure. We work with them.

## EATING & FOOD CHOICE ISSUES

### "I don't know what to eat"

**What this usually means:** Overwhelm, or customer wants guidance on food choices

**Response to customer:**
"Start simple: Pick a protein you like (chicken, fish, beef), add any vegetables, add a carb. Same thing a few times a week is fine. You don't need variety; you need simplicity you'll actually repeat."

**What to suggest:**
1. Protein: Pick 2-3 you like and rotate
2. Vegetables: Pick 3 you like and rotate
3. Carbs: Pick 2-3 and rotate
4. Don't overcomplicate. Simplicity = sustainability.

**Key insight:** Consistency beats variety. Routine is easier to follow.

---

### "Can I eat [specific food]?"

**What this usually means:** Customer unsure if food is allowed

**Response to customer:**
"Nothing is forbidden. Everything has points. If it fits your daily budget, you can eat it. The question is whether you want to spend your points on it. That's your choice."

**How to respond:**
1. Nothing is "forbidden"
2. Calculate/estimate points if needed
3. Fits budget? Have it. Doesn't fit? Save for another day.
4. Emphasize: Choice is theirs, not shame

**Key insight:** This is about choice and trade-offs, not restriction.

---

### "I went to dinner and had pasta and bread and wine. Did I ruin it?"

**What this usually means:** Customer spiral after social eating, needs validation

**Response to customer:**
"You didn't ruin anything. You lived your life. Tomorrow you return to structure. One meal—even an indulgent one—doesn't break the system. Returning does."

**What to suggest:**
1. Validate: Social eating is part of real life
2. No guilt. No compensation tomorrow.
3. Just rhythm: Return at next meal.
4. This is the whole point of the method

**Key insight:** If the method can't accommodate one indulgent dinner, it's not sustainable. This method does.

**What to suggest:**
1. Refer to clinic: They'll personalize protein strategy
2. Plant-based proteins: Legumes, tofu, tempeh, nuts, seeds, plant meat
3. Combination rule: Legumes + grains together = complete protein
4. Points system works the same

**Key insight:** This method works for any diet preference. Clinic personalizes.

## WEIGHT & PROGRESS ISSUES

### "I'm not losing weight. What am I doing wrong?"

**What this usually means:** Customer frustrated, might be doing many things right but seeing no scale movement

**Response to customer:**
"First: Are you losing consistently, just slowly? If yes, you're not doing anything wrong. If truly nothing is moving, let's check: Are you hitting your protein targets? Counting liquid points? Actually following the timing? Your clinic will help diagnose."

**Diagnostic steps:**
1. How long? (Less than 2-3 weeks = too early to panic)
2. Is scale down at all, just slowly? (This is normal, keep going)
3. If truly nothing: Check execution
   - Are you hitting protein targets?
   - Are liquid points sneaking in?
   - Are you consistent with timing?
   - Are you staying within points?
4. Refer: Clinic reviews personalized targets

**Key insight:** Progress isn't linear. Slow loss is still loss. Refer if truly stuck.

---

### "My weight went up. I failed."

**What this usually means:** Customer misreading normal fluctuations or one bad day

**Response to customer:**
"Weight goes up and down. One day up doesn't mean you failed. Look at the weekly trend, not daily. If you're up for a whole week and doing everything right? That's plateau. We adjust with clinic. One day? That's just normal."

**What to suggest:**
1. Weight fluctuates daily (water, hormones, food timing)
2. Look at weekly trend, not daily
3. Up one day? Normal.
4. Up all week? Clinic review. Not failure; just plateau.

**Key insight:** Daily weight is noise. Weekly trend is signal.

---

### "I hit a plateau. What do I do?"

**What this usually means:** Progress stopped after losing initially, customer needs adjustment strategy

**Response to customer:**
"Plateaus happen. Your body adapts. Usually means: more protein, fewer liquid points, or adjusting points budget. This is where clinic personalization matters. Don't just restrict more; that backfires."

**What to suggest:**
1. Plateaus are normal after initial loss
2. Don't restrict more (backfires)
3. Check: Protein still high? Consistency still there? Liquid points creeping in?
4. Clinic adjusts targets, may reduce points slightly, or increases protein
5. This is personalization territory

**Key insight:** Plateaus need clinic adjustment, not customer restriction.

---

### "I lost weight but I'm tired all the time"

**What this usually means:** Calorie deficit too aggressive, or not enough protein, or poor sleep

**Response to customer:**
"Extreme fatigue isn't normal. Could be points too low, sleep poor, or not enough protein. Tell your clinic. They adjust to a more sustainable pace. Weight loss isn't worth exhaustion."

**What to suggest:**
1. This needs clinic review
2. Could be: points budget too aggressive, protein too low, sleep issues, medical
3. Don't push through exhaustion
4. Clinic adjusts to sustainable pace

**Key insight:** This is medical/personal territory. Refer to clinic immediately.

## SLIP-UPS & RELAPSE

### "I had a bad day and ate way over my points. Now I'm spiraling."

**What this usually means:** All-or-nothing thinking, catastrophizing one bad day

**Response to customer:**
"One day over doesn't erase your progress. You didn't reset to square one. Tomorrow you return to structure. That's it. One meal doesn't define the whole method. Returning does."

**What to suggest:**
1. Validate: This happens. Normal.
2. No punishment tomorrow
3. No compensation
4. Just rhythm: Return at next meal
5. This is the whole point of the method—it survives real life

**Key insight:** The method's strength is that it survives slip-ups. Teach return.

---

### "I fell off for 3 days. Is it over?"

**What this usually means:** Customer in shame spiral, thinks they've failed completely

**Response to customer:**
"Three days off doesn't erase what came before. You return now. The system didn't break; you just took a break. Returning is what matters. You've already proven you can do this."

**What to suggest:**
1. What triggered the 3 days? (Stress? Vacation? Life?)
2. Acknowledge: Normal. Happens.
3. Now: Return to structure TODAY
4. No "punishment week" or compensation
5. Just return

**Key insight:** Relapse is normal. Returning is the skill.

---

### "I fell off for 2 weeks. Should I give up?"

**What this usually means:** Customer in deep shame spiral, considering quitting

**Response to customer:**
"No. You're still here asking. That's commitment. Two weeks off = you learned something about yourself. Maybe you needed a break. Now: You return. Not punishment; just structure. Your clinic is here to help you adjust if needed."

**What to suggest:**
1. 2 weeks off is painful, not fatal
2. What happened? Why did you stop? (This is valuable info)
3. Now: You return (not Monday, not Jan 1—today)
4. Tell clinic what triggered it
5. Clinic helps adjust to prevent this again

**Key insight:** Returning after longer breaks is harder. Clinic support crucial.

---

### "I was doing great, then had one bad meal and quit completely"

**What this usually means:** All-or-nothing thinking triggered by one slip

**Response to customer:**
"That's the old pattern. One meal isn't 'ruining it.' That's just one meal. Return at the next meal. This time, the system doesn't fall apart because of one choice. That's the whole point."

**What to suggest:**
1. Normalize slip-ups
2. Teach return: One meal = one meal. Not reset.
3. The system is DESIGNED to survive this
4. Returning proves it works

**Key insight:** All-or-nothing thinking is the real enemy. Return muscle is the fix.

## WEEK-BY-WEEK WHAT TO EXPECT

### Week 1
- Hunger waves (normal, comes in cycles)
- More food thoughts (habit, not hunger)
- Mornings hardest (days 1-3)
- By day 4-7: Usually feels easier

**What to tell customer:** "This is normal. By day 4 you'll notice it gets easier. Get through week 1."

### Week 2
- Rhythm feels normal
- Hunger quieter
- Mornings feel automatic
- Evenings more manageable
- Often first weight loss this week

**What to tell customer:** "Week 2 is when it clicks. You're adapting. Weight should start moving."

### Weeks 3-4
- System feels automatic
- Cravings quieter
- Afternoons steadier
- Energy improving
- Consistent weight loss trajectory

**What to tell customer:** "You're in rhythm now. This is your new normal."

### Months 2-3
- Progress steady
- System feels like your way of life, not a diet
- Energy high
- Clothes fitting better
- Usually hitting significant weight loss by now

**What to tell customer:** "You've got this. You've proven it works."

## EXERCISE & ACTIVITY QUESTIONS

### "Can I exercise while doing this?"

**What this usually means:** Customer wondering if activity affects the plan

**Response to customer:**
"Yes. Exercise is fine. Your clinic will adjust your points if you're very active. Don't count exercise calories; the points budget already accounts for activity level."

**What to suggest:**
1. Exercise is great, continue
2. Don't do extra "exercise calories" on top
3. Points budget set by clinic already factors activity
4. If very athletic: clinic may adjust targets

**Key insight:** Don't make them earn food through exercise. That's old diet thinking.

---

### "I started exercising and gained weight. What happened?"

**What this usually means:** Muscle gain or water retention from new exercise

**Response to customer:**
"Muscle weighs more than fat. Plus, new exercise causes water retention from muscle repair. Scale might not move for a few weeks, but body composition is changing. This is actually good. Look at how clothes fit, not just the scale."

**What to suggest:**
1. This is normal with new exercise
2. Scale will catch up in 2-4 weeks
3. Watch: How clothes fit, energy, strength
4. These are more accurate than daily scale
5. If concerned: Clinic review

**Key insight:** New exercise can mask weight loss for a few weeks. Normal and good.

**What this usually means:** Customer with health conditions unsure if safe

**Response to customer:**
"Different conditions need different approaches. Your clinic will review your history and personalize. Things like diabetes, blood pressure, thyroid—we manage these. Tell your clinic everything."

**What to suggest:**
1. List of conditions requiring clinic review:
   - Diabetes (type 1 or 2)
   - Thyroid conditions
   - Heart conditions

   - Hormonal conditions
   - Medications affecting metabolism

3. Clinic personalizes around condition

## MOTIVATION & MINDSET ISSUES

### "I know what to do, but I can't stick to it"

**What this usually means:** Willpower/consistency struggle, not knowledge

**Response to customer:**
"That's not about knowing—it's about structure. The method removes willpower by building defaults. Instead of deciding every meal, you follow a structure. That's the whole point. Tell your clinic what's making it hard—stress? Busy weeks? They'll adjust structure, not ask you to try harder."

**What to suggest:**
1. This is normal struggle
2. Willpower is limited resource
3. Structure removes need for willpower
4. Clinic helps identify what's breaking structure
5. Then fix structure, not you

**Key insight:** If constantly failing willpower, the plan needs adjustment, not you.

---

## When to Say "Clinic Consult Needed"

Always refer to clinic for:

✅ Medical history or conditions
✅ Medications affecting metabolism
✅ Personalized protein/calorie targets
✅ Pregnancy/breastfeeding
✅ Eating disorder history
✅ Persistent medical symptoms
✅ Progress plateau with no clear cause
✅ Fatigue or feeling unwell
✅ Exercise prescription
✅ Mental health/emotional eating struggles

**Don't diagnose. Always default to clinic for personalization.**

## Key Troubleshooting Principles

**1. Validate first, then guide**
Every problem needs acknowledgment before solution.

**2. Normalize struggle**
This is normal. Everyone experiences these issues.

**3. Slip-ups are information**
What triggered it? That's data for adjustment.

**4. Return is the metric**
How fast you come back > how much you slipped.

**5. Structure over willpower**
If struggling constantly, structure needs tweaking.

**6. No shame, no blame**
Old diets used shame. This one uses structure.

**7. Refer to clinic early**
Better to ask than to guess. Especially medical questions.
