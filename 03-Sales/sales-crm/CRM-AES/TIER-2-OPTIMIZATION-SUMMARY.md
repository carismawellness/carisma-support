# Carisma Aesthetics CRM — Tier 2 Skills Optimization Summary

**Completed:** February 22, 2026
**Scope:** 5 Tier 2 skills repositioned for medical aesthetics sales excellence
**Context:** Building on Tier 1 foundation (consult-and-pitch, close-the-booking, first-time-converter optimized in Phase 1)

---

## Executive Summary

All 5 Tier 2 skills have been optimized for medical aesthetics context, with emphasis on:
- **Medical Credibility:** Positioning expertise, clinical ethics, and patient safety as primary differentiators
- **Naturalness Psychology:** Addressing the deep fear of over-treatment and unnatural results
- **High-Ticket Decision-Making:** Acknowledging the psychological complexity of €300–€3,000+ medical decisions
- **Trust-Based Selling:** Reframing from transactional sales to professional medical consultation
- **Conservative Philosophy:** Emphasizing restraint and progressive treatment pathways

## Tier 2 Skills Optimized

### 1. **Competitor Defense** ✓
**File:** `/CRM/CRM-AES/skills/competitor-defense.md`

**Key Changes:**
- Repositioned from spa differentiation to medical aesthetic clinical differentiation
- Added medical ethics framework: "Price is secondary to trust; discounting signals lower clinical standards"
- Enhanced competitor signal dictionary with medical aesthetics-specific signals:
  - "Natural approach" comparison (fear of over-treatment)
  - "Other clinics" comparison (uncertainty about clinical quality)
  - Results/naturalness challenges (past bad experiences)
- Rewrote differentiation stack to emphasize:
  - **Doctor-led medical model** (not nurse-run injection clinics)
  - **Philosophy of restraint** (conservative-first approach)
  - **Consultation depth** (20-30 min vs. 5-min injection clinics)
  - **Multi-modal approach** (lasers + injectables + skincare, not injection-only)
  - **Practitioner consistency** (ongoing relationships, not rotating providers)

**Key Additions:**
- Script 2 (Price Comparison): Reframed from "full experience value" to "clinical quality + expertise + philosophy = justified price"
- Script 3 (Why Carisma Different): Completely rewritten to emphasize medical ethics, consultation-first model, and philosophy of restraint
- Script 5 (Discount Request): Reframed price anchoring around clinical appropriateness, not just experience bundling
- Signature Move 3: "Medical Ethics Bridge" — redirects from price-shopping to trust-building

**Hesitation Type Addressed:** Fear of over-treatment, clinic ethics, naturalness concerns

**Sales Impact:** Shifts conversation from price comparison to clinical trust; positions Carisma as the ethical alternative to volume-driven clinics

### 2. **Objection Buster** ✓
**File:** `/CRM/CRM-AES/skills/objection-buster.md`

**Key Changes:**
- Completely reframed from transactional objection handling to medical decision psychology
- Updated research foundations to emphasize "every objection is an emotional signal rooted in medical or psychological concern"
- Added critical context: In medical aesthetics, hesitation is protective wisdom, not weakness
- Enhanced objection hierarchy to explicitly surface the *fears* beneath surface objections:
  - "Too expensive" often means "I'm not sure this is safe"
  - "I need to think about it" means "I'm not confident this will look natural"
  - "I'm not sure it's worth it" means "I'm terrified of regret"

**Key Additions:**
- Script 1 (Too Expensive — Medical Aesthetics version): Reframes from "spa experience value" to "consultation depth + expert judgment + ongoing care = clinical investment"
- Script 1b (Too Expensive variant): Explicitly surfaces the fear question: "Or is there something else — like worry about looking overdone — that's sitting at the back of it?"
- Script 1 (Worth It — Natural Results Focus): Reframed to emphasize "you'll look refreshed, not dramatically different; people won't know what changed; that's the mark of great aesthetic medicine"
- Signature Move 2: "Medical Ethics Reframe" — shows you prioritize medical safety over closing the booking

**Hesitation Type Addressed:** Naturalness fear, regret anxiety, practitioner trust, procedure safety concerns

**Sales Impact:** Transforms objection-handling from overcoming resistance to validating protective wisdom; builds trust through acknowledging fears rather than dismissing them

### 3. **Close Detector** ✓
**File:** `/CRM/CRM-AES/skills/close-detector.md`

**Key Changes:**
- Repositioned from spa upsell timing to medical aesthetics follow-up care timing
- Critical addition: Medical aesthetic close signals include **relief language** ("it will look natural"), **ownership language** ("when can I start?"), and **validation language** ("I'm so glad I talked to you")
- Completely rewrote Decision Logic table with medical aesthetics guardrails:
  - **NO plant if** patient expressed fear of over-treatment
  - **NO plant if** patient is hesitant about the main treatment
  - **NO plant if** upsell would introduce new cost concern post-decision
  - **Caution:** Only plant if clinically appropriate, not commercially driven
- Added critical medical ethics rule: "Never plant if it introduces new concern, complexity, or cost pressure"

**Key Additions:**
- Script 3 (Medical Aesthetics — Treatment Booked): Replaced spa cross-sell with follow-up clinical progression framing ("we'll check in at 2 weeks to assess")
- Decision Logic redesigned for medical context: Treatment booking + skincare → skincare protocol; Consultation-only → NO plant
- Emphasis on trust preservation over revenue capture at close moment

**Hesitation Type Addressed:** Over-treatment fear, timing appropriateness, clinical progression

**Sales Impact:** Shifts from revenue-maximizing "planting" to clinically appropriate follow-up suggestions; preserves trust by never feeling pushy post-medical-decision

### 4. **Bundle Builder** ✓
**File:** `/CRM/CRM-AES/skills/bundle-builder.md`

**Key Changes:**
- Complete reframing: From "spa day experience bundling" to "medical aesthetics treatment plan sequencing"
- Repositioned bundle psychology: Not about price anchoring tricks, but about **clinical synergy and progressive treatment pathways**
- Updated research foundations to emphasize clinical efficacy: "Botox + filler together is clinically better than isolated treatments"
- Rewrote When to Use section to emphasize **clinical picture reading**, not just multi-need signals:
  - Patient with lines + volume loss → Multi-phase plan
  - Patient expressing "I'm nervous about overdone" → Conservative Phase 1 approach
  - First-timer → Educational introduction to progressive pathway
  - **Do NOT** bundle if patient clearly wants one treatment only

**Key Additions:**
- Explicit guardrail: "Never bundle to maximize revenue — bundle to demonstrate best clinical approach"
- Bundle naming now reflects medical progression: "Phase 1 Conservative," "Your Progressive Plan," not "The Complete Experience"
- Decision Logic focuses on clinical synergy, not anchoring math
- Added critical note: "Conservative-first philosophy always"

**Hesitation Type Addressed:** Over-treatment fear, uncertainty about treatment sequencing, desire for natural results

**Sales Impact:** Transforms bundling from revenue technique to medical best practice communication; positions progressive plans as clinical wisdom, not upsell

### 5. **Upsell Booking** ✓
**File:** `/CRM/CRM-AES/skills/upsell-booking.md`

**Key Changes:**
- Complete reframing: From "post-purchase dopamine window" to "post-medical-decision clinical recommendations"
- Updated research foundations to emphasize clinical synergy and results amplification, not just financial anchoring
- Critical addition: "Conversion rate lower than spa because clinical appropriateness outweighs revenue volume"
- Completely rewrote When to Use guardrails:
  - **Do NOT use if** patient expressed anxiety about main treatment
  - **Do NOT use if** upsell would introduce new cost concern
  - **Do NOT use if** patient has "commitment issue" about primary treatment
  - **Only use if** complementary treatment would logically amplify results

**Key Additions:**
- Changed target AOV lift: €50–€150 (higher values, but lower conversion rate due to medical ethics)
- Added explicit note: "Clinical thinking always outweighs revenue thinking"
- Changed position metric: NOT "20–35% conversion" but "15–25% conversion" with emphasis on clinical appropriateness
- Reframed upsell as "How do we maximize your results?" not "What else can you buy?"

**Hesitation Type Addressed:** Results optimization vs. pressure, clinical progression, cost-benefit in medical context

**Sales Impact:** Lowers conversion expectations but builds trust; positions add-ons as medical advice ("skincare protocol to amplify this laser treatment"), not sales upsells
