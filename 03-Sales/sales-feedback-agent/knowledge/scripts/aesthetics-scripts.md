## Call Script (Inbound/Outbound)

### Step 1: Greet & Confirm Caller
**What to say:** "Hi, this is Rana calling from Carisma Aesthetics. Is this [name]?"
**CRM Action:** Confirm name & phone number
**Scoring note:** Must use brand name "Carisma Aesthetics", must ask to confirm caller by name. SDR should use their own name.

### Step 2: Confirm Call & Treatment History
**What to say:** "Perfect, I'm responding to your request to learn more about [Snatch your Jawline] have you had any aesthetic treatments before? Even something simple like a facial or Botox?"
**CRM Action:** Note if client is new/experienced
**Scoring note:** Must reference the specific offer/treatment the lead enquired about. Must ask about treatment history to gauge experience level.

### Step 3: Explore Specific Concerns
**What to say:** "What specific concerns have you noticed lately -- perhaps around [insert the offer target concern] -- that you'd like to improve?"
**CRM Action:** Note main concern in CRM
**Scoring note:** Must ask an open-ended question about the client's specific concerns. Should reference the offer's target concern area to guide the conversation.

### Step 5: Explain the Value
**What to say:** "I completely get it! Many of our patients visit us with [reiterate their concern they mentioned]; This is perfect timing because the [Insert offer name] seems like could be really beneficial for you;

The entire package has a total value of [insert perceived value from the packages sheet] but what we're going to do is give it to you for [insert actual price].

Your experience will first begin with a personalized consultation with our doctor; they will assess your skin, facial structure, and overall goals closely with you.

You'll receive [insert package]

Finally, you'll also get [bonus 1, bonus 2, etc] to complete your treatment

How does this sound?"
**CRM Action:** Confirm interest
**Scoring note:** MUST present perceived value BEFORE the actual price (anchoring). Must reiterate the client's concern. Must walk through the full package structure: consultation, main treatment, bonuses. Must end with a soft close ("How does this sound?").

### Step 6: Get Deposit
**What to say:** "You'll receive a deposit request through Fresha to confirm and secure your appointment."
**CRM Action:** --
**Scoring note:** Must mention Fresha deposit process. Do not skip -- deposit secures the booking.

### Step 7: Book Appointment
**What to say:** "We're based in St Julians area and we're open Monday-Saturday 9am till 7pm. What day and time usually works best for you?"
**CRM Action:** Book slot in calendar
**Scoring note:** Must mention location (St Julians). Must state operating hours. Must ask for preferred day/time.

### Step 8: Confirm Time & Details
**What to say:** "Great -- I've booked you for [Day, Time]. I'll send over a confirmation text shortly. Just need to take your [Email, DOB, etc.]."
**CRM Action:** Collect details: email, DOB, address, etc.
**Scoring note:** Must confirm the booked day and time back to the client. Must collect personal details for CRM.

### Step 9: Send Confirmation
**What to say:** "Perfect! You'll receive a confirmation text now with your appointment, our address, and contact info. Feel free to reach out anytime!"
**CRM Action:** Send confirmation text & CRM reminder
**Scoring note:** Must confirm that a text is being sent. Must mention address and contact info are included.

### Step 10: Close Warmly
**What to say:** "You're in great hands -- we're excited to meet you and get you started on your journey with us! Is there anything else I can assist you with?"
**CRM Action:** Add call notes in CRM
**Scoring note:** Must close warmly and offer further assistance. Must add call notes to CRM after the call.

## WhatsApp/Text Scripts by Scenario

### Scenario 1: New Leads (Consult)
**Trigger:** New lead comes in requesting a consultation
**Script:** Refer to the separate New Leads sheet for the full consult script.
**Key elements to check:** Timely outreach, brand name used, consultation offer made.

### Scenario 2: New Leads (Offer)
**Trigger:** New lead comes in responding to a specific offer
**Script:** Refer to the separate New Leads sheet for the full offer script.
**Key elements to check:** Offer name mentioned, perceived value vs actual price, urgency element.

### Scenario 3: Missed Chats Follow-Ups
**Trigger:** Lead sent a message that was not responded to in time
**Script:**
"Hi [First Name], this is Sarah from Carisma Aesthetics.

I saw your message earlier and just wanted to follow up -- I'd love to help you get started with [treatment/offer name].

We offer a free consultation with our practitioner Leticia, who has over 10 years of experience in aesthetics. During the session, we assess your skin, structure, and goals -- and provide you with a clear, personalized treatment plan.

We're also running a limited new client package that includes your consultation, a [starter treatment], and [bonuses] -- all for just [xx] (normally over [xx]). It's available to the first 15 clients this week.

How can I support you best?
Happy to answer questions or check availability if you're interested."
**Key elements to check:**
- Acknowledges the missed message
- Mentions practitioner name and experience (Leticia, 10+ years)
- Offers free consultation
- Presents offer with perceived value vs actual price
- Creates urgency (limited to first 15 clients this week)
- Ends with open question

### Scenario 4: No-Shows & Cancellations Recovery
**Trigger:** Client missed their appointment or cancelled
**Script:**
"Hi [First Name], this is Sarah from Carisma Aesthetics.

I noticed you weren't able to make it to your appointment today -- I completely understand that things come up!

If you're still interested, I'd love to help you rebook your consultation with [Insert practitioner name]. She's one of our most in-demand aesthetic doctors, and we'd love to make sure you don't miss the chance to speak with her.

Would you like me to check availability for you later this week or next?

No rush -- just let me know what works best, and I'll do my best to accommodate."
**Key elements to check:**
- Non-judgmental tone ("I completely understand")
- Mentions the practitioner by name
- Elevates practitioner status ("most in-demand")
- Offers specific rebooking options (this week or next)
- Low-pressure close

### Scenario 5: Booking Follow-Ups
**Trigger:** Lead expressed interest but has not yet booked
**Script:**
"Hi [First Name], this is Sarah from Carisma Aesthetics -- just checking in.

I really enjoyed our chat the other day about [treatment/concern] and just wanted to see if you had any questions or needed help deciding on next steps.

We'd still love to welcome you in for your booking with [insert practitioner] -- [he/she] is excellent at creating subtle, natural results that align with your goals.

Our availability is a little limited for new clients this week so wanted to make sure I can find a time that accommodates you best. No pressure at all -- just let me know how I can best support you, whether it's timing, more info, or anything else."
**Key elements to check:**
- References previous conversation topic
- Mentions practitioner by name
- Creates soft urgency (limited availability)
- Offers multiple ways to help (timing, info, etc.)
- Low-pressure tone

### Scenario 6: Consultation Follow-Ups
**Trigger:** Lead had a consultation but has not booked a treatment
**Script:**
"Hi [First Name], this is Sarah from Carisma Aesthetics -- just checking in.

I really enjoyed our chat the other day about [treatment/concern] and just wanted to see if you had any questions or needed help deciding on next steps.

We'd still love to welcome you in for your consultation with [insert practitioner] -- [he/she] is excellent at creating subtle, natural results that align with your goals.

Our availability is a little limited for new clients this week so wanted to make sure I can find a time that accommodates you best. No pressure at all -- just let me know how I can best support you, whether it's timing, more info, or anything else."
**Key elements to check:**
- References previous conversation
- Mentions practitioner by name
- Creates soft urgency (limited availability)
- Low-pressure but proactive

### Scenario 7: Appointment Confirmation + Upsell
**Trigger:** Client has an upcoming appointment
**Script:**
"Hi [First Name], this is Sarah from Carisma Aesthetics.

Just confirming your appointment with [Insert DR name], for [insert treatment] on [Day, Date] at [Time].

We're looking forward to welcoming you! Our clinic is located inside InterContinental hotel in St. Julian's, and parking is available at the Eden car park. We will give you free parking validation for 3 hours.

If you need anything ahead of your visit, just let me know.

By the way -- I checked and there's a bit of extra time during your session, so I can also slot in [add-on treatment] if you'd like.

A lot of clients who do [main treatment] find it really enhances their results, especially for [insert benefit -- e.g., glow, lift, tightening].

No pressure at all -- just let me know and I'll get it arranged for you."
**Key elements to check:**
- Confirms doctor name, treatment, day, date, and time
- Mentions clinic location (InterContinental hotel, St. Julian's)
- Mentions parking (Eden car park, free 3-hour validation)
- Upsell is positioned naturally ("extra time during your session")
- Social proof for upsell ("a lot of clients")
- No-pressure close

### Scenario 8: 1 Week Post-Procedure + Rebook + Referral
**Trigger:** 1 week after the client's procedure
**Script:**
"Hi [First Name], this is Sarah from Carisma Aesthetics -- just checking in after your [treatment name] last week.

I wanted to see how everything is going so far?

For best results, most [insert treatment] clients return for their next session around [typical timeframe], and I'd be happy to help you get that booked in if you're ready to continue.

Also -- if you've loved your experience, I have a small favor to ask.

We grow mostly through referrals from happy clients, and if someone comes to mind -- even just one friend or colleague -- who could benefit from feeling more confident in their skin, I'd be so grateful if you introduced us.

As a thank you, we'll give 25 credit to both of you toward your next treatments. If you just let me know their name or make a group chat, and I'll be able to take care of everything else.

It means a lot -- most of our favorite clients started with a simple referral like yours"
**Key elements to check:**
- Check-in on treatment results
- Mention recommended follow-up timeframe
- Offer to rebook
- Referral ask with specific incentive (25 credit for both)
- Make it easy (group chat or just a name)
- Emotional close ("most of our favorite clients started with a referral")

### Scenario 9: Abandoned Carts
**Trigger:** Lead started booking online but did not complete
**Script:**
"Hi [First Name], this is Sarah from Carisma Aesthetics.

I noticed you were about to book your [treatment/consultation] but didn't complete the final step -- totally understandable, life gets busy!

If you're still considering it, I'd be happy to help you wrap things up or answer any questions you might have. Most of our clients like to speak with us before confirming, and that's exactly what we're here for.

We're proud to be Malta's highest-rated aesthetics clinic, and I'd be glad to reserve a free consultation for you with our lead doctor. It's a chance to meet us, get personalised advice, and explore the best approach -- whether you choose to continue with us or not.

Would you like me to check times for this week?"
**Key elements to check:**
- Acknowledges the abandoned cart without being pushy
- Normalizes speaking before booking
- Social proof ("Malta's highest-rated aesthetics clinic")
- Offers free consultation with lead doctor
- No-obligation framing
- Specific call to action (check times this week)

### Scenario 10: Re-Engage Older Leads (4-6 Months)
**Trigger:** Client has not visited in 4-6 months
**Script:**
"Hi [First Name], this is Sarah from Carisma Aesthetics -- hope you've been doing great!

I noticed it's been a few months since your last [treatment name], and [insert treatment repeat timeline] is usually the ideal window for a follow-up to maintain and enhance your results.

To make it easier to get back on track, we're offering a limited-time rebooking incentive -- book your next [treatment name] this week and receive 25 credit toward any add-on.

Would you like me to check our availability for this week or next? I can also book a quick follow-up with [insert previous doctor name] if you'd prefer to reassess your plan."
**Key elements to check:**
- References specific past treatment
- Educates on ideal repeat timeline
- Offers rebooking incentive (25 credit)
- Mentions previous doctor by name for continuity
- Offers choice: rebook or reassess

### Scenario 11: Reach Out to New Followers (DM Only)
**Trigger:** New Instagram follower
**Script (3-step conversation):**

**Message 1:**
"Hi [First Name], this is Sarah from Carisma Aesthetics -- thank you so much for the follow!

I'm currently planning our next free educational video and content calendar we are making with our doctors on Instagram, and I'd love to hear from you directly.

Is there anything specific you'd like to learn more about when it comes to medical aesthetics, skin, confidence, or aesthetic treatments?"

**Message 2 (after they respond):**
"Thank you for sharing that -- it's really helpful!

Just out of curiosity... what's the biggest challenge you're facing right now when it comes to your skin, face, or body goals?"

**Message 3 (after 3rd response):**
"That's such a common concern -- and it's something we help clients with every day here at the clinic.

Depending on what you're looking for, we may be able to support you with [insert treatment type, e.g., skin treatments, body contouring, injectables].

If you'd like, I can arrange a free consultation with our aesthetic doctor to walk you through your options -- no pressure, just expert advice. Would that be helpful?"
**Key elements to check:**
- Opens with gratitude for the follow
- Asks for content input (engagement hook)
- Transitions naturally from content to concerns to consultation offer
- Never pitches directly in message 1
- Free consultation offer in message 3
- No-pressure framing throughout

### Scenario 12: Corporate Collaboration Outreach (Email Only)
**Trigger:** Outreach to companies for employee wellness partnerships
**Script:**
"Hi [Company Contact Name],

I hope you're well. My name is Sarah and I'm reaching out on behalf of Carisma Aesthetics -- Malta's highest-rated medical aesthetics clinic, located in St. Julian's.

We're currently inviting a select number of companies to offer their teams an exclusive 15% discount on all treatments, as part of their employee wellness benefits.

This includes popular services such as:
- Botox
- Fillers
- Laser Hair removal
- Skin rejuvenation, slimming injectables, and anti-aging treatments

-- all delivered by experienced medical professionals in a safe, luxury setting.

There's no cost or commitment required from your side -- employees simply mention your company name when booking, and we'll take care of the rest. It's an easy way to offer your staff a meaningful perk that supports their confidence and well-being.

You can learn more about us at www.carismaaesthetics.com

Let me know if this would be of interest. I'd love to help you bring this benefit to your team.

With Care,
Sarah"
**Key elements to check:**
- Professional tone
- Brand positioning ("Malta's highest-rated")
- Clear offer (15% discount, no cost to company)
- Lists specific treatments
- Easy mechanism (just mention company name)
- Includes website link
- Warm sign-off
