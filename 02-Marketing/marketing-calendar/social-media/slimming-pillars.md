### Social Media Voice Principles

**How Carisma Slimming sounds on social:**
- **Compassionate and validating.** We see them before we guide them. "Yes, this is hard. Yes, you've been trying. Yes, I believe you."
- **Evidence-led but human.** Every claim has receipts, but data never arrives cold. Science through a warm lens.
- **Empowering, not rescuing.** They are the hero. We are the guide. "You choose. We support."
- **Honest about difficulty.** We don't pretend it's easy. "This will be uncomfortable. That's where change lives."
- **Future-focused but present-centered.** We paint a real, achievable future while anchoring in today's small wins.

### Key Phrases to Weave In

- "You didn't fail; the plan failed you"

- "Pasta and wine still on the menu"

### Forbidden Language on Social

| Category | Never Use |
|----------|-----------|
| **Body shaming** | "Fix your body," "problem areas," "get rid of your belly," "disgusting fat," "embarrassing flab," "let yourself go" |
| **Blame/discipline framing** | "You lack discipline," "stop making excuses," "just try harder," "if you really wanted it" |
| **Toxic positivity** | "Just love yourself and it'll work out," "mindset is everything," "positive vibes only" |
| **Over-promising** | "Lose 10kg in 10 days," "guaranteed transformation," "results for everyone, no effort required," "melt away fat overnight" |
| **Fear-mongering** | "Your body is a ticking time bomb," "you'll get sick if you don't act now," "regret it forever" |
| **Reductive language** | "Drop 2 dress sizes," "goal weight," "beach body," "bikini ready," reducing someone to a number |
| **Sales pressure** | "Book NOW before it's too late!", "Only 3 spots left!", "Don't miss out!", "Flash sale!" |
| **Clinical jargon** | "Caloric recalibration protocol," "metabolic optimization," "cryolipolysis-induced apoptosis" |
| **Trendy slang** | "Slay queen," "literally life-changing bestie," "no cap this works," "OMG the results are insane" |
| **AI tells** | "Certainly!", "I'd be happy to," "Of course!", "Absolutely!", "Great question!" |
| **Em-dashes** | Never use em-dashes in any copy. Use periods, commas, or "and" instead. |

### The North Star for Every Post

> "Will this message help a woman feel seen, believed, supported, and capable of building a better life in her own body?"
>
> If yes, post it. If no, delete it.

## 3. Pillar 1: Pain-Solution

**Strategic Purpose:** Meet women where they are. The Pain-Solution pillar names the frustration they live with every day, validates their experience, and shows that a clear, medically-guided path exists. This is the workhorse pillar for converting awareness into free consultation bookings.

**Pillar Formula:**
1. Hook with a relatable weight/body concern (specific, vivid, real)
2. Validate the struggle (you are not alone, this is common, this is not your fault)
3. Educate on why it happens (science through a human lens)
4. Introduce Carisma's approach as a different path
5. Invite them to take the next step (consultation, DM, link in bio)

**Voice Reminder:** Validate before solving. Never say "fix your body." Say "a lighter, more energised version of how you want to feel is possible."

## 13. Do's and Don'ts (Social Media Guardrails)

### DO

1. **DO lead with the human, not the brand.** Faces outperform logos. Katya, Dr. Zaid, or real clients on camera always outperform branded graphics.
2. **DO validate before you sell.** Give validation and education freely. The consultation booking comes naturally when people trust you.
3. **DO respond to every comment and DM.** Social media is a conversation, not a billboard. Engagement signals drive algorithm distribution.
4. **DO use "Day 1 / Day X" framing** for all results content. This is compliant with Meta's policies and avoids "before and after" restrictions.
5. **DO show the process, not just the result.** Consultations, treatment footage, the body scan, the weekly check-in. Transparency builds trust.
6. **DO use on-screen captions on all video content.** 81%+ of social media video is watched without sound.
7. **DO normalize struggle.** Show that the journey has hard weeks, plateaus, slip-ups. Show how Carisma handles those moments.
8. **DO include Maltese cultural context.** References to pastizzi, festa season, Sunday lunch, beach culture, and late dinners make content feel local and real.
9. **DO batch-film content.** Schedule a 2-3 hour filming session weekly. Film 5-10 pieces in one session.
10. **DO repurpose content across platforms.** One Reel can become a TikTok, a Story series, a carousel, and a Facebook post with different framing.

### DON'T

1. **DON'T use fear-based language.** Never post anything that makes someone feel broken, disgusting, or urgently in need of fixing.
2. **DON'T use before/after terminology.** Meta will flag and restrict weight-loss before/after content.
3. **DON'T show close-up body parts designed to create insecurity.** No zoomed-in belly fat, no pinching skin, no circled "problem areas."
4. **DON'T use urgency or scarcity language organically.** No "only 3 spots left!" or "book before midnight!"
5. **DON'T make specific weight-loss guarantees in social content.** Use "up to 1kg per week" and "results vary."
6. **DON'T bash competitors or other weight-loss programs.** Frame as "a different approach" not "they're wrong."
7. **DON'T post and ghost.** Stay on the platform for 30 minutes after posting to respond to early engagement.
8. **DON'T use AI tells in captions.** No "Certainly!", "I'd be happy to!", "Great question!". Katya sounds human.
9. **DON'T use em-dashes in any copy.** Use periods, commas, or "and" instead.
10. **DON'T reduce women to a number.** Never lead with "lose X kg" as the primary promise. Lead with how they'll feel.
11. **DON'T use trending slang that conflicts with the brand voice.** No "slay," "bestie," "no cap." Warm and compassionate, not trendy.
12. **DON'T ignore negative comments.** Respond with grace: "We'd love to help. DM us or call +356 27802062."
