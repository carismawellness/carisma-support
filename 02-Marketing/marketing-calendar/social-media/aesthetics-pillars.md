| **Em-dashes** | Never use em-dashes in any copy. Use periods, commas, or "and" instead. |

### The North Star for Every Post

> "Will this post help someone feel confident, informed, safe, and gently invited to enhance their natural beauty?"
>
> If yes, post it. If no, delete it.

## 2. Content Pillar Overview

| Pillar | Definition | Purpose | Ratio |
|--------|-----------|---------|-------|
| **Pain-Solution** | Agitate a frustration, then offer your unique approach | Convert problem-aware audiences into consultation-curious leads | 30% |
| **Hooked Insight** | Deliver a surprising, disruptive or little-known fact or pro-level tip to stop the scroll | Build authority and shareability by educating on treatments | 30% |
| **Objection Flip** | Address and dismantle common patient fears, concerns, or myths | Build trust with hesitant audiences and reduce barriers to booking | 20% |
| **Viral Replication** | Go on trending content and replicate formats for the aesthetics space | Maximise reach and ride algorithm momentum | 20% |

**How pillar ratios translate to a 5-post week:**
- 1-2 Pain-Solution posts
- 1-2 Hooked Insight posts
- 1 Objection Flip post
- 1 Viral Replication post

(Rotate so every pillar gets represented each week.)

## 3. Pillar 1: Pain-Solution

**Strategic Purpose:** Meet patients where they are. The Pain-Solution pillar names the frustration they are living with every day, validates their experience, and then shows that a clear, expert-led path exists. This is the workhorse pillar for converting awareness into consultations.

**Pillar Formula:**
1. Name the pain (specific, vivid, relatable)
2. Validate the struggle (you are not alone, this is common)
3. Explain why common solutions have failed them
4. Introduce the Carisma approach as a different path
5. Invite them to take the next step (consultation, DM, link in bio)

**Voice Reminder:** Enhancement, not correction. Never say "fix your skin." Say "a clearer, more confident complexion is possible."

## 6. Pillar 4: Viral Replication

**Strategic Purpose:** The algorithm rewards formats it recognises as trending. The Viral Replication pillar takes trending content structures, audio, and formats from outside the aesthetics world and adapts them with a Carisma Aesthetics lens. This maximises reach, attracts new audiences, and keeps the feed feeling fresh and current.

**Pillar Formula:**
1. Identify the trending format, audio, or concept
2. Adapt it to an aesthetics context while maintaining the Carisma voice
3. Produce quickly (trends have a 1-2 week window)
4. Keep it authentic (not forced, not cringe)
5. Always connect back to expertise, treatments, or patient experience

---

### 6.1 How to Identify Replicable Trends

**Daily monitoring routine (15 minutes):**
1. Open TikTok and Instagram Reels Explore pages
2. Note any formats appearing 3+ times from different creators
3. Check if the format can be adapted to aesthetics (most can)
4. Save trending audio immediately (audio trends expire fast)
5. Check what aesthetics competitors in the UK, US, and Australia are doing with the trend

**Signals that a trend is worth replicating:**
- Multiple non-aesthetics creators using the same format
- The format has a clear structure that can be adapted
- The audio is trending and not yet oversaturated
- The format allows you to be educational, funny, or relatable
- It can be filmed quickly in the clinic or with minimal setup

**Signals to skip a trend:**
- It requires self-deprecation or body shaming
- It conflicts with the Carisma brand voice (urgency, fear, slang)
- The audio has problematic lyrics or associations
- It is already oversaturated in the aesthetics niche
- It would look forced or inauthentic for the brand

## 11. Do's and Don'ts (Social Media Guardrails)

### DO

1. **Lead with the human, not the brand.** Faces outperform logos. Practitioner-led content outperforms branded graphics. Put people on screen.
2. **Educate before you sell.** Give value freely. The sale comes naturally when people trust your expertise.
3. **Respond to every comment and DM.** Social media is a conversation, not a billboard. Engagement signals drive algorithm distribution.
4. **Use "Day 1 / Day 30" framing** instead of "before and after" for results content. Compliant with Meta's policies.
5. **Show the process, not just the result.** Consultations, treatment footage, healing timelines. Transparency builds trust.
6. **Use on-screen captions on all video.** 81%+ of social media video is watched without sound.
7. **Batch-film content.** 2-3 hour filming sessions weekly. Film 5-10 pieces in one session.
8. **Validate concerns before answering.** "That's completely understandable. Here's the reality..."
9. **Credit trending audio and formats.** Use within first 3-5 days of trending.
10. **Repurpose across platforms.** One Reel becomes a TikTok, Story series, carousel, and Facebook post.
11. **Maintain consistency.** The algorithm rewards consistent posting over volume spikes.
12. **Use the Sarah persona as the connective voice** across all platforms.

### DON'T

1. **Don't use fear-based language.** Never make someone feel broken or urgently in need. Enhancement, not correction.
2. **Don't use before/after terminology.** Use "progress," "journey," "Day 1 to Day 30." Meta flags before/after.
3. **Don't show close-ups designed to create insecurity.** No zoomed-in wrinkles, pore magnification, or circled "problem areas."
4. **Don't use urgency or scarcity organically.** No "only 3 spots left!" Reserve for truthful paid advertising only.
5. **Don't post overly polished "stock photo" content.** Authenticity outperforms polish 4x on click-through.
6. **Don't make medical claims.** "Results vary" and "consultation required" are required disclaimers.
7. **Don't bash competitors.** Never name or imply criticism of another clinic.
8. **Don't use AI tells in captions.** The Sarah persona sounds human, not robotic.
9. **Don't use em-dashes.** Periods, commas, or "and" instead.
10. **Don't use trending slang.** No "slay," "bestie," "no cap," "literally obsessed."
11. **Don't post and ghost.** Stay on the platform 30 minutes after posting for early engagement.
12. **Don't repost identical content across platforms.** Adapt format and caption for each.
13. **Don't ignore negative comments.** Respond with grace: "We'd love to make this right. Please DM us or call +356 27802062."

## 12. Content Creation Checklist

Before posting any piece of content, run through this:

- [ ] Does it pass the North Star test? ("Will this help someone feel confident, informed, safe, and gently invited to enhance their natural beauty?")
- [ ] Does it use enhancement language, not correction language?
- [ ] Are on-screen captions included on all video?
- [ ] Is the hook in the first 1.5 seconds (video) or the first line (caption)?
- [ ] Does it educate, entertain, or build trust?
- [ ] Is there a clear CTA (even if soft)?
- [ ] Does it feel like Sarah wrote/said it?
- [ ] Would a first-timer feel welcome reading/watching this?
- [ ] Is it platform-appropriate (right format, right length, right energy)?
- [ ] Are hashtags placed correctly?
- [ ] Is it free of forbidden language?
- [ ] Does it maintain the "philosophy of restraint" in how it talks about results?
- [ ] Have Maltese cultural references been included where relevant?
- [ ] Is the practitioner or team member credited appropriately?
