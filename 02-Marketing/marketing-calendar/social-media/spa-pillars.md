### Social Media Voice Principles

**How Carisma Spa sounds on social:**
- **Warm and enveloping.** Like stepping into a heated treatment room. Every word should feel like an exhale. Comfort first, information second.
- **Sensory and evocative.** Paint pictures with words. Warm stones on tired shoulders. The scent of eucalyptus as you breathe deeply. Cool marble under bare feet. The sound of water in a quiet room.
- **Elegant but never pretentious.** Luxury that welcomes, not excludes. Premium quality communicated through feeling, not price tags.
- **Permission-giving.** Every post should gently tell her: "You are allowed to stop. You are allowed to receive. This is not indulgence. This is care."
- **Grounded in Malta.** Mediterranean light, island pace, local warmth. Carisma Spa is a Maltese sanctuary, not a generic luxury brand.

### Forbidden Language on Social

| Category | Never Use |
|----------|-----------|
| **Clinical framing** | "Treatment protocol," "therapeutic intervention," "clinical results," "medical grade," "pathology" |
| **Sales pressure** | "Book NOW!", "Limited availability!", "Don't miss out!", "Only X spots left!", "Flash sale!", "Hurry!" |
| **Trendy slang** | "Slay," "self-care queen bestie," "literally life-changing," "OMG this spa is insane," "no cap" |
| **Guilt-inducing** | "You owe it to yourself" (implies debt), "Stop neglecting yourself" (implies blame), "What's your excuse?" |
| **Over-promising** | "Transform your life," "guaranteed relaxation," "permanent stress relief," "cure your burnout" |
| **Generic luxury cliches** | "World-class," "five-star experience," "unparalleled luxury," "best in Malta," "exclusive," "elite" |
| **Pushy wellness** | "You NEED this," "Your body is screaming for help," "You can't keep going like this" |
| **AI tells** | "Certainly!", "I'd be happy to," "Of course!", "Absolutely!", "Great question!" |
| **Em-dashes** | Never use em-dashes in any copy. Use periods, commas, or "and" instead. |

### The North Star for Every Post

> "Will this post help a woman feel invited, deserving, curious, and gently drawn toward a moment of stillness and care?"
>
> If yes, post it. If no, delete it.

## 3. Pillar 1: Pain-Solution

**Strategic Purpose:** Meet women where they are. The Pain-Solution pillar names the lifestyle frustration she is living with every day, validates her experience, and shows that a clear, sensory, restorative path exists. Unlike the clinical sister brands, this pillar leads with feeling, not diagnosis. The pain is emotional and physical exhaustion. The solution is permission to pause.

**Pillar Formula:**
1. Name the pain (specific, vivid, relatable)
2. Validate the struggle (you are not alone, this is real, this is not selfish)
3. Explain why her current "solutions" are not working (wine, scrolling, a hot bath at home)
4. Introduce the Carisma Spa experience as a different kind of rest
5. Invite her to take the next step (book, DM, link in bio, gift voucher)

**Voice Reminder:** Permission, not prescription. Never say "fix your stress." Say "your body is asking for stillness. We know how to give it."

3. "Burnout doesn't always look like breaking down. Sometimes it looks like getting through every day on autopilot."
4. "The most exhausted women I know never admit they're exhausted. They just keep going. This is for them."
5. "When was the last time someone looked after you the way you look after everyone else?"

```
You've been "fine" for six months. But your shoulders are knotted. Your sleep is shallow. You can't remember the last time you exhaled properly.

This isn't fine. This is your body asking for something you keep putting last.

A spa day is not a luxury. It's a reset. Two hours where nobody needs you, nobody asks you for anything, and someone's only job is to make you feel good.

At Carisma Spa, we've designed our Spa Day packages around exactly this. Arrive tense. Sink into warmth. Feel hands that know exactly where you hold your stress. Leave lighter. Softer. More like yourself.

You take care of everyone. Let someone take care of you.

Book your spa day via the link in bio. Or DM us "PAUSE" and we'll help you choose.

Peacefully, Sarah
```

4. Text slide: "A Carisma Spa Day. Two hours. No phone. No to-do list. Just warmth."

**Platform-Specific Notes:**

- **Facebook:** Longer caption with the full emotional arc. This topic resonates deeply with the 35-55 demographic on Facebook. Pair with a calm, warm photo of the spa environment.
- **TikTok:** "POV: You finally booked the spa day" format works well. Raw, authentic, less polished than Instagram.

**Brand Voice Guardrails for This Topic:**
- DO: Use second person ("you"). Paint the feeling of arrival, of release. Reference the roles she plays (mum, partner, colleague, carer).
- DO NOT: Use guilt ("you owe it to yourself") or imply she is broken. Never frame the spa as a reward for suffering. Frame it as care she deserves regardless.

## 6. Pillar 4: Viral Replication

**Strategic Purpose:** The algorithm rewards formats it recognises as trending. **Pillar Formula:**
1. Identify the trending format, audio, or concept
2. Adapt it for the spa/wellness context while maintaining the warm, permission-giving brand voice
3. Produce quickly (trends have a 1-2 week window)
4. Keep it authentic (not forced, not cringe)
5. Always connect back to the sensory experience, treatments, or the feeling of being cared for

### 6.1 How to Identify Replicable Trends

**Daily monitoring routine (15 minutes):**
1. Open TikTok and Instagram Reels Explore pages
2. Note any formats appearing 3+ times from different creators
3. Check if the format can be adapted to spa/wellness (most can)
4. Save trending audio immediately (audio trends expire fast)
5. Check what luxury hotel spas and wellness brands in the UK, US, and Bali are doing with the trend

**Signals that a trend is worth replicating:**
- Multiple non-spa creators using the same format
- The format has a clear structure that can be adapted
- The audio is trending and not yet oversaturated
- The format allows you to be sensory, warm, or gently humorous
- It can be filmed in the spa with minimal setup

**Signals to skip a trend:**
- It requires self-deprecation, body shaming, or negativity
- It conflicts with the Carisma brand voice (urgency, fear, slang, aggression)
- The audio has problematic lyrics or associations
- It is already oversaturated in the wellness niche
- It would look forced or inauthentic for the brand

## 11. Do's and Don'ts (Social Media Guardrails)

### DO

1. **Lead with feeling, not features.** Describe the sensation, the exhale, the warmth. Treatments are vehicles for feelings, not products to sell.
2. **Give permission.** Every post should tell her she is allowed to rest, to receive, to prioritise herself.
3. **Use sensory language.** Warm, soft, slow, deep, gentle, golden, still. Paint pictures with words.
4. **Respond to every comment and DM.** Social media is a conversation. Engagement signals drive algorithm distribution.
5. **Show the environment.** The spa space itself is the content. Treatment rooms, pools, robes, products, lighting.
6. **Use on-screen captions on all video.** 81%+ of social media video is watched without sound.
7. **Batch-film content.** 2-3 hour filming sessions monthly. Film 10-15 pieces in one session.
8. **Validate before inviting.** "I see you. I understand. And when you're ready, we're here."
9. **Reference Malta.** The island, the lifestyle, the light, the pace. Carisma is Maltese.
10. **Repurpose across platforms.** One Reel becomes a TikTok, Story series, carousel, and Facebook post (adapted for each).
11. **Maintain consistency.** The algorithm rewards consistent posting over volume spikes.
12. **Use the Sarah persona as the connective voice** across all platforms. Sign off with "Peacefully, Sarah."

### DON'T

1. **Don't use clinical language.** This is not Carisma Aesthetics. No "treatment protocols," "clinical results," or "therapeutic interventions." Use "experience," "session," "journey."
2. **Don't use guilt.** Never "you owe it to yourself" or "stop neglecting yourself." Permission, not guilt.
3. **Don't use urgency or scarcity organically.** No "only 3 spots left!" Reserve for truthful paid advertising only.
4. **Don't post stock-looking content.** Authenticity outperforms polish. Show your real spa, your real therapists, your real guests (with consent).
5. **Don't use "luxury" as a crutch.** Describe the experience, don't label it. Show, don't tell.
6. **Don't make it clinical.** If it sounds like it belongs on the Aesthetics page, rewrite it with more warmth and sensory language.
7. **Don't bash other spas or salons.** Never compare. Simply show the Carisma experience.
8. **Don't use AI tells in captions.** The Sarah persona sounds human, warm, and real.
9. **Don't use em-dashes.** Periods, commas, or "and" instead.
10. **Don't use trending slang.** No "slay," "bestie," "no cap," "literally obsessed."
11. **Don't post and ghost.** Stay on the platform 30 minutes after posting for early engagement.
12. **Don't repost identical content across platforms.** Adapt format and caption for each.

## 12. Content Creation Checklist

- [ ] Does it pass the North Star test? ("Will this help a woman feel invited, deserving, curious, and gently drawn toward a moment of stillness and care?")

- [ ] Does it give permission, educate, or build trust?
- [ ] Is there a clear CTA (even if soft)?

- [ ] Would a first-timer feel welcomed and curious reading or watching this?
- [ ] Is it platform-appropriate (right format, right length, right energy)?
- [ ] Are hashtags placed correctly?
- [ ] Is it free of forbidden language?
- [ ] Does it evoke a feeling (warmth, calm, permission, curiosity)?
- [ ] Have Maltese cultural references been included where relevant?
- [ ] Is the therapist or team member credited appropriately (if featured)?
