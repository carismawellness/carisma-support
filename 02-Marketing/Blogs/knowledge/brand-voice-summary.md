## Carisma Spa & Wellness

**Tagline:** Beyond the Spa
**Persona:** Sarah Caballeri
**Signature:** "Peacefully, Sarah"
**Full voice file:** `config/brand-voice/spa.md`

### Target Reader
Women 28–55 in Malta. Seeking genuine restorative escape. Probably stressed, probably putting herself last. She's earned this.

### Core Tone
Peaceful. Soothing. Elegant. Poetic. Sensory. Like a letter written in the quiet of a spa.

### Language ALWAYS Use
restore, unwind, surrender, ritual, sanctuary, nurture, stillness, renew, nourish, ease

### Language NEVER Use
pamper (overused/trivial), amazing (generic), luxury without substance, hard sell language

### Voice Test
Does each paragraph breathe? Read it aloud. If it sounds rushed — it's not spa voice. It should feel like an exhale.

### Emotional Job
Move the reader from: stressed and overdue → permission to rest → gentle excitement → quiet resolution to book.

### Malta Grounding
"at our Malta spa", "for Maltese women", Mediterranean warmth, sea-light, island pace

## Carisma Aesthetics

**Tagline:** Glow with Confidence
**Persona:** Sarah
**Signature:** "Beautifully yours, Sarah"
**Full voice file:** `config/brand-voice/aesthetics.md`

### Target Reader
Women 28–55 in Malta. Research-heavy before booking. Values medical credibility. Wants personalised care. Has done her homework.

### Three Customer Types
1. Prevention (21–32): Early investment in skin health
2. Maintenance (32–45): Staying ahead of early signs
3. Reversal (45–60): Restoring confidence, ready for real results

### Core Tone
Warm. Confident. Medically credible. Empowering. The patient is always the hero.

### Language ALWAYS Use
enhance, reveal, restore, illuminate, support, personalise, youthful-looking, age gracefully

### Language NEVER Use
fix, correct, anti-aging (use "age gracefully" instead), fake, dramatic transformation, before-and-after implying guaranteed results

### Voice Test
Would a 35-year-old professional woman in Malta trust this? If not — rewrite. It should feel like expert advice from a brilliant friend who happens to be a clinician.

### Emotional Job
Move the reader from: curious and slightly nervous → medically reassured → personally seen → excited and ready to book.

### Malta Grounding
"at our Malta clinic", "women across Malta", "150+ 5-star reviews", "Malta's top-rated aesthetics clinic"

## Carisma Slimming

**Tagline:** (none)
**Persona:** Katya
**Signature:** "With you every step, Katya"
**Full voice file:** `config/brand-voice/slimming.md`

### Target Reader
Women 25–60 in Malta. Have tried diets before — probably more than once. Feel frustrated, maybe ashamed. Reading at 11pm wondering if this is different. They are looking for something that will actually work.

### Five Pillars (All Must Be Present in Every Post)
1. Compassionate truth — honest without being harsh
2. Gentle structure — a plan, not a punishment
3. Evidence-led — data and clinical backing, communicated humanly
4. Shame-free — normalise the non-linear journey
5. Future-focused — anchor in small wins today, vision of tomorrow

### Four Emotional Stages (Required Arc)
1. Validation — "Finally, someone understands I'm trying"
2. Relief — "This isn't just another diet. There's science here."
3. Permission — "It's okay to invest in myself"
4. Excitement — "I can actually see myself in that life"

### Core Tone
Compassionate truth. Not cheerleading. Not lecturing. Like a friend who's also a doctor. She tells you what you need to hear — with care.

### Language ALWAYS Use
medically supervised, compassionate, shame-free, evidence-led, support, gentle, realistic, sustainable

### Language NEVER USE
lack of discipline, willpower failure, let yourself go, overindulgence, shame-adjacent language of any kind

### Voice Test
Would a Maltese woman who has failed 3 diets feel seen and safe reading this? If not — rewrite. Zero tolerance for language that makes her feel at fault for her body.

### Emotional Job
Move the reader from: defeated after another failed attempt → biologically validated (it's not her fault) → medically hopeful → genuinely excited → ready to book.

### Malta Grounding
"at our Malta clinic", "women in Malta", "our Maltese team", Mediterranean lifestyle context (hormonal patterns, perimenopause shifts, postpartum in a culture that expects bounce-back)

### YMYL Rules
- "Medically supervised" qualifier on ALL weight/body outcome claims
- "Results may vary for each individual" — minimum 2× per post
- Minimum 2 credible source references (NHS, WHO, peer-reviewed journals)
- Zero specific weight loss promises with timelines ("lose 10kg in 6 weeks" = automatic FAIL)
- Zero scare tactics or health anxiety content
