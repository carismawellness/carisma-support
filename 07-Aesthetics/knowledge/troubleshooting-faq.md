### "Which treatment is right for me?"
That depends on your goals. Here's a quick guide:

**For skin quality:**
- Instant glow → HydraFacial
- Texture and scarring → Microneedling, chemical peels
- Deep rejuvenation → PRP, mesotherapy, exosome GlowLift

**For body concerns:**
- Stubborn fat → Fat freezing or fat dissolving
- Muscle tone → Muscle stimulation
- Cellulite → Anti-cellulite treatments
- General detox → Lymphatic drainage

- **Laser:** Warm sensation. Settings adjusted for comfort. 3-5/10.
- **Fat freezing:** Cold and pressure initially. Numbness develops quickly.

## Section 7: Safety & Side Effects

### "What are the side effects?"
Common side effects vary by treatment:
- **Injectables:** Mild swelling, bruising, redness at injection sites. Usually resolves in 1-3 days.
- **Laser:** Temporary redness, sensitivity. Usually resolves within 24-48 hours.
- **Chemical peels:** Peeling, redness, sensitivity for 3-7 days depending on depth.
- **Microneedling:** Redness for 24-48 hours. Similar to mild sunburn.
- **Body treatments:** Temporary numbness, swelling, or tenderness in treated area.

**Sarah's voice:** "I'll walk you through everything to expect before we start. Most side effects are mild and temporary."

---

### "Is it safe?"
All Carisma Aesthetics treatments are:
- Performed by qualified medical professionals
- Using FDA-cleared or CE-marked equipment and products
- Preceded by full medical history assessment
- Covered by informed consent process
- Supported by emergency protocols

**Sarah's voice:** "Your safety is always our first priority. Every treatment follows strict medical protocols."

---

### "Are there any contraindications?"
Common contraindications include:
- Pregnancy or breastfeeding (for most treatments)
- Active skin infections in treatment area
- Certain medications (blood thinners for injectables)
- History of severe allergic reactions to treatment components
- Certain autoimmune conditions

Always disclose your full medical history during consultation.

---

### "What should I do/avoid before my treatment?"
General pre-treatment guidelines:
- Avoid blood thinners (aspirin, ibuprofen) for 7 days before injectables
- No alcohol 24 hours before treatment
- Arrive with clean skin (no makeup for facial treatments)
- Avoid sun exposure before laser treatments
- Disclose all medications and supplements

---

### "What should I do after my treatment?"
General post-treatment guidelines:
- Avoid touching or massaging treated areas (injectables)
- No strenuous exercise for 24 hours
- Avoid direct sun exposure (use SPF)
- No saunas, steam rooms, or hot baths for 24-48 hours
- Follow specific aftercare instructions provided by your practitioner

## Section 8: Specific Treatment Questions

### "What's the difference between Botox and fillers?"
- **Botox (wrinkle relaxers):** Relaxes muscles that cause dynamic wrinkles (forehead lines, crow's feet, frown lines). Best for lines that appear with movement.
- **Fillers:** Add volume and structure. Best for static lines, lip enhancement, cheek contouring, and areas that have lost volume.

Many patients benefit from both. Your consultation will determine the right approach.

**Sarah's voice:** "Botox relaxes movement lines. Fillers add volume. Many people benefit from both. I'll recommend what works for your specific goals."

---

### "What is Profhilo?"
Profhilo is an injectable bio-remodelling treatment. It spreads beneath the skin to deeply hydrate and stimulate collagen and elastin production. Typically requires 2 sessions, 4 weeks apart. Results include firmer, more hydrated, glowing skin.

**Sarah's voice:** "Profhilo gives your skin deep hydration and firmness from within. Two sessions, beautiful results."

---

### "What are polynucleotides?"
Polynucleotides (sometimes called Salmon DNA) are advanced injectable treatments that stimulate cellular regeneration. They improve skin quality, hydration, and elasticity at a cellular level.

---

### "Do you offer medical weight loss?"
Yes. We offer medically supervised weight management including GLP-1 treatments (Ozempic). This requires a medical consultation and ongoing supervision.

---

### "What aftercare do I need for Botox?"
Specific Botox aftercare guidelines:
- **Do not rub or massage** the treated areas for at least 24 hours
- **Delay facials and massages** for a minimum of 24 hours post-treatment
- **Avoid heat exposure** — no saunas, steam rooms, hot baths, or intense sun for 24-48 hours
- **Be careful with blood thinners** — aspirin, ibuprofen, and certain supplements (fish oil, vitamin E) can increase bruising risk. Discuss any medications with your doctor.
- **Stay upright** for 4 hours after treatment (no lying flat)
- **Avoid strenuous exercise** for 24 hours
- **Watch for swelling** — mild swelling is normal and resolves quickly. Contact the clinic if you notice anything unusual.

**Sarah's voice:** "I'll give you clear aftercare instructions on the day. The key things: don't rub the area, skip the gym for a day, and avoid heat. Simple."
