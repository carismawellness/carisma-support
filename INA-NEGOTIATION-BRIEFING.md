**Key Rules for This Sequence:**
- Each move is **smaller than the last** (€27K → €24K → €18K → €5.75K)
- Each move is **conditional** — attach real criteria, not arbitrary
- Final number is **intentionally non-round** (€119,750 not €120K) — signals precision, not more room
- **Hold silence after each anchor.** Do not negotiate against yourself.

## Act 1: Intelligence Gathering (20-30 min)

**Goal:** Learn everything without revealing your playbook.

**Your 5 Questions (in order — stick to this sequence):**

1. **"What's been the biggest challenge since the Numi situation ended?"**
   - *Why:* Discover his pain points, emotional state, urgency
   - *Listen for:* Cash burn, competitive threats, operational issues
   - *Your note:* This tells you his real walk-away number

2. **"What does the monthly picture look like — is it covering its costs?"**
   - *Why:* Confirm revenue vs. your assumptions. Get him to admit losses.
   - *Listen for:* Honesty about €20-25K revenue, rent level, payroll challenges
   - *Your note:* If he says it's breaking even, he's either lying or costs are lower than you think

3. **"What would a successful resolution look like for you personally?"**
   - *Why:* Uncover what "win" means to him (clean exit? Preservation of brand? Employee protection?)
   - *Listen for:* Values beyond just price. Time pressure. Ego protection.
   - *Your note:* This is where you find leverage beyond money

4. **"What's your ideal timeframe?"**
   - *Why:* Establish urgency. Every month costs him €1-2K in losses.
   - *Listen for:* "ASAP," "by Q1," "within 60 days" = high motivation
   - *Your note:* Use timeline as concession trade (e.g., "I can close faster if we land on price")

5. **"Have you had other conversations with interested parties?"**
   - *Why:* Gauge competition. Single buyer = more leverage for you.
   - *Listen for:* "Just exploratory," "nothing serious," "you're the first serious one" = zero competition
   - *Your note:* If there are others, you need to move. If not, you have time.

**Tactical Moves During Act 1:**
- **Mirror:** He says "revenue dropped 40%." You: "Revenue dropped 40%?" [silence, let him elaborate]
- **Label emotions:** "It sounds like you're frustrated with how much capital got tied up."
- **Calibrated question:** "How am I supposed to make sense of the investment you put in vs. what the market will pay for it?"

## Act 2: Reframe Value (20-30 min)

**Goal:** Redirect from sunk cost ("I invested €1M") to forward earnings ("This business generates €84K EBITDA").

**Your Three-Part Script:**

**Part A: Acknowledge the Sunk Cost (Empathy)**
> "I get it. You've invested real capital—€1M—into this space. I can see why you'd look at that number and feel the price should reflect it. You put in the work, the money, the risk."

**Part B: Introduce Market Reality (Logic)**
> "But here's what the market sees: Not what it cost to build. Not what it could earn if someone else runs it differently. The market asks: What does this business earn today? Right now. At this revenue level, with this rent, with this team. That's what acquisition pricing is based on. That's not a judgment of you. That's just how it works."

**Part C: The Pain Gap (The Redirect)**
> "Here's the thing: The €1M difference between what you invested and what the fair market value is—let's call that €800-850K loss—that's already gone. Whether you sell today or wait two years, that loss happened. Selling doesn't create the loss. Selling stops the additional losses from continuing."

**Then present your numbers:**
> "Current EBITDA at this revenue and rent level: approximately €84K annually. Using standard market multiples for wellness businesses (1-2x EBITDA given the risk and the operator-dependent model), fair value is roughly €84K to €170K. I'm proposing we start that conversation at €45K and work toward fair value from there."

**Equipment Appraisal Move:**
> "I want to be fair here. Why don't we get a third-party equipment appraisal? Independent appraiser values the moveable assets. That's not a judgment—that's just letting the market tell us what it's worth. If the appraisal comes in at €150K, we both feel better about the number."
