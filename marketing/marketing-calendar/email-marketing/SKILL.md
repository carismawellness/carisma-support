## 2. Quick-Start Workflow

When writing any email for any Carisma brand, follow this sequence:

### Step 1: Identify the Brand
Which brand is this email for? This determines persona, tone, vocabulary, CTA style, and footer elements.

### Step 2: Identify the Email Content Type
Which of the 7 universal content types? (See Section 3 below.) Each type has a distinct structure, purpose, and emotional register.

### Step 3: Identify the Context
- **Flow email** -- Which flow? Which position in the sequence? What is the trigger?
- **Broadcast/campaign** -- Which week of the month? What seasonal angle?
- **Seasonal campaign** -- Which season and which campaign burst?

### Step 4: Load the Brand-Specific Reference File
Open the relevant brand strategy document for:
- Detailed content pillar craft guidance
- Brand-specific subject line banks (15-16 per type)
- Emotional register definitions
- Approved brand phrases
- Treatment/service-specific angles

### Step 5: Choose the Emotional Register
Name it at the top of your draft before writing a word. Each brand defines its own registers:

| Spa Registers | Aesthetics Registers | Slimming Registers |
|--------------|---------------------|-------------------|
| Stillness | Confidence | Validation |
| Warmth | Authority | Curiosity |
| Awe | Empathy | Safety |
| Belonging | Excitement | Hope |
| Permission | Permission | Confidence |
| Curiosity | Curiosity | Permission |
| -- | Trust | -- |

**Rule:** An email operates in ONE register. Never two simultaneously.

### Step 6: Follow the 9-Section Template
Write each section in order (see Section 4 below). Adapt tone and vocabulary per brand.

### Step 7: Apply Brand Voice and Tone
Use the brand's approved vocabulary, power words, sensory triggers, and emotional vocabulary. Translate any clinical language into human experience.

### Step 8: Run the Pre-Send Checklist
Every item. No exceptions. (See Section 11.)

## 3. The 7 Universal Email Content Types

All three Carisma brands use the same 7 email content types. The strategic purpose, structure, and psychology are universal. The tone, examples, and angles adapt per brand.

### Content Type Interaction Map

The 7 types form a persuasion system that moves readers from awareness to action:

```
Curiosity (Type 6: Hooked Insight) --> Authority (Type 2: Insider Secrets) --> Recognition (Type 3: Pain-Solution)
    ^                                                                                     |
    |                                                                                     v
Differentiation (Type 7: Us vs Them) <-- Permission (Type 5: Objection Flip) <-- Proof (Types 1+4: B&A + Storytime)
```

**Cold subscribers:** Type 6 --> Type 2 --> Type 5 --> Type 1 --> Type 3
**Warm subscribers:** Type 2 --> Type 1 --> Type 3
**Lapsed subscribers:** Type 6 --> Type 4 --> Type 5 --> Type 3

### Type 1: Before & After / Transformation Stories

**Purpose:** Show real, believable transformations through narrative. Build trust that the service works for people "like me." The single most powerful conversion tool across all three brands because it answers: "Will this work for someone who looks/lives/feels like me?"

**Psychology:**
1. Social proof through identification -- she cares about someone who is like her
2. Narrative transportation -- her critical defences lower when she enters a story (Green & Brock, 2000)
3. Specificity builds credibility -- vague claims trigger scepticism; specific details trigger belief

**Universal Structure (250-450 words):**

| Section | Purpose | Word Count |
|---------|---------|-----------|
| Hook/Opening | Start at the emotional turning point, not the beginning (in media res) | 2-3 sentences |
| Before State | Numbers plus emotions. Age, life context, specific frustration, emotional weight | 3-4 sentences |
| The Journey/Treatment | High-level. Enough to signal credibility without giving away the method | 3-4 sentences |
| Results | Specific and honest. Include numbers AND life changes | 2-3 sentences |
| Lesson/Wisdom | The transferable takeaway the reader can use | 2-3 sentences |
| Guest Quote | Verbatim, in quotation marks, attributed. Short. Slightly imperfect. | Pull quote |
| Soft CTA | Invitation to explore, not a push to buy | 1-2 sentences |

**When to Use:** Welcome flow (social proof stage), cart abandon (final persuasion), win-back (remind of the feeling), monthly broadcasts (1-2 per month)

**KPI Targets:** Open rate 30-40%, CTR 4-6%, consultation/booking within 72 hours, forward rate 1-2%

**What Makes It Work vs What Kills It:**

| Works | Kills |
|-------|-------|
| Specific, relatable details (age, context, timeframes) | Generic details that describe anyone |
| Honest portrayal of struggle and plateaus | Making it sound too easy |
| Client as the hero, brand as the guide | Making the brand the hero |
| At least one direct client quote in real language | Superlatives: "amazing!" "incredible!" |
| Results through life changes, not just numbers | Leading with the number/metric instead of the story |
| Authentic images (same lighting, angle, no filters) | Filtered or edited before/after images |

**Subject Line Examples (Cross-Brand):**

| Subject Line | Best For |
|-------------|----------|
| "'I feel like myself again.' -- Maria, after 60 minutes" | Spa |
| "She had the same frown lines as you. Here is what changed." | Aesthetics |
| "47, two kids, office job. Here is what 12 weeks changed." | Slimming |
| "From clenched jaw to steady breath -- in one session" | Spa |
| "She expected dramatic. She got natural. And she cried with relief." | Aesthetics |
| "She sat in the car park for ten minutes before walking in" | Slimming |
| "Before the massage: tension. After: a version of herself she'd forgotten." | Spa |
| "20 years of Maltese sun. One treatment course. The glow is back." | Aesthetics |

### Type 2: Insider Secrets / Therapist's Notes

**Purpose:** Reveal pro-level tips, industry truths, or myths that readers find genuinely valuable. Position Carisma as the honest expert. Build the reciprocity engine -- give real value before asking for anything.

**Psychology:**
1. Information Gap Theory (Loewenstein, 1994) -- curiosity about what she does not know is irresistible
2. Authority through generosity -- giving away knowledge builds trust and eliminates competitors
3. Cognitive reframing -- changing how she thinks about her concern makes her receptive to guidance

**Universal Structure (150-300 words):**

| Section | Purpose |
|---------|---------|
| Counterintuitive Claim / Insider Frame | Lead with the surprise. Pattern interrupt. |
| The Myth / Common Misconception | Show why the common belief exists and why it is wrong |
| The Truth (Simply Explained) | Translate science into sensation and life impact. One analogy. |
| The Proof | One clinical reference or practitioner observation |
| Actionable Takeaway | Something the reader can do today |
| Soft CTA | Bridge from value to consultation/booking |

**When to Use:** Welcome flow (email 2-3, immediate value delivery), monthly broadcasts (highest open rates), between promotional sends (palate cleanser)

**KPI Targets:** Open rate 35-45% (highest of all types), save/forward rate 2-4%, reply rate 1-2%

**Rule:** ONE secret per email. Never two. Concentrated value.

**Subject Line Examples (Cross-Brand):**

| Subject Line | Best For |
|-------------|----------|
| "A therapist's secret: what to do after your massage" | Spa |
| "Most clinics won't tell you this about filler." | Aesthetics |
| "The real reason your belly will not move even when you diet" | Slimming |
| "Water after sauna isn't optional -- it's part of the therapy." | Spa |
| "The one question to ask before anyone touches your face." | Aesthetics |
| "Why your diet keeps working for 3 weeks then stops" | Slimming |
| "Our therapists never skip this step. Neither should you." | Spa |
| "Why your Botox isn't lasting as long as it should." | Aesthetics |

### Type 3: Pain-Solution

**Purpose:** Agitate a specific frustration the reader experiences daily, then offer the brand's unique solution. Bridge the gap between "I hate this" and "there is actually an answer." Converts because it articulates the reader's problem better than she could herself.

**Psychology:**
1. Problem awareness amplification -- naming the exact frustration makes her feel seen
2. Failed-solution stacking -- naming what she tried validates her effort and eliminates alternatives
3. Missing piece architecture -- showing the gap compels the mind to fill it

**Universal Structure (250-400 words):**

| Section | Purpose |
|---------|---------|
| Paint the Pain | One very specific scenario. Sensory detail. Emotional truth. |
| Show What They Have Tried | Name 2-3 specific failed approaches. Validate each attempt. |
| Reveal the Missing Piece | Name the gap their previous approaches left |
| Explain Your Approach | 3 clear, differentiated elements of the Carisma solution |
| Mini Social Proof | Compressed case study or patient quote |
| Soft CTA | Connect the specific pain to the specific solution |

**When to Use:** Welcome flow (conversion stage), monthly broadcasts (highest CTR), cart abandon, seasonal campaigns

**KPI Targets:** Open rate 28-38%, CTR 5-8% (highest of all types), booking conversion, unsubscribe below 0.3%

**Critical Rule:** Empathy is structural, not decorative. The validation of her struggle is the load-bearing wall of the email. If you remove the empathy and the email still works, it was never structural.

**Subject Line Examples (Cross-Brand):**

| Subject Line | Best For |
|-------------|----------|
| "That heaviness in your shoulders? It has a name. And a solution." | Spa |
| "The dark circles that concealer cannot actually fix." | Aesthetics |
| "Still fighting the same 8 kilos every year?" | Slimming |
| "Why your skin looks tired even when you've slept" | Spa |
| "If forehead lines are the first thing you see in the mirror, read this." | Aesthetics |
| "Menopause weight feels different because it is different." | Slimming |

### Type 4: Storytime POV

**Purpose:** Tell a personal or emotional story from a staff, patient, or client perspective that creates deep emotional connection through narrative. Bypasses the logical brain and speaks directly to the heart. These plant emotional seeds that grow into bookings days or weeks later.

**Psychology:**
1. Narrative transportation -- she is experiencing a journey, not evaluating marketing
2. Emotional memory formation -- story emails are remembered long after factual ones fade
3. Identification through specificity -- the more specific the character, the more universal the resonance

**Universal Structure (300-450 words):**

| Section | Purpose |
|---------|---------|
| Emotional Moment (in media res) | Drop in at the most charged moment. Not the beginning. |
| Rewind to Context | Who is she? How did she get here? One sentence of relatable context. |
| Inner Monologue and Fear | Get inside her head. The self-talk. The years of avoidance. |
| The Turning Point | The decision moment. Brief, powerful. Something shifted. |
| The Experience/Treatment | Brief. Described as an experience, not a procedure. |
| The After | Emotional ripple effects. Not just how she looks -- how she lives. |
| Soft CTA | "Your story starts with a conversation." |

**When to Use:** Welcome flow (emotional connection), monthly broadcasts, win-back (emotional reconnection)

**KPI Targets:** Open rate 30-40%, dwell time highest of all types, forward rate 2-5%, delayed conversion 7-14 days

**Writing Rules:**
- Start in the middle, not at the beginning
- Use present tense for immediacy in key scenes
- Sensory details are mandatory
- The treatment is mentioned only in passing -- the focus is the emotional journey
- End on an image, not a statement

**Subject Line Examples (Cross-Brand):**

| Subject Line | Best For |
|-------------|----------|
| "She came for the massage. She stayed for the peace." | Spa |
| "She cried after seeing her results. Here is why." | Aesthetics |
| "She cried in the changing room. Then she did this instead." | Slimming |
| "What happened when they stopped talking and started breathing together" | Spa |
| "She sat in our car park for 20 minutes before coming in." | Aesthetics |
| "The dress she put back on the rack. She is wearing it now." | Slimming |

### Type 5: Objection Flip

**Purpose:** Address and dismantle a common fear or objection head-on. Turn myths into facts. Transform hesitation into informed confidence. The conversion catalyst -- removes the specific barrier standing between the reader and her booking.

**Psychology:**
1. Acknowledging the objection earns trust -- naming the fear word for word drops the defensive wall
2. Radical transparency differentiates -- most brands brush off concerns; Carisma names and addresses them
3. Permission unlocks action -- reframing desire as healthy investment dissolves guilt

**Universal Structure (250-350 words):**

| Section | Purpose |
|---------|---------|
| Name the Fear Word for Word | Mirror their exact language. "What if I just gain it all back?" |
| Validate It | "You are not wrong for thinking this." Counterintuitive but essential. |
| Explain Where It Comes From | Why the fear exists -- bad experiences, media, misinformation |
| Give the Real Picture | Systematically dismantle with evidence and specific practices |
| The Proof | Patient quote or practitioner statement addressing the exact objection |
| The Reassurance / Reframe | Closing statement that repositions the decision |
| Soft CTA | Low-commitment. Consultation, not booking. Learning, not buying. |

**Core Objections by Brand:**

| Objection | Spa Version | Aesthetics Version | Slimming Version |
|-----------|------------|-------------------|-----------------|
| Time | "30 minutes is enough" | "One consultation changes everything" | "You need less time than you think" |
| Money | "EUR 120 is for the week of benefit" | "Confidence investment, not vanity spend" | "Invest in health, not another diet" |
| Fear of outcome | "Wellness is real, not frivolous" | "Looking fake won't happen here" | "Afraid of regain? Here's why this time is different" |
| Guilt/Vanity | "Not indulgence -- restoration" | "Not vanity -- clarity" | "Not selfish -- necessary" |
| Scepticism | "Evidence supports this" | "Safety protocols prevent bad outcomes" | "You tried systems, not the right system" |

**KPI Targets:** Conversion rate highest of all types, CTR 4-8%, first-time bookings from unengaged subscribers

**Subject Line Examples (Cross-Brand):**

| Subject Line | Best For |
|-------------|----------|
| "Is a massage a luxury? Ask your nervous system." | Spa |
| "Afraid of looking fake? Here is why that won't happen here." | Aesthetics |
| "Afraid you will just gain it all back? Read this first." | Slimming |
| "EUR 120 is not for an hour. It's for the week that follows." | Spa |
| "'Will people know?' The honest answer." | Aesthetics |
| "'I will have to give up all the food I love.' Actually, pasta is still on the menu." | Slimming |

### Type 6: Hooked Insight

**Purpose:** Deliver a surprising, disruptive, or little-known fact that stops the scroll and repositions how the reader thinks. Pattern interrupts that break through inbox noise. Positions Carisma as the thought leader who sees what others miss.

**Psychology:**
1. Cognitive dissonance -- challenging a belief compels reading to resolve the contradiction
2. The "aha" dopamine loop -- learning something new triggers neurochemical reward
3. Authority through intellectual generosity -- sharing counterintuitive insights positions above competitors

**Universal Structure (200-300 words):**

| Section | Purpose |
|---------|---------|
| Short, Punchy Insight | 1-2 sentences. The most counterintuitive part first. |
| Story or Mini Example | 2-3 sentences. Ground the insight in reality. |
| Simple Explanation | 2-3 sentences. The science behind the insight, in human language. |
| What Most People Do Wrong | 1-2 sentences. The common mistake. |
| How Carisma Uses This Insight | 2-3 sentences. Bridge to the brand's approach. |
| Simple Action Step | 1-2 sentences. Something she can try today. |

**When to Use:** Welcome flow (authority building), monthly broadcasts (highest share/forward rates), re-engagement

**KPI Targets:** Open rate 35-45% (tied with Insider Secrets for highest), forward/share rate 2-6%, save rate 2-4%

**Rule:** Shorter is better. 200-300 words maximum. More white space. More standalone sentences.

**Subject Line Examples (Cross-Brand):**

| Subject Line | Best For |
|-------------|----------|
| "One hour on the table. Seven days of better sleep." | Spa |
| "Botox doesn't remove wrinkles. It does this instead." | Aesthetics |
| "Losing 5 kilos is not the goal. Here is what is." | Slimming |
| "Heat therapy is not a luxury. It's medicine." | Spa |
| "Your face ages in this specific order. Most treatments ignore it." | Aesthetics |
| "Your environment matters more than your motivation" | Slimming |
| "The data is clear: your body needs this" | Spa |
| "After 25, you lose 1% of your collagen every year. By 50, half is gone." | Aesthetics |

### Type 7: Us vs Them

**Purpose:** Competitive positioning without naming competitors. Differentiate Carisma from alternatives, home treatments, cheap options, and the misinformation-driven DIY culture. Answer: "Why should I choose this over the dozen other options?"

**Psychology:**
1. Category creation -- positioning in a new category rather than competing in an existing one
2. Elimination of alternatives -- naming where each falls short clears the field
3. The Third Option effect -- "You have tried A and B. What if the answer is C?"

**Universal Structure (250-400 words):**

| Section | Purpose |
|---------|---------|
| Name the Common Options | 2-3 sentences. What she usually has to choose from. |
| Acknowledge What Each Is Good For and Where It Fails | 4-6 sentences. Be fair. Strengths then gaps. |
| Introduce Your Third Option | 2-3 sentences. Carisma sits in a category of its own. |
| Show 3 Clear Points of Difference | 3-4 sentences. Specific, provable differentiators. |
| Short Client Example or Quote | 2-3 sentences. Validation from someone who tried alternatives first. |
| Invite Self-Categorisation | 1-2 sentences. "Which category are you stuck in right now?" |

**When to Use:** Welcome flow (final email, positioning before conversion), monthly broadcasts (maximum 1 per month), seasonal campaigns

**KPI Targets:** Open rate 25-35%, CTR 3-7%, new subscriber conversion

**Critical Rule:** NEVER name competitors. Attack categories, not companies. The tone is respectful and factual. Overusing this type sounds desperate -- maximum once per month.

**Subject Line Examples (Cross-Brand):**

| Subject Line | Best For |
|-------------|----------|
| "You wouldn't skip your dentist. Why skip your reset?" | Spa |
| "The difference between a good clinic and the right clinic." | Aesthetics |
| "Diets, gyms, or machines only? There is a smarter fourth option." | Slimming |
| "Wellness is not a reward. It's a rhythm." | Spa |
| "Cheap Botox exists. Here is why we don't offer it." | Aesthetics |
| "We are not a gym. We are not a diet. Here is what we are." | Slimming |

## 4. Universal Email Template Architecture (9 Sections)

Every Carisma email, regardless of brand, follows this 9-section high-conversion template. The structure is universal; the tone adapts per brand.

### Section 1: Subject Line

**Function:** Stop the scroll. Create a micro-moment of curiosity or emotional recognition that earns the open.

**Universal Rules:**
- 40-60 characters maximum (mobile-first)
- Curiosity-driven or emotionally resonant -- NEVER promotional
- Second-person ("you/your") whenever possible
- No urgency, no all-caps, no excessive exclamation marks, no emojis (generally)
- Preview text extends the subject line (90-140 characters), never repeats it

**Brand-Specific Feel:**

| Brand | Subject Line Feels Like... |
|-------|--------------------------|
| Spa | A whisper that makes you lean in |
| Aesthetics | A friend who knows your concern texting: "I need to tell you something" |
| Slimming | A coach who finally understands saying: "Let me explain what is really going on" |

**Anti-Patterns (NEVER, any brand):**
- "FLASH SALE! 30% off!"
- "Don't miss our latest offer!"
- "Book now -- limited availability!"
- "You deserve to treat/pamper yourself!"
- "Lose 10kg in 10 days!"
- "LIMITED Botox appointments!"

### Section 2: Hero Header

**Function:** First line after opening. One bold, short statement that anchors the entire email emotionally.

**Universal Rules:**
- Maximum 10-12 words
- Statement, not question
- Typographically prominent -- larger font, breathing room
- Evokes the brand's core promise

**Brand Examples:**

| Brand | Example Headers |
|-------|----------------|
| Spa | "Stillness is waiting for you." / "Where the steam remembers your name." |
| Aesthetics | "This is what real results look like." / "The fear is valid. The truth is freeing." |
| Slimming | "Your willpower is not the problem." / "She was exactly where you are right now." |

### Section 3: Hero Visual

**Function:** A single powerful image that triggers a response before a word of body copy is read.

**Brand-Specific Visual Direction:**

| Brand | Visual Style | Colour Palette |
|-------|-------------|---------------|
| Spa | Sensory: steam, water, warm stone, soft light, closed eyes | Amber, cream, soft gold, deep teal, stone |
| Aesthetics | Clinical-warm: treatment results, radiant skin, practitioner in action | Soft gold, blush pink, cream, warm white |
| Slimming | Lifestyle: real women in real moments (walking, cooking, laughing) | Soft golds, warm greys, muted greens, cream |

**Universal Anti-Patterns:** Stock photos of models. Clinical/sterile environments. Busy collages. Heavy text overlays. Before/after grids without context.

### Section 4: Opening Copy (Hook + Scene)

**Function:** The emotional bridge between the visual and the value. Opens with the reader's current state and pivots to possibility.

**Universal Rules:**
- 2-4 sentences maximum
- Starts with "you" (second-person)
- Acknowledges the reader's state before offering anything
- Short sentences. Breathing room.

**Brand Tone:**

| Brand | Opening Approach |
|-------|-----------------|
| Spa | Sensory scene-setting. "The steam rises. Your shoulders drop." |
| Aesthetics | Concern recognition. "You see it every morning. The lines that do not match how you feel inside." |
| Slimming | Validation first. "You have been trying. That is the part nobody sees." |

### Section 5: Educational / Proof Layer

**Function:** 1-3 sentences of evidence, insight, or expert credibility that grounds the emotional promise.

**Universal Rules:**
- Concise authority, never a lecture
- Cite science accessibly (parenthetical, never footnoted)
- Frame in terms of how the body feels, not what the data says

**Brand Framing:**

| Brand | Evidence Style |
|-------|--------------|
| Spa | "Your body already knows this. Science just confirmed it." Feelings first, findings second. |
| Aesthetics | Clinical authority translated into results. "This kind of result is typical when..." |
| Slimming | Science through metaphor. "Think of cortisol as your body's alarm system." |

### Section 6: Transformation / Social Proof

**Function:** One mini-testimonial or transformation story. Social proof through genuine human experience.

**Universal Rules:**
- ONE testimonial or story -- never a wall of reviews
- Emphasise emotional transformation, not service mechanics
- First-person voice when quoting, first names or initials only
- Match testimonial to email's audience segment when possible

### Section 7: Primary CTA (Singular Focus)

**Function:** One button. One invitation. The beginning of a relationship, not a transaction.

**Universal Rules:**
- Single button -- NEVER multiple CTAs competing
- Language is an invitation, not a command
- Breathing room above and below the button

**Brand CTA Style:**

| Brand | CTA Tone | Examples |
|-------|----------|---------|
| Spa | Soft invitation | "Book your reset" / "Reserve your sanctuary" / "Step inside" |
| Aesthetics | Confident invitation | "Book your consultation" / "Start your confidence journey" / "See what is possible" |
| Slimming | Gentle encouragement | "Start your conversation" / "Book your free consultation" / "Take the first step" |

**NEVER Use (Any Brand):** "Book now" / "Buy now" / "Shop now" / "Get started" / "Claim your offer" / "Don't miss out" / "Act now" / "Limited time" / "Hurry"

### Section 8: Secondary Add-On Block (Optional)

**Function:** Brief, contextually relevant tie-in to a seasonal offering, package, or complementary service.

**Universal Rules:**
- 1-2 sentences maximum
- Text link only -- no second button
- Must feel organic, not a random upsell
- Can be omitted entirely if it dilutes the email

### Section 9: Footer

**Function:** Institutional information delivered with brand warmth. The gentle landing.

| Element | Spa | Aesthetics | Slimming |
|---------|-----|------------|----------|
| Logo | Carisma Spa & Wellness | Carisma Aesthetics | Carisma Slimming |
| Tagline | "Beyond the Spa." | "Glow with Confidence." | "Doctor led weight loss to transform your life" |
| Closing Line | "The world can wait." | "Your confidence journey starts here." | -- |
| Signature | "Peacefully, Sarah" | "Beautifully yours, Sarah" | "With you every step, Katya" |
| Required | Address, unsubscribe, social links | Address, unsubscribe, social links | Address, unsubscribe, social links, phone |
| Optional | Location selector | -- | Trust badges, Fresha link |

## 5. Email Flow Architecture

### Universal Flow Types

All three brands use these core flows. Timing and length vary by brand.

| Flow | Purpose | Typical Length | Trigger |
|------|---------|---------------|---------|
| **Welcome** | Stranger to first booking/consultation | 6-7 emails, 14-21 days | New subscriber |
| **Browse Abandon** | Re-engage treatment/page browsers | 3 emails, 7 days | Browsed without booking |
| **Cart/Booking Abandon** | Complete the booking | 3 emails, 5 days | Started booking, left |
| **Thank You (Post-Service)** | Build repeat relationship | 4-5 emails, 14-30 days | Completed visit/treatment |
| **Win-Back** | Re-engage lapsed guests/clients | 4 emails, 30 days | 60-90+ days inactive |
| **VIP/Loyalty** | Grow lifetime value | Quarterly touchpoints | 3+ visits/treatments |
| **Seasonal Campaign** | Time-specific conversion | 3-6 emails, campaign period | Calendar trigger |

### Welcome Flow Architecture (Cross-Brand)

The welcome flow follows the same emotional arc across all brands: **Trust --> Value --> Proof --> Permission --> Conversion**

| Position | Spa (6 emails, 14 days) | Aesthetics (7 emails, 18 days) | Slimming (7 emails, 21 days) |
|----------|------------------------|-------------------------------|------------------------------|
| Email 1 | Brand intro from Sarah | Brand intro from Sarah | Brand intro from Katya |
| Email 2 | Sanctuary (desire) | Insider Secret (value) | Insider Secret (value) |
| Email 3 | Therapist's Tip (value) | Before & After (proof) | Storytime POV (connection) |
| Email 4 | Transformation Story (proof) | Hooked Insight (authority) | Pain-Solution (the system) |
| Email 5 | Objection Flip (permission) | Objection Flip (permission) | Objection Flip (permission) |
| Email 6 | Design Your Escape (conversion) | Pain-Solution (solution) | Case Study (proof) |
| Email 7 | -- | The Invitation (conversion) | Us vs Them + CTA (conversion) |

### Cart/Booking Abandon Flow (Cross-Brand)

| Position | All Brands |
|----------|-----------|
| Email 1 (1-2 hours) | Gentle, warm reminder. No pressure. Reference the specific item. |
| Email 2 (Day 1-2) | Objection flip or complete experience framing. Address hesitation. |
| Email 3 (Day 4-5) | Transformation story + final invitation. Social proof closes. |

### Win-Back Flow (Cross-Brand)

| Position | All Brands |
|----------|-----------|
| Email 1 (Day 60-90) | Thoughtful check-in. Genuine care. "We've been thinking about you." |
| Email 2 (Day 67-97) | New insight or transformation reminder. Reconnect emotionally. |
| Email 3 (Day 74-104) | Objection flip / reframe why returning matters. |
| Email 4 (Day 90-120) | Open door. No pressure. "Whenever you're ready. We're here." |

### Brand-Specific Unique Flows

| Brand | Unique Flow | Details |
|-------|------------|---------|

## 13. Writing Rules & Forbidden Language

### Universal Rules (All Brands)

1. **Second-person always.** "You" is the only pronoun that matters. Not "our clients." Not "women." You.

2. **One CTA per email. One button. One invitation.** Never compete with yourself.

3. **Never use urgency, scarcity, or pressure.** No "limited time." No "only X spots left." No countdown timers.

4. **Never use shame language.** Not "fix your body/face." Not "you let yourself go." Not "lack of discipline."

5. **Always translate clinical language into human experience.** Not "lymphatic drainage" -- "the gentle release of what your body has been holding."

6. **Short sentences. Short paragraphs. Breathing room.** White space is content.

7. **UK English spelling. Always.** Colour, not color. Favour, not favor. Honour, not honor. Practise (verb), practice (noun).

8. **Empathy is structural, not decorative.** Validation is the load-bearing wall. If you remove the empathy and the email still works, it was never structural.

9. **Sign off in persona. Close with warmth.** Always an open door, never a closed deadline.

10. **Read it aloud.** If any sentence sounds like marketing, rewrite it. If it sounds like a knowledgeable, caring human, keep it.

### Universal Forbidden Language

| Category | NEVER Use | Use Instead |
|----------|-----------|-------------|
| AI tells | "Certainly!" "I'd be happy to" "Of course!" "Absolutely!" "Great question!" | Natural, human phrasing |
| Sales pressure | "Hurry!" "Limited-time!" "Book now!" "Don't miss out!" "Only X left!" | "Whenever you're ready." "The door is open." |
| Hype language | "THE BEST!" "INCREDIBLE!" "You WON'T BELIEVE!" "AMAZING!" | Understated confidence. Let results speak. |
| Generic wellness | "We welcome all customers" "Our expert team" "World-class" | Specific, personal, intimate language |

### Brand-Specific Forbidden Language

**Spa:** NEVER use "pamper yourself," "treat yourself," "you deserve a treat," or any cliche wellness language. NEVER use untranslated clinical terms.

**Aesthetics:** NEVER use "fix your wrinkles," "anti-ageing" (say "skin rejuvenation"), "look 10 years younger," or condescending language ("you probably don't know this").

**Slimming:** NEVER use "cheat meal," "clean eating," "guilty pleasure," "detox," "cleanse," "dream body," "bikini ready," "goal weight," or em-dashes. NEVER reduce a person to a number. NEVER use fear-mongering about health.

## 14. Expert Insights

### Deliverability for the Malta Market

- **Promotional tab:** Keep image-to-text ratio below 40:60. Maintain high reply rates. Avoid spammy phrases.
- **List hygiene:** Clean every 90 days. Move non-openers to sunset flow first, then suppress.
- **Send consistency:** Same day and time each week. Inconsistent patterns trigger spam filters.
- **Authentication:** SPF, DKIM, DMARC properly configured. Non-negotiable for inbox placement.

### Optimal Send Times (Malta)

- **Primary:** Tuesday 10:00-11:00 CET -- mid-morning, post-school-drop-off. Highest open rates.
- **Secondary:** Thursday 10:00-11:00 CET -- end-of-week consideration. Strong for booking conversion.
- **Tertiary (Spa):** Sunday 09:00-10:00 CET -- reflective mood. Strong for Sanctuary and Science content.
- **Tertiary (Aesthetics):** Friday 13:00-14:00 CET -- weekend planning. Good for aspirational content.
- **Avoid:** Monday (inbox overload), Saturday (low engagement), late evenings.

### A/B Testing Priorities (All Brands)

1. **Subject lines** -- Always. Test every broadcast. Minimum 1,000 subscribers per variant.
2. **CTA language** -- Monthly. Test invitation warmth vs. action clarity.
3. **Content type** -- Quarterly. Which type drives highest conversion per segment?
4. **Send time** -- Quarterly. Test primary vs. secondary slot for 4 weeks.
5. **Email length** -- Semi-annually. 150 vs. 300 word versions.

**What NOT to test:** Brand voice, emotional register, CTA count (always one), urgency tactics (never).

- **Attribution window:** 72 hours post-click, 7 days post-open

### Ethical Considerations

These apply most critically to Slimming, but the principles are universal:

1. **No email should make a woman feel worse about herself than she did before she opened it.** This is the absolute floor, not the ceiling.
2. **Transformation, not correction.** Always about becoming, never about fixing.
3. **Consent over conversion.** The reader always has the power. Never manufacture shame to drive action.
4. **Mental health awareness.** Some subscribers may be in vulnerable states. The tone must be therapeutic enough that vulnerable readers feel supported, not triggered.

### The Trust Principle (All Brands)

Trust is the conversion mechanism. Not urgency. Not scarcity. Not FOMO.

**Spa builds trust through:** Consistency of emotional tone. Every email feels like the spa itself.
**Aesthetics builds trust through:** The Trust Ladder (Competence --> Integrity --> Benevolence --> Identification). Most clinics stop at Rung 1. Carisma builds to Rung 4.
**Slimming builds trust through:** Radical transparency, honest limitations, named science, vulnerability in storytelling, and the Extended Care Commitment guarantee.
