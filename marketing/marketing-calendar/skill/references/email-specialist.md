# Email Marketing Specialist Reference

Channel specialist guidance for email campaign planning within the marketing calendar.

**Persona:** 30-year email marketer & CRO specialist

**Full skill:** Invoke `email-marketing-content-strategy` at `marketing/marketing-calendar/email-marketing/SKILL.md`. That skill contains the complete email planning system. This reference summarises what the calendar strategy agent needs.

---

## Email Rhythm

- **3 emails per week per brand** (~12-13 per month)
- **Alternating pattern:** Design promo -> TB_ relationship -> Design promo -> TB_ relationship
- **Send days:** Tuesday, Thursday, Saturday (typical) -- adjust around holidays

---

## Two Email Types

### Design Emails (Promotional)
- **Naming:** No prefix. Use offer name or seasonal theme
  - Examples: `Easter Final Offer`, `Spring Packages`, `Spa Day Promo`, `Summer Glow Launch`
- **Content:** Promotional with pricing grid, seasonal imagery, clear CTA
- **Goal:** Drive bookings/purchases
- **Template:** HTML design with images, offer details, pricing, CTA button

### TB_ Emails (Text-Based Relationship)
- **Naming:** Prefix with `TB_`
  - Examples: `TB_Easter Last Chance`, `TB_Self Care Sunday`, `TB_Katya's Story`, `TB_Spring Reset Tips`
- **Content:** Personal letter from brand persona. No pricing, no product imagery, pure value
- **Goal:** Build relationship, trust, engagement
- **Template:** Plain text style (may have minimal formatting). Personal sign-off from persona

## 7 Universal Content Types (from email-marketing-content-strategy)

The email skill defines 7 content types that drive the weekly rotation. The calendar agent MUST use these when planning email arcs:

| # | Content Type | Purpose | Email Style |
|---|-------------|---------|-------------|
| 1 | Before & After | Show transformation, build belief | Design |
| 2 | Insider Secrets | Reveal what practitioners know | TB_ or Design |
| 3 | Pain-Solution | Validate pain, offer the fix | Design |
| 4 | Storytime POV | Emotional narrative from persona | TB_ |
| 5 | Objection Flip | Dismantle fears, build confidence | TB_ or Design |
| 6 | Hooked Insight | Surprising fact as entry point | TB_ |
| 7 | Us vs Them | Differentiate from alternatives | Design |

**Weekly rotation rule:** Never repeat the same content type in consecutive emails. Rotate through all 7 across a month.

## Creative Brief Format (added as cell notes)

Every email cell in the spreadsheet gets a cell note with its creative brief. The full email skill defines a 9-section template. For the calendar, use this enriched format:

### Design Email Brief Template
```
Content Type: [one of 7 universal types]
Emotional Register: [Warm Invitation / Confident Guidance / Compassionate Validation]
Subject Line: [40-60 chars, brand voice, one of 10 formulas from email skill]
Preview Text: [90-140 chars, conversational, never repeats subject]
Hero Header: [H1 that reinforces the subject line hook]
Hook: [opening line that stops the scroll]
Body Angle: [promotional angle -- offer, urgency, seasonal]
CTA: [invitation language -- "Explore your options" not "BUY NOW"]
Tone: [brand-specific -- warm/clinical-warm/compassionate]
Visual Direction: [hero image, product grid, lifestyle shot, pricing table]
Offer Details: [specific package, price, terms, expiry]
Notes: [special instructions -- e.g., "include countdown timer"]
```

### TB_ Email Brief Template
```
Content Type: [one of 7 universal types -- typically Storytime POV or Hooked Insight]
Emotional Register: [brand-specific register]
Subject Line: [personal, non-promotional -- like a friend texting]
Preview Text: [conversational, intriguing, 90-140 chars]
Hook: [personal opening from persona -- story, question, reflection]
Body Angle: [relationship building -- tips, stories, validation, education]
Sign-off: [brand persona signature]
Tone: [personal letter, conversational, warm]
Notes: [NO pricing, NO offers, NO product imagery, NO CTA buttons]
```

## Monthly Email Arc Design

Each month's emails should follow a thematic arc tied to the key occasion:

**Week 1:** Awareness / Teaser -- introduce the theme or upcoming occasion
**Week 2:** Education / Value -- deepen engagement with tips, stories, benefits
**Week 3:** Offer / Promotion -- the main promotional push
**Week 4:** Urgency / Last chance -- final call, scarcity, wrap-up

Mix design and TB_ emails across the arc so the recipient never gets 3 promos in a row.

## Subject Line Formulas (from email-marketing-content-strategy)

The email skill provides 10 proven formulas. Use a mix across the month:

1. **Curiosity Gap:** "The secret to [desired outcome]..."
2. **Social Proof:** "[X] women in Malta already..."
3. **Direct Benefit:** "Get [specific result] in [timeframe]"
4. **Question:** "Still thinking about [treatment/concern]?"
5. **Personalisation:** "[Name], this is for you"
6. **Urgency:** "Last day: [offer description]"
7. **Storytelling:** "When [persona] first tried..."
8. **Myth Bust:** "Think [common belief]? Think again."
9. **Seasonal:** "[Season/occasion] is the perfect time to..."
10. **Exclusive:** "For our [segment] only: [offer]"

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| All design emails, no TB_ | Alternate. TB_ builds trust that makes promos convert. |
| TB_ email with pricing or offers | TB_ = NO pricing. Pure relationship. |
| Wrong persona sign-off | Spa/Aesthetics = Sarah. Slimming = Katya. |
| More than 4 emails per week | 3 per week max. Don't burn the list. |
| Missing creative briefs on email cells | Every email cell needs a cell note with full brief. |
| Same subject line pattern every week | Vary: use different formulas from the 10 above. |
| Same content type in consecutive emails | Rotate through all 7 universal types across the month. |
| Planning emails without the email skill | ALWAYS invoke email-marketing-content-strategy. It has domain knowledge that cannot be replicated ad hoc. |
| Generic CTA like "BUY NOW" | Use invitation language matching brand register. |
| Missing emotional register in brief | Every brief MUST specify the brand's emotional register. |
