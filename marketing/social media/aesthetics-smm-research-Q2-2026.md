### Pillar 3: Objection Flip — Best Sub-Topics for Q2

**Top 4 sub-topics for Q2 season:**

1. **"Botox freezes your face"** — The single most common objection in aesthetics. Run in May or June. Acknowledge the fear genuinely: "You've seen the frozen faces. I understand exactly why that puts you off." Then reframe: the frozen look comes from over-treatment, not from Botox itself. CAMP-028 (Bridal Party) or CAMP-036 (Father's Day male angle) provide natural pegs.

2. **"Fillers always look fake"** — The second-most common objection. Powerful because it is true — badly done filler is very visible. Sarah's credibility is built precisely on this: "You've only noticed the bad ones." Best in April (bridal season, CAMP-006) or May.

3. **"Chemical peels thin the skin"** — Directly tied to April's peel campaign (CAMP-008). A patient thinking "peels damage your skin" will not book. Acknowledge the fear ("I understand why the word 'acid' sounds alarming"), then deliver the science. One April post.

4. **"Once pigmentation fades, it's gone forever"** — Powerful because many patients believe one treatment = permanent result. They stop their SPF, stop their maintenance. This objection flip educates on the cycle and creates ongoing engagement with the clinic. Best in May (Melanoma Monday/CAMP-023 follow-up).

**Additional objection flips (use once each):**
- "Microneedling damages your skin" (May — reassurance during bridal prep window)
- "Profhilo is just a moisturiser" (April or June — wherever Profhilo naturally surfaces)
- "HydraFacial is just a fancy facial" (June — summer maintenance CAMP-031)
- "PRP is only for hair" (May — bridge from hair to skin PRP after CAMP-023)

**Tone reminder for Objection Flip posts:** Never be dismissive of the fear. Start with genuine acknowledgement ("I completely understand why you've heard this and believed it"), then deliver the reality with warmth and evidence. End with a soft, confidence-restoring invitation — not a hard CTA.

### Pillar 4: Viral/Replicate — Recommended Formats for Q2

**6–7 posts across Q2. Formats to adapt to Sarah's voice:**

1. **POV format** — "POV: you're sitting in the consultation chair for the first time and realising this is not what you expected." Low-production, high-relatability. April or May.
2. **"Before I came to Carisma" carousel/reel** — Trending transformation testimonial format. Adapt to Sarah's empowering lens: not "I was broken," but "I was already me — I just needed to feel like it again." April (CAMP-002) or May.
3. **"When did you last…" format** — "When did you last look in the mirror and felt genuinely confident?" Stop-scroll question that opens a conversation. May or June.
4. **Day-by-day result reel** — "Day 1 / Day 7 / Day 30" skin transformation format. Clinically grounding (realistic timeline) and visually compelling. April (peel season) or May (post-HydraFacial). Reference CAMP-008 or CAMP-031.
5. **Reaction format** — Sarah reacts to a common skincare claim ("You just need to drink more water for glowing skin"). Keeps it warm and educational, not condescending. May or June.
6. **"Things I stopped recommending" format** — Sarah lists skincare myths or over-hyped products that do not deliver what clinical treatments can. Builds clinical authority. June.
7. **"Get ready with me" for a treatment** — Behind-the-scenes as a soft GRWM: "Getting ready for a HydraFacial — here's what to expect." Combines Viral format with Behind-the-Clinic warmth. June, CAMP-031.

**Viral format adaptation rules for Aesthetics:**
- Keep the trend's format/mechanic but replace content with Carisma treatments, Sarah's voice, and UK English
- Never use a trending sound or format that feels jarring against a clinical/warm brand
- Viral posts for Aesthetics should feel like a knowledgeable friend who happens to have the best skincare knowledge in Malta — not a TikTok aesthetic clinic
