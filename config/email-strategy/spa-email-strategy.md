---

## Brand Voice & Tone

- **Tone:** Peaceful, soothing, charming, warm, elegant, purposeful, honest
- **Writing style:** Simple yet poetic. Minimal and spacious. Second-person ("you", "your"). Subtle persuasion — never push, always invite.
- **NEVER:** Hard sells, rushed or pressured language, cluttered copy, clinical or transactional phrasing
- **Subject line style:** Curiosity-driven, emotionally resonant, short (under 45 characters ideal)
- **Emotional register:** Speak to the reader's desire for stillness, restoration, and being truly known

## High-Conversion Email Template (9-Section Structure)

Every Carisma Spa email should follow this architecture. Adapt proportions per content type, but preserve the structure.

### Section 1 — Subject Line
- 1–2 variants: curiosity-driven, emotionally resonant
- Target: under 45 characters for mobile display
- Style: incomplete thought, sensory hook, or soft promise
- Example: "Your body already knows how to rest." / "One hour. Seven days of peace."

### Section 2 — Hero Header
- One short, bold line embodying the ritual or promise
- Maximum 8 words
- Should feel like the title of an experience, not a product
- Example: "Stillness Is the Deepest Luxury."

### Section 3 — Hero Visual
- Sensory photography: steam rising from water, warm stone on skin, soft candlelight, hands on a shoulder, natural textures
- Minimal or no text overlay — let the image breathe
- Aspect ratio: 2:1 or full-bleed depending on template
- No stock-photo feeling. All imagery should feel like it belongs in Carisma's world.

### Section 4 — Opening Copy (Hook + Scene)
- 2–3 sentences only
- Open with the reader's current emotional state (tension, exhaustion, longing for quiet)
- Pivot to the invitation — don't describe, evoke
- Example: "You've been carrying a weight you haven't named. Some weeks, it lives in your shoulders. Come let it go."

### Section 5 — Educational / Proof Layer (Authority)
- 1–2 sentences: a science-backed fact or a therapist's clinical insight
- Grounds the emotion in credibility without breaking the tone
- Example: "Research shows that massage reduces cortisol by up to 31%, while increasing serotonin — the shift you feel isn't just relief. It's chemistry."

### Section 6 — Transformation / Social Proof (Relatability)
- A mini testimonial (1–2 sentences) or a one-line story from a guest
- First name only. No last names. Keep it real, not polished.
- Example: "Maria came in for a lunch break facial. She told us she hadn't felt that calm in two years."

### Section 7 — Primary CTA (Singular Focus)
- One button only. No competing links.
- Soft, inviting language — not "BUY NOW", not "CLICK HERE"
- Options: "Book your reset" / "Design your ritual" / "Reserve your sanctuary" / "Begin your escape"
- Button style: warm gold, wide padding, clean serif font

### Section 8 — Secondary Add-On Block (Optional)
- Short seasonal or package tie-in (2–3 lines max)
- Use only when there is a genuinely relevant upsell or event
- Example: "Looking to share the experience? Our Couple's Ritual is available every weekend this month."

### Section 9 — Footer
- Logo + tagline "Beyond the Spa."
- Full address (all 8 Malta locations or primary location)
- Unsubscribe link
- Closing line: "The world can wait."

## Campaign Planning Notes

- **Monthly cadence:** 2–4 emails per month (1 aspirational anchor, 1 educational or insider, 1 seasonal or package, 1 optional transformation/community)
- **WhatsApp support:** Use WhatsApp blasts to support high-priority seasonal campaigns and unicorn offers. Keep copy brief and personal — 2 sentences max.
- **Peak send times:** Tuesday–Thursday mornings (9–11am Malta time) for wellness content. Friday afternoon for weekend bookings.
- **Segmentation priorities:** First-time visitors (onboarding sequence), lapsed clients (re-engagement), loyal monthly visitors (VIP series), gift card purchasers (gifting occasions)
