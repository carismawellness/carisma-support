# Carisma Aesthetics — Email Strategy

**Brand:** Carisma Aesthetics
**Tagline:** "Glow with Confidence"
**Persona:** Sarah
**Signature:** "Beautifully yours, Sarah" / "Here's to your confidence — beautifully yours, The Carisma Team."

**Resources for reference:** Follow @Wellcopymax & @walshy.mails on Instagram for inspiration.

---

## Brand Voice & Tone

- **Tone:** Warm, reassuring, confident edge, modern, elegant
- **DO:** Empowering language, personalisation, natural beauty emphasis, clinical credibility wrapped in approachable luxury
- **DON'T:** "Fixing flaws", aggressive or pressured sales language, generic statements, before/after framing that implies something was wrong before
- **Email sign-off:** "Here's to your confidence — beautifully yours, The Carisma Team."
- **Language note:** Speak as a trusted friend who happens to be a medical expert. Never condescending. Always informed, always kind.
- **Day 1 / Day 30 framing:** Replace "before/after" with "Day 1 / Day 30" — this frames results as a journey, not a correction.

## 8 Email Content Types

### 1. Unicorn Offers

**Goal:** Drive immediate bookings through irresistible limited-time packages with high perceived value and genuine scarcity.

**Structure:**
- Lead with the specific value stack (what's included + total worth vs. price)
- State the genuine scarcity (limited slots, deadline, or one-time occasion)
- One clear CTA: "Claim your spot" / "Book before [date]"
- Social proof line (optional): "32 bookings in 24 hours last time."

**Tone:** Excited but not desperate. This is a gift, not a fire sale.

**Subject line examples:**
- "The treatment package we only offer once a year."
- "48 hours only — our most wanted bundle."
- "4 treatments. One price. Limited to 10 clients."

**WhatsApp:** Unicorn offers should always be supported with a WhatsApp blast. Keep to 2 sentences: the offer and the deadline.

### 2. Before & After Case Studies (Day 1 / Day 30)

**Goal:** Showcase real, believable client results. Build the trust that the system works for people "like me."

**Framing rule:** NEVER use "before/after." Always use "Day 1 / Day 30" (or relevant timeframe). This positions results as a journey, not a correction of a flaw.

**Structure:**
- Hook: client situation at Day 1 (specific, relatable, non-shaming)
- Treatment plan followed (with approvals in place)
- Day 30 visual or described result
- Client quote (2–3 sentences, first name only)
- Brief credibility note: "Our approach uses [specific treatment] to [mechanism]."
- Soft CTA: "See if this treatment is right for you."

**Subject line examples:**
- "Day 1 vs Day 30: Sophie's skin journey."
- "What 4 sessions of [treatment] did for her confidence."
- "She wasn't sure it would work. Then she saw Day 30."

**Content angles:**
- Skin rejuvenation journey (dull to luminous)
- Filler for natural enhancement
- Laser treatment for pigmentation
- PRP therapy results
- Chemical peel transformation

**Compliance note:** Always ensure written consent is secured for any photography or quotes used. Anonymise where requested.

### 3. Insider Secrets

**Goal:** Position Carisma Aesthetics as the informed, honest clinic that tells clients what others won't. Build authority and trust.

**Structure:**
- Open with a counterintuitive or surprising professional insight
- Explain the myth or common misconception
- Deliver the real information (clinical, clear, not jargon-heavy)
- Show how Carisma's approach uses this knowledge
- Soft CTA: "Book a free consultation to learn what's right for your skin."

**Subject line examples:**
- "Most clinics won't tell you this before filler."
- "The skin prep step almost everyone skips."
- "Why some Botox results last longer than others."

**Content angles:**
- What to do before a filler appointment
- Why hydration matters more than any treatment
- The truth about downtime with laser
- How to make results last longer
- What questions to ask before any injectable treatment

### 4. Pain-Solution

**Goal:** Agitate a specific frustration a potential client is living with, then offer the precise solution.

**Structure:**
- Open with the pain (specific, not generic — "still breaking out at 35?" not "skin problems?")
- Show empathy: name the failed solutions they've already tried
- Reveal the missing piece (what's really going on)
- Explain the solution in 3 clear bullets
- Short social proof or micro-example
- Soft CTA: "Book a free skin consultation."

**Subject line examples:**
- "Still breaking out at 35? Here's why and how to fix it."
- "Why your skincare routine isn't touching your pigmentation."
- "You've tried everything for those lines. Here's what actually works."

**Content angles:**
- Adult acne
- Hormonal skin changes
- Stubborn pigmentation / sun damage
- Under-eye hollowing
- Forehead lines that no cream can touch

### 5. Story Time POV

**Goal:** Make readers feel something. They mentally try on the story, then see themselves taking action.

**Structure:**
- Start at an emotional moment (the moment of decision, the mirror moment, the consultation)
- Rewind to give context
- Show the inner monologue — the doubt, the hope
- Describe the turning point at Carisma Aesthetics
- The emotional "after" — not just results, but how they feel about themselves
- Soft CTA

**Subject line examples:**
- "She cried after seeing her results. Here's why."
- "She almost didn't book. Then she did."
- "The moment she stopped hiding from photos."

**Content angles:**
- A client who almost cancelled her consultation out of fear
- A mother who finally did something for herself
- A woman who thought it was too late to feel confident in her skin
- Someone who came in for one thing and discovered they needed something different

---

### 6. Objection Flip

**Goal:** Take a common fear or misconception about aesthetics treatments and calmly, honestly dismantle it.

**Structure:**
- Name the fear word for word ("Afraid of looking fake?")
- Validate it ("That's one of the most common concerns we hear — and honestly, it makes complete sense.")
- Explain where it comes from (media, bad examples, outdated techniques)
- Give the real picture with reassuring facts
- Explain Carisma's specific safeguards and philosophy
- Invite questions or a no-obligation consultation

**Subject line examples:**
- "Afraid of looking fake? Here's why that won't happen here."
- "Think injectables are only for older skin? Let's talk."
- "Is Botox safe? The honest answer."

**Objections to address:**
- "I'll look overdone or unnatural"
- "It's not for my age"
- "What if I don't like the results?"
- "I've heard it's painful"
- "I'm not sure if I need it"
- "Will people notice?"

---

### 7. Hooked Insight

**Goal:** Deliver a surprising fact that stops the scroll and reframes how the reader thinks about their skin or a treatment.

**Structure:**
- Open with the punchy counterintuitive insight (1–2 sentences, no preamble)
- Short story or mini example to make it real
- Simple, clear explanation of the science or mechanism
- What most people do wrong based on this
- How Carisma's protocol is built around this insight
- Simple action the reader can take today

**Subject line examples:**
- "Botox doesn't remove wrinkles. It does this instead."
- "The real reason your skin looks dull (it's not dehydration)."
- "SPF won't undo this. But one treatment will."

**Content angles:**
- How injectables work (not what people assume)
- Why collagen loss starts in your late 20s
- The role of facial volume loss in ageing (not just wrinkles)
- What "skin quality" actually means vs surface texture
- Why sunscreen is the most evidence-based anti-ageing tool

---

### 8. Us vs Them

**Goal:** Differentiate Carisma Aesthetics from generic clinics, DIY trends, and uninformed options — without attacking competitors directly.

**Structure:**
- Name the common alternatives (generic clinics, Instagram trends, at-home devices)
- List what each option does well and where it falls short
- Introduce Carisma's approach as a considered "third option"
- 3 clear differentiators (consultation depth, practitioner expertise, treatment philosophy)
- Short client example or quote
- Invite them to experience the difference

**Subject line examples:**
- "Not all clinics are the same. Here's the difference."
- "Why we don't just offer the most popular treatment."
- "The consultation most clinics skip — and why we never do."

**Comparison angles:**
- High-street discount clinics vs. clinical-excellence approach
- DIY treatments and at-home devices vs. professional results
- Trend-chasing vs. outcome-focused protocols
- Consultations that take 5 minutes vs. consultations that take 45

## Campaign Planning Notes

- **Monthly cadence:** 2–4 emails per month (1 offer/promotional, 1 educational or insider secret, 1 case study or story, 1 seasonal or objection flip)
- **WhatsApp:** Always pair Unicorn Offer emails with a WhatsApp blast. Other types: use WhatsApp for follow-up if no open within 48 hours of send (for high-value campaigns only).
- **Peak send times:** Tuesday–Thursday, 9–11am Malta time. Avoid Mondays and Fridays.
- **Segmentation priorities:** New leads / consultation requests (nurture sequence), active clients (loyalty + upsell), lapsed clients (re-engagement), treatment-specific segments (skincare vs. injectables)
- **Compliance:** All before/after (Day 1/Day 30) content requires written client consent. Sensitive conditions (acne, scarring) require especially careful, non-shaming framing. Follow Maltese healthcare advertising guidelines.
