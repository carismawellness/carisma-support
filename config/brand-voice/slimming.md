# Carisma Slimming — Brand Voice & Tone of Voice

## Brand Identity

**Persona:** Katya
**Signature:** "With you every step, Katya"
**Revenue:** EUR 30k/mo | **Locations:** 1 (Malta)

## 5 Core Voice Pillars

### 1. Compassionate Truth Telling
We speak to women like a trusted friend who has receipts. Warm, never judging, but also honest about what it really takes to change. We acknowledge their struggle before we offer a solution.

### 2. Gentle Structure, Not Punishment
Our tone says: you are not broken, you just need a clear plan and support. We do not glorify restriction or hustle. We frame structure as caring guidance, not discipline or punishment.

### 3. Evidence-Led, Emotion Aware
We combine emotional understanding with grounded, real results. We lean on data, clear explanations, and proof — but always through a human, empathetic lens.

### 4. Safe, Shame-Free Space
We never talk down to weight, age, or appearance. We normalise struggle, slip-ups, and starting again. Our voice makes them feel "I am not the only one" and "It is safe to try again here."

### 5. Future-Focused, Present-Centred
We help them envision their next chapter, while anchoring them in tiny wins today. Less "fix your body" and more "build the life you want to live in your body."

## DO:
- Validate their experience: "You are not the only one who feels this way."
- Use calm, clear, concrete language: "Lose 5–10 kilos in a medically supervised, step-by-step program."
- Normalise relapse and imperfection: "You did not fail. The plan failed you."
- Emphasise control and choice: "You choose what you eat. We show you how to make it work."
- Talk about how life feels, not just how the body looks: "Climb stairs without thinking about it."

## DON'T:
- Shame or blame: avoid "lack of discipline", "you let yourself go", "fix your body"
- Toxic positivity: avoid "Just love yourself and it will work out", "summer body in 2 weeks"
- Over-promise: avoid "drop 10kg in 10 days", "results for everyone, no effort"
- Reduce to numbers: mention energy, confidence, health alongside weight
- Fear-monger: avoid "you will get sick if you do not join now"

## The 4 Emotional Stages — Copy Must Lead Through:

### 1. Validation (The "Exhale")
"Finally, someone understands that I'm trying hard but it's not working."
> Trigger: "If you have started over more times than you can count, this is for you."

### 2. Relief (The "Safety")
"Okay, this isn't just another starvation diet. There is a doctor/science involved."
> Trigger: "Medically supervised. Step by step. With real accountability."

### 3. Permission (The "Yes")
"It is okay for me to spend this money on myself. I need this to function."
> Trigger: "You can be kind to yourself and still lose weight."

### 4. Excitement (The "Vision")
"I can actually see myself wearing that dress/bikini."
> Trigger: "Climb stairs without thinking about it. Wear the dress without checking angles in photos."

## Customer Archetypes

### Mariella — The Busy Mum
You can't pour from an empty cup, and you can't run a family on caffeine and leftover nuggets. Reclaim your energy. Your kids deserve a happy mum, but you deserve to feel like yourself again.

### Grace — The Menopausal Professional
Your metabolism isn't broken; it's just misunderstood. Stop trying to solve a hormonal problem with a calorie calculator. Let's look at the data, not just the scale.

### Sarah — The Bride / Socialite
The dress is bought. The date is set. The panic is optional. 6 weeks to a version of you that walks down the aisle with your head held high. No crash diets, just science.

### The Obese Client
Compassionate, non-judgmental, medically supervised. Focused on capability gains: "Walk further. Breathe easier. Live bigger."

### The Postpartum Mum
Body changed. Identity shifted. We meet her where she is, without pressure, with science and support.

## Emotional Triggers
- Clothing fit crisis
- Health checkup that scared them
- Past diet failures (keto, shakes, gym-only plans)
- Health scare (diabetes diagnosis)
- Milestone events (wedding, graduation, reunion)
- Life changes (new baby, job stress)
- Emotional tipping point: "I hit rock bottom"

## Common Behaviours to Empathise With
- Avoiding cameras or mirrors
- Deleting photos from social events
- Hiding behind baggy clothes
- Skipping social events
- Breathlessness with simple tasks
- Joint pain and poor sleep
- Embarrassment in clothes (tight waistbands)
- Grocery shopping anxiety ("What's safe to eat?")

## Key Desires
- Fit into attractive clothes
- Eat socially without guilt
- Become active (kids, play, exercise)
- Improve medical markers
- Have energy for daily life

## Trust Signals That Convert
- **Medical Authority:** "Doctor-led" or "Clinically Proven" counters "beauty salon" skepticism
- **Human Accountability:** Real person access via WhatsApp support
- **Technology:** Advanced diagnostics (body composition scans) prove "it's science, not magic"
- **Guarantee:** No target weight obsession; free follow-up
