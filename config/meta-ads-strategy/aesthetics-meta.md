## Customer Archetypes

Three distinct personas by life stage and primary motivation. Tailor copy, creative, and offer angle to each.

### Archetype 1: The Reverser (Age 45–60)
**Profile:** She sees the signs of aging and wants to reverse them. Fine lines are visible. Volume has been lost. Her face looks tired even when she's not. She has the budget and is ready to invest — she just needs the right clinic to trust.
- **Primary desire:** Turn back the clock. Look as alive as she feels inside.
- **Main fear:** Looking "done" or unnatural. She wants people to think she's simply aged well.
- **Trigger moment:** A photo that made her cringe. A health milestone. A big event coming up.
- **Trust needs:** Doctor-led, medically credible, real results from real clients her age.
- **Best hook style:** "You don't have to look your age" / "Still you — just refreshed"
- **Key treatments:** Botox, thread lifts, dermal fillers (cheeks, under-eyes), skin resurfacing

### Archetype 2: The Slow-Downer (Age 32–45)
**Profile:** She's starting to notice the first signs. Fine lines are forming. Her skin looks less bright. She's not in crisis — she's proactive. She wants to slow the process before it accelerates.
- **Primary desire:** Maintain. Invest now so she doesn't need to overinvest later.
- **Main fear:** Over-treating, looking different, not worth the cost.
- **Trigger moment:** Noticed lines forming. Friend raved about a treatment. Turned 35 or 40.
- **Trust needs:** Natural results, "prevention over correction" framing, consultation-first approach.
- **Best hook style:** "Start early, stay ahead" / "The 20-minute treatment changing how she looks at 40"
- **Key treatments:** Botox (preventative), HydraFacial, skin boosters, microneedling, chemical peels

### Archetype 3: The Preventor (Age 21–32)
**Profile:** She's skin-conscious from a young age. She follows skincare influencers. She understands that SPF and hydration are not enough. She's beginning to explore professional treatments.
- **Primary desire:** Protect and optimise her skin. Look great now, prevent problems later.
- **Main fear:** Needles, downtime, doing too much too young, judgment from others.
- **Trigger moment:** Turned 25. Noticed hyperpigmentation. Bad breakout season.
- **Trust needs:** Educational content, gentle introduction, "your first treatment" framing.
- **Best hook style:** "What your skincare routine is missing" / "Your 20s are the best time to start"
- **Key treatments:** HydraFacial, chemical peels, microneedling, skin consultations

## Pain Points

Address these in copy and creative hooks:

- **Fine lines forming** — "I can see them when I laugh now and I'm only 38"
- **Tired look that won't go away** — "I sleep 8 hours and still look exhausted"
- **Makeup not sitting right** — "Foundation creases into every line by 11am"
- **Volume loss** — "My cheeks look hollow. I don't look like myself."
- **Dull, uneven skin tone** — "My skin looks grey. The glow is just gone."
- **Pores and texture** — "My skin texture in photos mortifies me"
- **Dark circles / under-eye hollowing** — "People ask if I'm okay. I'm fine. I just look tired."
- **Jowl formation** — "My face shape is changing and I don't know what to do"

## Trust Signals for Aesthetics Ads

Always include at least one of:
- "Doctor-led treatments"
- "Clinically proven results"
- "Rated 5★ by 150+ clients in Malta"
- "Malta's top-rated aesthetics clinic"
- "Medical consultation included"
- Awards or accreditations
- Named doctor/practitioner credentials

---

## Creative Rules for Aesthetics

- Use "Day 1 and Day 30" framing — NEVER "before and after" (Meta policy)
- Lead with transformation, not treatment features
- Always include a free consultation offer or low-commitment first step
- Medical credibility must be balanced with warmth — never cold or clinical
- Natural beauty emphasis: "still you, just better" not "fixed your flaws"
- Avoid: aggressive sales language, exaggerated claims, "miracle" language
