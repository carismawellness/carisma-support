## BHUGOS Framework (Primary Copy Structure)

The BHUGOS framework is the primary structure for all Meta Ads copy. Every ad should hit all 6 elements, in order:

- **H — Hook** (first 3 seconds of video / primary text above the fold): Stop the scroll. Callout the exact person. One line.
- **B — Benefit** (what they get): What transformation or outcome does the prospect receive?
- **U — Urgency** (deadline, limited spots): Why must they act NOW? Time-bound or quantity-bound.
- **G — Guarantee** (de-risk the decision): What removes the fear of commitment?
- **O — Offer** (the specific deal, with perceived value + price + scarcity): The full value stack — what's included, what it's worth, what they pay, how few spots remain.
- **S — Social proof** (reviews, case studies, awards, # of clients helped): Validated trust signals.

---

## 3-Line Hook Formula (Primary Text)

The first visible lines before "See more" — these must earn the tap.

- **Line 1:** Callout ideal customer + Malta (e.g., "Summer ready skin for Malta moms")
- **Line 2:** Pain point solved
- **Line 3:** Perceived value + real price + scarcity ("only 15 spots")

**Example:**
```
Attention Malta moms: summer is 8 weeks away.
Still dealing with stubborn skin that no cream can fix?
Get the HydraFacial Package worth €250 — only €99. Just 15 spots available.
```

---

## Body Copy Structure

After the hook, build the case:

- **Line 1:** Agitate the pain point further and amplify the benefit
- **Line 2:** What is included in the package (bullet points or emojis)
- **Line 3:** Perceived value + Price + Scarcity
- **Line 4:** Social proof (reviews, case studies, helped X # of clients)
- **Line 5:** CTA

**Mandatory Disclaimer (append to every ad):**
> "Spots for this offer are limited to the first 15 available spots (if you're still reading this, that means we may still have a spot available!)"
>
> [Phone Number] | [Address]

---

## Headline Options

Headlines render at 40 characters or fewer. Use these proven formulas:

- "Only 15 spots"
- "[LIMITED] Only €99"
- "Free consultation this week"
- "Save €[X] this [month/week]"
- "Book in [Location] — [offer]"

---

## RMBC Copywriting Method

Three high-converting short-form structures for primary text variants:

- `[insert benefit] — [price or discount] 👉`
- `[insert solved concern] → [price or discount] 👉`
- `[insert time urgency] → [price or discount] 👉`

**Examples:**
```
Smoother, fresher skin — €99 (worth €250) 👉
No more fine lines showing through your makeup → €239 with free facial included 👉
Last 3 spots this month → Book your transformation now 👉
```

---

## 3 Pillars of Ad Copywriting

Every piece of copy must pass this test:

1. **Very specific** — Name the treatment, name the price, name the number of spots, name the location. Vague copy does not convert.
2. **Concise and sharp** — Cut every word that does not earn its place. If it does not add meaning, delete it.
3. **Fifth-grade reading level** — Run copy through a readability check. Short sentences. Common words. No jargon. No medical terminology unless explained.

## Video Structure (6 Steps)

### Step 1 — Pattern Interrupt (First 2–3 Seconds)
Goal: earn the watch. Options:
- Surprising statistic on screen
- Striking Day 1 / Day 30 transformation reveal
- Practitioner holding treatment device close to camera
- Bold text over a clean background

### Step 2 — Clear Problem & Relatability
Speak the prospect's language:
> "If you've tried creams and nothing worked…"
> "Every morning you look in the mirror and wish the tired look would just go away…"

### Step 3 — Social Proof / Transformation
Prove it works before they have to trust it:
- Day 1 / Day 30 client transformation
- Client testimonial clip
- Clinic environment establishing trust

### Step 4 — Authority Anchor
Build credibility fast:
- Award logo (World Luxury Spa Awards)
- Clinic exterior
- Practitioner credentials
- "Rated 5★ by 150+ clients in Malta"
- Years in operation

### Step 5 — Offer & Urgency
The payoff:
> "Book your free consultation this week and save €50 on your first session"

Be specific: exact price, exact saving, exact number of spots, exact deadline.

### Step 6 — Simple Call-to-Action
One action only:
> "Tap below to book instantly"
> "Click the link to claim your spot"
> "Send us a message now"

## Creative Refresh Strategy

Use this hierarchy before briefing new production:

1. **Add seasonal wrappers** to existing creatives (overlay text, intro card)
2. **Rotate intros/hooks** every 1–2 weeks — keep core body identical
3. **Swap 1–2 Day 1/Day 30 photos** for new client results
4. **Alternate offer wording** (€50 off vs. free add-on)
5. **Change background music or captions styling** for video

Full creative refresh (new production) required when all refresh options are exhausted or frequency exceeds 3x consistently.

## Hook-Substance-Payoff Framework

Every ad follows this three-part narrative arc:

- **Hook:** Evoke curiosity, pose a question, play into a mass desire. Stop the scroll.
- **Substance:** Engage them to keep watching/reading until the end. Build the case. Justify the offer.
- **Payoff:** Reward for watching/reading. Answer the question you opened with. Deliver the offer with full value stack.

---

## $100M Offers 5-Step Ladder

Apply this ladder when building or reviewing any offer for Meta Ads:

1. **High-value** — Pricing must make sense in the Malta market. Research competitor pricing first.
2. **Stack bonuses** to maximize perceived value (e.g., treatment + free add-on + consultation)
3. **Clear and compelling guarantee** — Must have a deadline. Use seasonal occasions to justify urgency.
4. **Strong, urgent call to action** — One specific step. Book now. Not "contact us."
5. **Social proof** — Attach validation to the offer: reviews, client count, awards.

## 3 Steps to Creating Winning Creatives

1. **Define the exact precise target persona** — Not "women 25–55 in Malta." Specifically: "A 42-year-old professional in Sliema who has tried every serum and is embarrassed that her makeup creases by 11am." Name her. Know her morning routine.

2. **Find the messaging and words that resonate with her niche-specific pain** — Use the exact language she uses in her own head. Not clinical language. Not brand language. Her words.

3. **Create ad creatives that appeal to each persona with messaging tailored across funnel stages** — Cold = educate and intrigue. Warm = prove and build trust. Hot = urgency and offer.

## EGC Content Types (Priority Order)

Deploy in this sequence — highest priority first:

1. **Video with results hook** — MOST IMPORTANT. Transformation or outcome shown in first 3 seconds.
2. **Video with price hook** — Lead with the offer value (e.g., "€99 for a treatment worth €250")
3. **B-roll video showing treatment** with top banner PAIN POINT + bottom banner price overlay
4. **Video showing the treatment process** — Demystify the experience, reduce fear of trying
5. **Video showing Day 1 / Day 30** transformation (NOT "before/after" — Meta policy compliance)
6. **Video with science-backed hook** explaining treatment protocol
7. **UGC video** explaining treatment in client's own voice
8. **Static with pain point + price + perceived value**
9. **Case studies** — Use Day 1 and Day 30 framing to comply with Meta advertising rules

## Before/After Rule — Meta Policy Compliance

**NEVER use the words "before" and "after" in Meta Ads copy or creative text.**

Use "Day 1 and Day 30" framing instead.

- Wrong: "Before and After Botox Results"
- Correct: "Day 1 vs Day 30 — natural, refreshed results"

All campaigns must have minimum 2–3 transformation references (Day 1/Day 30) distributed across: ad videos, landing page, and email sequences.

---

## UGC Brief Requirements

All influencers and UGC creators must receive a written brief before filming. Brief must include:

- Brand name and product/treatment being featured
- Pain point to address in the video
- 2–3 hook options to choose from (they may adapt but must keep the intent)
- Desired outcome of the video (what should the viewer think/feel/do)
- Do list: key messages to include, mandatory mentions (offer, location, CTA)
- Don't list: words/claims to avoid, imagery to avoid, brand voice violations

---

## Creative Diversity Rule

Creative diversity is critical for Meta's algorithm performance:

- Multiple creative formats (video, static, carousel) must run simultaneously
- Multiple angles must run simultaneously across cold/warm/hot audiences
- Sequential diversity beats simultaneous — slow drip-feed of new creative outperforms all-at-once launch
- Never launch all new creatives in the same week — stagger releases

---

## Rule of One

**Each ad communicates ONE specific pain point, ONE offer, ONE CTA.**

Never try to say everything in one ad. An ad that tries to address three pain points addresses none. An ad with two CTAs produces no action.

- One pain point
- One offer
- One CTA

---

## Copy Focus Rule

**Copy must focus on the PROSPECT, not the business.**

Focus exclusively on benefits for the customer. Be hyper-specific about PAIN POINTS. The business exists to serve the prospect's transformation — never talk about "our clinic," "our team," "our award-winning service" without immediately connecting it back to what that means for the prospect.

---

## Social Proof Requirements

Every ad must include at minimum ONE of the following:

- Case studies (Day 1 / Day 30 format)
- Scarcity signal: "only 15 spots"
- Social proof: awards ("World Luxury Spa Award Winners"), review count ("#150+ 5-star reviews"), market position ("Malta's top-rated clinic")
- Client count ("Join 2,000+ women in Malta")

---

## Ad Scheduling Rule

**All ads must be scheduled to go live at 12AM the next day.**

Never activate an ad immediately before end of business day. Activating late in the day wastes ad spend during the learning phase when the algorithm cannot optimise properly.

## Frequency Management

**Keep frequency below 3x at all times.**

When frequency exceeds 3x:
- CTR drops
- CPM rises
- Cost per lead rises
- ROAS tanks

Frequency above 3x triggers mandatory creative refresh. Do not wait for CPL to deteriorate — act at frequency 2.8x.

---

## ROAS Targets

| Target | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Target ROAS | 3x–4x+ |
| Break-even ROAS | 2x |

Campaigns below 2x ROAS for 7+ days are candidates for pause or creative refresh.

---

## 4 Considerations Determining Daily Ad Spend

Evaluate these 4 factors before setting or changing daily budget:

1. **ROAS performance vs target** — Is the campaign at, above, or below target ROAS?
2. **Number of creatives needed to exit learning phase** — Each ad set needs sufficient budget to generate 50+ conversions/week to exit learning
3. **Market size** — Malta population 520k plus seasonal demand factors. Scale budget to market capacity.
4. **Stock availability** — Treatment capacity and appointment availability must support the lead volume the budget will generate

---

## Ad Refresh Cadence

- Refresh ads **at least once per week**
- Rotate hooks while keeping core body copy identical
- Track which hook rotation produces the CTR lift
- No creative should run longer than 6 weeks without a complete refresh plan in place
