## Customer Archetypes

Five distinct personas. Each has a different trigger, different fear, different language.

### Archetype 1: Mariella — The Busy Mum (Age 32–48)
**Profile:** Runs a household, works full or part-time, puts everyone else first. She's gained weight through pregnancies or life stage creep. She's not devastated — she's frustrated. She's tried diets. Nothing sticks long-term.
- **Trigger:** Saw a photo of herself at a family event. Her clothes don't fit anymore.
- **Primary desire:** Feel like herself again. Have energy. Fit into the clothes she loves.
- **Main fear:** No time for it. Another program that doesn't work. Judgement from others.
- **Trust needs:** Flexible program, can fit around her schedule, WhatsApp check-ins.
- **Messaging:** Empathetic, practical, "we fit around your life not the other way around."

### Archetype 2: Grace — The Menopausal Professional (Age 48–60)
**Profile:** She's always been relatively in control of her weight. Then menopause hit. The weight redistributed. Her waist disappeared. Nothing she's done before works anymore.
- **Trigger:** Doctor told her to watch her weight. Her body doesn't respond the way it used to.
- **Primary desire:** Understand what's happening and regain control. Science-backed answers.
- **Main fear:** That this is just how it is now. Judgment at the gym.
- **Trust needs:** Doctor-led, clinical credibility, body composition science, not just calories.
- **Messaging:** Evidence-led, "your body has changed — so should your approach."

### Archetype 3: Sarah — The Bride/Socialite (Age 24–38)
**Profile:** An upcoming event is the trigger. Wedding, holiday, class reunion, birthday party. She has a very specific goal and a hard deadline.
- **Trigger:** Engagement. A holiday booked. A milestone birthday.
- **Primary desire:** Look and feel amazing for the occasion. Specific body goals.
- **Main fear:** Running out of time. Not seeing results fast enough.
- **Trust needs:** Clear results timeline, treatment plan aligned to her event date.
- **Messaging:** Goal-oriented, timeline-specific, "we'll get you there."

### Archetype 4: The Obese Client (Age 35–65)
**Profile:** Her weight is significantly impacting her health and daily life. She has tried many times. She needs medical support, not willpower advice.
- **Trigger:** Health scare. Doctor's orders. Difficulty doing everyday activities.
- **Primary desire:** Get healthier. Reduce pain. Live longer.
- **Main fear:** Judgment. Being treated differently. Programs built for people who don't need much to lose.
- **Trust needs:** Non-judgmental environment, medical supervision, compassionate support.
- **Messaging:** "This is about health, not a number. We've supported people exactly like you."
- **Sensitivity:** Extra care required. No before/after imagery without explicit consent and careful framing.

### Archetype 5: The Postpartum Mum (Age 26–42)
**Profile:** Baby is here and she's struggling to feel like herself again. Her body has changed. She's sleep-deprived and time-poor. She wants to feel confident again without feeling like she's being selfish.
- **Trigger:** Baby is now 4+ months old. She's feeling ready to address her body.
- **Primary desire:** Feel like herself again. Regain confidence without extreme sacrifice.
- **Main fear:** Being judged for caring about her appearance. The program being too demanding.
- **Trust needs:** Compassionate, flexible, celebrates non-scale victories, no shame.
- **Messaging:** "Your body did something incredible. Now let's take care of it."

## Common Pain Behaviours (For Creative & Copy Reference)

These are the physical and social behaviours your prospect is currently experiencing. Reference them in copy to create instant recognition:

- Avoiding cameras at social events
- Hiding in baggy clothes or dark colours
- Choosing what to wear based on what hides most, not what looks best
- Breathlessness climbing stairs or playing with children
- Joint pain (knees, hips, lower back) that limits activity
- Feeling self-conscious at the beach or pool — avoiding swimming
- Turning down invitations because "I don't want to be in photos"
- Starting diets repeatedly and quitting within 2 weeks
- Comparing herself to how she used to look

## Slimming-Specific Creative Rules

- NEVER use shame-based imagery or language
- NEVER use the words "fat," "overweight," "obese," "problem area," "trouble zone"
- NEVER show scales as the primary success metric
- NEVER retouch transformation images — real bodies, real results
- ALWAYS frame weight as a health journey, not a cosmetic fix
- ALWAYS use "Day 1 / Day 30" framing — NEVER "before/after" (Meta policy compliance)
- ALWAYS get explicit written consent before using transformation content in ads
- Celebrate non-scale victories: energy, clothing fit, health markers, confidence
- Frame past failures as system failures, not personal failures: "the programs failed you"
