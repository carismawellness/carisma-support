# Branding Guidelines

## Tone of Voice

### Do & Don't Language Guidelines

#### ✅ DO:

**Validate their experience**
- "You are not the only one who feels this way."
- "If you have started over more times than you can count, this is for you."

**Use calm, clear, concrete language**
- "Lose 5 to 10 kilos in a medically supervised, step by step program."
- "We plan your meals, movement and check ins together."

**Normalize relapse and imperfection**
- "You did not fail. The plan failed you."
- "We design something you can live with on a bad day, not just a perfect one."

**Emphasize control and choice**
- "You choose what you eat. We show you how to make it work."
- "Your plan adapts to weekends, stress, and real life."

**Talk about how life feels, not just how the body looks**
- "Climb stairs without thinking about it."
- "Wear the dress without checking angles in photos."

#### ❌ DON'T:

**Do not shame or blame**
- Avoid: "lack of discipline", "you let yourself go", "fix your body"

**Do not use toxic positivity**
- Avoid: "Just love yourself and it will work out", "summer body in 2 weeks"

**Do not over promise**
- Avoid exact extreme claims like "drop 10kg in 10 days", "results for everyone, no effort"

**Do not reduce them to a number**
- Avoid only talking about kilos and sizes without mentioning energy, confidence, health

**Do not use fear mongering around health**
- Avoid: "You will get sick if you do not join now", "your body is a ticking time bomb"

## Core Voice Principles

### 1. Compassionate Truth Telling
We speak to women like a trusted friend who has receipts. Warm, never judging, but also honest about what it really takes to change. We acknowledge their struggle before we offer a solution.

### 2. Gentle Structure, Not Punishment
Our tone says: you are not broken, you just need a clear plan and support. We do not glorify restriction or hustle. We frame structure as caring guidance, not discipline or punishment.

### 3. Evidence Led, Emotion Aware
We combine emotional understanding with grounded, real results. We lean on data, clear explanations, and proof, but always through a human, empathetic lens.

### 4. Safe, Shame Free Space
We never talk down to weight, age, or appearance. We normalize struggle, slip ups, and starting again. Our voice makes them feel "I am not the only one" and "It is safe to try again here."

### 5. Future Focused, Present Centered
We help them envision their next chapter, while anchoring them in tiny wins today. Less "fix your body" and more "build the life you want to live in your body."

## Copy Examples by Format

### a) Social Captions

**For the Busy Mum (Mariella):**
"You can't pour from an empty cup, and you can't run a family on caffeine and leftover nuggets. Reclaim your energy. Your kids deserve a happy mum, but you deserve to feel like yourself again."

**For the Menopausal Professional (Grace):**
"Your metabolism isn't broken; it's just misunderstood. Stop trying to solve a hormonal problem with a calorie calculator. Let's look at the data, not just the scale."

**For the Bride/Socialite (Sarah):**
"The dress is bought. The date is set. The panic is optional. 6 weeks to a version of you that walks down the aisle with your head held high. No crash diets, just science."

### b) Video Openers (UGC / TikTok style)

- "Stop eating salads if they leave you bloated and hungry an hour later. Here is why your 'healthy' diet is actually stressing your metabolism..."
- "If you're dreading the fitting room lights, keep watching. We need to talk about the 'apron' belly and why crunches won't touch it."
- "Malta has the highest obesity rate in Europe, but it's not because we are lazy. It's because we are surrounded by carbs and stress. Here is how to navigate it without staying home forever."

### c) Email Subject Lines

- "The last time you have to start over"
- "What if your next 6 months did not look like your last 6 years?"
- "Read this before you try another diet"
- "You can be kind to yourself and still lose weight"
- "It's not the pastizzi. It's the insulin."
- "Why you gained weight (and it wasn't your fault)."
- "45 and stuck? Read this."
- "The truth about 'Mummy Tummy' (and how to fix it)."

## Emotional Journey

When a woman reads our copy, she should feel a specific progression of emotions:

### 1. Validation (The "Exhale")
"Finally, someone understands that I'm trying hard but it's not working."

### 2. Relief (The "Safety")
"Okay, this isn't just another starvation diet. There is a doctor/science involved."

### 3. Permission (The "Yes")
"It is okay for me to spend this money on myself. I need this to function."

### 4. Excitement (The "Vision")
"I can actually see myself wearing that dress/bikini."
