## Campaign Types for Local Service Lead Generation

Google Ads for local service businesses requires a multi-format strategy. Each campaign type serves a distinct role in the funnel. Running only one type leaves intent uncaptured and audiences unreached.

### 1. Search Campaigns (Highest Intent — Most Important)

Search campaigns intercept people who are actively searching for exactly what Carisma offers. A person searching "botox Malta" or "spa day packages Malta" has high commercial intent — they are ready or nearly ready to book. This is the most valuable traffic source in the account and should always receive the majority of budget.

Structure search campaigns around three keyword themes:
- **Branded:** Queries that include the Carisma brand name. Always run a separate branded campaign to protect the brand SERP and capture warm intent at low CPC. Never let branded terms compete inside non-branded campaigns.
- **Service:** Queries for the treatment or category (e.g., "massage Malta", "lip fillers Malta", "weight loss clinic Malta"). Core of the always-on search strategy.
- **Pain-point / occasion:** Queries that describe the problem or occasion rather than the solution (e.g., "stress relief Malta", "birthday gift for her Malta", "menopause belly treatment"). These capture earlier-stage intent and often have lower CPCs with less competition.

### 2. Performance Max (PMax) — Broad Discovery + Maps Integration

Performance Max campaigns run across all Google channels simultaneously: Search, Display, YouTube, Gmail, Discover, and Google Maps. Google's algorithm determines where to serve the ads based on the asset groups and audience signals provided.

PMax is most powerful in two scenarios for Carisma:
- **Remarketing:** Targeting past website visitors with Google's cross-channel reach. This is typically the highest-ROAS campaign in the account because it re-engages warm audiences.
- **Discovery expansion:** Reaching new audiences who have not searched yet but match the demographic and behavioral profile of existing customers.

PMax also integrates with Google Maps, increasing visibility for "spa near me" and "clinic near me" searches on mobile — high-converting in Malta's small geographic market.

### 3. Local Service Ads (LSAs) — Google Guaranteed

Local Service Ads appear above standard search results and include a "Google Guaranteed" badge. Unlike standard Google Ads, LSAs charge per lead (phone call or message), not per click. This makes budget control more predictable for service businesses.

LSA requirements:
- Minimum 5 verified Google reviews
- Business license verification
- Background check (applicable to medical/treatment businesses)

LSAs are particularly effective for high-intent queries: "spa near me", "aesthetics clinic near me", "slimming treatment Malta". Running LSAs alongside search campaigns maximizes SERP real estate and increases the probability of capturing the click.

### 4. Display and Remarketing (RLSA)

Remarketing campaigns re-engage people who visited Carisma websites but did not convert. These audiences are already aware of the brand, making them significantly cheaper to convert than cold traffic.

RLSA (Remarketing Lists for Search Ads) allows bid adjustments on search campaigns for past visitors. A user who visited the Botox page last week and searches "botox Malta" today should receive a higher bid and a tailored ad — they are the warmest possible prospect.

Audience segments to build and target:
- All website visitors (past 30 days)
- Service page visitors (past 30 days) — higher intent
- Cart/booking form visitors who did not convert — highest intent
- Past customers (email list match) — for upsell and rebooking

### 5. Call-Only Campaigns

In markets where phone-first behavior is dominant — and Malta is one of them — call-only campaigns capture leads who prefer to call rather than fill in a form. The ad shows a phone number and click-to-call button; there is no destination URL.

Call-only campaigns are particularly effective for:
- Aesthetics bookings where patients want to ask medical questions before committing
- Slimming programs where prospects want to understand the consultation process
- Spa bookings from older demographics who are less comfortable with online forms

Set call length threshold at 60 seconds to qualify conversions. Calls under 60 seconds are exploratory, not committed.

## Campaign Structure Best Practices

### Branded vs. Non-Branded Separation

Always maintain separate campaigns for branded and non-branded keywords. Reasons:
1. **Budget control:** Branded terms convert at 5-10x the rate of non-branded and cost a fraction of the CPC. Mixing them dilutes budget allocation — the algorithm may over-invest in non-branded if branded is more efficient.
2. **Performance clarity:** Cross-brand reports become unreadable when branded and non-branded KPIs are blended. You cannot diagnose non-branded performance if branded results mask the true picture.
3. **Competitor protection:** If a competitor bids on "Carisma Spa", having a separate branded campaign with high bids ensures Carisma always outranks them on its own name.

### Themed Ad Groups

Each ad group should contain 10–20 keywords that share the same search intent theme. The test: would a single ad copy set be equally relevant to every keyword in the group? If not, split the group.

Example of correct theming:
- Ad group "Botox Malta" contains: "botox malta", "botox near me malta", "anti-wrinkle injections malta", "botox clinic malta", "botox treatment malta". All of these are served by the same ad copy.
- Ad group "Lip Fillers Malta" is separate because lip filler searchers have different intent and need different copy.

Example of incorrect theming:
- Ad group "Aesthetics" containing: "botox malta", "lip filler malta", "hydrafacial malta", "micro-needling malta". These require completely different ads and landing pages.

### Match Types

**Starting structure:**
- Use Broad Match with smart bidding (Target CPA or Target ROAS) as the primary match type for established campaigns with sufficient conversion data. Google's broad match has improved significantly and works well with smart bidding.
- Use Exact Match for branded keywords and proven top converters to maintain tight control.
- Add Phrase Match for terms where you want some expansion but with more control than Broad.

**Negative keyword management is non-negotiable.** Add negatives every week from the Search Terms report. A campaign running without weekly negative keyword additions is bleeding budget.

### RSA Ads

Minimum 3 RSA ads per ad group. Target "Good" or "Excellent" ad strength for all RSAs. Google uses RSA performance as a Quality Score signal — strong RSAs improve QS, reduce CPC, and improve ad rank.

### Asset Groups in PMax

Create 3–5 distinct asset groups per PMax campaign. Each asset group should represent a different audience segment or messaging angle:
- Asset Group 1: Brand awareness (logo, brand name, brand benefits)
- Asset Group 2: Treatment-specific (service images, treatment benefits, specific CTA)
- Asset Group 3: Social proof (review screenshots, awards, testimonials)
- Asset Group 4 (optional): Seasonal or offer-specific

Provide maximum assets in each group: 5 headlines, 5 long headlines, 5 descriptions, 5 square images, 5 landscape images, 1+ logo, 1+ video (even a 15-second video dramatically improves PMax performance).

## Bidding Strategy Progression

Start with conservative bidding and progress to smart bidding as conversion data accumulates. Skipping stages leads to algorithm instability and wasted spend.

### Stage 1: Maximize Conversions (0–30 conversions)

Use Maximize Conversions without a target CPA when launching a new campaign. This gives Google's algorithm full latitude to find conversions and build a dataset. Do not set a target CPA yet — the algorithm needs to learn first.

Requirement to progress: 30 tracked conversions in the past 30 days.

### Stage 2: Target CPA (30–100 conversions)

Once you have 30+ conversions, set a Target CPA. The critical rule: set the initial Target CPA at 20% above the actual CPA you observed during Stage 1. This gives the algorithm room to operate. Setting a Target CPA below current actual CPA causes the algorithm to restrict impressions aggressively, killing volume.

Example: If Stage 1 delivered leads at EUR 35 CPA, set initial Target CPA at EUR 42. Gradually lower toward EUR 25 target over 4–6 weeks as the algorithm stabilizes.

Requirement to progress: 100+ conversions with stable conversion rate.

### Stage 3: Target ROAS (When Average Order Value Varies)

Switch to Target ROAS when you have reliable revenue values attached to conversions and treatment values vary significantly. This is most applicable to Aesthetics where a botox consultation (EUR 80) and a full filler treatment (EUR 600) should generate different bid levels.

For Spa and Slimming where treatments are more uniform, Target CPA is usually sufficient.

### CPL Targets by Brand

These are the maximum acceptable cost per lead. Campaigns delivering above these thresholds for 7+ consecutive days require intervention.

| Brand | CPL Target | Notes |
|-------|-----------|-------|
| Carisma Spa & Wellness | EUR 15–25 | Mass market, higher volume, lower treatment values |
| Carisma Aesthetics | EUR 25–50 | Premium market, lower volume, higher treatment values |
| Carisma Slimming | USD 30–55 | Medical positioning, longest sales cycle |

Always cross-reference with `config/kpi_thresholds.json` for current approved targets. The table above is directional guidance; the config file has the operative thresholds.

## Ad Extensions (Mandatory for All Campaigns)

Extensions increase ad real estate, improve Quality Score, and improve CTR. Every campaign must have all applicable extensions live before going active.

### 1. Location Extension
Link to the Google Business Profile for all Carisma locations. Shows the address, map pin, and distance to the user. Critical for local services. Without this, you are giving up valuable SERP space.

### 2. Call Extension
Link the brand's primary phone number. Set call tracking to record and count calls over 60 seconds as conversions. In Malta, phone-first behavior is common — especially for medical and high-ticket services.

### 3. Sitelinks (Minimum 4 per campaign)
Direct users to high-converting pages. Recommended sitelinks:
- "Book Now" → booking/contact page
- "Treatments" or "Our Services" → treatment menu
- "Gift Vouchers" → gift card purchase page (Spa) or equivalent
- "About Us" or "Meet Our Team" → trust-building page
- "Special Offers" or "Promotions" → seasonal offer page

Each sitelink needs a 25-character headline and two 35-character description lines.

### 4. Callout Extensions (6–8 minimum)
Short trust signals that appear beneath the ad. Examples:
- "World Luxury Spa Awards"
- "Doctor-Led Treatments"
- "150+ 5-Star Reviews"
- "Free Consultation"
- "10 Locations in Malta"
- "Medically Supervised"
- "No Crash Diets"
- "Book Online 24/7"

### 5. Structured Snippets
List specific services or features in a scannable format. Header options: "Services", "Treatments", "Amenities".
Examples: "Swedish Massage, Hot Stone, Hammam, Couples Spa" (Spa) / "Botox, Lip Fillers, Hydrafacial, Microneedling" (Aesthetics)

### 6. Lead Form Extension
Capture enquiries directly within the SERP without requiring a landing page visit. The user fills in name, email, and phone directly in Google. High-intent, low-friction. Particularly effective for free consultation offers.

### 7. Promotion Extension
Use for seasonal offers and flash sales. Shows a special offer tag directly in the search result.
Examples: "Valentine's Day Sale — Save 20% on Couples Spa" / "January Offer — Free Slimming Consultation"

## Quality Score Optimization

Quality Score (QS) is Google's relevance rating for each keyword. It is calculated on a 1–10 scale based on three components: Expected CTR, Ad Relevance, and Landing Page Experience.

QS directly impacts both ad rank and CPC. A keyword with QS 8 pays significantly less per click than the same keyword with QS 5 at the same ad position. Maintaining high Quality Scores is not optional — it is a core cost efficiency lever.

**Target:** QS 7–10 for all keywords. QS below 5 requires immediate action.

### Expected CTR Improvement
- Include the exact keyword in Headline 1 of the RSA
- Use ad copy that closely mirrors the intent of the search query
- Improve CTR with compelling CTAs and strong USPs
- Monitor CTR weekly; below 2% CTR on exact match keywords = rewrite the ad

### Ad Relevance Improvement
- Each ad group should contain only keywords from a single intent theme
- RSA headlines should include multiple variations of the keyword theme
- Avoid generic ads that apply to every service — specificity improves relevance scores

### Landing Page Experience Improvement
- The landing page H1 must mirror the keyword theme (a person clicking "botox malta" must land on a page whose H1 mentions "botox" — not a generic treatments page)
- Page load speed below 3 seconds on mobile (Malta users are mobile-first)
- Clear CTA above the fold matching the ad's CTA
- Service information must match the ad's claims — no bait-and-switch
- Use Google PageSpeed Insights to audit landing page performance

### QS Diagnostic Actions

| QS Component | Below Average Signal | Action |
|-------------|---------------------|--------|
| Expected CTR | Low ad engagement despite impressions | Rewrite headlines, add stronger CTAs, improve offer specificity |
| Ad Relevance | Keyword theme doesn't match ads | Restructure ad group, add keyword to headlines |
| Landing Page | High bounce rate, slow load | Work with web team on page speed and message match |

## Conversion Tracking Setup

Accurate conversion tracking is the foundation of smart bidding. Without it, the algorithm optimizes for clicks, not results.

### Primary Conversions (Bid-Optimized)

| Conversion | Trigger | Value |
|-----------|---------|-------|

### Secondary Conversions (Observation Only — Do Not Bid)

| Conversion | Trigger | Purpose |

| WhatsApp click | Click on WhatsApp button/link | High-intent signal in Malta market |

Slimming leads often require 7–14 days between initial enquiry and consultation booking. Never evaluate Slimming CPA against a 3-day window. Use 14-day attribution minimum for Slimming campaign analysis.

## Malta Market Specifics

Malta is a unique advertising environment. Standard UK or US benchmarks do not apply.

### Volume Reality

Malta has a population of 520,000. Monthly search volume for most local service keywords is 50–500 searches per month — not 5,000. This has direct implications:
- **Campaign learning period is longer.** Getting to 30 conversions for Target CPA bidding may take 2–3 months rather than 2–3 weeks.
- **Statistical significance takes longer.** Do not call A/B test winners after 50 clicks. Wait for minimum 100 clicks and 30 conversions.
- **Impression Share matters more.** With limited total volume, losing 30% impression share to competitors is losing 30% of a small pool. Protect impression share aggressively.

### CPC Reality

CPCs in Malta are often higher than UK equivalents because fewer advertisers compete but search volume is also low — Google prices clicks at a premium when volume is scarce. Budget for:
- Spa terms: EUR 0.50–1.50 per click
- Aesthetics terms: EUR 2.00–8.00 per click (botox/filler highly competitive)
- Slimming terms: USD 1.50–4.00 per click

### Geographic Targeting

Set a 15km radius from each clinic location. Malta is 316 km² — the entire island fits within this radius from most central locations. However, radius targeting still matters for:
- Gozo (if expanding there)
- Excluding searches from users outside Malta (VPN usage, traveling Maltese nationals)
- Future clinic expansion planning

Always combine radius targeting with location exclusions for irrelevant geographies.

### Language and Audience

English is the primary language for Google Ads in Malta. Maltese-language ads are possible but the ROI is lower — most Maltese residents use English for service searches. During peak tourist season (June–August), consider adding Italian and German ad copy variants, as a significant portion of Malta's tourist market comes from Italy and Germany.

### Word-of-Mouth Effect

Malta has a strong word-of-mouth culture. Branded search volume increases significantly after social campaigns, PR, and satisfied customer referrals. This means:
- Branded campaigns should always be running, even at minimal budget
- Branded QS should be maintained at 9–10
- Branded CPA is almost always the lowest in the account — protect and prioritize it

## Performance Max Strategy for Local Services

PMax requires different management than search campaigns. The following protocol applies to all Carisma PMax campaigns.

### Asset Group Design

Create 3–5 asset groups per PMax campaign. Each asset group targets a different audience or messaging angle:

**For Spa Remarketing PMax:**
- Asset Group 1: Past visitors, general spa messaging
- Asset Group 2: Service page visitors (massage, facial), treatment-specific messaging
- Asset Group 3: Gift card visitors, gift-oriented messaging

**For PMax discovery campaigns:**
- Asset Group 1: Core brand awareness
- Asset Group 2: Most popular treatment category
- Asset Group 3: Offer/seasonal angle

### Audience Signals

Provide audience signals to accelerate algorithm learning. PMax uses signals as starting points, not hard constraints, but strong signals reduce learning time.

Recommended signals:
1. Remarketing lists (all past visitors + converted customers)
2. Customer email match (upload existing customer CSV)
3. Custom intent segments (people searching treatment keywords)
4. In-market audiences: "Beauty Salons & Spas", "Cosmetic Procedures", "Weight Management"
5. Affinity audiences: "Luxury Shoppers", "Health & Wellness"

### PMax Management Rules

- Review Insights tab weekly for top search themes. Surface high-performing themes to the Creative Strategist for exact match keyword expansion.
- If branded search campaign is running, exclude branded terms from PMax to prevent cannibalization and budget waste.
- Do NOT pause asset groups individually without 30 days of data — the algorithm needs time to optimize.
- Add audience exclusions: exclude converted customers from acquisition campaigns.
- Set the same location radius as search campaigns.

## Budget Allocation

### Default Allocation Framework

| Campaign Type | Budget Share | Rationale |
|--------------|-------------|-----------|
| Search (non-branded) | 55% | Highest intent, primary lead driver |
| Branded Search | 5% | Low CPC, high conversion, brand protection |
| Performance Max | 25% | Discovery + Maps + remarketing reach |
| Remarketing / RLSA | 10% | Lowest CPL, warm audience |
| Competitor Keywords | 5% | Intercept competitor brand searches |

This is a starting framework. Adjust based on performance data. If remarketing CPL is 50% below Search CPL, reallocate more budget there. The framework guides starting point, not permanent allocation.

### Budget Adjustment Triggers

Increase budget when:
- Campaign is losing >30% impression share to budget constraints (not rank)
- CPA is 20% below target AND conversion volume is limited by budget
- Seasonal period with documented demand spike approaching

Decrease budget when:
- CPA exceeds target by 20% for 7+ consecutive days despite optimization attempts
- Demand-toggle trigger engaged (e.g., LHR campaign when fully booked)
- Brand strategic pivot reduces priority of a specific treatment category

## Local Service Ads (LSAs)

### Qualification Requirements

To run LSAs, Carisma must:
1. Have minimum 5 Google reviews per location (currently strong across all brands)
2. Complete Google Guaranteed business verification
3. Pass Google's background check process for service businesses
4. Maintain a minimum 3.0 star rating (maintaining 4.5+ currently)

### LSA Management Protocol

- Check LSA dashboard weekly for new leads and response rate
- Respond to all LSA leads within 1 hour during business hours (response rate affects LSA ranking)
- Dispute invalid leads (wrong service area, spam) within 30 days for credit
- Monitor cost-per-lead from LSAs vs. standard search CPL — LSAs should be comparable or lower
- Align LSA service categories with highest-performing search campaign themes

### LSA + Search Campaign Coordination

Running LSAs and search campaigns simultaneously is recommended — they can occupy different positions on the same SERP. LSAs appear at the very top (above even standard search ads) and search ads appear below. This double coverage increases the probability that Carisma captures the click.

### Attribution Model

Use **Data-Driven Attribution (DDA)** as the default model. DDA uses machine learning to assign conversion credit across the full user journey (multiple clicks, multiple sessions). This is more accurate than last-click attribution, which over-credits the final ad click and under-credits awareness-driving keywords.

Switch to time decay if Data-Driven is unavailable due to insufficient conversion volume (requires 3,000+ clicks and 300+ conversions in 30 days). Never use last-click attribution for cross-campaign analysis — it will systematically undervalue branded and PMax campaigns.

### Search Term Report Analysis Protocol (Weekly)

1. Pull search terms report for the past 7 days for all campaigns
2. Sort by cost (descending)
3. Review top 50 queries per brand
4. For each query, classify: Relevant (keep), Irrelevant (add as negative), New opportunity (add as keyword)
5. Add all irrelevant queries as negatives at the campaign or account level
6. Add high-performing new queries as phrase or exact match keywords in the appropriate ad group
7. Flag any queries spending EUR 5+ with zero conversions for immediate negative addition
8. Share findings with Creative Strategist weekly — search term intelligence drives keyword strategy

### Auction Insights Review

Pull Auction Insights monthly for all search campaigns. Review:
- **Impression Share:** What percentage of eligible impressions you won vs. competitors
- **Overlap Rate:** How often competitor ads show alongside yours
- **Position Above Rate:** How often competitors appear above you
- **Top of Page Rate:** Percentage of impressions appearing at the top of SERP

If a new competitor appears in Auction Insights with >20% overlap rate, escalate to the Creative Strategist and Google Ads Specialist for competitive response planning.

## Quality Score Monitoring Protocol

Check Quality Score for all keywords weekly. A keyword's QS is visible in the keyword view with the diagnostics column enabled.

### QS Classification

| QS Score | Status | Action Required |
|---------|--------|----------------|
| 9–10 | Excellent | Maintain — do not change structure |
| 7–8 | Good | Minor optimization if CTR below benchmark |
| 5–6 | Average | Review ad relevance and landing page match |
| 3–4 | Below Average | Restructure ad group or rewrite ads urgently |
| 1–2 | Poor | Consider pausing keyword and rebuilding in new ad group |

### QS Degradation Alert

If any primary keyword (high spend keyword) drops below QS 6 compared to the previous week, escalate to:
1. Report Analyst: Flag in the weekly report
2. Creative Strategist: Review ad group structure and keyword themes
3. Copywriter: Rewrite RSA ads for better keyword relevance
4. Google Ads Specialist: Review landing page experience and approve action plan

## A/B Testing Framework

All Google Ads tests must follow single-variable isolation. Testing multiple variables simultaneously makes it impossible to determine what drove the improvement.

### Test Types and Minimum Requirements

| Test Type | Variable | Min Clicks | Min Duration | Success Metric |
|-----------|----------|-----------|-------------|----------------|
| Headline angle | Primary benefit claim | 100 per variant | 14–21 days | CTR improvement |
| CTA | Call to action language | 100 per variant | 21–28 days | Conversion rate |
| Offer framing | Discount vs. free trial vs. guarantee | 30 conversions per variant | 21–28 days | CPA improvement |
| Landing page | Destination URL | 30 conversions per variant | 21–28 days | CPA improvement |
| Bidding strategy | Target CPA vs. Max Conversions | 30 conversions per variant | 30 days | CPA efficiency |

### Test Design Protocol

1. Write the hypothesis before launching: "Changing [X] from [A] to [B] will improve [metric] by approximately [N]% because [rationale]."
2. Define the success criteria before launching: "We will call [B] the winner if it outperforms [A] by at least [N]% at 95% statistical confidence."
3. Set a decision date before launching. Do not extend tests indefinitely if they run past the planned end date without a clear winner.
4. Document all test results regardless of outcome — losing tests are as valuable as winning tests.
