## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

### Strategic Position

Carisma Slimming occupies a unique market position combining **clinical credibility** (30+ years doctor-led experience) with **consumer-friendly pricing** (€199 vs. €2,000-€4,000 market rate). This creates three competitive advantages:

1. **Medical Authority vs. DIY Apps** — Real doctor oversight vs. automated algorithms (Noom, Weight Watchers)
2. **Transparent Pricing vs. Hidden Costs** — €199 upfront vs. "call for quote" (LaserAway, Ideal Image)
3. **Professional-Grade vs. Consumer Devices** — FDA-cleared EMSculpt NEO vs. ineffective at-home EMS ($50-$150 knock-offs)

### Market Opportunity

**2026 Landscape Shifts:**
- **GLP-1 medications** (Ozempic, Wegovy) mainstreaming creates $100B market BUT also counter-demand for natural alternatives
- **Meta's Andromeda algorithm** rewards creative diversity over audience targeting precision
- **Carousel ads** delivering 30-50% lower cost per conversion than single-image ads
- **UGC-style content** outperforming polished ads by 4x on engagement
- **Q1 CPMs** at 13-month low ($15.74 global, down from $25.22 in November 2025)

### Recommended Creative Strategy

**The Carisma Formula:**
1. **Format:** Carousel ads (5-card storytelling) using raw UGC testimonials
2. **Messaging:** Doctor-led credibility + transparent pricing + natural GLP-1 alternative
3. **Production:** 15 diverse ad concepts across 4 offer types
4. **Budget:** 40% Medical Weight Loss, 30% CoolSculpting, 20% EMSculpt NEO, 10% Skin Tightening
5. **Target KPI:** <€30 CPL (consultations), >1.5% CTR, >70% show rate

## COMPETITOR RESEARCH FINDINGS

### Category 1: Medical Weight Loss Competitors

**Analyzed:** Weight Watchers, Noom, Slimming World, V Shred, Warrior Babe, Join Weight Care

#### Key Findings

**1. GLP-1 Medication Integration (Major Trend)**
- **Weight Watchers** pivoted to GLP-1 prescription program ("Med+")
- Telehealth companies (Willow Health, others) prescribing to anyone: "You don't need to be obese to start a GLP-1"
- **Regulatory backlash:** 35 state attorneys general pressuring Meta over misleading weight-loss drug ads
- **Market size:** $100B projected by 2030-2035

**2. Behavior Change Over Quick Fixes**
- **Noom** spent $21M+ on digital ads (January 2022), emphasizes "backed by science," psychology-driven
- Uses quiz funnels, "83% of users improved habits" social proof
- **Pricing:** $60/month recurring subscription (no endpoint)

**3. Custom Plans + "Science-Backed" Claims**
- **V Shred** uses aggressive body-type quiz → custom workouts → supplement upsells
- **Criticism:** Registered dietitians note plans are "generic templates," not truly personalized
- **Customer sentiment:** Feeling "scammed" by upsells

**4. Community + Accountability**
- **Warrior Babe** targets women 40+ with menopause focus, "transformed 20,000+ women"
- **Slimming World** emphasizes group support, weekly weigh-ins
- Strong retention through peer motivation

#### Competitive Weaknesses (Carisma Advantages)

| Competitor | Weakness | Carisma Counter |
|------------|----------|-----------------|
| **Weight Watchers** | Recurring subscription, no defined endpoint | One-time €199 package, clear beginning/end |
| **Noom** | App-based, no in-person medical oversight | Doctor-led, in-person consultation + treatment |
| **V Shred** | Generic templates despite "custom" claims | True personalization via doctor assessment |
| **Telehealth GLP-1** | Prescribing to unqualified patients (regulatory scrutiny) | "Clinically appropriate" gatekeeping |

### Category 2: CoolSculpting / Fat Freezing Competitors

**Analyzed:** LaserAway, Ideal Image, In The Zone Cryo, Kalon Co., Radient Reshape, Arviv Aesthetics

#### Key Findings

**1. "Fat Reduction Without Surgery" (Universal Hook)**
- Messaging: "Freeze away fat," "Non-invasive," "No downtime"
- **Best practice:** Don't say "CoolSculpting" in ads—use emotional language instead
- Focus on specific areas: abdomen, thighs, arms, love handles

**2. Pricing Strategy**
- **Typical market rate:** $500-$1,500 per session
- **Full treatment packages:** $3,000-$4,000 for 4-6 sessions
- **Promotional strategy:** "At least 50% off" due to intense competition
- **Intro offers:** $99-$199 for first area (loss leader)

**Case Study:** One practice spent $4k/month on ads → 45-80 leads/month → revenue grew from $34k to $82k/month over 18 months

**3. Before/After Photo Ban (Critical Compliance)**
- ⚠️ **COMPLETELY BANNED on Meta** (Facebook & Instagram ads AND landing pages)
- Violations = ad rejection + account penalties
- Use video testimonials, text descriptions, clinical stats instead

**4. Proof Elements (What's Allowed)**
- Clinical studies: "20-25% fat reduction in treated areas"
- Video testimonials (no visual before/after comparisons)
- Text-based case studies

#### Competitive Weaknesses (Carisma Advantages)

| Competitor | Weakness | Carisma Counter |
|------------|----------|-----------------|
| **LaserAway, Ideal Image** | $3,000-$4,000 pricing, hidden costs | €199 transparent pricing (90% discount) |
| **Generic Med Spas** | No doctor-led oversight | 30+ years clinical experience, doctor approval required |
| **All competitors** | "Call for pricing" consultation required | Pricing displayed upfront before booking |

**Gap 4: Clinical Gatekeeping vs. "Anyone Can Buy"**

## CREATIVE TRENDS RESEARCH (2026)

### Top 5 Trends to Implement Immediately

#### 1. Carousel Ads Are the #1 Converting Format

**Performance Data:**
- 30-50% lower cost per conversion vs. single image ads
- 20-30% lower cost per click
- Higher CTR on Instagram specifically

**Why It Works:**
- Storytelling across multiple cards
- Shows multiple benefits/proof points
- Interactive engagement
- Perfect for sequential narrative (Problem → Solution → Results → Proof → Offer)

**Action for Carisma:**
- Shift 40%+ of ad budget to carousel format
- Create 5-card stories per offer type
- Test educational carousels (5 reasons, 3 myths, package breakdown)

---

#### 2. UGC-Style Content Outperforms Polished Ads

**Performance Data:**
- UGC drives 4x higher click-through rates than branded content
- 6.9x higher engagement than brand-produced content
- "Ugly ads" (raw, authentic) beating polished professional ads

**Why It Works:**
- Feels native to feed, not like an ad
- Builds trust through authenticity
- Bypasses "ad blindness"
- Meta's algorithm rewards content that doesn't "feel" like traditional advertising

**Critical Shift:**
- Scripted testimonial UGC is dead (too predictable)
- **Winning formats:** Value-first content, expert commentary, pattern-interrupt videos, raw client reactions

**Action for Carisma:**
- Film raw, phone-shot testimonials from real clients
- "Day in the life" content at clinic
- Staff-created informal educational videos
- Avoid over-editing, perfect lighting, corporate aesthetics

---

#### 3. Short-Form Video: 6-15 Seconds with Sub-5 Second Hooks

**Performance Data:**
- Meta recommends under 15 seconds for all placements
- Optimal length: 6-15 seconds for awareness/consideration
- Hook must land within 3-5 seconds or viewers scroll

**Format Breakdown by Objective:**
- **Awareness:** 6-15 seconds
- **Consideration:** 15-30 seconds
- **Conversion:** Up to 60 seconds if comprehensive overview

**Action for Carisma:**
- Test multiple opening frames to find what retains past 3 seconds
- Use movement and sound immediately at video start
- Design for sound-on AND sound-off (always add captions)
- Create vertical 9:16 format for Reels/Stories priority

---

#### 4. Creative Diversity Over Creative Perfection

**Performance Data:**
- Meta's Andromeda algorithm increased model capacity by 10,000x
- Your creative now determines WHO sees your ad (not just targeting)
- Repeating same visual with different text can hurt performance + increase CPMs

**Why It Works:**
- Algorithm rewards diverse creative angles
- Each creative reaches different micro-audiences
- Multiple angles = more data for algorithm optimization

**Action for Carisma:**
- Run 5-10+ different creative concepts simultaneously
- Test different motivators: pain points, pleasure, testimonials, curiosity, education
- Vary visual style dramatically (not just text overlays)

**Critical Rule:**
- Don't take one winning image and create 10 versions with different headlines
- Meta sees these as the same ad and may penalize with higher CPMs

---

#### 5. Static Images Still Drive 60-70% of Conversions

**Performance Data:**
- Despite video dominance in feeds, static images drive 60-70% of Meta conversions
- Vertical format (4:5 ratio - 1080×1350) performs best in feed
- Easier to test at scale, lower production barriers

**Action for Carisma:**
- Don't abandon static images for video-only strategy
- Use 4:5 vertical format to maximize mobile screen real estate
- Test simple, bold text-on-image ads
- High-contrast designs that stop the scroll

---

### Declining Tactics (What to Avoid)

**1. Manual Placement Restrictions**
- ❌ Restricting to "Instagram Reels only" or "Facebook Feed only"
- ✅ Use Advantage+ placements, let algorithm optimize

**2. Overly Polished, Corporate Creative**
- ❌ Professionally shot studio ads with perfect lighting
- ✅ Raw, phone-shot, authentic content

**3. Repeating Same Visual with Different Text**
- ❌ One image with 15 headline variations
- ✅ Truly diverse creative concepts with different imagery

**4. Granular Manual Targeting**
- ❌ Highly specific audience segments, manual bid adjustments
- ✅ Broader targeting, let creative determine audience

**5. Single Creative Assets**
- ❌ Running one or two ads per campaign
- ✅ 5-10+ diverse concepts simultaneously

- 30% CoolSculpting (€300) — proven demand, search volume
- 20% EMSculpt NEO (€200) — growing awareness, education needed
- 10% Skin Tightening (€100) — test demand, cross-sell to Aesthetics

**Creative Testing:**
- Run all 15 ad concepts simultaneously (diverse creative = algorithm advantage)
- Monitor performance daily
- Pause losers after 48 hours if CPA >2x target
- Double budget on winners showing strong CPL performance

---

### Optimization Cadence

**Daily (Days 1-7):**
- Check CPL, CTR, CPC per ad
- Pause ads with CTR <0.8% after 48 hours
- Pause ads with CPA >2x target after 72 hours
- Reallocate budget to winning ads

**Weekly (Weeks 2-4):**
- Analyze winning angles (which hooks worked?)
- Create 3-5 variations of top performers
- Refresh creative to avoid fatigue (ads decline after 2-4 weeks)
- Test new messaging based on learnings

**Bi-Weekly:**
- Review consultation show-rate and close-rate data
- Adjust messaging if leads are low-quality
- Optimize landing pages based on user feedback
- Create new UGC content from recent clients

## MALTA MARKET COMPLIANCE

### Regulatory Framework

- **Malta Competition and Consumer Affairs Authority (MCCAA):** National oversight
- **Consumer Affairs Act (Chapter 378):** Primary advertising regulation
- **Broadcasting Act (Chapter 350):** Secondary advertising regulation
- **EU Regulation 609/2013:** Total diet replacement for weight control
- **EU Nutrition and Health Claims:** Unauthorized claims prohibited
