## 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

**Project:** Acquisition of INA Spa & Wellness, Malta
**Client:** Carisma Wellness Group
**Classification:** Confidential -- Investment Committee Grade

### Recommendation: CONDITIONAL BUY at EUR 300-350K

The INA Spa & Wellness acquisition presents a defensible investment opportunity for a strategic acquirer with existing wellness operations and clinic launch capability. The deal is not, however, without material risks that require resolution before commitment.

### Key Findings:

1. **The deal is fundamentally a clinic bet, not a spa buy.** The spa alone generates EUR 72K EBITDA and cannot justify the EUR 500K total investment at acceptable returns. The clinic must reach at least EUR 150K annual EBITDA by Year 2 for the deal to clear a 15% hurdle rate. This is the single most important variable in the model.

2. **Valuation at EUR 350K purchase price (4.8x current EBITDA) is at the upper boundary of fair for a static single-location spa.** Market comps for European single-location spas trade at 3.5-5.0x EBITDA. The price is market-fair but not cheap -- it is not the "steal" the negotiation materials suggest.

3. **The probability-weighted expected IRR of 39.8% passes the hurdle rate, but the distribution is highly skewed.** The Bear case produces a 12.8% unlevered IRR (below the 18% hurdle), the Base case produces 32.4%, and the Bull case produces 81.8%. The expected value is pulled upward by the Bull scenario, which our analysis finds implausible.

4. **Several critical assumptions remain unverified:** 12-month revenue history, lease assignment terms, equipment condition, staff retention willingness, clinic regulatory requirements, and actual client base size. Any of these could materially alter the investment thesis.

5. **Downside risk is manageable but real.** If the clinic fails entirely, the spa-only business at EUR 72K EBITDA against a EUR 500K investment produces a negative NPV of approximately EUR 200K over 13 years at 18% discount rate. This is a real capital loss scenario.

### Conditions for Proceeding:
- Verified 12-month revenue at EUR 28K+/month spa
- Landlord written confirmation of lease assignment at EUR 5K/month or below
- Equipment appraisal confirming EUR 100K+ FMV
- Confirmed availability of licensed clinic practitioners in Malta
- Staff retention commitments from key therapists

## 2. STRATEGIC & MARKET ANALYSIS

### 2.1 Market Validation: Malta Wellness & Medical Aesthetics

**Malta's wellness market is real but small.** Malta has a population of approximately 530,000 with 2.8M+ tourist arrivals annually (pre-pandemic levels largely restored by 2024). The island has a growing expatriate community and an aging-but-affluent local population that drives demand for health and wellness services.

**Tourism trajectory:** Malta's tourism has recovered to approximately 95% of 2019 levels by 2025, with continued growth driven by its EU membership, English-speaking population, favorable tax regime, and expanding airline connectivity. The Malta Tourism Authority projects 3-4% annual growth through 2030. This is a stable but not explosive growth environment.

**Demand drivers for spa treatments in Malta:**
- Hotel guests (seasonal, high-volume, low-repeat): ~50-60% of revenue for hotel-adjacent spas
- Local residents (year-round, moderate frequency, moderate repeat): ~25-30%
- Expatriate community (growing, higher spending, moderate repeat): ~10-15%
- Regional medical tourism (small but growing for aesthetics): ~5%

The model's Customer Analysis sheet reveals a critical insight: **retained clients alone generate only EUR 10.5K/month of the EUR 30K spa target.** The remaining EUR 19.5K (65%) must come from walk-ins, hotel referrals, and new client acquisition. This makes the business highly dependent on foot traffic and the hotel partnership -- a structural vulnerability.

**Competitive intensity:** Malta has a limited number of established spas (3-4 significant players), which creates scarcity value but also a thin market. The barrier to entry is moderate -- a new competitor with EUR 300-500K could establish a comparable operation. The medical aesthetics market is less developed, presenting genuine first-mover opportunity, but also carries the risk of limited local demand.

### 2.2 Growth Assumptions Stress Test

The model assumes 5% annual revenue growth across 13 years. This is the compounding engine that drives returns. Here is what happens under alternative growth assumptions:

**Stress Test Results (estimated, Base case @ EUR 500K investment):**

| Growth Rate | Year 13 Revenue | Year 13 EBITDA | Cumulative UFCF | Estimated IRR |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **2%** (recession/stagnation) | EUR 910K | EUR 290K | EUR 2.1M | ~22-24% |
| **3%** (below-trend) | EUR 995K | EUR 330K | EUR 2.5M | ~25-27% |
| **4%** (moderate) | EUR 1,090K | EUR 380K | EUR 2.9M | ~28-30% |
| **5%** (model assumption) | EUR 1,293K | EUR 436K | EUR 3.3M | 32.4% |
| **6%** (above-trend) | EUR 1,410K | EUR 500K | EUR 3.7M | ~35-37% |

**Key insight:** Even at 2% growth, the Base case still clears a 15% hurdle. But the 5% assumption is optimistic for a mature spa component. The clinic may drive 5%+ in early years as it ramps, but expecting 5% compound growth for 13 years assumes the clinic never plateaus -- unlikely in a 530K population market.

### 2.3 Clinic Viability Assessment

**The clinic is where the value lives -- and where the risk concentrates.**

Base case clinic economics: EUR 238.5K Year 1 revenue (partial year, starting Month 3), growing to EUR 360K+ by Year 2. Base case clinic EBITDA: EUR 4.2K Year 1 (essentially breakeven during ramp), growing to EUR 55K Year 2 and EUR 61K Year 3.

**Critical questions:**

1. **Medical aesthetics regulation in Malta:** Malta's medical aesthetics market is regulated by the Superintendent of Public Health and the Medical Council of Malta. Practitioners performing injectable treatments (Botox, fillers) must be licensed medical professionals. The regulatory environment is clear but compliance requirements are non-trivial. **Must verify: Are licensed practitioners available for hire at the budgeted EUR 5.5K/month nurse salary? This seems low for a qualified medical professional in Malta.**

2. **Patient acquisition cost:** The model assumes EUR 250 base CAC for clinic patients, with Meta Ads at EUR 150/client and Google at EUR 200/client. Based on Carisma's existing operations in performance marketing, these figures appear reasonable for the Malta market. However, the model assumes a 20% conversion rate from leads to patients -- **this is optimistic for a new, unbranded clinic. Industry benchmarks for medical aesthetics suggest 10-15% lead-to-patient conversion. At 12% conversion, CAC doubles to EUR 500, and the LTV/CAC ratio drops from 7.2x to 3.6x -- still viable but significantly tighter.**

3. **Patient lifetime value:** The model assumes 3 visits/year at EUR 200/visit = EUR 600/year with 3-year lifetime = EUR 1,800 LTV. These assumptions are reasonable for an established clinic but aggressive for a new entrant. **A more conservative LTV estimate: 2 visits/year at EUR 180/visit = EUR 360/year with 2.5-year lifetime = EUR 900 LTV.** This is still above the EUR 250 CAC (3.6x ratio), so unit economics work even under stress.

4. **Clinic growth trajectory:** The model projects clinic revenue of EUR 9K (Month 3), EUR 15K (Month 4), EUR 19.5K (Month 5), EUR 24K (Month 6), EUR 27K (Month 7-9), EUR 30K (Month 10-12). This implies growing from zero to EUR 30K/month in 10 months. **This is aggressive.** For context, a medical aesthetics clinic in a new market typically takes 12-18 months to reach steady-state revenue. The model assumes 10 months.

5. **Sensitivity: What if clinic reaches only EUR 100K EBITDA (vs EUR 213K Base case)?**

If the clinic generates only EUR 100K EBITDA (approximately half of the spa+clinic Base case total), combined with spa EBITDA of EUR 72K, total EBITDA is EUR 172K. Year 1 cumulative UFCF improves but total 13-year returns drop significantly:

- **Estimated IRR: ~20-22% (vs 32.4% Base case)**
- **Payback: ~5 years (vs 3.7 years)**
- **MOIC: ~4x (vs 6.6x)**

This still clears a 15% hurdle but barely clears the 18% risk-adjusted rate. **The deal becomes marginal if clinic underperforms by 50%.**

## 3. FINANCIAL DUE DILIGENCE & STRESS TESTS

### 3.1 Cash Flow Scrutiny

The Enhanced model (v2.0) materially improves on v1.0 by including marketing costs (5-8% of revenue), maintenance capex (EUR 9K/year at 3% of equipment value), taxes (5% effective rate), and working capital changes. These corrections reduce the UFCF/EBITDA conversion ratio to approximately 85-91%, which is realistic.

**Stress test each assumption:**

| Stress Variable | Base Case | Stress Case | Annual Impact | 13-Year NPV Impact (est.) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Maintenance capex | EUR 9K/year | EUR 15K/year | -EUR 6K/year | -EUR 30-40K NPV |
| Post-acquisition attrition | 20% | 35% | -EUR 36K Year 1 revenue | -EUR 25-30K NPV |
| Marketing spend | 5-8% | 8-10% | -EUR 12-18K/year | -EUR 60-90K NPV |
| Working capital trough | EUR 30K | EUR 50K | -EUR 20K Year 0 | -EUR 20K NPV |
| Combined stress | -- | All above | -EUR 80K+ Year 1 | -EUR 135-180K NPV |

**Under combined stress, Base case NPV drops from EUR 467K to approximately EUR 287-332K.** Still positive, still above zero, but the margin of safety narrows considerably.

### 3.2 Valuation Reality Check

**Multiple analysis:**

| Valuation Metric | Value | Market Range | Assessment |
|---|---|---|---|
| Purchase price / Current EBITDA | EUR 350K / EUR 72K = 4.86x | 3.5-5.0x | Fair (upper boundary) |
| Total investment / Year 1 Base EBITDA | EUR 500K / EUR 81K = 6.2x | 4.5-7.0x (spa+clinic) | Fair for hybrid model |
| Purchase price / Year 1 Base EBITDA | EUR 350K / EUR 81K = 4.3x | 3.5-5.0x | Fair |
| Seller asking / Current EBITDA | EUR 650K / EUR 72K = 9.0x | 3.5-5.0x | **Expensive (2x market)** |

### 3.3 Downside Protection

**Worst case: Spa only, clinic never launches.**

- Annual revenue: EUR 360K (stable)
- Annual EBITDA: EUR 72K (current proven level)
- At EUR 350K purchase + EUR 150K clinic capex = EUR 500K sunk
- 13-year cumulative EBITDA: ~EUR 400K (declining due to cost inflation with 0% growth)
- NPV at 18%: approximately EUR -250K to EUR -300K (significantly negative)

**This is the uncomfortable truth: if the clinic fails entirely, the deal destroys approximately EUR 250-300K of value.** The spa-only business cannot justify a EUR 500K investment at any reasonable discount rate.

However, if the clinic fails, the EUR 150K capex was wasted but the EUR 350K purchase price still acquires a business worth EUR 252-326K (3.5-4.5x EUR 72K EBITDA). The capital loss is primarily the wasted clinic capex (EUR 150K) plus the difference between purchase price and fair value (EUR 25-100K). **Total downside in disaster: EUR 175-250K loss, or 35-50% of invested capital.**

## 4. OPERATIONAL DUE DILIGENCE

### 4.1 Revenue Verification

The model claims EUR 30K/month spa revenue (EUR 360K annual) producing EUR 72K EBITDA (20.1% margin). **This must be verified through:**

1. **Bank statements (last 12-24 months):** Monthly deposits should average EUR 28-32K for spa revenue. Look for trends -- is revenue flat, growing, or declining?
2. **POS/booking system data:** Transaction counts and average ticket values. If the spa processes 400-500 transactions/month at EUR 60-75 average, that confirms EUR 24-37K monthly revenue.
3. **Seasonality verification:** The earlier workbook guide mentioned EUR 24K-43K monthly range. The enhanced model uses a flat EUR 30K. Actual seasonality pattern matters for cash flow planning.

**Key concern:** The two negotiation documents contain different EBITDA figures. The earlier document cites EUR 123K EBITDA (at EUR 30K/month spa plus clinic projections), while the later document cites EUR 72K current EBITDA (spa only) and EUR 81K Year 1 EBITDA (with clinic ramp and marketing costs). The enhanced v2.0 model appears more conservative and credible. **Verify which EBITDA figure represents auditable current reality.**

## 5. DUE DILIGENCE CHECKLIST

**Must-Verify Before Signing LOI:**

- [ ] **12-month revenue detail:** Monthly bank statements or POS data confirming EUR 28K+/month average spa revenue. Trend direction (flat/growing/declining).
- [ ] **Customer data:** Client list size, repeat rate, acquisition source mix (hotel vs. local vs. walk-in). NPS or review scores (Google, TripAdvisor).
- [ ] **Staff roster:** Names, roles, actual salaries (not model assumptions), start dates, contract terms (employee vs. contractor), willingness to remain post-acquisition.
- [ ] **Equipment inventory:** Detailed schedule with manufacturer, model, age, condition. Independent FMV appraisal. Any leased or financed equipment (Numi or other).
- [ ] **Lease agreement:** Full copy. Remaining term confirmation. Escalation clauses. Assignment provisions. Landlord consent requirements. Break clauses.
- [ ] **Tax/legal clean:** No pending litigation. No tax disputes. No employment claims. No regulatory violations. Clean UCC/lien search.
- [ ] **Clinic regulatory requirements:** Medical aesthetics licensing in Malta. Practitioner availability at budgeted salary. Timeline for regulatory approval.
- [ ] **Hotel partnership terms:** Written agreement. Commission rate. Exclusivity provisions. Renewal terms.
- [ ] **Landlord pre-contact:** Informal confirmation of willingness to assign lease. Expected rent adjustments if any.
- [ ] **Competitor intelligence:** Number of spa/clinic operators within 5km. Pricing comparison. Any known new entrants.

## 6. SCENARIO ANALYSIS

### 6.1 Bull Case: Everything Works

- Spa EUR 45K/month, Clinic EUR 55K/month
- Year 1 revenue: EUR 943K, Year 1 EBITDA: EUR 334K
- 13-year cumulative UFCF: EUR 8.85M
- IRR: 81.8%, MOIC: 17.7x

**Assessment:** This scenario requires the clinic to reach capacity ceiling (94% of moderate-utilization max). In a market of 530,000 people, achieving EUR 660K annual clinic revenue (implying 3,300+ procedures/year at EUR 200 average) is aggressive. This requires dominant market share in Malta's medical aesthetics market -- possible but unlikely for a first-year operation. **Probability: 15% (our estimate) vs. 25% (model).**

### 6.2 Base Case: Reasonable Execution

- Spa EUR 30K/month, Clinic EUR 30K/month
- Year 1 revenue: EUR 576K, Year 1 EBITDA: EUR 81K
- 13-year cumulative UFCF: EUR 3.28M
- IRR: 32.4%, MOIC: 6.6x

**Assessment:** This requires the clinic to reach 51% utilization within 10 months and the spa to maintain current performance. Given Carisma's existing brand, marketing capability, and operations expertise, this is achievable. The largest risk is clinic ramp timing and the availability of qualified practitioners. **Probability: 45% (our estimate).**

### 6.3 Downside Case: Clinic Disappoints

- Spa EUR 25K/month (declining), Clinic peaks at EUR 10K/month
- Year 1 revenue: EUR 420K (estimated), Year 1 EBITDA: EUR -20K (estimated)
- Revenue grows 2%, costs grow 3.5%
- 13-year cumulative UFCF: EUR 800K-1.0M (estimated)

**Assessment:** IRR approximately 8-12%. Does not clear the 18% hurdle rate. MOIC approximately 1.6-2.0x -- capital is returned but barely. This scenario occurs if clinic marketing fails, if practitioner quality is poor, or if Malta's population simply does not adopt medical aesthetics at the rates assumed. **Probability: 30% (our estimate).**

### 6.4 Disaster Case: What Kills the Deal

- Clinic never launches (regulatory block, practitioner unavailable, or pandemic)
- Spa revenue drops to EUR 20K/month (major competitor opens)
- Revenue grows 0-1%, costs grow 4%

**Assessment:** EBITDA turns negative by Year 5-7. 13-year cumulative UFCF: EUR 300-400K against EUR 500K investment. IRR: negative. MOIC: 0.6-0.8x. **This is a real capital loss of EUR 100-200K. Probability: 10% (our estimate).**

**Combined expected IRR under our adjusted weights:** Approximately 28-31% (vs. model's 39.8%). **The deal remains attractive but with meaningfully lower expected returns than the model suggests.**

## 7. INVESTMENT DECISION & RECOMMENDATION

### 7.1 Strategic Fit

**CONDITIONAL YES.** This acquisition fits a multi-location wellness platform strategy. It provides:
- Entry into the Malta market with an operating business generating cash flow from Day 1
- A clinic testing ground with lower risk than greenfield (leveraging existing spa client base for cross-sell)
- Operational synergies with Carisma's existing brand, marketing, and management capabilities
- Optionality: if the clinic model proves viable in Malta, it can be replicated across Carisma's hotel partnerships

The strategic value beyond financial returns is significant: this is effectively a EUR 350K option on a clinic business model with EUR 2-8M NPV potential.

### 7.2 Financial Attractiveness

**Risk-adjusted returns are acceptable.** Even under our more conservative probability weighting (28-31% expected IRR), the deal clears the 18% hurdle rate with meaningful margin. The downside is bounded: worst realistic case (Disaster, 10% probability) loses EUR 100-200K -- painful but survivable for a wellness group with multi-million annual revenue.

### 7.3 Go/No-Go Decision

**CONDITIONAL BUY.**

The investment is attractive on risk-adjusted terms. The clinic optionality justifies the premium over pure spa valuation. However, several conditions must be satisfied before committing capital:

1. Revenue verification confirms EUR 28K+ monthly spa average
2. Lease assignment is confirmed feasible at EUR 5-5.5K/month or below
3. Equipment appraisal confirms EUR 100K+ FMV with no undisclosed financing
4. Qualified clinic practitioner identified and available for hire at budgeted cost
5. No litigation, tax, or regulatory surprises in due diligence

If any of these conditions fail, the price must be renegotiated downward or the deal should be abandoned.

### 7.4 Price Recommendation

**Recommended offer range: EUR 300-350K purchase price (plus EUR 150K clinic capex = EUR 450-500K total investment).**

**Justification:**
- EUR 300K (4.1x EBITDA): Appropriate if due diligence reveals any weakness (lower revenue, higher rent, equipment issues). Still within market range.
- EUR 320K (4.4x EBITDA): Fair value for a clean deal with all conditions verified. Our recommended target.
- EUR 350K (4.86x EBITDA): Maximum purchase price. Only justified if all due diligence is clean, lease terms are favorable, and equipment is in excellent condition.
- Above EUR 350K: The deal math weakens. IRR drops below 25% in Base case. Walk away.

**Structure recommendation:** EUR 290-300K cash at close + EUR 20-30K performance earnout (contingent on spa maintaining EUR 25K+/month for 12 months post-close). This structure protects against revenue overstatement and aligns seller incentives through transition.
