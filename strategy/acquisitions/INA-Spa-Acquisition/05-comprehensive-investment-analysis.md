## SECTION 2: FINANCIAL ANALYSIS — DETAILED VALUATION MODEL

### 2.1 Valuation Methodology Comparison

The original model uses **EBITDA multiples** (0.8x–2.5x range) applied to current EBITDA (near-zero at mid-rent). This is valid but incomplete. Institutional investors require **three valuation approaches**:

#### Approach 1: EBITDA Multiple Valuation (Current)
- **Formula:** Estimated Year 2 EBITDA × Multiple = Enterprise Value
- **Base Case:** €70K revenue → €13,860 EBITDA (Year 2 stabilized) × 1.5x = €20,790
- **Problem:** This is WAY too low. Multiple is derived from comparable transactions, which are unknown
- **Improvement:** Use **European spa M&A comparables analysis** to derive market multiples

#### Approach 2: Lease-Adjusted Sum-of-Parts Valuation (Current)
- **Formula:** (Equipment FMV) + (Lease NPV) + (Goodwill) = Enterprise Value
- **Current:** €215K equipment + €254K lease NPV − €50K goodwill = €419K
- **Problem:** Assumes all components transfer with same value. Lease value is conditional on landlord assignment approval
- **Improvement:** **Probability-weight lease NPV** based on assignment approval likelihood (75% base case)

#### Approach 3: DCF Valuation (MISSING — CRITICAL)
- **Formula:** PV(Year 1–10 Free Cash Flow) + PV(Terminal Value) = Enterprise Value
- **Why missing:** This is the gold standard for acquisitions. Shows investor the 10-year cash flow story
- **Required:** Build detailed 5–10 year DCF with:
  - Month-by-month Year 1 ramp to stabilization
  - Fixed/variable cost separation
  - Post-acquisition capex schedule (equipment replacement, upgrades)
  - Tax implications (depreciation shields, corporate tax rate)
  - Terminal value (exit multiple or perpetual growth rate)
  - WACC discount rate (12–15% reflecting execution risk)

**REQUIRED DELIVERABLE:** DCF workbook showing NPV at different acquisition prices (€80K, €120K, €150K, €200K, €250K) and IRR outcomes

## APPENDIX: GLOSSARY OF TERMS

- **EBITDA:** Earnings Before Interest, Tax, Depreciation, Amortization (operational profit)
- **NPV:** Net Present Value (present-day value of future cash flows)
- **DCF:** Discounted Cash Flow (valuation methodology using NPV)
- **WACC:** Weighted Average Cost of Capital (discount rate for DCF)
- **IRR:** Internal Rate of Return (annualized return on investment)
- **CAC:** Customer Acquisition Cost (marketing spend per new customer)
- **LTV:** Customer Lifetime Value (total profit from a customer over relationship life)
- **Lease Premium:** Value of below-market lease terms (rent savings vs. market rate)
- **Contingent Liability:** Obligation that may arise (e.g., staff severance if key person leaves)
- **Earnout:** Contingent payment tied to business hitting performance targets post-acquisition
