## 1. MODEL ARCHITECTURE CRITIQUE

### 1.1 What Works

The 4-scenario structure (INA Actual, BAU, Base, Bull) is sound in principle. It gives you:
- A verified baseline (INA Actual)
- A "do-nothing" improvement case (BAU)
- A planning case (Base)
- A stretch target (Bull)

This is standard practice for acquisition modeling. The BAU column is particularly smart -- it isolates the value of operational fixes (cutting overstaffing from 11 to 8 therapists, reducing SG&A) from the revenue growth plays. That distinction matters in negotiation: BAU improvements represent "easy wins" that any competent operator would capture, which argues against paying the seller for that value.

### 1.2 What Is Missing from the Architecture

**Time dimension.** This is the single biggest structural problem. The model presents four scenarios as if they are alternative steady states, but they are not. They are sequential phases:
- INA Actual = Today (Year 0)
- BAU = Months 1-6 (quick operational fixes, no new revenue streams)
- Base = Year 1-2 (clinic added, modest growth, repricing underway)
- Bull = Year 3+ (full optimization, all levers pulling)

Without a time axis, the model implies that you could choose to operate at "Base" or "Bull" from Day 1. You cannot. The jump from EUR 430K to EUR 961K revenue requires hiring aesthetics therapists, an aesthetics nurse, slimming therapists, launching new service lines, repricing existing services, and building demand. That is 18-36 months of execution, not a scenario toggle.

**Recommendation:** Add a row for "Year" or "Phase" at the top. Label them: INA Actual (Year 0), BAU (Year 0.5), Base (Year 2), Bull (Year 3+). This grounds the model in reality and prevents you from making promises you cannot deliver.

**Rent escalation.** Your model uses EUR 81,500 for rent in BAU, Base, and Bull. But rent is not flat -- it escalates. The fixed base jumps from EUR 50K to EUR 60K in Year 3 (2026), then grows ~3% annually. The 5% turnover component grows automatically with revenue. At EUR 961K revenue (Base), total rent is approximately EUR 60K + EUR 48,050 = EUR 108,050. At EUR 1.321M (Bull), total rent is EUR 60K + EUR 66,050 = EUR 126,050. Your model undercharges rent by EUR 26K in Base and EUR 45K in Bull.

**CAPEX/investment layer.** There is no row showing the investment required to get from INA Actual to each scenario. The jump to Base requires hiring 4 additional staff (aesthetics therapist, aesthetics nurse, 2 slimming therapists), which requires recruitment costs (EUR 3-5K), training (EUR 5-8K), clinic room conversion (EUR 50-65K), regulatory costs (EUR 3-5K), and marketing investment above the EUR 40K shown. These upfront costs should appear either as a capital line below the P&L or in a separate investment bridge.

**Working capital / cash flow timing.** Revenue of EUR 961K does not arrive evenly. Malta spa revenue has a pronounced Q3 trough (July revenue was EUR 22K monthly in 2025). Even at Base scenario revenue, you will have months where revenue drops to EUR 55-65K while your fixed costs (wages at EUR 26.5K/month, rent at EUR 9K/month) remain constant. The model needs a cash flow sensitivity or at minimum a note on seasonal working capital requirements.

**Tax.** No income tax line. Malta's effective corporate rate post-imputation is ~5%, but this still matters. At Base EBITDA of EUR 398K less EUR 25K depreciation = EUR 373K profit before tax, your tax bill is approximately EUR 18.7K. At Bull, it is approximately EUR 30K. These are real cash outflows missing from the model.

## 5. ANSWERS TO YOUR 9 SPECIFIC QUESTIONS

### Question 1: "500k pre VAT?"

**Answer: Likely yes, or very close to it.**

The P&L shows EUR 430,000 "ex VAT" for Trading Income. However, the revenue breakdown (service categories) sums to only EUR 401,329. The gap of EUR 28,671 is likely retail sales or other income not categorized in the service breakdown.

Additionally, the data room P&L shows EUR 426,018 in trading income plus EUR 7,721 in other income = EUR 433,739 total. With Malta's 18% VAT on spa services, gross revenue (including VAT) would be approximately EUR 507,600.

If the seller is quoting "EUR 500K" they may be referring to gross revenue including VAT, or total revenue including some categories not captured in your breakdown. Neither is deceptive -- but you must clarify the basis.

**What to ask Monday:** "When you say EUR 500K, is that gross revenue including VAT, or net revenue ex VAT? Can you show me the monthly VAT returns for the last 12 months?"

### Question 2: "Under reported?"

**Answer: Almost certainly yes, to some degree.**

Malta's cash economy is well documented, particularly in hospitality and personal services. Common patterns:
- Tips not reported as business income (standard and legal -- tips are the employee's income, not the business's)
- Cash transactions not fully recorded (illegal but common in Malta)
- Gift voucher sales recognized on redemption rather than sale (creates timing differences)
- Hotel guest packages where revenue is split with the hotel and only the spa's share appears

The EUR 28,671 gap between the service breakdown and P&L total income could be unreported revenue that surfaces only in bank records but not in the booking system.

**How this affects valuation:** If revenue is underreported, the business is worth more than the P&L suggests. But you should NOT pay for unreported revenue because:
1. You cannot verify it
2. It may represent tax non-compliance that creates liability for the buyer
3. The seller cannot credibly argue "our real revenue is X" without also admitting tax evasion

**What to ask Monday:** "I notice a gap between the service category breakdown and the total trading income. Can you reconcile that for me? Also, what percentage of revenue is cash versus card?" If cash exceeds 25%, that is a yellow flag for underreporting.

### Question 3: "Rent agreement on net revenue?"

**Answer: The 5% turnover fee applies to ALL sales -- gross revenue, not net.**

From the lease analysis (Clause 3.1): The turnover component is 5% of ALL sales, with a minimum threshold of EUR 350K. This means:
- It applies to total trading income, not profit
- It applies before deducting any costs
- It includes retail product sales, gift voucher sales, and any other income generated from the premises
- The minimum ensures Fortina gets at least 5% x EUR 350K = EUR 17,500 even if revenue dips

**Critical implication for your model:** Every euro of new revenue you generate (from clinic expansion, slimming, repricing) costs an additional 5 cents in rent. This creates a marginal rent rate of 5% on all incremental revenue. At EUR 500K incremental revenue (to reach Base), that is EUR 25,000 in additional rent. Your model does not reflect this.

**What to ask Monday:** "Can I see the actual lease agreement? Specifically, I want to confirm whether the 5% turnover fee is calculated on gross revenue, net revenue, or revenue from specific categories only."

### Question 4: "Are utilities sub-metered?"

**Answer: You must verify, but hotel-embedded spas in Malta typically have one of three arrangements:**

| Arrangement | How It Works | Impact on P&L |
|-------------|-------------|---------------|
| Sub-metered | Spa has its own electricity/water meters; billed by hotel based on actual usage | Variable cost -- increases with usage |
