## Why EUR 200-220K Is Fair

### 1. The Business Is Currently Loss-Making

After accounting for the depreciation of the EUR 738K fit-out, the business posted a **EUR 18,978 loss** in 2025. EBITDA of EUR 36.5K is not profit -- it is cash flow before the cost of replacing the assets that generate it. A buyer who pays based on EBITDA alone inherits an asset base that is depleting without adequate earnings to sustain it.

### 2. Wage Structure Requires Restructuring

At **49.2% of revenue** (EUR 209K for 13 staff across 7 rooms), labour costs are 10-15 percentage points above the 35-40% industry benchmark. This means any buyer must invest management time and capital into restructuring before the business can generate a real return. That restructuring cost and risk should not be priced into the seller's valuation.

### 3. The Lease Creates Risk, Not Just Value

| Lease Factor | Detail |
|---|---|
| Remaining term | ~13 years (from 15-year term, mid-2020) |
| Base rent + turnover fee | EUR 71.7K/yr and growing ~3% annually |
| Assignment clause | At landlord's **absolute discretion** |
| Complex sale clause | Landlord can terminate; payout = EUR 150K + depreciated investment |

The lease is simultaneously the most valuable asset and the single greatest risk. Assignment is not guaranteed. Until the landlord consents, the buyer has no certainty of tenure. This risk alone justifies a significant valuation discount.

### 4. Comparable Transactions Support This Range

| Method | Low | High | Basis |
|--------|----:|-----:|-------|
| EBITDA multiple (4-6x) | 146K | 219K | Standard range for single-location, sub-10% margin spa |
| Revenue multiple (0.3-0.5x) | 128K | 213K | Applied to breakeven/marginal businesses only |
| DCF (13-year, 18% discount) | 195K | 326K | As-is operations to full optimization scenario |
| Replacement cost (depreciated) | 175K | 310K | EUR 857K invested, 2 years of depreciation, thin secondary market |
| **Independent FMV opinion** | **200K** | **280K** | **Chartered accountant assessment, weighted methodology** |

The convergence zone across all methodologies is **EUR 190K -- 220K**. Our offer sits squarely within it.

### 5. Seller's Capital Invested Is Not a Valuation Input

We respect that EUR 857K was invested in this business. That figure represents historical cost -- a sunk cost in valuation terms. No standard valuation methodology (IVS, ASA, NACVA) permits sunk cost to determine fair market value. Value is determined by what a business produces going forward, not what was spent building it.
