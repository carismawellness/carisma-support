# TERM SHEET

## Proposed Acquisition of INA Spa & Wellness

---

**CONFIDENTIAL**

This Term Sheet (the "Term Sheet") sets out the principal terms and conditions upon which **Carisma Wellness Group** (or its nominee) (the "Buyer") proposes to acquire the business known as **INA Spa & Wellness** (the "Business") from **Well Being Services Limited** (and/or its shareholders, as applicable) (the "Seller").

**Date:** [________] March 2026

---

### IMPORTANT NOTICE

This Term Sheet is a non-binding expression of interest and statement of the principal terms upon which the Buyer would be willing to proceed to negotiate a definitive agreement for the acquisition of the Business. Save for Clauses 14 (Exclusivity), 15 (Confidentiality), 16 (Costs), and 17 (Governing Law), which are intended to be legally binding, this Term Sheet does not create any binding obligation on either party and is subject to the negotiation and execution of a definitive Sale and Purchase Agreement (the "SPA") in a form satisfactory to both parties and their respective legal advisors.

---

## 1. PARTIES

| | |
|---|---|
| **Buyer** | Carisma Wellness Group (or nominee to be designated prior to completion) |
| **Seller** | Well Being Services Limited (C [________]) and/or its shareholders |
| **Target Business** | INA Spa & Wellness, operating from the Fortina Hotel Complex, Tigne Seafront, Sliema, Malta |

---

## 2. TRANSACTION STRUCTURE

2.1. The proposed transaction shall be structured as an **asset purchase** (the "Transaction"), pursuant to which the Buyer shall acquire all assets, goodwill, client base, equipment, inventory, and intellectual property of the Business, and shall assume the lease of the premises (the "Lease"), subject to landlord consent.

2.2. The assets to be acquired shall include, without limitation:

   (a) All equipment, machinery, fixtures, and fittings used in the operation of the Business, including the Candela laser device and T-Shape body contouring device;

   (b) All inventory, consumables, products, and supplies held at the premises;

   (c) Goodwill and the right to carry on the Business;

   (d) All client records, booking data, and customer databases;

   (e) All intellectual property rights associated with the Business, including the trading name "INA Spa & Wellness" (to the extent owned by the Seller);

   (f) All supplier contracts and commercial arrangements (to the extent assignable);

   (g) The Seller's rights and obligations under the Lease (subject to Clause 5.1); and

   (h) All regulatory licences and permits (to the extent transferable).

2.3. **Alternative structure:** Should the parties, following tax and legal advice, determine that a share purchase of Well Being Services Limited would be materially more advantageous for either or both parties, the Buyer is willing to consider restructuring the Transaction as a share purchase, subject to satisfactory due diligence on the company's liabilities, tax history, and compliance status.

---

## 3. PURCHASE PRICE

3.1. The total headline purchase price for the Transaction shall be **EUR 240,000** (two hundred and forty thousand euro) (the "Purchase Price"), structured as follows:

| Component | Amount (EUR) | Terms |
|---|---|---|
| Cash at Close | 200,000 | Payable on completion by bank transfer to the Seller's designated account |
| Performance Earnout | 20,000 | Subject to the conditions set out in Clause 3.3 |
| Seller Financing Note | 20,000 | Subject to the terms set out in Clause 3.4 |
| **Total Headline Price** | **240,000** | |

3.2. **Cash at Close.** The sum of EUR 200,000 shall be payable to the Seller on the date of completion (the "Completion Date") by irrevocable bank transfer to the Seller's designated bank account, or to an escrow account held by a Maltese notary public, as the parties may agree.

3.3. **Performance Earnout.**

   (a) An additional sum of EUR 20,000 (the "Earnout Payment") shall be payable to the Seller, subject to the Business achieving average monthly revenue of not less than EUR 35,000 for twelve (12) consecutive calendar months following the Completion Date (the "Earnout Period").

   (b) Revenue for the purposes of this Clause shall mean gross revenue from all spa and wellness services and product sales, as recorded in the Business's point-of-sale system and verified against bank statements, excluding VAT.

   (c) The revenue metric has been selected (rather than EBITDA or net profit) to provide a transparent, objective, and independently verifiable benchmark that is not susceptible to manipulation through cost allocation by either party.

   (d) The Earnout Payment shall be held in escrow by a Maltese notary public from the Completion Date. Upon satisfaction of the Earnout condition, the escrow agent shall release the funds to the Seller within fourteen (14) business days of verification. If the Earnout condition is not satisfied within twenty-four (24) months of the Completion Date, the escrowed funds shall be returned to the Buyer.

   (e) During the Earnout Period, the Buyer shall operate the Business in the ordinary course and shall not take any action with the primary purpose of artificially suppressing revenue to avoid the Earnout Payment.

3.4. **Seller Financing Note.**

   (a) The sum of EUR 20,000 (the "Seller Note") shall be evidenced by a promissory note executed at completion, bearing interest at the rate of 4% per annum, with a term of three (3) years from the Completion Date.

   (b) The Seller Note shall be repayable in twelve (12) equal quarterly instalments of principal plus accrued interest, commencing ninety (90) days after the Completion Date.

   (c) The Seller Note shall be subordinated to all operating obligations of the Business, including payroll, rent, supplier payments, and any senior financing arrangements.

   (d) The Seller Note shall be secured by a first-ranking charge over the portable equipment of the Business (specifically the Candela laser and T-Shape devices), subject to release upon full repayment.

   (e) The Buyer shall have the right to prepay the Seller Note in whole or in part at any time without penalty.

---

## 4. WORKING CAPITAL AND INVENTORY

4.1. The Seller shall deliver the Business on the Completion Date with a minimum net working capital of EUR 10,000 (ten thousand euro), calculated as current assets less current liabilities of the Business.

4.2. All consumables, products, supplies, and inventory held at the premises on the Completion Date shall be included in the Purchase Price at no additional cost to the Buyer.

4.3. The Seller's security deposit held under the Lease shall either be assigned to the Buyer or refunded to the Seller and replaced by a new deposit provided by the Buyer, as the landlord may require.

---

## 5. CONDITIONS PRECEDENT

5.1. Completion of the Transaction shall be conditional upon the satisfaction (or waiver by the Buyer) of the following conditions precedent on or before the Long Stop Date (as defined in Clause 10):

   (a) **Lease Assignment.** Fortina Developments Limited (the "Landlord") shall have provided written consent to the assignment of the Lease from the Seller to the Buyer (or shall have agreed to grant a new lease to the Buyer on terms no less favourable than the existing Lease), in a form satisfactory to the Buyer and its legal advisors;

   (b) **Due Diligence.** The Buyer shall have completed legal, financial, and operational due diligence on the Business and shall be satisfied, in its sole discretion, with the results thereof;

   (c) **Equipment Inspection.** An independent inspection of all material equipment, including the Candela laser and T-Shape devices, shall have confirmed that such equipment is in good working order with a minimum remaining useful life of twelve (12) months;

   (d) **No Undisclosed Liabilities.** The Buyer shall have confirmed, to its reasonable satisfaction, that there are no undisclosed liabilities, pending or threatened litigation, regulatory proceedings, or material contractual disputes affecting the Business;

   (e) **Competition Clearance.** If required, clearance from the Malta Competition and Consumer Affairs Authority (or any successor body) shall have been obtained;

   (f) **Regulatory Licences.** All regulatory licences, permits, and approvals necessary for the continued operation of the Business (including any licences required by the Malta Medicines Authority for medical devices) shall have been confirmed as transferable to the Buyer or obtainable by the Buyer prior to or promptly following completion; and

   (g) **No Material Adverse Change.** No Material Adverse Change (as defined in Clause 6) shall have occurred between the date of this Term Sheet and the Completion Date.

5.2. The Buyer shall use reasonable endeavours to satisfy the conditions precedent within the timescales contemplated by this Term Sheet. The Seller shall provide all reasonable cooperation and access to information to facilitate their satisfaction.

---

## 6. MATERIAL ADVERSE CHANGE

6.1. A "Material Adverse Change" shall be deemed to have occurred if, between the date of execution of the SPA and the Completion Date:

   (a) The EBITDA of the Business falls below EUR 2,000 per calendar month for any three (3) consecutive months; or

   (b) Any event, circumstance, or change occurs that has, or would reasonably be expected to have, a material adverse effect on the financial condition, operations, assets, or prospects of the Business.

6.2. In the event of a Material Adverse Change, the Buyer shall have the right, at its sole discretion, to:

   (a) Renegotiate the Purchase Price to reflect the diminished value of the Business;

   (b) Extend the Long Stop Date to allow the Business to recover; or

   (c) Terminate this Term Sheet and any binding agreement without liability.

---

## 7. LEASE RISK ALLOCATION

7.1. The parties acknowledge that the Lease is fundamental to the value of the Business.

7.2. If, as a condition of consenting to the assignment of the Lease (or granting a new lease), the Landlord requires a rent increase exceeding ten percent (10%) above the rent schedule contained in the existing Lease, the Buyer shall have the right to:

   (a) Reduce the Purchase Price by an amount equal to the net present value of the incremental rent over the remaining term of the Lease (discounted at 8% per annum); or

   (b) Terminate this Term Sheet and any binding agreement without liability.

---

## 8. EMPLOYEES

8.1. In accordance with Malta's Transfer of Business (Protection of Employment) Regulations (S.L. 452.85), all employees of the Business shall transfer to the Buyer on the Completion Date on their existing terms and conditions of employment.

8.2. The Buyer shall offer continued employment to all current employees of the Business on terms no less favourable than their existing terms at the date of transfer.

8.3. For the avoidance of doubt, the Buyer shall not be obligated to maintain any specific headcount or organisational structure following the completion of the transition period, provided that any subsequent changes are implemented in compliance with applicable Maltese employment law, including the Employment and Industrial Relations Act (EIRA).

8.4. The Seller shall provide the Buyer with full details of all employees, including contracts, length of service, remuneration, accrued entitlements, and any outstanding disputes or claims, as part of the due diligence process.

---

## 9. REPRESENTATIONS AND WARRANTIES

9.1. The SPA shall contain customary representations and warranties by the Seller, including (without limitation) representations as to:

   (a) Title to and condition of the assets;

   (b) Accuracy of financial information provided during due diligence;

   (c) Compliance with applicable laws and regulations;

   (d) Absence of undisclosed liabilities and litigation;

   (e) Validity and enforceability of material contracts;

   (f) Employee matters and compliance with employment law;

   (g) Tax compliance and absence of outstanding tax liabilities;

   (h) Condition and ownership of equipment (free from encumbrances, liens, and charges); and

   (i) Accuracy and completeness of all information provided to the Buyer.

9.2. **Equipment Warranty.** Without limiting the generality of the above, the Seller specifically warrants that all equipment included in the Transaction, and in particular the Candela laser and T-Shape devices, are in good working order, free from any encumbrance, lien, or third-party claim, and have a remaining useful life of at least twelve (12) months as at the Completion Date.

9.3. The representations and warranties shall survive for a period of eighteen (18) months from the Completion Date.

9.4. The aggregate liability of the Seller under the representations and warranties shall be capped at EUR 20,000 (twenty thousand euro), with a de minimis threshold of EUR 1,000 (one thousand euro) per individual claim (claims below the de minimis shall not be recoverable).

---

## 10. COMPLETION

10.1. The parties shall use all reasonable endeavours to satisfy the conditions precedent and complete the Transaction within sixty (60) days of the execution of the SPA (the "Target Completion Date").

10.2. In the event that the conditions precedent have not been satisfied (or waived) by the date falling ninety (90) days after execution of the SPA (the "Long Stop Date"), either party may terminate the SPA by written notice to the other, and neither party shall have any further liability to the other, save in respect of any antecedent breach.

---

## 11. SELLER TRANSITION SUPPORT

11.1. The Seller (and/or its principal(s)) shall provide transition and consulting support to the Buyer for a period of ninety (90) days following the Completion Date, at a commitment of not less than ten (10) hours per week.

11.2. Such support shall include, without limitation: introduction of the Buyer to key clients, suppliers, and hotel management; transfer of operational knowledge; assistance with staff transition; and such other matters as the Buyer may reasonably request.

11.3. The transition support obligation is included in the Purchase Price and no additional compensation shall be payable by the Buyer.

---

## 12. RESTRICTIVE COVENANTS

12.1. **Non-Compete.** The Seller and its principals shall not, for a period of two (2) years from the Completion Date, directly or indirectly own, manage, operate, control, be employed by, consult with, or participate in any business that provides spa, wellness, or aesthetic services in Malta.

12.2. **Non-Solicitation.** The Seller and its principals shall not, for a period of two (2) years from the Completion Date, directly or indirectly solicit, induce, or encourage:

   (a) Any employee or contractor of the Business to terminate their employment or engagement with the Buyer; or

   (b) Any client or customer of the Business to cease or reduce their use of the services provided by the Business.

12.3. The parties acknowledge that the restrictive covenants in this Clause 12 are reasonable in scope, geography, and duration, and are necessary to protect the goodwill acquired by the Buyer.

---

## 13. DEPOSITS

13.1. Any security deposit, rental deposit, or other deposit held by the Landlord or any third party in connection with the Lease or the operation of the Business shall, at the Seller's option, be either:

   (a) Assigned and transferred to the Buyer, with a corresponding reduction in the net amount payable at completion; or

   (b) Refunded to the Seller, with the Buyer providing a replacement deposit directly.

---

## 14. EXCLUSIVITY (BINDING)

14.1. From the date of execution of this Term Sheet, the Seller grants the Buyer an exclusive period of forty-five (45) days (the "Exclusivity Period") during which the Seller shall not:

   (a) Solicit, initiate, encourage, or facilitate any enquiry, proposal, or offer from any third party relating to the sale or transfer of the Business or any material part thereof;

   (b) Provide any information or access to any third party for the purpose of enabling or facilitating any competing transaction;

   (c) Enter into any discussions, negotiations, or agreements with any third party regarding the sale or transfer of the Business; or

   (d) Accept any offer or enter into any agreement for the sale of the Business.

14.2. The Exclusivity Period may be extended by mutual written agreement.

---

## 15. CONFIDENTIALITY (BINDING)

15.1. Each party shall treat as strictly confidential, and shall not disclose to any third party (other than its professional advisors on a need-to-know basis), the existence and terms of this Term Sheet and all information received from the other party in connection with the proposed Transaction.

15.2. This obligation of confidentiality shall survive the termination of this Term Sheet for a period of two (2) years, irrespective of whether the Transaction proceeds to completion.

15.3. This Clause shall not apply to disclosures required by law, regulation, or court order, provided that the disclosing party gives the other party prompt written notice of such requirement (to the extent legally permitted).

---

## 16. COSTS (BINDING)

16.1. Each party shall bear its own costs and expenses (including legal, accounting, and advisory fees) incurred in connection with the negotiation, preparation, and execution of this Term Sheet and the Transaction.

---

## 17. GOVERNING LAW (BINDING)

17.1. This Term Sheet, and any dispute arising out of or in connection with it, shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of Malta.

17.2. The courts of Malta shall have exclusive jurisdiction to settle any dispute arising out of or in connection with this Term Sheet.

---

## 18. GENERAL

18.1. This Term Sheet may be executed in counterparts, each of which shall constitute an original.

18.2. No variation of this Term Sheet shall be effective unless made in writing and signed by both parties.

18.3. This Term Sheet (together with any documents referred to herein) constitutes the entire understanding between the parties with respect to the subject matter hereof and supersedes all prior discussions, representations, and agreements.

18.4. Nothing in this Term Sheet shall constitute or be deemed to constitute a partnership, joint venture, or agency relationship between the parties.
