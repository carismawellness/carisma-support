# INA SPA MALTA ACQUISITION
## Operational Execution Roadmap
### 14-Month Implementation Plan with KPI Gates

**Document Version:** 2.0 (Post-Expert Critique)
**Date:** February 22, 2026
**Prepared for:** Internal Operations & Board

---

## OVERVIEW

This document outlines the three-phase operational approach to transforming INA Spa from a distressed €22.5K/month business into a profitable €70–90K/month operation. Each phase includes specific milestones, KPI gates, investment requirements, and contingency protocols.

**Core Principle:** Phase advancement is **contingent on KPI gates being met.** If gates are missed, the phase extends or pivots. This prevents capital waste on unsuccessful strategies.

## PHASE 1: STABILIZATION & DIAGNOSTICS
### Duration: Months 1–3
### Objective: Prevent deterioration and build foundation for growth

**Mission Statement:** Lock in current revenue (€22.5K), retain key staff, establish operational baseline, and diagnose why revenue declined 50%.

### 1.1 Week 1 Actions (Pre-Close & Day 1)

| Activity | Owner | Deliverable | Investment |
|----------|-------|-------------|-----------|
| **Staff retention interviews & contracts** | HR | 2–3 therapists signed to 18-month contracts with vesting bonuses | €5–10K |
| **Landlord meeting (confirm rent & signal competence)** | Legal + Ops | Written letter from landlord confirming €8K/month rent and assignment willingness | €0 |
| **Parallel systems setup** | IT | Carisma POS, booking, CRM systems installed parallel to existing; no data loss | €5K |
| **Customer communication launch** | Marketing | Email/SMS to all current customers: "New ownership, same great experience" + loyalty offer | €2K |
| **Regulatory pre-inquiry** | Legal | Initial inquiry to Malta Health Authority: timeline and requirements for medical aesthetics licensing | €1K |

**Week 1 Cost:** €13–18K

**Week 1 KPI Targets:**
- ✅ 100% of key staff signed to retention contracts
- ✅ Landlord written approval obtained
- ✅ Systems operational with zero customer-facing disruption
- ✅ Zero customer service incidents during transition

**Risk if Week 1 Fails:**
- Staff leave immediately → Customer churn → Revenue collapse
- Landlord threatens assignment → Deal at risk
- Systems down → Customer experience damaged
- Solution: Have backup staff (part-time therapist from Carisma St. Julian's) on standby; ensure landlord contact established pre-close

### 1.2 Weeks 2–4: Diagnostic Phase

#### Customer Analysis
| Week | Activity | Method | Deliverable |
|------|----------|--------|-------------|
| **Week 2** | Customer NPS Survey | 100-customer survey (phone/SMS) | NPS score, satisfaction drivers, churn risk identification |
| **Week 2** | Retention Interview | Call top 10 customers by revenue | Understand why they stay, what risks, renewal likelihood |
| **Week 3** | Revenue Concentration Analysis | Export booking system data | Top 20 customers = ?% of revenue |
| **Week 3** | Repeat Rate Analysis | 12-month booking history | % of customers returning >1x per year |
| **Week 4** | Churn Cohort Analysis | Compare Year 1 vs. Year 2 customer lists | Root cause of 50% decline (did customers leave or no new bookings?) |

**Deliverable:** Customer Health Report (3-page summary with NPS, retention risk, concentration analysis)

#### Operational Baseline
| Week | Activity | Method | Deliverable |
|------|----------|--------|-------------|
| **Week 2** | Equipment Condition Assessment | Physical walk-through with technician checklist | Equipment condition rating (A/B/C/D); urgent repairs identified |
| **Week 2** | Competitor Pricing Analysis | Mystery shop Numi, other spas; collect pricing | Competitive price comparison; Carisma positioning opportunities |
| **Week 3** | Staff Training Needs | Interview current therapists | Skill gaps; training requirements; staff development plan |
| **Week 3** | Booking System Audit | Export and analyze 12-month booking data | Capacity utilization; peak/trough patterns; pricing variability |

**Deliverable:** Operational Baseline Report (capacity utilization %, current margin %, growth constraints identified)

#### Root Cause Analysis: Why Did Revenue Decline 50%?
This is the critical diagnostic. Build hypothesis matrix:

| Hypothesis | Evidence | Probability |
|-----------|----------|-------------|
| **Numi partnership breakup** — Key clients left when Numi separated | Customer list pre/post Numi split. Numi's revenue post-separation. | 30% |
| **Operator incompetence** — Numi cut marketing, neglected space | Ad spend history (Meta/Google). Reviews timeline. Staff tenure. | 35% |
| **Market saturation** — Competitors consolidated share | Numi revenue trend. Competitor openings 2022–2025. | 25% |
| **Seasonality miscasting** — €22.5K is low season, not "decline" | 24-month revenue calendar. Identify peaks vs. troughs. | 10% |

**Root Cause Interview Template:**
- Departing customers: "Why did you stop coming?"
- Current staff (therapists): "What changed in Year 1 vs. Year 2?"
- Numi contact (if reachable): "Why did you separate from INA?"

**Deliverable:** Root Cause Analysis (1-page hypothesis with supporting evidence; probability-weight outcomes)

**Mission Statement:** Shift from defense to offense. Acquire new customers, optimize pricing, launch medical aesthetics, and build operational efficiency.

### 2.2 Pricing Optimization (Months 4–9)

**Current State:** Pricing is flat (all treatments €75–85). No upsell strategy.

**Opportunity:** Implement tiered pricing and package bundling.

#### Pricing Tiers
| Package | Price | Margin | Target Customer | Expected Uptake |
|---------|-------|--------|-----------------|-----------------|
| **Standard Service** | €75 | 40% | Price-sensitive, first-time | 60% |
| **Premium Service** | €95 | 45% | Repeat, quality-focused | 25% |
| **Luxury Package (3x service)** | €240 | 50% | High-value, vacationers | 15% |

**Bundling Strategy:**
- 6-treatment package: €420 (€70/treatment vs. €75 standard) — incentivizes repeat
- Couples package: €180 (2x€95 premium) — drives incremental revenue
- Corporate wellness: €50/person (bulk discount) — new B2B channel

**Expected Impact (Month 4–9):**
- Average treatment price increase: €75 → €82–85 (10–13% uplift)
- Monthly revenue uplift: €22.5K × 10% = €2.25K additional
- Cumulative: €22.5K + €2.25K (pricing) + €3.75K (new customers) = €28.5K by Month 9

## CONTINGENCY PROTOCOLS

### Scenario 1: Revenue Ramp Misses Month 6 Gate (€40K target, actual €35K)

**Diagnosis Protocol:**
- Which channel underperformed? (Google LSA, Instagram, hotel partnerships?)
- Is it CAC-related (customers too expensive to acquire) or conversion-related (leads not converting)?
- Is pricing too high or positioning misaligned with market?

**Response Options:**
1. **Increase marketing spend €1K/month** to 2 underperforming channels (48-hour test)
2. **Reduce prices 5–10%** to test price sensitivity
3. **Pivot to referral-based acquisition** (customer referral bonuses, hotel partnerships) — lower CAC
4. **Extend stabilization phase 4 weeks** (delay Phase 3 start)

**Hold Point:** Do NOT launch full medical aesthetics expansion (3 rooms) if revenue gate is missed. Proceed with part-time soft launch only.

### Scenario 2: Key Therapist Leaves (Month 3–6)

**Impact:** Loss of 1 of 3 therapists = 8–10K monthly revenue at risk

**Response Protocol:**
- **Immediate (24 hours):** Activate backup therapist from Carisma St. Julian's (part-time coverage)
- **Week 1:** Emergency recruitment (place job posting; contact freelance therapists)
- **Week 2:** Interview + hire replacement (may need 2–3 week onboarding)
- **Financial impact:** Temporary revenue dip €3–5K/month for 3–4 weeks; then recovery

**Mitigation (Proactive):** Redundancy—ensure at least 2 backup therapists available from Carisma network

### Scenario 3: Equipment Appraisal Comes In Low (€120K vs. €250K assumed)

**Impact:** Acquisition price overpaid by €50–100K

**Response Protocol:**
- **Immediate:** Renegotiate acquisition price down by amount of valuation shortfall
- **Rationale:** Equipment is part of the asset package; if it's worth less, total acquisition value is lower
- **Alternative:** Renegotiate to earnout structure (lower upfront, higher if revenue targets hit)

**Financial mitigation:** Had built €50K "liability reserve" in working capital planning; use to offset delta

### Scenario 4: Medical Aesthetics Licensing Delayed (6-month delay instead of 3-month assumed)

**Impact:** Medical aesthetics revenue stream delayed 3–6 months; revenue ramp slowed

**Response Protocol:**
- **Adjust Phase 2 timeline:** Hold aesthetic clinic fit-out spend until licensing approved
- **Redeploy capital:** Increase spa marketing spend (€2–3K more/month) to offset aesthetics delay
- **Adjust Phase 3 start:** Delay full clinic expansion 6 months; continue soft launch in one room

**Financial mitigation:** Based Phase 2 revenue targets (€40–60K) on spa growth + modest aesthetics. Aesthetics revenue is upside not critical path.

### Scenario 5: Customer Retention Drops Below 50% (Churn Higher Than Expected)

**Impact:** Customer acquisition spend not generating lasting value; CAC payback extended

**Response Protocol:**
- **Diagnosis:** Conduct exit interviews with churned customers (Why did you leave?)
- **Pivot:** If churn is quality-related, improve service (training, staffing)
- **If churn is price-related:** Hold on price increases; focus on value (loyalty programs, packages)
- **If churn is brand perception:** Accelerate rebrand timeline (rename, new signage, marketing refresh)

**Financial mitigation:** Reduce marketing spend by 20% (increase CAC efficiency focus over volume)

### Scenario 6: Competitive Price War (Numi/competitors cut prices 20%+)

**Response Protocol:**
- **Do NOT follow price cuts directly** (erodes margins unsustainably)
- **Differentiate:** Promote medical aesthetics offering (competitors don't have)
- **Bundle:** Create value packages (spa + aesthetics combo at competitive total price)
- **Loyalty:** Offer loyalty discounts to existing customer base (retain share)

**Financial mitigation:** Medical aesthetics higher margins (50% vs. 40% for spa); volume shift to aesthetics partially offsets spa price pressure

## EXECUTION PRINCIPLES

**1. Ruthless Prioritization**
- Phase 1: Stabilization beats growth
- Phase 2: New customer acquisition beats pricing optimization
- Phase 3: Operational efficiency beats new service launches

**2. Data-Driven Decisions**
- Every gate decision backed by KPIs, not instinct
- If gates missed, diagnose root cause before pivoting
- Monthly dashboard transparent to stakeholders

**3. Capital Discipline**
- Each phase has a fixed investment budget
- Overages require board approval
- Contingency fund is for emergencies, not scope creep

**4. Staff Development**
- Therapists/aestheticians are the revenue engine
- Retention bonuses and training investments are non-negotiable
- Career development (cross-training, advancement) planned from Day 1

**5. Customer Obsession**
- NPS is leading indicator of revenue sustainability
- Exit interviews required for all churned customers >€1K LTV
- Loyalty programs designed to extend repeat rate beyond industry average
