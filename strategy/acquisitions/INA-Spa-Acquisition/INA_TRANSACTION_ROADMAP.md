**Prepared for:** Carisma Wellness Group

**Deal:** Acquisition of INA Spa & Wellness, Numi Hotel, Malta
**Agreed Price Range:** EUR 220K-340K (negotiation ongoing)
**Deal Type:** Asset Purchase with Lease Assignment

## 1. THE FULL STEP-BY-STEP PROCESS

This is the chronological sequence from "price roughly agreed" to "Day 1 of Carisma operations." The process has five phases running partially in parallel.

### PHASE 1: PRE-TRANSACTION SETUP (Weeks 1-2)

**Step 1.1 -- Engage Your Professional Team**
Retain your transaction lawyer, accountant, and employment law advisor immediately. Do not wait until due diligence starts. In Malta, the legal community is small; the best M&A practitioners book out weeks in advance. You need your lawyer involved before you sign any binding documents.
- **Who does it:** Buyer (you)
- **Timeline:** Days 1-5

**Step 1.2 -- Sign the Letter of Intent / Heads of Terms**
Draft and execute a non-binding LOI that locks in the agreed price range, deal structure (asset purchase), key conditions precedent, exclusivity period (60-90 days), and timeline. The LOI should explicitly state that the deal is conditional on landlord consent to lease assignment.
- **Who does it:** Your lawyer drafts; both parties sign
- **Timeline:** Days 5-10

**Step 1.3 -- Request Due Diligence Documents**
Deliver a formal DD request list to the seller (see Section 3 below). The seller should have 10-14 days to produce documents. Your lawyer should manage this process to ensure nothing is forgotten.
- **Who does it:** Your lawyer delivers the request; seller produces documents
- **Timeline:** Days 7-14

**Step 1.4 -- Initiate Landlord Contact**
This is the single most time-sensitive step. The lease assignment requires Numi Hotel's written consent. Approach the landlord informally first -- introduce Carisma, explain your track record (50+ hotel spas), and gauge willingness. Do NOT wait until the APA is drafted.
- **Who does it:** You (buyer principal) with your lawyer present
- **Timeline:** Begin Day 7; ongoing through Phase 2

### PHASE 2: DUE DILIGENCE (Weeks 2-6)

**Step 2.1 -- Financial Due Diligence**
Your accountant reviews 24 months of bank statements, POS reports, tax filings, VAT returns, and management accounts. The goal is to confirm that EUR 28-30K monthly spa revenue is real, identify seasonality patterns, and verify that EBITDA is approximately EUR 72K.
- **Who does it:** Your accountant, supervised by your lawyer
- **Timeline:** Weeks 2-4

**Step 2.2 -- Legal Due Diligence**
Your lawyer reviews the lease agreement (assignment provisions, rent escalation clauses, permitted use, break clauses), all employment contracts, supplier agreements, insurance policies, and any regulatory filings. Malta-specific: check for any obligations registered with the Malta Business Registry.
- **Who does it:** Your transaction lawyer
- **Timeline:** Weeks 2-5

**Step 2.3 -- Equipment Appraisal**
Engage an independent equipment appraiser to assess the fair market value of all spa and medical equipment (including the Candela laser and T-Shape device). These are high-value medical devices that require specific expertise to value.
- **Who does it:** Independent equipment appraiser
- **Timeline:** Weeks 2-3

**Step 2.4 -- Employment Assessment**
Review all 14 employment contracts. Identify key-person dependencies (which 1-2 therapists drive 40-50% of revenue). Understand each employee's length of service (critical for notice period calculations if restructuring). Under Malta's Transfer of Business (Protection of Employment) Regulations [S.L. 452.85], all employees transfer automatically to the new owner with their existing terms intact.
- **Who does it:** Your employment lawyer
- **Timeline:** Weeks 2-4

**Step 2.5 -- Regulatory Review**
Verify licensing requirements for medical devices (Candela laser, T-Shape). The Malta Medicines Authority regulates medical devices under S.L. 458.59. Confirm whether existing permits transfer with the business or require new applications. Check health and safety compliance with Environmental Health Directorate standards.
- **Who does it:** Your lawyer with input from a regulatory consultant if needed
- **Timeline:** Weeks 3-5

**Step 2.6 -- Commercial Due Diligence**
Visit the premises. Meet staff individually (with seller's permission). Review client booking data. Assess the hotel relationship. Evaluate the competitive landscape. Talk to the hotel GM about the partnership going forward.
- **Who does it:** You (buyer principal) with your operations team
- **Timeline:** Weeks 3-5

**Step 2.7 -- Landlord Negotiation**
Formalise the lease assignment. Obtain written confirmation from the landlord that: (a) assignment is permitted, (b) rent stays at EUR 5K/month (or within acceptable range), (c) permitted use includes medical aesthetics, and (d) no additional capex requirements are imposed.
- **Who does it:** Your lawyer negotiates; you attend key meetings
- **Timeline:** Weeks 3-6 (this is often the longest lead item)

### PHASE 3: TRANSACTION DOCUMENTATION (Weeks 5-8)

**Step 3.1 -- Draft the Asset Purchase Agreement (APA)**
For this deal, an APA is the appropriate structure (not a Share Purchase Agreement), because you are buying the business assets -- equipment, lease, client base, goodwill, staff transfer -- not shares in a company. The APA should include: purchase price and payment terms, asset schedule, lease assignment as condition precedent, employee transfer provisions, representations and warranties, indemnification clauses, earnout mechanics (if applicable), non-compete and non-solicitation covenants, and transition services provisions.
- **Who does it:** Your lawyer drafts; seller's lawyer reviews
- **Timeline:** Weeks 5-7

**Step 3.2 -- Draft the Lease Assignment Agreement**
This is a tripartite agreement between seller, buyer, and landlord. It transfers all lease obligations to Carisma. Must be reviewed by both your lawyer and a property/lease specialist.
- **Who does it:** Your lawyer, with landlord's lawyer involvement
- **Timeline:** Weeks 5-7 (parallel with APA)

**Step 3.3 -- Prepare Employee Transfer Documentation**
Under Malta's TUPE-equivalent regulations (S.L. 452.85), you must: (a) inform all employees in writing at least 15 working days before transfer, (b) consult with employees or their representatives, (c) provide details of the transfer date, reasons, and implications. Employees transfer on existing terms. If you plan to restructure from 14 to 8-9 staff post-close, the redundancies must happen AFTER the transfer and must follow EIRA rules (LIFO principle, proper notice periods, re-employment rights).
- **Who does it:** Your employment lawyer prepares; you execute
- **Timeline:** Weeks 6-8

**Step 3.4 -- Negotiate Non-Compete / Non-Solicitation**
The seller should agree not to open a competing spa/wellness business within Malta for 2-3 years and not to solicit transferred employees or clients for 2 years. In Malta, non-competes are enforceable if they are reasonable in scope, geography, and duration.
- **Who does it:** Your lawyer drafts as part of APA
- **Timeline:** Included in APA drafting

**Step 3.5 -- Notarisation**
In Malta, a notary public is required to authenticate certain transfers. If the deal involves any transfer of immovable property rights (which a long-term lease may be treated as), the notary must be involved. Even for pure asset deals, having the APA notarised provides stronger legal standing in Malta. The notary also handles any stamp duty obligations.
- **Who does it:** Malta-based notary public
- **Timeline:** Week 7-8

### PHASE 4: CLOSING (Weeks 8-10)

**Step 4.1 -- Satisfy All Conditions Precedent**
Before closing, verify that ALL conditions from the LOI and APA are met: landlord consent obtained in writing, financial DD complete with no material adverse findings, equipment appraisal confirmed, no undisclosed liabilities, regulatory approvals obtained. If any condition fails, renegotiate or walk.
- **Who does it:** Your lawyer confirms; you make go/no-go decision
- **Timeline:** Week 8

**Step 4.2 -- Final Price Negotiation**
If DD uncovered issues (revenue lower than represented, equipment needs replacement, hidden liabilities), adjust the price accordingly with documented justification. Any earnout terms should be finalised and memorialised in the APA.
- **Who does it:** You negotiate; your lawyer documents
- **Timeline:** Week 8-9

**Step 4.3 -- Execute the APA**
Sign the Asset Purchase Agreement. In Malta, both parties should sign before a notary if the agreement involves long-term lease rights. Pay the purchase price (typically by bank transfer to an escrow account held by the notary or a lawyer). Release funds upon confirmation of all conditions.
- **Who does it:** Both parties sign; notary authenticates
- **Timeline:** Week 9

**Step 4.4 -- Execute the Lease Assignment**
The landlord, seller, and buyer all execute the lease assignment agreement. This formally transfers the 13-year lease to Carisma. The original lease and the assignment deed should both be registered.
- **Who does it:** Tripartite execution; notary may authenticate
- **Timeline:** Week 9 (same day as or within days of APA)

**Step 4.5 -- File Regulatory Notifications**
File any required notifications with the Malta Business Registry (if the business was registered as a commercial partnership). Update trade licenses. Notify the Inland Revenue Department of the change in business ownership for VAT and tax purposes. Notify the Employment and Training Corporation (Jobsplus) of the employee transfers.
- **Who does it:** Your accountant and lawyer
- **Timeline:** Week 9-10

**Step 4.6 -- Transfer Operations**
Handover of keys, passwords, booking systems, client databases, supplier contacts, bank accounts (or new account setup). The seller should introduce you to the hotel management, key clients, and suppliers during the transition period.
- **Who does it:** Seller (with you receiving)
- **Timeline:** Weeks 9-10

### PHASE 5: POST-CLOSING AND DAY 1 (Weeks 10-14)

**Step 5.1 -- Transition Period (90 Days)**
The seller cooperates for 90 days post-close: introductions, client handover, operational knowledge transfer. This should be unpaid (baked into purchase price), but the seller's cooperation is critical for client retention.
- **Timeline:** Weeks 10-22

**Step 5.2 -- Staff Restructuring (If Proceeding)**
If reducing from 14 to 8-9 employees, execute redundancies under EIRA rules: identify redundant positions (not people), apply LIFO (Last In, First Out) within each job class, provide proper notice based on length of service, and pay any outstanding entitlements. Redundant employees have re-employment rights for 12 months if their position reopens.
- **Timeline:** Weeks 12-18 (do NOT restructure on Day 1; allow transition to settle first)

**Step 5.3 -- Carisma Branding and Systems Integration**
Rebrand the spa to Carisma standards. Integrate booking systems, CRM, marketing, and operational procedures. Begin planning the clinic build-out.
- **Timeline:** Weeks 10-22

## 2. PROFESSIONALS TO ENGAGE

### 2.1 Transaction Lawyer (ESSENTIAL)

**What they do:** Draft and negotiate the LOI, APA, lease assignment agreement, non-compete clauses. Conduct legal DD. Manage the closing process. Advise on Malta-specific legal requirements throughout.

**When needed:** Phase 1 through closing (Day 1 to Week 10)

**Estimated cost:** EUR 8,000-15,000 for a deal this size in Malta. Malta law firms typically charge EUR 150-300/hour for partner-level commercial work. For a EUR 220-340K asset deal, expect 50-80 hours of legal work. Some firms may offer a fixed fee of EUR 10,000-12,000 for the full transaction.

**How to find:** Malta's legal community is small. The top commercial/M&A firms include Ganado Advocates, Camilleri Preziosi, GVZH Advocates, Fenech & Fenech, Chetcuti Cauchi, and Mamo TCV. For a deal this size, a mid-tier firm with commercial transaction experience is ideal -- you do not need the most expensive firm, but you need someone who has handled asset purchases and lease assignments before.

### 2.2 Employment Lawyer (ESSENTIAL)

**What they do:** Review all 14 employment contracts. Advise on TUPE transfer obligations (S.L. 452.85). Draft employee notification and consultation documents. Advise on post-acquisition restructuring (redundancy process, notice periods, LIFO compliance). Represent you if any employee disputes arise.

**When needed:** Phase 2 through post-closing (Weeks 2-18)

**Estimated cost:** EUR 3,000-6,000. May be the same firm as your transaction lawyer if they have an employment law department.

**Essential or optional:** ESSENTIAL. You are planning to reduce staff from 14 to 8-9. Getting this wrong in Malta can result in unfair dismissal claims, Industrial Tribunal proceedings, and reinstatement orders. Malta's employment protections are strong.

### 2.3 Accountant / Auditor (ESSENTIAL)

**What they do:** Financial due diligence -- verify revenue, EBITDA, tax compliance, VAT returns. Review 24 months of bank statements and POS data. Identify any tax liabilities that transfer with the business. Advise on tax-efficient deal structuring. Handle post-closing tax filings and notifications.

**When needed:** Phase 2 and closing (Weeks 2-10)

**Estimated cost:** EUR 3,000-5,000 for financial DD. Plus EUR 1,000-2,000 for ongoing post-close tax advisory.

**How to find:** Malta's major accounting firms include KPMG Malta, PwC Malta, EY Malta, Deloitte Malta, BDO Malta, and RSM Malta. For a deal this size, a mid-tier firm like BDO or RSM provides excellent value. Alternatively, a respected local firm like CSB Group or Fenlex can handle this efficiently.

### 2.4 Notary Public (ESSENTIAL in Malta)

**What they do:** In Malta, a notary is a public official who authenticates deeds, witnesses signatures, and ensures the legality of transfers. For this deal, the notary will: authenticate the APA (particularly if it involves transfer of lease rights treated as immovable property interests), handle stamp duty calculations and payment, potentially hold escrow funds, and maintain the public deed in their notarial archives.

**When needed:** Phase 3-4 (Weeks 7-10)

**Estimated cost:** EUR 2,000-5,000. Malta notary fees are typically 1-2.5% of the transaction value, though for asset deals (as opposed to property sales) the fee may be negotiated. On a EUR 300K deal, expect EUR 3,000-4,500.

**Essential or optional:** ESSENTIAL. Malta law requires notarial involvement for transfers of certain rights, and having the APA notarised provides significantly stronger legal protection.

### 2.5 Equipment Appraiser (ESSENTIAL)

**What they do:** Independent fair market valuation of all spa and medical equipment (Candela laser, T-Shape, treatment beds, sauna, hydro equipment, reception furniture). Assess remaining useful life and maintenance condition. Identify any equipment that is leased or financed (liens).

**When needed:** Phase 2 (Weeks 2-3)

**Estimated cost:** EUR 1,500-3,000. Medical device appraisals cost more than standard equipment due to the specialist knowledge required.

**How to find:** There are limited equipment appraisers in Malta specifically for spa/medical devices. Options: (a) engage a Maltese chartered surveyor with commercial equipment experience, (b) bring in an appraiser from the seller's equipment suppliers (Candela, T-Shape manufacturers often have partner appraisers), or (c) use a European valuation firm with Mediterranean coverage.

### 2.6 Business Valuator (RECOMMENDED)

**What they do:** Provide an independent fair market valuation of the business as a going concern. Useful for: (a) validating your negotiating position, (b) supporting any earnout calculations, (c) providing a reference for stamp duty purposes.

**When needed:** Phase 1-2 (Weeks 1-4)

**Estimated cost:** EUR 2,000-4,000 for a formal valuation opinion. A desktop valuation (less formal) may cost EUR 1,000-1,500.

**Essential or optional:** RECOMMENDED but not essential. You already have extensive internal valuation work (the 04_DEAL_PARTNER_SYNTHESIS and 03_INVESTMENT_BANKER_VALUATION_MEMO). An independent opinion strengthens your negotiating position and may be useful for tax purposes.

### 2.7 Insurance Broker (ESSENTIAL)

**What they do:** Review the seller's existing insurance policies (public liability, professional indemnity, equipment, employer liability). Arrange new policies in Carisma's name effective from closing date. Ensure no gap in coverage. Advise on any additional coverage needed for the clinic build-out.

**When needed:** Phase 3-4 (Weeks 5-10)

**Estimated cost:** EUR 500-1,000 broker fee. Insurance premiums are a separate ongoing cost (EUR 3,000-6,000/year for a spa of this size).

**How to find:** Malta's insurance market is dominated by GasanMamo, Middlesea Insurance, Atlas Insurance, and Elmo Insurance. Any commercial insurance broker (such as Laferla, MIB, or KYTE Insurance) can arrange the required policies.

### 2.8 Lease / Property Advisor (OPTIONAL BUT RECOMMENDED)

**What they do:** Review the lease terms specifically -- rent escalation, break clauses, permitted use, assignment provisions, landlord obligations. Advise on whether the rent of EUR 5K/month is below market (creating lease value) and help negotiate the assignment terms with the landlord.

**When needed:** Phase 2-3 (Weeks 2-7)

**Estimated cost:** EUR 1,000-2,000. May be handled by your transaction lawyer if they have property expertise.

### 2.9 Regulatory Consultant (OPTIONAL)

**What they do:** Advise on Malta Medicines Authority requirements for medical device operation (Candela laser, T-Shape). Confirm whether existing registrations transfer or require new applications. Advise on clinic licensing for the planned aesthetics expansion.

**When needed:** Phase 2 (Weeks 3-5)

**Estimated cost:** EUR 1,000-2,000

**Essential or optional:** OPTIONAL for this phase (the medical devices are already operational), but will become ESSENTIAL when you launch the clinic component post-acquisition.

## 3. DUE DILIGENCE CHECKLIST

### 3.1 Financial Due Diligence

**Documents to request:**
- [ ] 24 months of bank statements (all business accounts)
- [ ] Monthly POS/booking system reports (transaction counts, average ticket value)
- [ ] Annual management accounts for the last 3 years
- [ ] VAT returns for the last 3 years
- [ ] Income tax returns and assessments for the last 3 years
- [ ] Social security (NI) contribution records for all employees
- [ ] Accounts payable and receivable aging schedules
- [ ] Cash register records and daily reconciliations
- [ ] Gift card/voucher outstanding liabilities (unredeemed balances are a hidden liability)
- [ ] Prepaid treatment packages (similar hidden liability -- clients who paid but have not yet received services)

**What to verify:**
- Monthly revenue averages EUR 28-30K (not inflated by one-off events)
- Revenue trend is flat or positive (not declining)
- EBITDA of EUR 72K is confirmed after all real costs
- No material gap between bank deposits and reported revenue (cash leakage)
- VAT is current and fully compliant
- No outstanding tax assessments or disputes with the Inland Revenue

**Red flags:**
- Revenue figures that do not reconcile with bank statements
- Large cash transactions (suggests off-books revenue that cannot be relied upon)
- Declining monthly trend masked by annual averages
- Unpaid VAT or social security contributions (these become YOUR liability)
- Prepaid packages exceeding EUR 10K (you inherit the service obligation)

## 4. LEGAL DOCUMENTS NEEDED

### 4.1 Already Completed
- **NDA / Confidentiality Agreement** -- Already signed between parties

### 4.2 Pre-Transaction Documents

**Letter of Intent (LOI) / Heads of Terms**
Non-binding document setting out: agreed price range, deal structure (asset purchase), exclusivity period (60-90 days), key conditions precedent, target timeline, expense allocation (each party bears own costs), and governing law (Laws of Malta).

### 4.3 Core Transaction Documents

**Asset Purchase Agreement (APA)** -- This is the correct structure (NOT a Share Purchase Agreement). You are buying business assets (equipment, lease, goodwill, client base, staff transfer), not shares in a company. If the seller operates through a Maltese company and you are buying the company itself, an SPA would apply -- but for this deal, the APA is appropriate because you want the assets without inheriting any corporate liabilities.

The APA should contain:
- Identification of parties
- Definition of purchased assets (detailed schedule)
- Definition of excluded assets and retained liabilities
- Purchase price and payment mechanism (including any escrow)
- Earnout provisions (if applicable -- EUR 30K contingent on EUR 25K+/month spa revenue for 12 months)
- Representations and warranties (seller's statements about the business)
- Indemnification provisions (seller indemnifies buyer for breaches, EUR 20K cap, 18-month survival period)
- Conditions precedent (landlord consent, DD satisfaction, regulatory approvals)
- Employee transfer provisions (compliance with S.L. 452.85)
- Non-compete and non-solicitation covenants
- Transition services provisions (90-day cooperation period)
- Governing law and dispute resolution (Malta courts or Malta Arbitration Centre)

**Lease Assignment Agreement (Tripartite)**
Between seller (current tenant), buyer (new tenant), and landlord (Numi Hotel). This transfers all lease rights and obligations. Must confirm: rent amount, remaining term, permitted use, any landlord consent conditions.

**Deed of Sale / Transfer Deed**
In Malta, if the asset transfer includes rights treated as immovable property (which a long-term commercial lease may be), a formal deed before a notary may be required. Even if not strictly required, notarisation provides stronger legal protection and is standard practice for deals of this size in Malta.

### 4.4 Employment Documents

**Employee Transfer Notice**
Written notice to all 14 employees informing them of the transfer, the transfer date, the reasons, and the legal and economic implications. Must be delivered at least 15 working days before the transfer date.

**Consultation Record**
Documentation of the mandatory consultation process with employees or their representatives.

**Retention Agreements (for key staff)**
Separate agreements with top 1-2 therapists offering retention bonuses (EUR 3,000-5,000 each) contingent on 12-month continued employment, paid quarterly.

**Redundancy Notices (post-closing, if applicable)**
If restructuring from 14 to 8-9 staff, formal redundancy notices complying with EIRA: identifying the redundant positions, applying LIFO, providing proper notice periods based on length of service.

### 4.5 Ancillary Documents

**Non-Compete / Non-Solicitation Agreement**
Seller agrees not to: (a) operate or be involved in a competing spa/wellness/aesthetics business in Malta for 24-36 months, (b) solicit any transferred employees for 24 months, (c) solicit any clients for 24 months.

**Transition Services Agreement**
Seller provides 90 days of cooperation: introductions to hotel management, key clients, suppliers; knowledge transfer on operations, systems, scheduling; availability for questions (reasonable hours).

**Escrow Agreement** (if using escrow)
If part of the purchase price is held in escrow for indemnification purposes (EUR 20K holdback for 18 months), a formal escrow agreement with the escrow agent (typically the notary or a lawyer's client account).

### 4.6 Malta-Specific Filings and Notifications

- Inland Revenue Department: notification of business transfer, VAT registration transfer or new registration
- Malta Business Registry: update of commercial partnership registration (if applicable)
- Jobsplus: notification of employee transfer
- Environmental Health Directorate: notification of change of operator (for health and safety permits)
- Malta Medicines Authority: notification of change in device operator registration
- Insurance transfers: notification to all insurers of change of policyholder

**Note on stamp duty:** This is the most variable cost. If the transfer is structured purely as a sale of business assets (goodwill, equipment, client base), stamp duty may apply at 2% on marketable securities or 5% on transfers deemed to include immovable property rights. The lease assignment may be treated as a transfer of an interest in immovable property, attracting the higher 5% rate on the lease value. Your notary and accountant must advise on the optimal structure. At a purchase price of EUR 300K, stamp duty could range from EUR 6,000 (2% on business goodwill) to EUR 15,000 (5% on the full amount if treated as immovable property transfer). Budget conservatively at EUR 10,000-12,000.

## 7. RISKS AND GOTCHAS

### 7.1 Malta-Specific Pitfalls

**The landlord has all the power.** In Malta, commercial lease assignments are entirely governed by the lease contract -- there is no statutory right to assign. If the lease requires landlord consent, the landlord can refuse, impose conditions, or demand a rent increase. This is the number one deal-killer. Mitigation: contact the landlord EARLY, present Carisma's credentials (50+ hotel spas), and negotiate in good faith. Have a walk-away price for rent increases (EUR 6K/month maximum per your existing analysis).

**Malta's TUPE equivalent is strict.** Under S.L. 452.85, ALL employees transfer automatically with their existing terms. You cannot cherry-pick. You cannot use the transfer as grounds for dismissal. Any post-transfer redundancies must follow EIRA procedures (LIFO within each job class, proper notice, re-employment rights for 12 months). First-time acquirers in Malta often underestimate how protective the law is -- getting this wrong leads to Industrial Tribunal claims that are expensive and reputationally damaging.

**The LIFO rule is absolute.** When making redundancies, you MUST terminate the most recently hired employee in each affected job class first. You cannot simply choose to keep the "best" employees and let go of the others. This means your restructuring plan must be built around job classes, not individual performance. Work closely with your employment lawyer to define job classes correctly.

**Stamp duty on goodwill.** Many first-time acquirers in Malta are surprised to learn that stamp duty applies not just to property but also to business goodwill, trade names, and intellectual property. The Duty on Documents and Transfers Act taxes these transfers. Goodwill is calculated as two years' profit based on the last five years of operation. Your accountant and notary must calculate this correctly to avoid penalties.

**Seasonal cash flow crunches.** Malta spa businesses see 30-40% revenue variance between peak (July-October) and trough (January-February). If you close the deal in a trough month and also absorb restructuring costs and transition friction, your cash position can get tight quickly. Ensure the EUR 30-50K working capital reserve is in place before closing, not after.

### 7.2 Deal-Specific Risks

**Revenue overstatement.** The business claims EUR 30K/month. If actual verified revenue is EUR 24-25K/month, the entire financial model shifts dramatically. The EBITDA drops from EUR 72K to approximately EUR 36-45K, and the fair value drops to EUR 130-200K. Revenue verification through bank statements (not management reports) is non-negotiable.

**Equipment condition.** The Candela laser and T-Shape device are high-value items. If either needs replacement within 2 years, that is EUR 50-100K of unplanned capex. Insist on maintenance logs and consider a manufacturer inspection.

**Hotel dependency.** If 50-65% of spa revenue comes from hotel guests, the hotel relationship is THE commercial risk. What happens if the hotel renovates and closes for 6 months? What happens if the hotel changes hands? What happens if the hotel launches its own spa? Your lease should have provisions addressing these scenarios (rent abatement during closure, non-compete within the hotel property).

**Client attrition post-acquisition.** Industry standard is 20-30% client loss when ownership changes. Some clients are loyal to the seller personally, some to specific therapists. If 2-3 key therapists leave simultaneously, client attrition could reach 35-40%. The retention bonuses for key therapists are not optional -- they are essential downside protection.

**Clinic ramp risk.** The clinic is where 70-80% of your returns come from, but it does not exist yet. If the clinic never launches (regulatory block, cannot find a practitioner, pandemic), you are left with a EUR 72K EBITDA spa that cannot justify the total investment. The mitigation: phase the clinic capex (EUR 50K initial, EUR 100K upon launch confirmation) so you limit losses if the clinic proves unviable.

### 7.3 What First-Time Acquirers in Malta Often Miss

1. **Prepaid packages and gift vouchers.** These are hidden liabilities. Clients who already paid but have not received services will expect those services from you. Get a complete schedule of outstanding prepaid obligations and deduct them from the purchase price or have the seller settle them pre-close.

2. **Employee leave balances.** Under Malta law, accrued but unused vacation leave must be honoured by the new employer. Get a schedule of all leave balances as of the transfer date. These are your obligations from Day 1.

3. **The social security trap.** Verify that the seller has been paying all National Insurance contributions (both employer and employee portions) for all 14 staff. If contributions are outstanding, the tax authorities may pursue the new business owner. Demand a tax clearance certificate from the Inland Revenue before closing.

4. **Informal arrangements.** In Malta's small business environment, many things operate on handshake agreements -- supplier terms, hotel referral arrangements, staff overtime practices. Get EVERYTHING in writing before closing. If it is not documented, it may not survive the ownership change.

5. **The personal guarantee.** Some commercial leases in Malta require the tenant to provide a personal guarantee. If the lease assignment requires you (or a Carisma director) to personally guarantee the rent for 13 years, factor this into your risk assessment. It changes the downside calculus significantly.
