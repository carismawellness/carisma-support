## II. DIVERGENCE ANALYSIS

### Where They Disagree (and How Deal Partner Resolves)

**1. Opening Offer**
- M&A recommends EUR 250K (3.5x EBITDA) — anchor most aggressively
- Banker recommends EUR 280K (3.9x EBITDA) — show seriousness while anchoring

**Deal Partner decision: EUR 280K**
- Rationale: EUR 250K might insult seller and reduce likelihood of counter; EUR 280K shows you are a serious buyer while still anchoring at low end of fair range. Better negotiation psychology.

**2. Target Price**
- M&A recommends EUR 300-320K (4.2-4.4x EBITDA)
- Banker recommends EUR 340-360K (4.7-5.0x EBITDA)

**Deal Partner decision: EUR 340K**
- Rationale: M&A is calibrated to EUR 400K total investment (EUR 250K capex). Banker accounts for EUR 500K total investment (EUR 150K clinic capex). EUR 340K supports EUR 490K total, which is realistic for clinic platform development. At EUR 340K, Base case IRR is 24% and Bear case is 13% — acceptable risk/reward. M&A's EUR 320K target is too aggressive (would push Bear case below 13%).

**3. Walk-Away Price**
- M&A recommends EUR 380K (5.2x EBITDA)
- Banker recommends EUR 420K (5.8x EBITDA)

**Deal Partner decision: EUR 380K**
- Rationale: At EUR 380K (+ EUR 150K capex = EUR 530K total), Base case IRR drops to 18% (equals cost of capital) and Bear case approaches 10% (unacceptable). M&A's EUR 380K is conservative but appropriate given micro-market execution risk. Don't stretch to EUR 420K.

**4. Deal Structure (Earnout vs. Clean Purchase)**
- M&A: No earnout (earnouts create friction in small deals)
- BCG & Banker: EUR 20-30K earnout (protects against revenue overstatement, aligns seller)

**Deal Partner decision: EUR 30K earnout**
- Rationale: Earnout is small relative to deal size (8% of purchase price) and serves dual purpose: (a) protects you from revenue inflation, (b) motivates seller cooperation during transition. For a deal where customer retention is critical, seller cooperation matters more than in capital-intensive businesses.

**5. Scenario Probability Weighting**
- Model: Bear 25%, Base 50%, Bull 25%
- BCG: Bear 30%, Base 45%, Bull 15%, Failure 10%
- M&A: Implicit Bear 35%, Base 50%, Bull 15% (from return analysis)

**Deal Partner decision: Use BCG weights (30/45/15/10)**
- Rationale: Failure case is real for micro-market clinic launch (regulatory, practitioner unavailability). Bull case (EUR 55K/month clinic) approaches capacity limits. BCG's weights better reflect risk of executing clinic in small market.

## III. INVESTMENT THESIS

### What Are We Buying?

**Three distinct assets in one transaction:**

1. **Proven spa operation:** EUR 30K/month revenue (EUR 360K/year), EUR 72K EBITDA, with 13-year lease and stable local customer base. This is the foundation.

2. **Clinic platform opportunity:** Unproven clinic platform generating zero EBITDA today, with potential to reach EUR 150-200K EBITDA by Year 2-3. This is where 70-80% of returns live.

3. **Lease location:** 13-year commercial lease at EUR 5K/month (below market), providing runway for clinic investment and location optionality.

### Why Is This Attractive for Carisma?

**Carisma brings unique competitive advantages that de-risk the clinic launch:**

1. **Existing brand recognition** in Malta wellness market (you already operate in Malta)
2. **Proven clinic infrastructure:** Medical aesthetics nurses, treatment protocols, supplier relationships
3. **Digital marketing capability:** Meta Ads, Google, content production, customer acquisition systems
4. **Operational depth:** Experience running multiple locations, staff management, compliance
5. **Financial capacity:** EUR 500K investment is material but manageable for multi-million revenue platform

**For a greenfield buyer (without these advantages), recommend passing at any price above EUR 250K.** The clinic execution risk would be too high.

### What Must Go Right?

**Three critical success factors:**

1. **Clinic reaches EUR 150K EBITDA by Year 2** — not Year 3, not "eventually," but within 12 months of clinic launch. This is non-negotiable. If clinic reaches only EUR 100K EBITDA, deal IRR drops to 20-22% (still acceptable but marginal). If clinic never launches, deal destroys EUR 250K.

2. **Spa maintains EUR 25K+/month baseline** — post-acquisition attrition should not exceed 20%. Key therapists (top 1-2) must stay. Customer retention in first 6 months is critical.

3. **Lease assignment confirmed at current or near-current terms** — must be written confirmation from landlord before you move. Deal dies if blocked or if rent increases to EUR 6K+.

### What Is the Downside?

**Worst case: Clinic fails entirely, spa underperforms**

- Total investment: EUR 500K (EUR 340K purchase + EUR 150K clinic capex)
- Revenue declines to EUR 20K/month spa (major competitor enters)
- Clinic is abandoned after EUR 150K sunk
- Result: Capital loss of approximately EUR 250-300K (50% of investment) over 5+ years

**Mitigants:**
- Even in disaster case, spa alone is worth EUR 288K (3.5x EUR 72K EBITDA) — recovers 60% of purchase price
- Clinic capex (EUR 150K) can be phased: EUR 50K initial, EUR 100K upon clinic launch (reduces risk)
- Carisma's existing operations provide fallback value if this asset underperforms

## VI. SIX GO CONDITIONS (Must Verify Before LOI)

All of these must be satisfied before signing any offer letter or moving forward:

**1. LEASE ASSIGNMENT CONFIRMED**
- Written confirmation from landlord that assignment is permitted
- Rent remains at EUR 5-5.5K/month (or below)
- No undisclosed conditions or capex requirements
- **Why:** Deal dies if blocked or rent increases to EUR 6K+ (breaks economics)

**2. REVENUE VERIFICATION**
- 24 months of bank statements showing EUR 28K+ monthly spa average
- Seasonality pattern confirmed (EUR 24-43K monthly range if it exists)
- Trend direction: flat or positive (not declining)
- **Why:** If revenue is actually EUR 20K/month, deal is materially misrepresented

**3. EQUIPMENT APPRAISAL**
- Independent FMV assessment confirming EUR 100K+ value
- No major items requiring near-term replacement (5+ years remaining useful life)
- All equipment owned free and clear (no hidden liens)
- **Why:** Overvalued equipment reduces purchase price justification

**4. CLINIC PRACTITIONER IDENTIFIED**
- Licensed medical professional (nurse or aesthetician) willing to join post-close
- Available at EUR 5.5K/month budgeted salary (or you have alternative at similar cost)
- Commitment/LOI in place (not just verbal interest)
- **Why:** Without practitioner, clinic launch is impossible; this is THE bottleneck

**5. STAFF RETENTION COMMITMENTS**
- Top 1-2 revenue-generating therapists sign 12-month employment agreements
- Retention bonus of EUR 3-5K each in escrow (EUR 6-10K total, paid quarterly on continued employment)
- Other staff offered continuity of employment at current terms
- **Why:** Key-person risk is highest risk after clinic execution

**6. LEGAL CLEAN**
- No pending litigation (verify with legal counsel)
- No tax disputes or outstanding assessments
- No employment claims or regulatory violations
- Clean UCC search (no equipment financing or liens)
- **Why:** Hidden liabilities become your liabilities post-close

**If ANY of these conditions fails:** Renegotiate purchase price downward or walk away. Do not proceed with gaps.
