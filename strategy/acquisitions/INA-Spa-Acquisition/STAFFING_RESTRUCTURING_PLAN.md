**Prepared for:** Carisma Wellness Group Board
**Date:** 6 March 2026
**Classification:** Confidential — Board & Advisory Only

## Executive Summary

Labour cost is the single biggest drag on INA Spa's profitability. At EUR 209,574 per year the wage bill consumes **49.2% of revenue** — roughly 10-14 percentage points above the 35-40% industry benchmark for spa operations in Malta. That gap represents EUR 38-60K in annual margin erosion.

This plan achieves a target **38% wage-to-revenue ratio within 18 months** primarily through revenue growth (adding clinic services and cross-sell), supported by roster optimisation, natural attrition, and selective restructuring where legally and commercially justified. The strategy deliberately avoids mass layoffs, which would destroy client relationships, trigger legal exposure, and undermine staff morale during a critical transition period.

**Target outcome:** EUR 47K annual labour savings on a relative basis, achieved through growing revenue against a flat-to-slightly-reduced wage bill.

## 2. Legal Framework

### 2.1 Transfer of Business Regulations (S.L. 452.85)

Malta's TUPE-equivalent legislation mandates:

- **Automatic transfer:** All employees transfer to Carisma on their existing terms and conditions. No employee consent is required for the transfer itself.
- **Protection from dismissal:** Dismissal "by reason of the transfer" is automatically unfair. This protection applies before and after the transfer date.
- **ETO exception:** Dismissals are permitted for "economic, technical or organisational reasons entailing changes in the workforce." This is narrow — it requires genuine business restructuring, not just cost reduction to improve margins.
- **Information and consultation:** The transferor (seller) and transferee (Carisma) must inform employee representatives of the transfer, its reasons, and its implications. If no representatives exist, employees must be informed directly.

### 2.2 What You CAN Do

- [x] Transfer all staff on existing terms (mandatory)
- [x] Set new performance expectations and KPIs after a reasonable settling period
- [x] Introduce new training requirements
- [x] Optimise rosters and shift patterns (within contracted hours)
- [x] Offer voluntary changes to compensation structure (base + bonus)
- [x] Manage underperformance through proper disciplinary process
- [x] Make roles redundant for genuine ETO reasons (with proper process)
- [x] Offer voluntary redundancy packages
- [x] Hire new staff on Carisma's standard terms (including performance-linked pay)

### 2.3 What You CANNOT Do

- [ ] Dismiss any employee "by reason of the transfer"
- [ ] Unilaterally change terms and conditions of employment
- [ ] Reduce salaries or benefits without individual written consent
- [ ] Impose a new probationary period on transferred employees
- [ ] Make changes during the first 30 days (practically — to avoid any appearance of transfer-related dismissal)

### 2.4 Key Legal Insight — Low Redundancy Exposure

INA opened in January 2024. Maximum employee service is approximately 2 years. Under the EIRA:

- **Redundancy payment:** 1 week's pay per year of continuous service
- **Maximum exposure per employee:** ~2 weeks' pay
- **Total maximum redundancy cost** (if all 13 were made redundant, which is not planned): ~EUR 8,000-10,000

This is an unusually favourable position. The financial risk of restructuring is minimal compared to the EUR 47K annual savings target.

### 2.5 Required Documentation

For any restructuring action, maintain:

1. Written business case demonstrating ETO justification
2. Consultation records (dates, attendees, topics discussed)
3. Individual meeting minutes signed by employee
4. Written notice with statutory minimums observed
5. Appeal process offered and documented
6. Settlement agreements (where applicable) reviewed by employment lawyer

**Legal advisor:** Engage a Malta employment law firm (Fenech & Fenech, Ganado Advocates, or GVZH) before executing any Phase 3 actions. Budget EUR 3,000-5,000 for advisory.

## 4. Phase 2: Optimise (Days 31-90)

**Objective:** Improve productivity within existing headcount and terms. Prepare the ground for clinic integration. Begin natural attrition management.

### 4.1 Roster Optimisation

Current likely setup: Fixed Monday-Saturday shifts for all therapists regardless of demand.

**Proposed changes (within existing contracted hours):**

- Analyse booking patterns to identify peak days/hours (likely Thursday-Saturday, 10:00-15:00)
- Create A/B shift rosters: full staffing during peak, reduced during off-peak
- Introduce split shifts for therapists willing (with incentive)
- Reduce double-coverage at reception during quiet periods
- **Target impact:** 10-15% improvement in therapist utilisation without changing contracted hours

### 4.2 Multi-Skilling Programme

This is the highest-value intervention in Phase 2. Therapists who can perform both spa and clinic services are dramatically more productive.

| Training Area | Target Staff | Duration | Cost Est. | Revenue Potential |
|---------------|-------------|----------|-----------|-------------------|
| Facial aesthetics assist (prep, aftercare) | 3-4 therapists | 2 weeks | EUR 1,500 | Enables clinic launch |
| IV therapy preparation | 2 therapists | 1 week | EUR 800 | Supports IV drip menu |
| Advanced skincare (chemical peels, microneedling) | 2-3 therapists | 4 weeks | EUR 3,000 | EUR 30-50K/year |
| Booking management + CRM | 1 receptionist | 2 weeks | EUR 500 | Reduces admin headcount need |

- Participation is voluntary but positioned as career development
- Therapists who upskill become eligible for higher-tier services (and eventually performance bonuses)
- **Budget:** EUR 5,000-8,000 for Phase 2 training

### 4.3 Performance Framework

Introduce individual KPIs after Day 45 (gives staff 6 weeks to settle):

| KPI | Target | Measurement |
|-----|--------|-------------|
| Utilisation rate | 70% of available hours booked | Booking system |
| Average ticket value | EUR 55+ (up from est. EUR 48) | POS data |
| Client retention | 60%+ rebooking rate | CRM |
| Upsell rate | 15%+ of appointments include add-on | POS data |
| Client satisfaction | 4.5+ rating | Post-visit survey |

Present these as team goals first, then individual targets. Frame as "here's how we'll grow together" — not surveillance.

### 4.4 Natural Attrition Management

In any team of 13, annual turnover of 1-2 people is normal (Malta spa sector turnover ~15-20%).

**Protocol when someone resigns:**

1. Conduct exit interview — understand reasons, capture client handover needs
2. Evaluate: Does this role need backfilling at all?
3. If yes: Can an existing multi-skilled employee absorb the work?
4. If still yes: Hire on Carisma terms (base + performance bonus, flexible rostering)
5. Document the decision and financial impact

**Target:** Do not replace 1-2 natural departures in Year 1 = EUR 15-18K annual saving.

### 4.5 Decision Gate

At Day 90, review:
- [ ] Roster changes implemented and bedded in
- [ ] Multi-skilling programme underway (at least 2-3 therapists enrolled)
- [ ] Performance KPIs communicated and baseline measured
- [ ] Natural attrition tracker active
- [ ] Clinic service launch plan finalised with staffing requirements

## 5. Phase 3: Restructure (Days 91-180)

**Objective:** Make targeted structural changes where justified by data and business need. This is where the difficult decisions happen — only if Phase 2 optimisation is insufficient.

### 5.1 Role Redesign Assessment

Based on Phase 1-2 data, evaluate whether any roles can be consolidated:

| Current Role | Potential Redesign | Trigger |
|-------------|-------------------|---------|
| Receptionist x2 | Receptionist x1 + Booking/CRM Manager x1 | If booking system automation reduces front-desk need |
| Spa Manager | Spa & Clinic Operations Manager | If clinic launches and needs coordination |
| Underperforming therapist | Managed exit via performance process | If KPIs consistently missed after coaching |
| Low-utilisation therapist | Redeployed to clinic prep/aftercare | If multi-skilling complete and willing |

**Important:** Role redesign must be justified by genuine organisational change (ETO), not just cost reduction. Document the business rationale thoroughly.

### 5.2 Performance Management Process

For employees consistently missing KPIs after reasonable support:

1. **Informal conversation** (documented) — Week 1
2. **Formal verbal warning** with improvement plan — 30-day review period
3. **Written warning** if insufficient improvement — 30-day review period
4. **Final warning** — 14-day review period
5. **Dismissal for capability reasons** — with notice period and appeal right

Total process: minimum 10-12 weeks. This is not fast, but it is legally defensible. Cutting corners here creates unfair dismissal liability.

### 5.3 Voluntary Measures (Preferred)

Before any compulsory action, offer:

- **Voluntary redundancy:** Statutory minimum (1 week per year of service) plus a small enhancement (e.g., additional 2 weeks' pay). Total cost per person: ~EUR 1,000-1,500. Offer to any role identified as surplus.
- **Voluntary hours reduction:** Some therapists may prefer part-time. This reduces wage cost while retaining the employee and their client base.
- **Voluntary role change:** Therapists may welcome a move to clinic services with new training. Frame as career progression.

### 5.4 Compulsory Restructuring (Last Resort)

If voluntary measures are insufficient and genuine ETO reasons exist:

1. Prepare written business case (reviewed by employment lawyer)
2. Consult with affected employees (minimum 2 formal meetings)
3. Consider alternatives (redeployment, reduced hours, retraining)
4. Apply fair selection criteria if multiple employees in the same role
5. Issue notice with statutory minimum + any contractual enhancement
6. Provide written reasons and right of appeal
7. Process final payments correctly (outstanding leave, notice pay, redundancy)

**Budget for compulsory restructuring:** EUR 3,000-5,000 (statutory minimums are low given short service).

### 5.5 Compensation Redesign (New Hires and Voluntary Converts)

| Component | Current (Est.) | Proposed (New Hires) | Proposed (Existing — Voluntary) |
|-----------|---------------|---------------------|-------------------------------|
| Base salary | EUR 15-18K (100% fixed) | EUR 12-14K (75% of current) | Current base minus 10-15% |
| Service commission | None | 8-12% of personal service revenue | 8-12% of personal service revenue |
| Retail commission | None | 10% of retail sales | 10% of retail sales |
| Rebooking bonus | None | EUR 2 per rebooking secured | EUR 2 per rebooking secured |
| Estimated total (at target utilisation) | EUR 15-18K | EUR 18-22K | EUR 17-20K |

**Key principle:** Existing employees cannot be forced onto the new structure. Offer it as an opt-in with a guaranteed minimum matching their current salary for the first 6 months (transitional guarantee). This eliminates downside risk and encourages adoption.

For **new hires**, Carisma's standard performance-linked terms apply from Day 1.

### 5.6 Decision Gate

At Day 180:
- [ ] Role redesign implemented (if applicable)
- [ ] Performance management cases resolved or in final stages
- [ ] Voluntary measures uptake recorded
- [ ] Any compulsory actions completed with proper documentation
- [ ] New compensation structure live for new hires; opt-in offered to existing staff
- [ ] Clinic service launched with trained staff

## 6. Phase 4: Growth Staffing (Months 6-18)

**Objective:** Staff for revenue growth, not just cost management. This is where the acquisition thesis pays off — adding clinic revenue on a relatively fixed cost base.

### 6.1 Revenue-Linked Hiring Triggers

Do not hire speculatively. Each new hire must be justified by revenue:

| Revenue Milestone | Hiring Action | Role | Est. Cost |
|-------------------|--------------|------|-----------|
| EUR 500K annualised | Confirm existing headcount sufficient | — | EUR 0 |
| EUR 550K annualised | Hire 1x clinic aesthetician (part-time) | Aesthetician | EUR 10-12K |
| EUR 600K annualised | Convert aesthetician to full-time | — | EUR 16-18K |
| EUR 650K annualised | Hire 1x nurse (IV therapy, injectable assist) | Registered nurse | EUR 22-25K |
| EUR 700K+ annualised | Evaluate additional therapist | Therapist | EUR 14-16K |

**Rule:** No hire is approved unless trailing 3-month revenue annualised exceeds the trigger threshold AND the wage ratio remains at or below 38%.

### 6.2 Seasonal Flexibility

Malta spa demand peaks:
- **High season:** April-October (tourism + wedding season)
- **Holiday peaks:** Christmas, Valentine's, Mother's Day
- **Low season:** November-February (except Christmas)

Build a flexible staffing layer:
- Maintain a pool of 2-3 vetted part-time therapists available on-call
- Use fixed-term contracts (3-6 months) for summer peak
- Cross-train existing staff to cover seasonal demand spikes before hiring
- **Budget:** EUR 5,000-8,000/year for seasonal flex pool

### 6.3 Career Progression Framework

Retention is cheaper than recruitment. Create visible career paths:

| Level | Title | Requirements | Base Range |
|-------|-------|-------------|-----------|
| 1 | Junior Therapist | Basic qualifications | EUR 12-14K |
| 2 | Therapist | 1 year + advanced cert | EUR 14-16K |
| 3 | Senior Therapist | 2 years + 3 specialisms + mentoring | EUR 17-19K |
| 4 | Lead Therapist / Clinic Specialist | 3 years + clinic certs + team lead | EUR 20-24K |
| 5 | Operations Manager | 5 years + management qualification | EUR 28-35K |

Performance bonuses and commissions apply at all levels. The combination of base growth + commission creates meaningful earnings potential (EUR 22-30K for a strong Level 3-4 therapist).

## 11. Key Principles

1. **Revenue growth first, cost cutting second.** The acquisition thesis is about adding clinic revenue on the existing cost base. Every decision should be evaluated through this lens.

2. **No surprises.** Every staff member should understand the direction of travel. Communicate early, communicate often. Rumours are more damaging than difficult truths delivered respectfully.

3. **Legal compliance is non-negotiable.** The cost of getting it right (EUR 3-5K in legal fees) is a fraction of the cost of getting it wrong (tribunal claims, reputation damage, staff exodus).

4. **Retain the best, develop the middle, manage the rest.** The top 3-4 therapists are the backbone of client retention. They get investment, career paths, and performance upside. The bottom 1-2 get fair process, support, and — if necessary — a dignified exit.

5. **Measure everything.** Decisions are made on data, not assumptions. Utilisation rates, rebooking rates, average ticket, client satisfaction — these numbers tell you what's working and what isn't.

6. **Patience pays.** The temptation to cut fast and save immediately is real. Resist it. The 18-month timeline exists because sustainable restructuring compounds — slash-and-burn destroys value.
