4. **Revenue sustainability estimate:** For forward projections, I would use EUR 400–420K as the baseline, not EUR 426K. The 2025 figure was boosted by a very strong Q1 that may not repeat at the same growth rate.

## 4. LEASE RISK ANALYSIS — THE MOST CRITICAL FINDING

The full lease contract reveals risks that are significantly worse than previously assumed.

### 4.1 Assignment at Landlord's Absolute Discretion

Clause 5.26 states that assignment requires landlord written consent, which may be withheld **at the landlord's absolute discretion**. Furthermore, a **change of control** of the lessee (Well Being Services Limited) is deemed an assignment and also requires consent.

**What this means for Carisma:**
- Whether Carisma buys the company shares or the business assets, landlord consent is required
- The landlord can refuse for any reason or no reason
- The landlord can use the consent process to renegotiate terms (higher rent, additional deposits, personal guarantees)
- This is a deal-killer risk. If the landlord says no, or demands onerous conditions, the deal dies

**Mitigation:** Landlord consent must be obtained IN WRITING before Carisma commits any capital. Not "in principle" — actual written consent with terms preserved.

### 4.2 Complex Sale Termination Right

Clause 9.3 gives the landlord the right to terminate the lease if the Complex (the entire Fortina development) is sold to a third party. The lessee receives:
- EUR 150,000 one-time payment
- Reimbursement of investment at depreciated value (0% depreciation in first 2 years, then 10% per year thereafter)

**Current depreciation calculation:**
- The lessee invested approximately EUR 738K in fit-out
- The lease commenced in 2020 (approximately 6 years ago)
- Depreciation: 0% for Years 1–2, then 10%/year for Years 3–6 = 40% depreciated
- Remaining value: EUR 738K x 60% = EUR 442,800
- Total payout if Complex sold: EUR 150,000 + EUR 442,800 = EUR 592,800

**For the current owner, this is actually not a terrible exit.** They invested EUR 858K and would receive EUR 593K if the Complex is sold — a 31% loss, but better than many alternatives.

**For Carisma as the new lessee, this is dangerous:**
- Carisma would pay EUR 200K to acquire the business
- Invest EUR 100K in clinic capex
- If the Complex is then sold, Carisma's fit-out investment (EUR 100K) would only have accumulated perhaps 0–2 years of depreciation, so recovery would be EUR 90–100K
- The existing fit-out depreciation continues from its original schedule
- Carisma would receive: EUR 150K + whatever the depreciated value of the total fit-out is at that point (declining each year)
- **Critical question:** Does the investment reimbursement transfer to a new lessee, or is it calculated on the original lessee's investment only? The contract language says "reimburse the investment effected by the Lessee" — this likely means the original lessee's investment, not any additional investment by an assignee

**This risk is real.** The Fortina Complex is a major development in Sliema. If the owners (Fortina Developments Limited / the Zammit Tabona family) decide to sell or restructure, the spa lease could be terminated. Carisma would receive EUR 150K plus a depreciation-adjusted refund, but would lose the business, the client base, the clinic build-out, and the brand investment.

The contract is dated January 10, 2020. The rent commencement date is defined as July 1, 2020 or when the hotel opens for business (whichever is later). The info pack states the spa opened in January 2024, but the lease economics suggest the operator was paying rent well before that.

Based on the rent schedule:
- Year 1 (2020/21): EUR 5,000 (concessionary)
- Year 2 (2021/22): EUR 50,000
- Year 3 (2022/23): EUR 60,000
- Year 4 (2023/24): EUR 61,800
- Year 5 (2024/25): EUR 63,654
- **Current: Year 6 (2025/26): EUR 65,563**

The 2025 P&L shows rent of EUR 71,689. If the fixed component was approximately EUR 63–65K (Years 5–6 overlap), the turnover fee accounts for the remainder, which aligns with 5% on approximately EUR 130–170K of revenue above the EUR 350K minimum (the fee on EUR 426K revenue is EUR 21,301; EUR 63,654 + EUR 21,301 = EUR 84,955 — slightly above the P&L figure of EUR 71,689, suggesting the lease year and calendar year don't perfectly align, or the turnover fee is calculated differently).

**Best estimate of remaining term: approximately 9–10 years (lease expires ~2035).**

The first 5 years are "di fermo" (binding/irrevocable), which have now passed. The remaining 10 years are "di rispetto," meaning the **lessee** may terminate with 6 months' notice. This is actually favorable — Carisma has an exit option if the business underperforms.

### 4.4 Permitted Use

This is the "sign the deal" price. If the seller accepts EUR 200K, close confidently.

Above EUR 250K, the deal only works if the clinic succeeds. That is speculation, not investment.

## 9. REVISED NEGOTIATION FRAMEWORK

### 9.1 The Seller's Situation — Weaker Than We Thought

The data room reveals the seller's position is worse than we assumed:
- The business lost EUR 19K after depreciation in 2025
- They invested EUR 858K and are earning EUR 36.5K EBITDA — a 4.3% cash-on-cash return
- At the current trajectory, they will never recover their investment from operations
- Their exit options are: (a) sell to Carisma, (b) sell to another buyer (limited pool in Malta), (c) continue operating at near-breakeven, (d) negotiate a Complex sale termination payout (~EUR 593K)

Option (d) is actually the seller's strongest alternative. If the Fortina Complex were sold, the seller would receive approximately EUR 593K (EUR 150K payment + EUR 443K depreciated investment). This is higher than what Carisma should pay. **However, the seller cannot trigger this — only the landlord can, and only upon a sale of the Complex.** It is not a reliable exit for the seller.

### 9.2 Four-Phase Negotiation Sequence

**Phase 1: Opening**
- Offer: EUR 160K cash at close
- Message: "We value the business based on its actual earnings of EUR 36.5K EBITDA. At 4.4x EBITDA, our offer is at market for a single-location spa with sub-10% margins."
- Expected response: Rejection, counteroffer at EUR 350–400K

**Phase 2: First Move**
- Offer: EUR 180K + EUR 15K earnout (EUR 195K total)
- Message: "We've included an earnout to demonstrate confidence in the revenue trajectory. The earnout is contingent on the spa maintaining EUR 35K+/month average for 12 months."
- Conditions: Landlord consent obtained in writing, equipment inspection complete
- Expected response: EUR 300K counteroffer

**Phase 3: Splitting Distance**
- Offer: EUR 195K + EUR 20K earnout (EUR 215K total)
- Message: "This represents 5.9x EBITDA total consideration — well above market. We are pricing in the lease value, the equipment, and the growth trajectory."
- Conditions: All due diligence substantially complete
- Expected response: EUR 260–280K or acceptance

**Phase 4: Final Offer**
- Offer: EUR 200K + EUR 25K earnout (EUR 225K total)
- Message: "This is our best and final offer. It values the business at over 6x EBITDA. We believe this is fair given the actual financial performance."
- If rejected: Walk away. Do not counter above EUR 250K.

### 9.3 The Revenue Argument — How to Counter the Seller's "But Revenue Is EUR 426K"

The seller will inevitably argue that revenue of EUR 426K justifies a higher price. Here is the response:

**"Revenue is not profit. Your business generates EUR 426K in revenue and EUR 36.5K in EBITDA — an 8.6% margin. After depreciation, the business loses EUR 19K per year. We are buying a business, not a revenue line. In comparable European spa transactions, businesses at 8–9% EBITDA margins trade at 0.2x–0.4x revenue, which would value your business at EUR 85K–170K. Our offer of EUR 200K is already generous relative to your earnings."**

If the seller cites their EUR 858K investment:

**"Your investment was EUR 858K. Your annual return on that investment is 4.3%. In 13 years of lease, the business will generate approximately EUR 475K in cumulative EBITDA at current levels — just over half of the original investment. We are not responsible for the gap between what you invested and what the business earns. That gap reflects construction costs that are now sunk, not business value."**

### 9.4 Deal Structure Recommendation

Given the risks identified, I recommend a structure with more protection than the February 22 reports suggested:

- **Equipment warranty:** Seller warrants all equipment is functional and owned free-and-clear
- **Non-compete:** 3-year, Malta-wide, spa and wellness services
