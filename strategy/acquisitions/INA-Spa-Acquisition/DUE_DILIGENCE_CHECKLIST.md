## How to Use This Checklist

- **Status** tracks where each item stands: `Received`, `Partially Received`, `Requested`, `Outstanding` (not yet requested), or `N/A`
- **Priority** indicates deal impact: `CRITICAL` (must resolve before signing), `Important` (should resolve before signing), `Nice-to-Have` (can resolve post-close or waive)
- Items marked **CRITICAL** are conditions precedent — the deal should not close without them
- Items marked `Received` have been provided via the seller's data room or lease contract
- Update the `Notes` column as documents are reviewed

## Critical Path Items (Must Resolve Before Signing)

These are the items that MUST be resolved before the APA is executed. If any of these cannot be satisfactorily resolved, the deal should not proceed.

### Deal-Killers (Walk Away If Not Resolved)

1. **Landlord written consent for lease assignment (4.03)** — The lease grants the landlord absolute discretion to withhold consent. Without written consent with preserved terms, there is no deal. Begin informal approach immediately.

2. **Landlord confirmation that medical aesthetics is permitted use (4.09)** — If restricted to "spa only," the entire clinic growth thesis collapses.

3. **Complex sale termination risk assessment (4.07, 4.18)** — If the Fortina Complex is likely to be sold, the lease can be terminated regardless of assignment consent. Assess probability through market intelligence.

4. **Equipment lien/financing verification (2.07, 7.08)** — If equipment is financed or has security interests, the seller may not own what they are selling.

### Must-Have Before Signing

5. **Bank statements — 24 months (1.06)** — Revenue must reconcile with bank deposits. Non-negotiable for financial DD.

6. **VAT returns and tax compliance (1.07, 1.08)** — Outstanding tax liabilities transfer to the buyer in an asset deal. Must confirm clean tax status.

7. **All 13 employment contracts (3.01)** — Terms, notice periods, and TUPE transfer obligations must be fully understood before committing.

8. **Salary details per employee (3.03)** — Cannot plan restructuring without knowing individual compensation.

9. **Key-person dependency meeting (3.09, 10.04)** — Meet the manager and senior therapist individually. Assess flight risk.

10. **Insurance policy review (5.04, 8.05)** — Current coverage may be inadequate (EUR 3,358/yr is low). Must understand exposure before taking on operations.

11. **Candela laser status (7.02, 8.01)** — Single most valuable portable asset. Service history, MMA registration status, and operator license transferability must be confirmed.

12. **Hotel partnership agreement terms (6.05)** — 22% of revenue comes from the hotel. Must understand if this relationship is contractual and whether it transfers.

13. **Non-compete and non-solicitation (10.02, 10.03)** — Must be in the APA. Without these, the seller could open a competing spa and solicit staff/clients.

14. **Prepaid liabilities quantification (1.14, 1.15)** — Hidden liabilities that reduce effective purchase price. Must be itemized before closing.
