## Key Tactical Reminders

✅ **DO:**
- Get the rent figure first (determines everything)
- Ask questions in Act 1, reveal nothing
- Hold silence after naming a number (minimum 7 seconds)
- Make concessions conditional (always extract something in return)
- Introduce third-party equipment appraisal (neutralizes equipment pricing debate)
- Slow the close (due diligence = negotiating leverage)

❌ **DON'T:**
- Volunteer the lease premium calculation (discover rent, reveal premium later)
- Close the deal in the first meeting even if he accepts
- Fill silence yourself (let him talk)
- Argue about sunk cost (redirect to forward earnings)
- Reveal your walk-away price
- Appear eager or rush

---

## After the Meeting

- Wait 24-48 hours before follow-up
- Send brief email only (max 3 sentences)
- Commission independent equipment appraisal
- Request financial documentation for due diligence
- Introduce your accountant/solicitor for second meeting
- Use due diligence as a negotiating buffer
