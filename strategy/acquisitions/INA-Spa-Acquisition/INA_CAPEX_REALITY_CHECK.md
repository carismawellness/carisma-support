# INA CAPEX REALITY CHECK: What It Actually Should Have Cost

**Date:** March 6, 2026
**Classification:** CONFIDENTIAL — Negotiation Support Document
**Benchmark:** Carisma Novotel Spa Buildout (2024–2025)

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The INA seller claims a total fit-out investment of **EUR 857,884** (EUR 737,884 construction + EUR 120,000 equipment). Using Carisma's own Novotel spa buildout as a verified benchmark and scaling for INA's larger scope, **a realistic cost for the same facility is EUR 370,000–420,000** — roughly **half** of what the seller actually spent.

The seller overpaid by an estimated **EUR 340,000–370,000 (80–90%)** due to:
1. 100% local Maltese procurement vs international sourcing
2. No competitive tendering (single-source contractors)
3. Premium hotel-complex contractor pricing
4. Likely change orders and scope creep during construction
5. Over-specced systems (EUR 144K for electrical/plumbing in a 7-room spa)

**Bottom line:** The seller's sunk cost is irrelevant to fair market value. What matters is replacement cost — and Carisma could replicate this facility for ~EUR 400K, making the EUR 200–220K purchase price a bargain even on a replacement cost basis.

## WHY THE SELLER OVERPAID: ROOT CAUSES

### 1. 100% Local Procurement (vs. International Sourcing)
Novotel sourced custom joinery from Turkey (Dekorfim), water features from specialist manufacturers (Juno), treatment beds from Germany (Gharieni), and decorative elements from Turkey (Izmir Peyzaj). Only assembly labour and small items were purchased locally in Malta.

INA sourced everything locally: Domestica (furniture), Uniq (tiles + sanitary), local contractors for all works. Malta's small market means limited competition and premium pricing on everything from tiles to electrical work.

**Estimated markup: 40–80% on manufactured goods**

### 2. Single-Source Contractors (No Competitive Tendering)
The CAPEX table shows one contractor per category — DTE for all electrical/plumbing, Daiken for all HVAC, Alberta for all security/systems. No evidence of competitive bids. In Malta's small contractor market, single-sourcing guarantees premium pricing.

**Estimated markup: 20–40% vs. competitive tendering**

### 3. Hotel-Complex Premium
Contractors working within the Fortina Hotel complex charge premium rates due to:
- Access restrictions and scheduling around hotel operations
- Insurance requirements for working in occupied buildings
- Coordination with hotel management
- Premium materials to match hotel finishes

**Estimated markup: 15–25%**

### 4. Probable Change Orders
The EUR 144K electrical/plumbing line suggests significant scope changes during construction. A well-planned M&E installation for this scale should cost EUR 65–80K. The EUR 64–79K excess likely represents unplanned changes, rework, or scope additions.

## NEGOTIATION IMPLICATIONS

### What This Means for the Deal

1. **The seller CANNOT credibly argue "I invested EUR 858K."** They overpaid for the buildout. Their sunk cost is their problem, not the buyer's.

2. **Replacement cost for Carisma is ~EUR 533K** (if building from scratch with Carisma's procurement efficiency). But you're NOT building from scratch — you're acquiring an operating business with the fit-out already in place.

3. **At EUR 220K purchase price, you're getting:**
   - A EUR 468K fit-out (realistic replacement value)
   - EUR 65K of equipment (fair market value)
   - A EUR 426K revenue business with EUR 36.5K EBITDA
   - Total value: EUR 533K+ of hard assets alone
   - **You're paying 41 cents on the dollar of realistic replacement cost**

4. **The "sunk cost fallacy" is the seller's biggest weakness.** They look at EUR 858K invested and think the business must be worth at least that. But the market doesn't pay for overspend — it pays for what those assets produce (EUR 36.5K EBITDA).

### Counter to "But I Spent EUR 858K Building This"

> *"We respect the investment you made in building this beautiful spa. However, the market values businesses on what they earn, not what they cost to build. Your fit-out cost EUR 738K, but our own Novotel spa buildout — similar scope, same Malta market — cost EUR 147K for 4 rooms. Scaled to your 7 rooms, a realistic cost is EUR 400–470K. The EUR 270K+ difference reflects procurement decisions, not asset value. Our offer of EUR 220K is based on what the business actually earns (EUR 36.5K EBITDA at a 6x multiple), not what it cost to build."*

### Counter to "The Equipment Alone is Worth EUR 120K"

> *"The Candela laser has a fair market value of approximately EUR 50K based on used equipment listings. The T-Shape, which is an older-generation body contouring device, has a realistic resale value of EUR 10–15K. Total portable equipment value is EUR 65K. The remaining EUR 670K+ of your investment is embedded in leased walls, floors, plumbing, and HVAC that cannot be removed when the lease ends. Those assets have zero salvage value outside this lease."*
