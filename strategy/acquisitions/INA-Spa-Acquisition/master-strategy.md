|---|---|---|---|

**Each concession:** smaller than last. **Every one:** conditional. **Final number:** non-round (signals precision, not room).

## The Sunk Cost Redirect — Your Core Script

When he says "I put €1M into this place...":

> "I understand completely why you look at what was put into this space and feel the number should reflect that. But the market — any buyer — asks one question: what does this business earn today? Not what it cost to build, not what it could earn — what it earns right now. That's not a judgment of you. It's just how acquisitions are priced."

**The pain gap calculation:**
- He invested: €1,000,000

- Loss already crystallized: **€800,000–850,000 — whether he sells or not**

> "The €800,000 is already gone. Selling today doesn't create that loss — it stops the additional losses from continuing."

## The Four Acts of the Meeting

### Act 1: Intelligence Gathering (First 20-30 min)
**Goal:** Learn everything. Reveal nothing.

**5 essential questions in order:**
1. "What's been the biggest challenge since the Numi situation ended?"
2. "What does the monthly picture look like — is it covering its costs?"
3. "What would a successful resolution look like for you personally?"
4. "What's your ideal timeframe?"
5. "Have you had other conversations with interested parties?"

**Techniques:** Mirroring, labeling, calibrated "what/how" questions. Silence after his answers.

---

### Act 2: Reframe Value (20-30 min)
**Goal:** Redirect from sunk cost to forward earnings. Position equipment appraisal as neutral third party.

**Key moves:**
- Use the sunk cost redirect script (see above)
- Introduce EBITDA framework in plain language
- Propose equipment appraisal: "Let's let the market tell us what it's worth."
- Do NOT yet name any price

---

### Act 3: The Offer (20-30 min)
**Goal:** Drop anchor, justify it, and begin concession sequence.

**The anchor drop script:**
> "Based on what the business is generating today and the investment needed to bring underutilized spaces up — I'm looking at this in the range of €40,000 to €50,000. Let me walk you through how I got there..."

**Then:** Silence. 7 seconds minimum. Do not fill it.

**Then:** Use Ackermann sequence from above. Each move is conditional. Each follows his response.

---

### Act 4: Close or Exit (10-15 min)
**Goal:** Either reach agreement in principle or exit gracefully.

**Trial close:** "It sounds like if we could land somewhere around [TARGET], we'd have a deal. Am I reading that right?"

**Graceful exit:** "I think we see the value differently right now. Let me have my team put together a formal analysis. If after that we're still far apart, we'll know."

**Do NOT close today even if he accepts.** Introduce due diligence process. Slow = power.

## Post-Meeting Protocol

- **Wait 24-48 hours** before follow-up (eagerness transfers power)
- **Send brief email only** — max 3 sentences
- **Introduce accountant/solicitor** for second meeting (formalizes process, slows close)
- **Request financial documentation** within 72 hours if positive
- **Commission independent equipment appraisal** (neutralizes pricing debate)
- **Never close in meeting 1** — due diligence is your buffer and negotiating lever
