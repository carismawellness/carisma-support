## Complete Seller Arguments (All 40)

### TIER 1: STRONGEST ARGUMENTS (Must Counter First)

**1. "I'm selling a clinic platform, not a static spa"**
- Claims clinic synergies justify 6-8x EBITDA multiples
- Argues buyer gets pre-built foundation for clinic launch

**Counter**: Clinic isn't built yet. You own a spa. I'm building the platform. I'm taking all clinic risk (€500K capex, execution risk, client cross-sell unproven). Charge me for a spa. Let me capture clinic upside.

---

**2. "You'd spend €500K-750K to build what I've built"**
- Claims capex avoidance alone justifies premium price
- Implies buyer saves massive time and money by acquiring vs. starting

**Counter**: I'd build smarter for €350K using used equipment, efficient contractors, lean staffing. Your €1M was inefficient capital allocation (7.2% ROI). Don't charge me premium for your poor decisions. Capex avoidance worth maybe €150K, not €300-400K.

---

**3. "I invested €1M; it's embedded in this asset"**
- Reframes sunk cost as embedded value
- Claims equipment, client base, systems, reputation all transfer

**Counter**: Equipment depreciated 60-70%. Fair asset value today: €350-500K. You're asking me to subsidize your €500K loss. Real transferable assets (equipment, location, systems) worth €200-250K. Price accordingly, don't hide loss in premium multiple.

---

**4. "You're paying for 13 years of revenue certainty"**
- 13-year lease locks in location
- Claims this de-risks business significantly vs. alternatives

**Counter**: 13-year lease locks in COSTS, not revenue. It's a LIABILITY for me (I'm locked in even if clinic fails). Should REDUCE price, not increase. Client retention is actually 70-80%, so no "certainty"—expect 20-30% attrition just from ownership change.

---

**5. "You're buying 18 months of development time"**
- Claims time compression from building vs. acquiring has massive value
- Implies buyer loses €250-300K+ in opportunity cost by building from zero

**Counter**: Time value is maybe €100-150K max, not €300-400K. You're charging me €300K+ premium for €150K in actual benefit. And I can accelerate by 40% anyway with capital and focus. Don't double-dip on capex savings AND time value.

---

**6. "Market multiples don't apply—I'm a growth platform"**
- Growth companies trade at 6-8x EBITDA, not 3.5-4.5x
- Claims clinic opportunity justifies growth multiple

**Counter**: Growth multiples require PROVEN growth. You have 0% revenue growth. Clinic synergies are unproven (my innovation, not yours). You get 3.5-4.5x market multiple for static business. If clinic works, I capture that upside. Fair valuation: 4x × €72K = €288K.

---

**7. "I have alternative buyers—hotel chains, medical groups, wellness operators"**
- Implies other buyers would pay premium for strategic value
- Threatens to walk if price isn't right

**Counter**: If real alternatives existed, you'd show me. You haven't. Strategic buyers actually pay LESS (they rebrand, fire staff, integrate differently). Hotels: €200K. Medical groups: €150K. No real alternatives exist—you're bluffing.

---

**8. "I can hold this forever—€72K/year is a living"**
- Establishes strong walk-away position
- Claims confidence in business, not desperation to sell

**Counter**: Right, you CAN hold. So why negotiate down from €650K? Because you want liquidity NOW, not €72K/year for 10 more years. That motivation should push your floor DOWN, not your ask UP. You're motivated to sell. Don't pretend you're not.

---

**9. "Revenue multiple 1.5-2x applies to service businesses like mine"**
- Service businesses trade at 1.5-2x revenue
- €360K revenue × 1.8x = €648K

**Counter**: 1.5-2x applies to HIGH-recurring revenue (90%+ retention). Your spa has 20-30% annual churn—LOW recurring quality. You deserve 0.5-1x revenue multiple, not 1.5-2x. EBITDA multiple is more accurate anyway: 3.5-4.5x × €72K = €250-325K.

---

### TIER 2: SECONDARY ARGUMENTS

**10. "I'm transferring client relationships worth €50-100K"**
- Claims client lifetime value is separate asset
- 500 clients × €5K-10K lifetime value = significant value

**Counter**: Client relationships follow the LOCATION and SERVICE, not you. I'll retain them because the business continues, not because you "transfer" them. This is baked into EBITDA multiples. Don't double-count. If client lists were worth €100K, why haven't you sold them to competitors?

---

**11. "You're getting staff worth €50K+"**
- Trained therapists save hiring/onboarding costs
- Therapists stay under new ownership (implied)

**Counter**: Staff is at-will employment. 20-40% leave when ownership changes. Real hiring cost: €3-5K per person × 5 = €15-25K max. Not €50K. And you can't guarantee retention. If staff is valuable, guarantee it or don't count it.

---

**12. "I'm not charging for transitional services"**
- Implied benefit of 3-month transition period
- Claims this is value add

**Counter**: Transitional services slow me down and create confusion. I'd rather you exit fully so I have clear control. Your presence keeps staff focused on you, not me. This is a COST, not a benefit.

---

**13. "Comparable acquisitions support €600K+ pricing"**
- Claims market comps for spa acquisitions show 5-6x EBITDA
- Uses comps to justify asking price

**Counter**: Comps at 5-6x are for growing businesses or premium locations. Your business has 0% growth and declining margins over time. You get 3.5-4.5x. Even that is premium given the declining trajectory.

---

**14. "This market is undersaturated—scarcity value applies"**
- Only 3-4 established spas in Malta
- Limited supply = premium pricing

**Counter**: Scarcity applies if I have NO alternatives. But I do—I can build from zero. Scarcity doesn't overcome math. Fair valuation is 3.5-4.5x EBITDA. Scarcity might push to top of range (4.5x), not 9x.

2. **Counter the platform argument first** - it's their strongest

4. **Establish scarcity cuts both ways** - you have build alternative

- Wait 24 hours before responding
- Send brief 3-sentence email
- Reference your offer calmly
- Suggest formal due diligence process
- Don't re-negotiate without new info
