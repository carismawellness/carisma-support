**PROCEED WITH ACQUISITION -- CONDITIONAL BUY**

This transaction represents a rare opportunity to consolidate 95%+ of Malta's weight management market, acquire 50 years of brand equity that cannot be replicated, and create a platform for the emerging GLP-1 management opportunity. The buyer's negotiating position is exceptionally strong given the seller's disengagement, absence of competing bidders, and declining operational trajectory under current management. We recommend pursuing this acquisition at EUR 2.0M target (4.0x EBITDA) with a seller-financed structure that can extend nominal consideration to EUR 2.5-2.8M while preserving buyer returns. The GLP-1 disruption risk to the slimming business (15-30% revenue erosion over 3-5 years) is a genuine headwind but is offset by the pivot opportunity into GLP-1 management clinics and the significant synergy value (EUR 1.42M NPV over 10 years).

# 6. TAX OPTIMIZATION

## Malta Corporate Tax Structure

Malta operates a unique full imputation tax system that is one of the most favorable in the EU for tax planning:

| Tax Parameter | Rate | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Corporate income tax (headline) | 35% | Applies to company profits |
| Effective rate (after refund) | 5% | 6/7ths refund on distributed dividends to non-resident/holding company shareholders |
| Capital gains tax | 0-35% | Depends on structure; transfer of business as going concern may qualify for exemptions |
| Stamp duty (share transfer) | 2% | On market value of shares (for Malta-sited assets) |
| Stamp duty (asset transfer) | 5% | On immovable property; 2% on other assets |
| VAT | 18% (standard) | Wellness services standard-rated; some medical exemptions |
| Withholding tax on dividends | 0% | Under participation exemption (if holding company used) |

## Asset Deal vs. Share Deal Analysis

### Asset Deal

| Factor | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Structure** | Buy individual assets (equipment, client list, IP, contracts) |
| **Stamp duty** | 2% on movable assets; 5% on any immovable property |
| **Tax basis step-up** | Full step-up in tax basis; depreciation on purchase price |
| **Goodwill amortization** | Deductible over useful life (typically 10-15 years in Malta) |
| **Liabilities** | Buyer does not assume undisclosed liabilities (clean break) |
| **Contract transfer** | Each contract (hotel leases, supplier agreements) must be individually assigned |
| **VAT** | Transfer of going concern (TOGC) may be VAT-exempt under Article 8 of Malta VAT Act |
| **Estimated tax cost** | EUR 20-40K (stamp duty on EUR 1-2M of assets) |
| **Annual tax benefit** | EUR 7-10K (goodwill amortization deduction of EUR 133-200K/yr for 10 years at 5% effective rate) |

### Share Deal

| Factor | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Structure** | Buy shares of the company that owns the business |
| **Stamp duty** | 2% on market value of shares |
| **Tax basis** | No step-up; historical depreciated values continue |
| **Goodwill** | Not deductible (stays on balance sheet at historical) |
| **Liabilities** | Buyer inherits ALL liabilities (known and unknown) |
| **Contract continuity** | All contracts continue automatically (no re-assignment needed) |
| **VAT** | No VAT on share transfer |
| **Estimated tax cost** | EUR 40-50K (2% stamp duty on EUR 2-2.5M share value) |
| **Due diligence cost** | Higher (must verify all historical liabilities) |

### Recommendation: ASSET DEAL

An asset deal is strongly preferred for the following reasons:

1. **Liability protection:** Buyer does not inherit unknown liabilities from a 50-year-old business that likely has informal arrangements, potential employment claims, or tax issues.

2. **Goodwill amortization:** EUR 1.0-1.5M of purchase price allocable to goodwill, deductible over 10-15 years, saving EUR 7-10K annually at Malta's 5% effective rate. Over 10 years: EUR 70-100K NPV tax savings.

3. **Contract renegotiation opportunity:** Asset deal requires contract assignment, giving buyer the chance to renegotiate hotel lease terms, supplier contracts, and employment agreements.

4. **Clean start:** New entity, fresh books, proper systems from day one.

**Downside:** Hotel lease assignments require landlord consent. This is a condition precedent that must be secured before closing.

# 7. NEGOTIATION STRATEGY

## Ackermann Negotiation Model (5 Rounds)

The Ackermann model uses diminishing concessions to anchor expectations and signal firmness. We establish the target at EUR 2,000K and construct the sequence to converge at that level.

### Pre-Negotiation Intelligence

| Factor | Assessment | Impact |
|---|---|---|
| **Seller motivation** | Petra is disengaged, fed up, and wants out | Strong buyer leverage |
| **Competing offers** | None | No competitive pressure on buyer |
| **Seller initiated?** | No -- buyer approached via accountant | Buyer controls pace |
| **Financial urgency** | None apparent (business still generates cash) | Moderate -- no fire sale |
| **Emotional attachment** | Mixed -- family legacy, but Petra not the founder | Can be leveraged both ways |
| **Information asymmetry** | Buyer has limited verified data; seller holds the books | Risk for buyer until due diligence |
| **Time pressure** | None on either side | Buyer should not rush |

### Five-Round Sequence

**Round 1: Opening Bid**
- **Offer: EUR 1,500,000** (3.0x EBITDA)
- Framing: "Based on our analysis of comparable transactions in the European wellness sector, and accounting for the GLP-1 disruption risk to the slimming business, current market multiples for owner-dependent wellness businesses range from 2.5-3.5x EBITDA. Our opening position reflects the midpoint of that range."
- Present comparable transactions showing 3.0-4.0x range for similar businesses.
- Expect seller counter: EUR 2,500,000 - EUR 3,000,000 (5.0-6.0x)
- Purpose: Anchor low. Establish data-driven negotiation tone.

**Round 2: Counter (+20% of total concession budget)**
- **Offer: EUR 1,700,000** (+EUR 200K from Round 1)
- Framing: "We recognize the value of the 50-year brand and the 4,900-client base. We've increased our offer by EUR 200,000 to reflect these intangibles. However, the GLP-1 risk and the need for EUR 200-400K in equipment modernization remain significant concerns."
- Add: "We would also consider a seller financing structure at a higher headline number if that's important to you."
- Expect seller counter: EUR 2,300,000 - EUR 2,500,000
- Purpose: Show willingness to move, but link every concession to a specific value driver.

**Round 3: Counter (+10% of total concession budget)**
- **Offer: EUR 1,850,000** (+EUR 150K from Round 2)
- Framing: "We've now increased our offer by EUR 350,000 from our opening position. We've incorporated value for the brand, the hotel relationships, and the operational platform. But our due diligence concerns around unverified EBITDA and the equipment condition limit how much further we can go."
- Add earnout language: "We could structure an additional EUR 150,000 as a performance-based earnout tied to EBITDA maintenance."
- Expect seller counter: EUR 2,200,000 - EUR 2,400,000
- Purpose: Diminishing concessions signal approaching limit. Earnout introduces creative deal structure.

**Round 4: Counter (+5% of total concession budget)**
- **Offer: EUR 1,950,000 + EUR 150,000 earnout** (EUR 2,100K total potential)
- Framing: "Petra, I want to be transparent. This is very close to our maximum. We've been in discussions for some time, and I want to find a way to make this work for both of us. The EUR 150K earnout gives you an additional EUR 2.1M total if the business performs as your accountant suggests."
- If seller financing is acceptable: "Alternatively, we could structure this as EUR 1.2M upfront plus EUR 1.0M over 5 years, giving you EUR 2.2M total."
- Expect seller counter: EUR 2,100,000 - EUR 2,200,000 (converging)
- Purpose: Create urgency. Show flexibility on structure, firmness on value.

**Round 5: Final Offer (Odd Number)**
- **Offer: EUR 2,017,500** (or with seller financing: EUR 2,200,000 50/50 over 5 years)
- Framing: "This is our final offer. EUR 2,017,500 reflects our complete analysis -- every methodology, every risk factor, every opportunity. The odd number reflects the precision of our valuation work, not a round number we picked arbitrarily. We believe this is fair for both parties."
- If rejected: "We respect your decision. Our offer remains open for 14 days. After that, we will need to reassess our capital allocation priorities."
- Purpose: Odd number signals precision and finality. Time limit creates gentle urgency without desperation.
