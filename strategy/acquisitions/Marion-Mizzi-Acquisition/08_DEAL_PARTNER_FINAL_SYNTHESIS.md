## 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

### Verdict: CONDITIONAL BUY

**Recommended at EUR 1,750K or below. Walk away above EUR 2,000K.**

Marion Mizzi Wellbeing represents a strategically compelling but execution-intensive acquisition for Carisma Wellness Group. The target offers an established 30-year brand, a diversified revenue base across spa, slimming, and aesthetics verticals, three leased retail locations in Malta, and a loyal client base of approximately 3,000-4,000 active customers. The deal would consolidate Carisma's position in Malta's fragmented wellness market and accelerate entry into medical aesthetics — a segment growing at 12-18% annually.

However, this is not a simple bolt-on. The target faces structural headwinds from GLP-1 pharmaceutical disruption to its slimming business, volume decline masked by price increases, key-person dependency on the founder, and lease vulnerability across all three locations. The buyer — Carisma Wellness Group — is a first-time acquirer with no M&A track record, which introduces material execution risk reflected in a 21% cost of equity.

**The investment thesis rests on three pillars:**

| Pillar | Description | Confidence |
|--------|-------------|------------|
| **Market consolidation** | Post-acquisition HHI of 5,694-6,676 creates defensible position in weight management | 75% |
| **Synergy capture** | EUR 228K annual risk-adjusted synergies (NPV EUR 1.67M at 17% blended rate) | 65% |
| **Aesthetics pivot** | Medical aesthetics repositioning generates EUR 2.49M revenue by Year 5 (base case) | 55% |

**Key numbers at a glance:**

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| **Unified fair value range** | EUR 1,350K — 2,150K |
| **Recommended target price** | EUR 1,750K |
| **Opening offer** | EUR 1,350K |
| **Hard walk-away** | EUR 2,000K |
| **Expected IRR at target price** | ~19.5% |
| **Hurdle rate (derived)** | 20.2% |
| **Overall risk score** | 7.2/10 (HIGH) |
| **Total cost of ownership (5 years)** | EUR 3,890K — 4,505K |
| **Tax savings (Structure A, 10 years)** | EUR 1,370K |
| **Timeline to close** | 16-18 weeks |
| **Minimum cash reserve at close** | EUR 327K |

**The conditional nature of this recommendation is critical.** The deal is attractive only if:

1. Price does not exceed EUR 2,000K (above which median IRR falls to 11%, below hurdle)
2. At least 3 of 5 leases are secured for 5+ years pre-close
3. MCCAA clearance is obtained or a credible defense strategy is in place
4. Seller commits to 24-month transition with meaningful earnout linkage
5. Buyer secures EUR 300-500K revolving credit facility pre-close

If any of these conditions cannot be met, the recommendation reverts to **NO BUY**.

## 2. RESOLVED INCONSISTENCIES

The Critic Review (Report 07) identified five material cross-report inconsistencies. Each has been resolved through the addendum process. Below is the definitive reconciliation.

### Inconsistency 1: Valuation Range Dispersion

**The Problem:** Original reports produced wildly different ranges — BCG implied EUR 1.5-2.5M, Goldman Sachs EUR 1.4-2.3M, Medical Aesthetics EUR 1.8-3.0M, M&A Specialist EUR 1.3-2.0M. The spread was EUR 1.7M from lowest to highest bound.

**Root Cause:** Different EBITDA baselines, inconsistent discount rates, and the Medical Aesthetics team pricing in a bull-case growth trajectory as their base case.

**Resolution:**
- WACC unified at **21%** across all analyses (Goldman Sachs Addendum 02A), incorporating a 2.5% acquirer inexperience premium that was missing from original work
- Medical Aesthetics revenue of EUR 3.5M reclassified from base to **bull case (25% probability)**; base case set at EUR 2.49M (Addendum 03A)
- Precedent transactions **removed entirely** from the football field — no valid Malta wellness comparables exist (Addendum 02A)
- EBITDA baseline reconciled at EUR 494K normalized (FY2024), with acknowledgment that volume is declining 10-20% from peak, masked by 4-6% annual price increases (Addendum 04A)

**Unified range:** EUR 1,350K — 2,150K (midpoint EUR 1,750K)
**Status:** FULLY RESOLVED

---

### Inconsistency 2: Synergy Estimates

**The Problem:** BCG projected EUR 444K gross annual synergies; M&A Specialist estimated EUR 280-350K; Goldman Sachs used EUR 400K in their models. No report provided a consistent risk-adjusted figure.

**Root Cause:** Different inclusion criteria (gross vs. net), different realization timelines, and no shared methodology for probability-weighting.

**Resolution:**
- Gross synergies validated at **EUR 444K** annually (BCG methodology confirmed)
- Risk-adjusted synergies set at **EUR 228K** annually after applying realization probabilities: 40% Year 1, 70% Year 2, 85% Year 3 (Addendum 01A — revised down from original 60/80/95%)
- Synergy NPV at 17% blended discount rate = **EUR 1.67M** (Addendum 01A)
- Critical qualifier: Synergies require **dedicated integration manager** (EUR 55-75K/year) plus EUR 85-125K therapist retention spend — these costs were underestimated by 2-3x in original reports

**Unified figure:** EUR 228K annual risk-adjusted; NPV EUR 1.67M
**Status:** FULLY RESOLVED

---

### Inconsistency 3: GLP-1 Impact Timing and Severity

**The Problem:** BCG modeled GLP-1 impact beginning Year 3; Medical Aesthetics assumed Year 2; Risk Analyst showed it already affecting customer acquisition in 2025. Cost estimates for the pivot ranged from EUR 38K (Medical Aesthetics original) to EUR 270K (Medical Aesthetics Addendum).

**Root Cause:** GLP-1 drugs are already available in Malta via private prescription. The original reports assumed regulatory lag that does not exist.

**Resolution:**
- GLP-1 impact timeline: **already underway**, with material revenue effect beginning **Year 1-2** (Addendum 06A)
- Year 1 pivot cost: **EUR 173-270K** (4.6-7.1x higher than original EUR 38K estimate) (Addendum 03A)
- Total 5-year transformation CapEx: **EUR 727K — 1,020K** (vs. EUR 448K original) (Addendum 03A)
- Probability-weighted revenue impact: competitive response reduces base case Year 5 revenue by 7-11% (Addendum 03A, Scenario B at 55% probability)
- Slimming client segmentation reveals 20-25% are slimming-only clients at highest churn risk; multi-service clients (25-30%) show higher retention

**Unified position:** GLP-1 is a present and accelerating threat requiring immediate pivot planning and EUR 173-270K Year 1 investment. The pivot is manageable but cannot be deferred.
**Status:** FULLY RESOLVED

---

### Inconsistency 4: EBITDA Baseline and Sustainability

**The Problem:** Reports used different EBITDA figures — EUR 494K (normalized FY2024), EUR 460K (adjusted), EUR 520K (pre-restructuring). No report adequately addressed whether current EBITDA is sustainable given volume decline.

**Root Cause:** Incomplete adjustment for one-off items and failure to separate price-driven from volume-driven growth.

**Resolution:**
- Normalized EBITDA: **EUR 494K** (FY2024), confirmed as appropriate baseline (Addendum 04A)
- Volume trend: **declining 10-20% from peak**, masked by 4-6% annual price increases (Addendum 04A)
- Sustainability assessment: Current EBITDA is **not fully sustainable** without the acquisition. Price increases will hit a ceiling; volume decline will accelerate with GLP-1 adoption
- Forward EBITDA: Year 1 post-acquisition likely **EUR 420-460K** (integration disruption + transition costs), recovering to EUR 500K+ only with successful synergy capture

**Unified position:** Use EUR 494K as the trailing baseline but model forward from EUR 440K (midpoint of disrupted range) for conservative projections.
**Status:** FULLY RESOLVED

---

### Inconsistency 5: Recommended Deal Structure

**The Problem:** Goldman Sachs recommended 60/40 deferred payments; M&A Specialist recommended pure earnout; BCG was silent on structure; Legal recommended asset purchase but did not specify payment mix.

**Root Cause:** Each expert optimized for their domain (tax efficiency, risk allocation, integration flexibility) without cross-referencing.

**Resolution:**
- Structure: **Asset purchase + Malta holdco** (Legal/Tax optimal — Structure A) (Addendum 05A)
- Payment mix: **60% cash / 25% seller note / 15% earnout** (Addendum 04A)
- Seller note: 3-year term at 3.5% interest (below market 4.5-5.5% = hidden EUR 12,750 annual discount) with revenue-based covenant (Addendum 04A)
- Earnout: tied to client retention (>80% at 12 months) and revenue maintenance, payable at 18 and 36 months
- Tax savings from Structure A: **EUR 1.37M over 10 years** via 6/7ths Malta refund mechanism (Addendum 05A)
- Asset purchase enables cherry-picking of contracts and clean liability separation

**Unified structure:** Asset purchase through Malta holdco, 60/25/15 cash/note/earnout split
**Status:** FULLY RESOLVED

## 4. UNIFIED DEAL STRUCTURE

### 4.1 Recommended Structure

**Asset Purchase through Malta Holding Company (Structure A)**

```
                    ┌─────────────────┐
                    │  Carisma Group   │
                    │  (Existing)      │
                    └────────┬────────┘
                             │ 100% ownership
                    ┌────────▼────────┐
                    │  Malta HoldCo    │
                    │  (New entity)    │
                    └────────┬────────┘
                             │ Asset purchase
                    ┌────────▼────────┐
                    │  Marion Mizzi    │
                    │  Assets + Brand  │
                    │  + Contracts     │
                    └─────────────────┘
```

**Rationale:** Asset purchase enables selective acquisition of contracts, clean liability separation, and optimal tax treatment. Malta HoldCo unlocks the 6/7ths tax refund mechanism, saving EUR 1.37M over 10 years versus alternative structures.

### 4.2 Payment Structure

| Component | Amount | % of Price | Terms |
|-----------|--------|------------|-------|
| Cash at close | EUR 1,050K | 60% | Funded from operations + bank facility |
| Seller note | EUR 437.5K | 25% | 3-year term, 3.5% interest, revenue covenant |
| Earnout | EUR 262.5K | 15% | Tied to client retention + revenue targets |
| **Total at EUR 1,750K** | **EUR 1,750K** | **100%** | |

### 4.3 Seller Note Terms

- **Principal:** 25% of final purchase price
- **Interest rate:** 3.5% per annum (below market rate of 4.5-5.5% — represents hidden EUR 12,750/year discount to buyer)
- **Term:** 36 months, interest-only first 12 months, then equal amortization
- **Revenue covenant:** If trailing 12-month revenue falls below 85% of baseline, principal payments defer by 6 months (no acceleration)
- **Security:** Second lien on acquired assets (subordinated to bank facility)
- **Prepayment:** Permitted without penalty after 18 months

### 4.4 Earnout Terms

- **Tranche 1 (Month 18):** 50% of earnout pool if client retention exceeds 80% at 12-month anniversary
- **Tranche 2 (Month 36):** 50% of earnout pool if Year 2 revenue maintains 90% of baseline
- **Measurement:** Independent review by agreed accountant; disputes resolved by arbitration
- **Cap:** Earnout cannot exceed 15% of purchase price (no upside sharing)
- **Floor:** Zero (earnout is genuinely at-risk)
- **Acceleration:** Full earnout payable on change of control within 36 months

### 4.5 Debt Capacity and Funding

| Source | Amount | Notes |
|--------|--------|-------|
| Cash from operations | EUR 650-800K | Existing Carisma cash + 6 months of cash generation |
| Bank term loan | EUR 350-450K | Secured against acquired assets; max 2.5x EBITDA |
| Revolving credit facility | EUR 300-500K | Pre-arranged for working capital / contingencies |
| **Total available** | **EUR 1,300-1,750K** | Covers cash-at-close component |

**Debt reality check (Addendum 02A):** Realistically available bank debt is EUR 700-900K (not the EUR 1,200K some original models assumed). The 60% cash component at EUR 1,750K requires EUR 1,050K — achievable but tight. At EUR 2,000K, cash at close rises to EUR 1,200K and strains funding.

### 4.6 Tax Optimization

| Structure | 10-Year Total Tax | Savings vs. Structure C |
|-----------|-------------------|-------------------------|
| **A: Asset purchase + Malta HoldCo** | **EUR 228K** | **EUR 1,370K** |
| B: Share purchase + Malta HoldCo | EUR 612K | EUR 984K |
| C: Direct acquisition (no HoldCo) | EUR 1,596K | Baseline |

Structure A delivers EUR 1.37M in tax savings over 10 years through the Malta 6/7ths refund mechanism. This alone improves IRR by approximately 3-4 percentage points. Implementation requires Malta HoldCo formation pre-close (4-6 weeks).

### 5.3 The Hurdle Rate Problem

The derived hurdle rate of 20.2% (Addendum 06A) slightly exceeds the expected IRR at our target price of EUR 1,750K (19.5%). This creates an apparent contradiction. Resolution:

1. **Tax savings not fully reflected:** Structure A's EUR 1.37M tax savings over 10 years add approximately 3-4pp to after-tax IRR, bringing effective return above hurdle
2. **Synergy NPV provides margin:** EUR 1.67M synergy NPV at 17% blended rate adds EUR 0.95/EUR invested at target price
3. **Strategic value not captured in IRR:** Market consolidation (HHI 5,694-6,676) creates pricing power and defensive moat not reflected in cash flow projections
4. **Exit multiple upside:** Base exit at 5-8x EBITDA in 5-7 years; if aesthetics pivot succeeds, medical aesthetics businesses trade at 8-12x

**Net assessment:** The deal marginally clears the hurdle on a risk-adjusted, after-tax, synergy-inclusive basis at EUR 1,750K. At EUR 1,500K it clearly clears. At EUR 2,000K it clearly does not.

### 5.4 The "Do Nothing" Comparator

This is the most important benchmarking exercise. From Addendum 06A:

- **Organic growth IRR:** Investing the same EUR 1,750K into expanding existing Carisma operations yields an estimated 65%+ IRR on incremental investment
- **Why the acquisition still makes sense:** Organic growth has capacity constraints (limited locations, talent pool, brand awareness ceiling in Malta). The acquisition provides step-change capacity that cannot be replicated organically within 3-5 years
- **The honest answer:** If Carisma has unlimited organic growth runway, the acquisition is suboptimal. The case for acquisition rests on the premise that organic growth is approaching its natural ceiling

**Board must confirm:** Does the organic growth thesis have capacity constraints that justify acquisition? If not, the rational choice is to invest organically.

### 5.5 Solvency Stress Test

From Addendum 06A — buyer solvency under adverse conditions:

The -45% dual underperformance scenario (both Carisma existing + Marion Mizzi underperforming by 45%) represents the point at which the combined entity's survival is threatened. This scenario has an estimated probability of 2-4%.

**Required safeguard:** EUR 327K minimum cash reserve at close + EUR 300-500K pre-arranged revolving credit facility.

## 10. FINAL RECOMMENDATION

### 10.1 The Verdict

**CONDITIONAL BUY at EUR 1,750K or below.**

This acquisition is strategically sound but financially tight. The deal works if — and only if — the price stays below EUR 2,000K, integration is executed with discipline, and the GLP-1 pivot is pursued proactively.

### 10.2 The Case FOR

1. **Strategic consolidation:** Creates the dominant wellness platform in Malta with a post-acquisition HHI of 5,694-6,676 in weight management. This market position is worth defending.

2. **Synergy value is real:** EUR 228K annual risk-adjusted synergies with NPV of EUR 1.67M at 17% blended rate. Even at 50% realization, synergies justify a EUR 200K premium over standalone value.

3. **Tax optimization is substantial:** EUR 1.37M saved over 10 years through Structure A. This is not speculative — it is a mechanical consequence of Malta's tax refund system applied correctly.

4. **Timing is right:** Waiting 12-18 months destroys EUR 556K in net value (Addendum 06A). GLP-1 headwinds mean the target is worth less tomorrow than today. The seller's BATNA weakens with each quarter of pharmaceutical disruption.

5. **Aesthetics pivot creates upside:** Medical aesthetics growing 12-18% annually. Base case EUR 2.49M Year 5 revenue; bull case EUR 3.5M. This segment has higher margins and lower pharmaceutical disruption risk than slimming.

6. **The alternative (waiting) is irrational:** Quantified option value analysis shows waiting costs EUR 866K in value while saving only EUR 310K in risk reduction. The mathematics favor acting now.

### 10.3 The Case AGAINST

1. **Returns are marginal:** Expected IRR of 17.8% (probability-weighted) falls below the derived hurdle rate of 20.2%. The deal only clears the hurdle with tax optimization and synergy credit. If either underperforms, returns are subpar.

2. **First-time buyer premium is real:** Carisma has never done an acquisition. Case study evidence (Athens: actual 14% vs. projected 19%; Cyprus: actual 9% vs. projected 22%) shows first-time acquirers consistently underperform projections by 5-13 percentage points.

3. **GLP-1 is an accelerating threat:** The pivot cost (EUR 173-270K Year 1, EUR 727K-1.02M total) is 4.6-7.1x higher than originally estimated. If the pivot fails, the slimming business — currently 20-25% of revenue — erodes faster than modeled.

4. **Organic growth may be superior:** Investing EUR 1,750K in existing Carisma operations yields an estimated 65%+ IRR. The acquisition must be justified on strategic grounds (capacity ceiling, market position) rather than pure financial returns.

5. **Concentration risk:** Combined entity in Malta's small market creates dependency. A single regulatory change, new competitor, or economic downturn affects both legacy and acquired operations simultaneously.

6. **Total cost of ownership is 5.1x EBITDA:** The headline 3.5x acquisition multiple obscures the true economic cost when integration, transformation, and contingencies are included.

### 10.4 Conditions for GO

All five conditions must be satisfied. Any single failure reverts the recommendation to NO BUY.
