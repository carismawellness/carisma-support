# Template: Complaint Acknowledgement (Initial Response)

**When to Use:** First response to a complaint or upset customer
**Channel:** WhatsApp, Email (match their channel)
**Tone:** Serious, caring, taking full ownership

**CRITICAL RULE:** Sign off with "With care, Sarah" NOT "Peacefully, Sarah"

---

## Template Text

Hi [CUSTOMER_NAME],

I've read your message carefully, and I want you to know that what you've shared is being taken seriously.

[SPECIFIC_ACKNOWLEDGEMENT: Reflect back what they described in one sentence, showing you listened]

That's not the experience we work to deliver, and I'm genuinely sorry it happened.

---

**Here's what happens next:**

1. I'm escalating this to our [MANAGER/DIRECTOR] today
2. They'll review exactly what you've described
3. You'll hear from someone in leadership within [TIMEFRAME: 24 hours / by end of business / tomorrow]
4. We'll discuss what happened and how to make this right

**In the meantime:**
If there's anything I should know to help us understand this fully, I'm here to listen. [OPTIONAL: "Can you tell me a bit more about [SPECIFIC_THING]?"]

---

**You have my word:**
We take complaints seriously because you matter. This isn't about brushing this away — it's about understanding and doing better.

I'm sorry you had this experience.

With care,
Sarah

---

## How to Use This Template

1. **Fill in all [PLACEHOLDERS]:**
   - [CUSTOMER_NAME] = "Maria"
   - [SPECIFIC_ACKNOWLEDGEMENT] = "You came for a massage and felt rushed through the treatment" (REFLECT BACK THEIR EXACT ISSUE)
   - [TIMEFRAME] = "24 hours"
   - [OPTIONAL_SPECIFIC_THING] = "how the therapist treated you" or "what happened during checkout"

2. **The [SPECIFIC_ACKNOWLEDGEMENT] is CRITICAL:**
   - Read their complaint
   - Write back in one sentence what you heard (show you listened)
   - Use their emotional language: if they said "dismissed," say "dismissed" back
   - If they said "rushed," say "rushed" back

3. **✏️ Personalise before sending:**
   - Match their energy (if casual, be casual; if formal, be formal)
   - Add warmth without minimizing: "I know this matters to you, and it matters to us too"
   - Don't be defensive — absorb the complaint fully

4. **Send** immediately (within 1-2 hours of their complaint)

---

## Variations

### For Mild Complaint
Soften slightly: "Thank you for being honest with us — that takes courage. I want to understand what happened so we can learn."

### For Severe/Threatening Complaint
Add escalation: "I'm bringing our director in immediately. Someone from leadership will contact you by phone within [HOURS] to discuss how we make this right."

### For Service Complaint (Food, Facilities, Booking Error)
Adjust acknowledgement: "Your experience with [SPECIFIC SERVICE] fell short, and that's entirely on us."

### For Therapist/Staff Complaint (Sensitive Topic)
Handle with extra care: "What you experienced during your treatment was not okay. We take this seriously, and this will be handled with the proper care it deserves."

### If Customer Wants Refund
Don't commit yet: "I hear that a refund matters to you. Let's have the conversation with [MANAGER] so you get the response you deserve — not a quick one, but a considered one."

---

## Critical Do's

✅ Read their complaint twice before responding
✅ Reflect back their specific issue (show you listened)
✅ Apologize genuinely (not "sorry if...")
✅ Escalate immediately (don't handle alone)
✅ Give a clear timeline for next contact
✅ Offer to listen more if they want
✅ Sign off with "With care, Sarah" NOT "Peacefully, Sarah"
✅ Send within 1-2 hours maximum

---

## Critical Don'ts

❌ Don't defend or explain ("actually, what happened was...")
❌ Don't repeat "sorry" over and over (sounds hollow)
❌ Don't make promises you can't keep ("we'll refund immediately")
❌ Don't ask them to prove their case ("exactly what happened?")
❌ Don't minimize ("it's not that big a deal")
❌ Don't rush to solve it alone (escalate first)
❌ Don't end with "Peacefully, Sarah" (wrong sign-off)

---

## Follow-Up

After this acknowledgement:
- Manager/director takes over within stated timeframe
- They dig into what happened
- They present solution/offer
- They follow up until customer feels heard
