### 12. 🔴 [SUPPORT] Complaint Handler
**File:** `skills/complaint-handler.md`
**Query Coverage:** 3–5% of conversations (upset customers)
**Trigger Phrases:** (Auto-detected)
- "Disgusted", "ridiculous", "unacceptable", "never again"
- "I want a refund"
- "Demand", "terrible", "awful", "complaint"
- "Disappointed", "shocked", "furious", "outraged"
- ALL-CAPS, multiple exclamation marks

**Complaint Severity Levels:**
1. **Mild** — Quiet disappointment ("I was a little disappointed...")
2. **Moderate** — Genuine upset ("This is not acceptable")
3. **Severe** — Furious / threatening ("I want a refund immediately")

**Framework:**
1. Absorb (read fully, identify what went wrong + expectations + emotions)
2. Validate (name specific emotion genuinely)
3. Own (take ownership of experience gap)
4. Resolve (concrete next step + timeline + owner)
5. Restore (reconnect to original promise, leave relationship intact)

**Resolution Toolkit (Tiered):**
- **Tier 1:** Acknowledgment + apology (all levels)
- **Tier 2:** Manager involvement (Moderate+)
- **Tier 3:** Goodwill gesture (Moderate+) — complimentary treatment / credit / return visit
- **Tier 4:** Financial resolution (Severe, when appropriate) — partial or full refund

**Key Features:**
- 12 scripts covering 12 key complaint scenarios
- Script categories: mild disappointment, moderate upset, severe anger, refund requests (approved/denied), negative review threats, therapist complaints, facility complaints, admin complaints, recovery offers, post-resolution warmth
- What NOT to do: defensive explanations, over-explaining, asking them to prove case, offering compensation before feeling heard, multiple apologies without action
- CRITICAL: Sign-off is "With care, Sarah" — NEVER "Peacefully, Sarah"

**Research Foundation:** Jeff Toister (service recovery paradox), HEAT model (Hear, Empathize, Apologize, Take Action), John Gottman (emotional de-escalation, feeling-naming)

**Sample Scripts:**
- "Thank you for sharing" opening (all levels)
- "What you described is not acceptable" ownership
- "I want someone from leadership to speak with you directly" escalation offer
- "We are going to issue a refund" resolution + apology beyond refund
- "We aren't able to offer a refund, but..." alternative recovery

### 13. 🔵 [KB] Update Knowledge Base
**File:** `skills/update-knowledge.md`
**Query Coverage:** 5% of conversations (knowledge updates)
**Trigger Phrases:**
- "Add this to the knowledge base: [info]"
- "Note this: [info]"
- "Can you remember this? [info]"
- "I learned that [info]"
- "The [X] has changed — [info]"
- "Update the [file] with: [info]"
- "Flag this as incorrect: [info]"
- "Just to let you know, [info]"
- "Actually, [contradicting something]: [info]"

**Two-Tier System:**
1. **Tier 1: shared-inbox.md** (Unverified — fast capture)
   - Format: `[AGENT UPDATE – name – YYYY-MM-DD] information`
   - Agent contribution → provisional flagging → manager review

2. **Tier 2: shared-knowledge.md** (Verified — trusted source)
   - Format: `[VERIFIED – name – YYYY-MM-DD] information`
   - Manager-confirmed facts → Claude treats as truth

**Process:**
1. Agent says "add this to KB"
2. Claude appends to shared-inbox.md with [AGENT UPDATE] tag
3. Claude flags provisional when used: "⚠️ Verify in Fresha before sending — inbox entry"
4. Manager reviews → promotes to shared-knowledge.md when verified

**Key Features:**
- Fast knowledge capture without false certainty
- Explicit provisional flagging (not ambiguous about unverified status)
- Contributor-Approver-Consumer model (separates roles)
- Single source of truth architecture (one canonical location)
- Never deletes — flags incorrect entries for review

**Research Foundation:** Knowledge management research (ServiceNow, Knowmax), Atlassian KM architecture, contributor-approver separation principle

**Sample Process:**
- Agent: "The Sunday price at Hyatt changed to €90"
- Claude: Appends to shared-inbox.md
- Claude: Uses it but flags "⚠️ Verify in Fresha before sending"
- Manager: Reviews, confirms, adds to shared-knowledge.md

## Brand Voice Consistency Across All Skills

**Unified Rules (Enforced in Every Script):**

✅ **DO:**
- Short, poetic sentences
- Second-person "you"
- Sensory words: warmth, stillness, breath, melt, restore, return, pause
- Emotional transformation focus
- Simplicity + elegance
- Honest, purposeful tone

❌ **DON'T:**
- "Certainly!", "I'd be happy to", "Of course!", "Absolutely!", "Feel free to"
- "Pamper yourself", "Treat yourself", "Hurry!", "Limited time!", "Don't miss out!"
- "We offer the best", "Amazing", "Fantastic", "Book now!"
- Technical jargon: myofascial release, lymphatic drainage
- Generic spa language applicable to any spa

**Sign-Off Variations:**
- **Standard:** "Peacefully, Sarah"

## Support & Troubleshooting

**Question:** How do I use a skill?
**Answer:** Type skill name in [brackets] — e.g., [consult-and-pitch]. Or let Claude auto-suggest when situation matches trigger phrases.

**Question:** What if Claude's draft doesn't sound like Sarah?
**Answer:** Check config/sarah-phrases.md and rewrite to match voice. Message-quality-check hook will flag AI-tells automatically.

**Question:** I learned something new — what do I do?
**Answer:** Say "Add this to the knowledge base: [info]" — Claude appends to shared-inbox.md for manager verification.

**Question:** A customer is angry — what do I do?
**Answer:** Complaint-handler auto-activates on negative sentiment. Follow the "With care, Sarah" sign-off rule (not "Peacefully, Sarah").

**Question:** How do I know if I'm doing this right?
**Answer:** Read onboard.md for 5-min walkthrough. Practice the 3 scenarios. Check your drafts against brand voice rules.
