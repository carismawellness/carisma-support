### Room-by-Room Specifications

#### ROOM A: Laser & Body Sculpting Room (10 sqm)

**Function:** Houses the two existing medical devices (Candela laser hair removal, T-Shape 2-Tone body sculpting) on a rotating schedule. This room is the primary revenue engine from Day 1.

**Equipment:**
- Candela GentleMax Pro (or equivalent) -- already owned
- T-Shape 2-Tone body sculpting device -- already owned
- Adjustable treatment bed with paper roll dispenser
- Protective eyewear station (laser safety)
- Skin cooling unit (if not integrated into Candela)
- Wall-mounted sharps container

**Special Requirements:**
- Laser safety: no windows or windows with opaque blinds/covers; laser warning light above door; Class 4 laser signage; interlock switch on door
- Electrical: dedicated 20A circuit for Candela; separate 16A circuit for T-Shape; surge protection on both
- Ventilation: mechanical extraction for plume evacuation during laser treatments (essential for Malta Health Authority compliance); minimum 6 air changes per hour
- Flooring: sealed, non-porous surface (vinyl or epoxy) for infection control
- Temperature: maintained at 20-22C (both patient comfort and device calibration)

**Revenue Capacity:**
- Laser hair removal: 6-8 treatments/day at EUR 80-150 per session (Malta pricing) = EUR 480-1,200/day
- T-Shape body sculpting: 3-4 treatments/day at EUR 120-200 per session = EUR 360-800/day
- Blended daily capacity (alternating devices): EUR 600-1,000/day
- Monthly (22 working days): EUR 13,200-22,000
- Utilization target Year 1: 50-60% = EUR 7,900-13,200/month

#### ROOM B: Aesthetics Suite (12 sqm)

**Function:** Multi-purpose treatment room for injectables, skin rejuvenation, microneedling, chemical peels, and PRP. This room generates the highest average ticket and requires the most clinical infrastructure.

**Equipment:**
- Hydraulic treatment chair (reclines fully for injectable procedures)
- LED ring light / surgical magnification lamp
- Microneedling device (Dermapen 4 or SkinPen Precision)
- Chemical peel workstation (lockable storage for glycolic, TCA, phenol peels)

**Special Requirements:**
- Plumbing: clinical hand wash basin with elbow-operated or sensor taps (reuse existing salon wash station plumbing runs)
- Storage: lockable cabinet for Schedule 4 substances (Botox, hyaluronidase)
- Lighting: overhead LED panel (5000K daylight) plus adjustable task lighting
- Ventilation: standard mechanical ventilation; chemical peel procedures require fume extraction hood or equivalent
- Waste: clinical waste bin (yellow) and sharps container; pharmaceutical waste collection
- Electrical: minimum 4 double sockets on separate circuits

**Revenue Capacity:**

#### ROOM D: Consultation & Pre-Treatment Room (8 sqm)

**Function:** Every clinical patient begins here. Medical history review, consent forms, before/after photography, treatment planning. Also serves as the practitioner's private workspace between treatments.

**Equipment:**

- Body composition analyzer (InBody or equivalent)

**Special Requirements:**
- Acoustic privacy: sound-dampened partition walls (patients discuss medical history)
- No special ventilation or plumbing beyond standard
- Electrical: standard office-grade
- This room does not directly generate treatment revenue but is essential for clinical workflow, upselling, and patient safety documentation

#### ROOM E: Sterilization & Storage (6 sqm)

**Function:** Central decontamination, sterilization, and medical supply storage. This room ensures regulatory compliance and separates clean/dirty workflows.

**Equipment:**
- Autoclave (Class B, 18-23 litre capacity for microneedling cartridge holders, reusable instruments)
- Ultrasonic cleaner (pre-sterilization decontamination)
- Clinical waste bins (yellow clinical, orange pharmaceutical, black general)
- Stainless steel work surfaces (clean side / dirty side separation)
- Shelving for consumables (needles, syringes, gloves, swabs, IV tubing, peels)
- Lockable pharmaceutical storage (temperature-monitored)
- PPE station (gloves, masks, gowns, eye protection)

**Special Requirements:**
- Ventilation: extract fan (autoclave generates steam)
- Plumbing: clinical-grade sink with elbow taps for instrument washing
- Electrical: dedicated circuit for autoclave (high wattage draw)
- This room does not generate revenue but is a regulatory requirement for any facility performing invasive procedures

## 5. REGULATORY REQUIREMENTS (MALTA)

### Facility Licensing

**Malta Medicines Authority (MMA):** Medical devices (Candela laser, T-Shape, microneedling devices, RF devices) fall under EU Medical Device Regulation 2017/745, transposed into Maltese law under S.L. 458.59. Devices must be CE-marked and registered. The facility operating these devices needs notification to the MMA. Class IIb and above devices (which includes most aesthetic lasers) require the facility to maintain a device log, incident reporting protocol, and evidence of operator training.

**Superintendent of Public Health:** Any establishment offering injectable treatments (Botox, fillers, PRP, IV drips) must be registered with the Superintendent of Public Health. This requires: a named medical director (licensed physician registered with the Malta Medical Council), premises inspection by Environmental Health Directorate officers, and compliance with infection control standards.

**Environmental Health Directorate:** Will inspect the premises for: adequate ventilation, clinical waste disposal arrangements (contract with licensed waste handler), sterilization equipment and protocols, hand washing facilities, clean/dirty workflow separation, temperature-controlled pharmaceutical storage.

### Practitioner Requirements

| Treatment Category | Who Can Perform | Who Must Supervise | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Laser hair removal | Trained therapist/nurse | Medical director (named, available) | Laser safety officer must be designated |
| Body sculpting (T-Shape) | Trained therapist/nurse | Not required for non-invasive | CE marking and training certificate required |
| Botox / fillers | Doctor or prescribing nurse | Doctor (for nurse prescribers, medical director) | Must be registered with Malta Medical Council |
| Microneedling (>0.5mm) | Nurse or doctor | Medical director | Depth >0.5mm classified as invasive in EU |
| Chemical peels (medium+) | Nurse or doctor | Medical director | Superficial peels can be performed by trained aestheticians |
| PRP therapy | Doctor or nurse | Doctor (blood draw is a medical act) | Centrifuge requires medical device registration |
| IV drips / infusions | Nurse or doctor | Doctor | IV cannulation is a medical act; doctor must prescribe |
| Peptide therapy / prescribing | Doctor only | N/A | Prescription required; off-label use requires informed consent |
| Blood draws | Nurse or phlebotomist | Doctor | Samples must be sent to MMA-registered laboratory |

**Key hire:** A part-time medical director (licensed Maltese physician, ideally with aesthetic medicine certification) is the single most important regulatory requirement. Without a named medical director, you cannot legally offer injectables, IV drips, PRP, or prescriptive treatments. Budget EUR 1,500-3,000/month for a part-time medical director (typically 1-2 days per week on-site, available by phone on all operating days).

### Clinical Waste Management

Contract with a licensed medical waste handler (Malta options: WasteServ Malta via a licensed private contractor, or GreenPak Coop). Clinical waste (yellow bags), sharps (yellow containers), and pharmaceutical waste (blue/purple) must be collected on a scheduled basis. Cost: approximately EUR 200-400/month depending on volume.

### Insurance

- Professional indemnity insurance: EUR 3,000-6,000/year (covers malpractice claims for aesthetic procedures)
- Public liability insurance: EUR 1,500-3,000/year
- Product liability (for IV solutions, injectables): typically bundled with professional indemnity
- Equipment breakdown insurance: EUR 500-1,000/year (covers Candela, T-Shape, and new equipment)

## 10. STRATEGIC INTEGRATION WITH CARISMA ECOSYSTEM

This clinic does not exist in isolation. It connects to Carisma's existing brand portfolio:

**Cross-referral with Carisma Aesthetics:** Patients needing advanced surgical aesthetics (rhinoplasty, blepharoplasty) are referred to Carisma Aesthetics. Carisma Aesthetics patients needing maintenance (Botox touch-ups, skin treatments, laser) are referred to INA Clinic. This bidirectional flow increases lifetime value across both entities.

**Cross-referral with Carisma Slimming:** GLP-1 patients losing weight through Carisma Slimming are the ideal candidates for body sculpting (T-Shape), skin tightening, and "Ozempic face" correction (fillers). This is the GLP-1 body contouring opportunity quantified in the Medical Aesthetics Industry Report at EUR 500K-1.0M additional body contouring revenue by Year 3 across the combined Carisma portfolio.

**Hotel guest funnel:** The Numi Hotel provides a captive audience of tourists and business travelers. Offer a curated "Express Menu" for hotel guests: 30-minute vitamin B12 shot (EUR 40), express facial peel (EUR 80), or laser treatment consultation (free, converts to booking). Hotel guests typically have higher willingness to pay and shorter decision cycles.

**Shared medical director:** One part-time medical director can serve both the INA Clinic and Carisma Aesthetics, reducing overhead. The medical director visits INA 1-2 days per week and Carisma Aesthetics 2-3 days per week, with phone availability on all operating days.

Execute this plan within the first 12 months post-acquisition, and the clinic alone justifies the entire INA Spa purchase price.
