## METHODOLOGY

Three specialist agents produced comprehensive DD question lists. This document:

1. **Removed duplication** -- Revenue verification appeared in all three lists. Employment details appeared in all three. Landlord consent appeared in all three. Consolidated to one question per topic.
2. **Cut overkill** -- This is a simple lease buyout at EUR 240K, not a EUR 50M corporate acquisition. No M&A lawyers are being engaged. Questions requiring expensive third-party advisors, formal MBR searches, or SPA-context legal opinions were removed.
3. **Kept what protects the buyer** -- Anything that could surface a material surprise post-close.
4. **Kept what moves the price** -- Anything whose answer adds or removes EUR 5K+ from the negotiation.
5. **Added the lease amendment discovery** -- The single most important new finding, not covered by any agent.

**Result:** 52 questions across 4 tiers. Plus a document request list, meeting questions, and a record of what was cut.

If the hotel management objects, the clinic must be positioned carefully. This has not been discussed with anyone yet.

**Flagged By:** BCG -- strongest on this. Not flagged by other agents.

**Flagged By:** Legal -- strongest on this. M&A also flagged.

## QUESTIONS FOR THE WEDNESDAY MEETING

*The top 15 questions to ask face-to-face. Conversational, not interrogatory. Ordered by importance.*

---

**1. The Lease Amendment**
"I've been studying the lease in detail and I noticed the turnover fee calculation and the commencement date seem to have been updated since the original January 2020 document. Were there amendments made? Do you have copies of any side letters or addenda?"

*Why ask this way:* Frames it as due diligence thoroughness, not accusation. If they confirm amendments exist, ask for copies. If they look surprised, the amendments may be informal -- which is a bigger problem.

**2. Landlord Temperature Check**
"Have you had any conversation with the Zammit Tabona family about the possibility of a change of operator? What is your read on how they would react?"

*Why ask this way:* Opens the topic without asking directly "will the landlord block this?" Gets the seller's read on the relationship.

**3. The Director's Role**
"Help me understand the management structure. The P&L shows wages for 13 staff, but I understand there is also a Spa Director. Can you walk me through how that role is compensated and what they actually do?"

*Why ask this way:* Invites disclosure without accusation. The seller must either confirm the excluded cost or explain the arrangement.

**4. Hotel Partnership**
"The hotel guest business is significant -- around 22% of revenue. Is there a written arrangement with Barcelo, or is it more of an informal understanding? Would that relationship continue under a new operator?"

*Why ask this way:* Directly assesses the fragility of EUR 93K in annual revenue.

**5. Marketing Function**
"Who handles the marketing for the spa? I noticed the advertising line in the costs but I am curious about the team behind it -- is it in-house, outsourced?"

*Why ask this way:* Surfaces the second excluded cost category naturally.

**6. Equipment Condition**
"The Candela is obviously a significant asset. Can you tell me when it was last serviced, roughly how many pulses are on it, and whether there are any upcoming maintenance needs? Same for the T-Shape."

*Why ask this way:* Gets directly at asset condition without requiring formal documentation upfront.

**7. Revenue Verification Path**
"We will need to do standard verification -- bank statements, VAT returns, that sort of thing. Is that something you are comfortable providing, and roughly how quickly could we get those?"

*Why ask this way:* Sets expectations for document requests. Gauges seller's willingness. A defensive reaction is itself informative.

**8. Staff Retention**
"If we proceed, the staff transfer under Maltese law. I want to understand: are there any key people you think might be flight risks? Anyone I should prioritize meeting?"

*Why ask this way:* Gets the seller to identify key people and flag potential departures without the buyer having to guess.

**9. Gift Vouchers and Prepaid Liability**
"Do you have a sense of how much is outstanding in gift vouchers and prepaid packages? Just a ballpark -- we will need the detailed schedule later."

*Why ask this way:* Gets an initial number without requiring formal documentation at the meeting.

**10. Medical Aesthetics Feasibility**
"Part of our interest is the potential to add aesthetics services. Have you ever explored that? Any sense of how the landlord would view it?"

*Why ask this way:* Tests whether the seller or landlord has already considered this, which affects feasibility assessment.

**11. Fortina Complex Future**
"Is the Fortina Complex itself stable? Any rumors about the building being sold or redeveloped?"

*Why ask this way:* Direct but conversational. The seller's comfort level with this question is itself informative.

**12. Non-Compete**
"If we do a deal, we would need a standard non-compete. Is that something you would be comfortable with in principle?"

*Why ask this way:* Early signal. If the seller hesitates, they may intend to stay in the market.

**13. Competitor Dynamics**
"Who do you see as INA's main competitors in the Sliema area? Has the competitive landscape changed much in the last year or two?"

*Why ask this way:* Gets competitive intelligence from the most informed source (the operator). Also reveals how aware the seller is of their market position.

**14. Seasonality and Cash Flow**
"July looked like a tough month. Is that purely seasonal, or were there other factors? How do you manage cash flow through the summer?"

*Why ask this way:* Gets at the cash flow reality without requesting bank statements in the meeting. The answer reveals whether director loans are needed to survive summer.

**15. Next Steps**
"If we can align on economics, what does the process look like from your side? How quickly could you provide the key documents, and what is your timeline for wanting to complete?"

*Why ask this way:* Tests seller urgency. A seller wanting to close quickly is more flexible on price. A seller who is relaxed about timing may have less financial pressure.

### What the Cuts Mean

The three agents collectively produced outstanding, comprehensive work. The Legal agent's lease analysis is world-class. The BCG agent's strategic framing is rigorous. The M&A agent's revenue verification methodology is exactly right.

But 322 questions for a EUR 240K handshake deal would overwhelm the seller, signal distrust, and delay the process. The 52 questions retained here cover every material risk while remaining proportionate to the deal size and complexity.

**The principle:** Ask only what you will ACT on. If the answer does not change the price, the decision, or the Day 1 operating plan, it does not belong in this list.
