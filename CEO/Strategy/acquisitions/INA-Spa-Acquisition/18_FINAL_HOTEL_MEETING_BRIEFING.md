## YOUR TWO DEAL BREAKERS: STATUS & STRATEGY

### DEAL BREAKER 1: HAIR SALON CONVERSION -- STRONGEST POSITION

**Legal ground:** Clause 5.9 states non-structural internal alterations "shall not be unreasonably withheld." Converting a hair salon to a treatment room is non-structural. All 3 agents agree this is legally defensible.

**How to present it:** "We plan to upgrade the hair salon into a premium treatment area. The works are purely cosmetic and internal -- new furniture, fresh paint, updated fixtures. We will submit full plans for your approval."

**If hotel resists:** You can escalate: request written reasons, commission independent survey confirming non-structural nature, assert right under Clause 5.9, and ultimately seek court declaration (3-6 months).

**Consensus success probability: 75-85%**

### DEAL BREAKER 2: SUBLEASE RIGHTS -- HARDER BUT ACHIEVABLE

**Legal reality:** Clause 5.27 gives the hotel "absolute discretion" -- no reasonableness standard. This is the hardest ask.

**The killer argument (all 3 agents agree):** The 5% turnover fee. Every euro of sub-tenant revenue flows through Carisma's books. The hotel currently receives ~EUR 21.5K/year at EUR 430K revenue. With Carisma and specialist operators, this grows significantly.

**QC-CORRECTED NUMBERS** (the 5% applies to ALL sales from euro one, NOT just the excess above EUR 350K):

| Scenario | Annual Revenue | Hotel's 5% Fee | Hotel Gain vs Today |
|----------|---------------|----------------|---------------------|
| INA Today | EUR 430K | EUR 21,500/yr | -- |
| Carisma Conservative | EUR 700K | EUR 35,000/yr | +EUR 13,500 |
| Carisma Base | EUR 900K | EUR 45,000/yr | +EUR 23,500 |

**Over 9 years, the hotel gains EUR 121K-211K in additional turnover fees.** Still compelling, but present the CORRECT baseline of EUR 21.5K -- not EUR 4K. Getting the baseline wrong destroys credibility instantly.

**Concessions to offer (in order -- only if needed):**
1. Approval rights over every sub-tenant (low cost, high perceived value)
2. Revenue transparency -- quarterly reports (costs nothing)
3. Quality standards covenant (costs nothing)
4. Revenue floor guarantee of EUR 600K minimum for fee calculation (moderate cost)
5. Modest rent increase EUR 2K/year (last resort -- EUR 18K over 9 years)

**Critical fallback: The Management Agreement Workaround.** If the hotel says NO to subleasing, structure specialist operations as management/service agreements. The specialist practitioner operates under Carisma's brand, Carisma retains possession, revenue flows through Carisma's books. This may not technically be "subletting" under Clause 5.27.

**QC WARNING on the workaround:** Clause 5.27 is broader than just "subletting" -- it also prohibits "sharing possession or occupation" and "granting rights over" the premises. A specialist practitioner physically treating clients in a dedicated room could be argued as sharing occupation. The management agreement is NOT a guaranteed fallback. **Plan for the possibility of operating entirely with employees, not contractors.**

**Consensus success probability: 55-65%** (but workaround reliability is lower than presented)

Easy for hotel to grant (costs them nothing now). Protects Mert's EUR 400K+ investment at lease expiry in 2035.

**Total potential savings: EUR 60-120K over remaining lease.**
