## Slide D: "Guaranteed Floor" -- Minimum Purchase Commitments

> **Key Takeaway:** Valmont is guaranteed EUR 235K over 3 years regardless of performance. In the Base Case, actual purchases more than double the floor -- EUR 486K vs EUR 235K. Carisma bears the inventory risk, not Valmont.

| Year | Minimum Commitment | Base Case Projection | Headroom |
|---|---|---|---|
| Year 1 | EUR 35,000 | EUR 36,880 | **+5%** |
| Year 2 | EUR 80,000 | EUR 160,280 | **+100%** |
| Year 3 | EUR 120,000 | EUR 289,060 | **+141%** |
| **3-Year Total** | **EUR 235,000** | **EUR 486,220** | **+107%** |

### Suggested Visual: Dual Bar Chart

For each year, show two bars side by side:
- **Solid bar:** Minimum Commitment (the contractual floor)
- **Taller bar:** Base Case Projection (the expected outcome)
- Annotate the gap percentage between them

### Why the Minimums Are Set This Way

- **Year 1 (EUR 35K):** Covers initial inventory stocking across 4 launch locations -- Carisma commits to purchasing regardless of sell-through
- **Year 2 (EUR 80K):** Signals confidence in the ramp -- set deliberately above conservative projections
- **Year 3 (EUR 120K):** Even the conservative scenario (EUR 131K) comfortably exceeds this floor
- **Carisma is willing to commit to annual performance benchmarks beyond minimum purchases**

## Slide G: "Market Context" -- Comparable Benchmarks

> **Key Takeaway:** Malta is an underserved market with comparable tourist volumes to Cyprus (where Valmont already distributes). The financial projections are conservative relative to benchmarks: 17% TAM capture with captive distribution, and EBITDA margins well below industry norms.

### Malta vs Cyprus: Why Malta is the Missing Market

| Metric | Malta | Cyprus | Implication |
|---|---|---|---|
| Population | 545K | 1.2M | Malta smaller but more concentrated |
| Annual tourists | ~4M | ~4M | **Comparable tourist volumes** |
| Tourist-to-resident ratio | **7.4x** | 3.3x | Malta has 2x higher tourist density |
| GDP per capita (PPP) | EUR 62,500 | EUR 48,000 | Malta 30% wealthier per capita |
| 5-star hotel concentration | High (small island) | Spread across larger geography | Malta: more captive spa audiences |
| Valmont presence | **None** | **Active distribution** | Malta is the gap |
| Ultra-premium skincare competitors | **None with physical retail** | Several | Malta is an empty market |

### TAM Capture Validation (Year 3)

| Scenario | Year 3 Product Revenue | TAM (EUR 2.25M midpoint) | Capture Rate |
|---|---|---|---|
| Conservative | EUR 176,000 | EUR 2.25M | 7.8% |
| **Base Case** | **EUR 382,000** | **EUR 2.25M** | **17.0%** |
| Upside | EUR 560,000 | EUR 2.25M | 24.9% |

**17% TAM capture is credible** given Carisma controls 10+ spa locations (captive distribution), has exclusive territory rights, and faces no competing ultra-premium physical retail presence.

| Benchmark | EBITDA Margin | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Year 1 new market launch (industry) | -5% to +10% | Industry research |
