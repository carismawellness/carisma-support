## Section 1: Malta — The Untapped Market

### Slide 1.1 — The White Space

Valmont is present in 1,800 points of sale worldwide. None of them are in Malta.

This is a market where 4 million tourists spend EUR 3.9 billion annually — in a country of 545,000 people. The tourist-to-resident ratio is 7.4 to 1, higher than any comparable European island market. GDP per capita exceeds Spain, Italy, and France. The economy grew 3.9% in 2025, among the fastest in Europe.

And yet, no ultra-premium skincare brand has established physical retail presence on the island. La Prairie, Sisley, La Mer — none have a dedicated distributor. None have a spa partner. They exist only in duty-free cabinets and online checkout carts. The entire tier above EUR 200 per product is, for all practical purposes, an empty shelf.

Valmont has active distribution in Cyprus — a market with a comparable tourist volume of 4 million visitors per year and a tourist-to-resident ratio of 3.3 to 1. Malta matches Cyprus on tourism but concentrates that demand into a tighter geography and a more consolidated 5-star hotel market. The opportunity is not theoretical. It is the same opportunity Valmont already recognised in Cyprus — simply unfilled.

The estimated luxury skincare market in Malta is EUR 2.0 to 2.5 million. That figure reflects both resident spending and the purchasing power of over 25 million annual guest nights, increasingly driven by the 45-plus traveller demographic — the core luxury skincare buyer.

### Slide 1.2 — A Strategic Test Market

Malta is small enough to be low-risk and complex enough to be meaningful.

As a full EU member state using the euro, Malta presents zero regulatory friction. Products already registered on the EU Cosmetic Products Notification Portal require no additional approval. There are no import duties, no currency conversion costs, no additional compliance. From a regulatory standpoint, entering Malta is identical to entering a new Italian or Greek city.

But what makes Malta strategically interesting is not just the market itself. It is the model it can prove. If a single spa operator can manage exclusive Valmont distribution across a cluster of luxury hotels — handling retail, treatments, wholesale, and e-commerce — that playbook becomes replicable. Mauritius. The Maldives. Seychelles. Bali. Markets where Valmont seeks presence but the economics do not justify a standalone distribution entity.

Malta is the proving ground. The investment is contained. The upside extends well beyond the island.

## Section 2: Carisma — The Partner

### Slide 2.1 — 35 Years in Luxury Hospitality

Carisma Wellness Group has been operating in Malta since 1990. Today the group manages spas inside 10 of the island's premier hotels — including InterContinental (IHG), Hyatt Regency, and Grand Hotel Excelsior, a member of The Leading Hotels of the World. An eleventh location, INA Spa, opens in May 2026.

These are not franchise arrangements. Carisma operates each spa directly: staffing, treatments, retail, training, guest experience. The group serves an estimated 50,000 spa guests annually and employs over 80 therapists across its network.

In parallel, the group runs Carisma Aesthetics, a doctor-led medical aesthetics clinic with a EUR 838,000 budget for 2026 and a 78.5% gross margin. This is not a cosmetic add-on — it is a clinical operation that gives Carisma credibility in the science-driven skincare conversation that Valmont's cellular cosmetics positioning demands.

Group revenue reached EUR 3.3 million in 2025, reflecting an 11-year compound annual growth rate of 15.6%. The 2026 target is EUR 5 million. Carisma doubled its pre-COVID revenue peak by 2024.

This is not a startup seeking a brand to build a business around. It is a mature operator offering a proven platform.

### Slide 2.2 — Distribution Already Proven

In October 2025, Carisma launched its own skincare brand — The Purest Solutions — and within five months built a complete distribution operation: 10 in-spa retail points, 8 external wholesale partners, a live Shopify e-commerce store with next-day islandwide delivery, and weekly stock tracking across every location.

The annualised run-rate reached EUR 82,000. The infrastructure — logistics, inventory management, consignment terms, staff training, partner onboarding — is already built. It is the same infrastructure Valmont would use from day one.

More importantly, Carisma's existing portfolio creates a natural brand architecture with room at the top:

| Tier | Brand | Price Range | Role |
|------|-------|-------------|------|
| Entry | The Purest Solutions | EUR 20--60 | The gateway product |
| Mid-tier | Phytomer | EUR 30--80 | The treatment-room staple |
| Premium | **Valmont** | **EUR 150--400+** | **The aspiration** |

These brands do not compete. They create a ladder. A client who begins with a EUR 40 moisturiser and then experiences a professional facial is pre-qualified for the Valmont conversation. This is the good-better-best retail architecture that leading hotel spas use worldwide — and at present, Carisma's top tier is empty.

### Slide 2.3 — Reach, Control, and Digital Stewardship

Carisma's network covers Malta comprehensively: 10 hotel spas, a medical aesthetics clinic, a slimming clinic, an e-commerce store, and 8 wholesale salon partners — 22 points of sale in total. Valmont products could be available across the island within 60 days of signing.

On the digital side, Carisma operates a full in-house marketing function — paid social, organic content, SEO, and CRM-driven communications across more than 15,000 active client profiles. For Valmont, this means targeted launch campaigns to the highest-spending spa and aesthetics clients, professional content production from 10 luxury spa settings, and SEO dominance for "Valmont Malta" from day one.

It also means complete grey market control. As the sole authorised Malta retailer, every Valmont product on the island flows through managed channels. Carisma controls the retail environment directly through owned spas — a structural advantage that eliminates the distribution leakage Valmont has worked to resolve globally. The group that cleaned CHF 40 million from grey market channels would be partnering with an operator where that problem cannot arise.

## Section 3: Why Not the Alternatives?

### Slide 3.1 — No Other Partner Can Deliver This

**Pharmacy chains** have islandwide retail presence but no spa infrastructure, no treatment capability, and no luxury service environment. Valmont displayed alongside mass-market skincare undermines the brand positioning that justifies a EUR 250 price point.

**Duty-free operators** offer high foot traffic at Malta International Airport but no spa integration — the channel through which Valmont generates 50% of its global turnover. The interaction is transactional, not relational.

**Independent hotel spas** — Myoka at the Hilton, Apollo at Corinthia — offer one or two doors. They lack island-wide coverage, wholesale capability, e-commerce, and clinical credibility. They cannot build a market.

**Regional Mediterranean distributors** have cross-border expertise but Malta is too small for them to prioritise. It would be a line item, not a focus — without local relationships, without operational control, without urgency.

Only one operator in Malta combines 10 hotel spas, a medical aesthetics clinic, a live e-commerce store, an active wholesale network, 35 years of operating history, and a proven track record of skincare brand distribution. The question is not whether Carisma is the right partner. It is whether any credible alternative exists.

## Section 5: Risk Mitigation and Commitment

### Slide 5.1 — All-In from Carisma, Zero Risk for Valmont

Carisma is committing EUR 75,000 in launch infrastructure — initial inventory, in-spa display units, therapist training, and launch marketing — before a single product is sold. Over three years, the guaranteed minimum purchase commitment totals EUR 235,000 in product orders from Valmont, structured as EUR 35,000 in Year 1, EUR 80,000 in Year 2, and EUR 120,000 in Year 3.

In the base case, actual purchases are projected to reach EUR 486,000 over three years — more than double the guaranteed floor.

Every cost is borne by Carisma. Inventory risk, staffing, logistics, marketing, display infrastructure — all of it sits on our balance sheet. Valmont's capital at risk is zero. The only investment Valmont makes is the decision to say yes.

### Slide 5.2 — Brand Integrity as a Non-Negotiable

Carisma's distribution model is built around controlled pricing across every channel — no discounting, authorised retailers only, and active monitoring for unauthorised online sellers. As the exclusive Malta distributor operating primarily through owned spas, Carisma eliminates the structural conditions that allow grey market diversion.

The go-to-market strategy is treatment-first. In the opening quarter, Valmont will be introduced exclusively through the facial menu — not pushed from the shelf. Every treatment becomes a product education moment. Valmont's own global benchmark is a 50% treatment-to-retail conversion rate, compared to a 25% industry average. We intend to build demand the way Valmont has always built demand: through the hands of a trained therapist, one client at a time.

If retail conversion falls below 30% after three months, we have a contingency — a curated starter set at approximately EUR 120 that lowers the entry barrier without compromising the brand's positioning.

The model is designed to protect the brand first and grow revenue second. That order of priority is deliberate.
