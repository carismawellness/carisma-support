## COMMON QUESTIONS ANSWERED

**Q: Do I need to integrate ALL of these into my Excel Sheet 6?**
A: No. Minimum = integrate Loss Aversion Reframe, Sunk Cost Redirect, and Expanded Act 1 (2 new questions). Nice to have = full integration. Essential = internalization (memorize scripts, know techniques).

**Q: Which document should I bring to the meeting?**
A: Print TACTICAL-REFERENCE-CARD.md only. Keep it visible. Leave the others at home (overstuffing your brain mid-negotiation is counterproductive).

**Q: Can I share these documents with my accountant/solicitor?**
A: Yes. Share EXECUTIVE-SUMMARY.md for context. Share FRAMEWORK-ANALYSIS.md if they want deep strategic understanding. Skip TACTICAL-CARD.md (that's personal prep).

**Q: What if I disagree with one of these frameworks?**
A: Adapt, don't abandon. If Mirror technique feels unnatural, practice it until it does. If Loss Aversion Reframe doesn't match your voice, rewrite it in your words (keep the core logic). The frameworks are robust; the language can be personalized.

**Q: What if seller seems unresponsive to these techniques?**
A: Some people are logical, some are emotional. These documents address emotional dimension. If he's purely logical, rely more on "your numbers and comps" (from Excel). If he's purely emotional, rely more on "validation and loss aversion" (from these frameworks). Most people are 60/40 emotional/logical.

**Q: Should I mention these frameworks to the seller?**
A: Absolutely not. Never say "I'm using BATNA analysis" or "This is loss aversion reframing." These are your internal tools, not his. To him, you're just a thoughtful buyer having a conversation.

**Q: When should I walk away?**
A: Check EXECUTIVE-SUMMARY.md, Red Lines section. If any condition is met, walk away with dignity. Also: if price goes > €200K without corresponding risk reduction, walk away.

## FINAL NOTES

1. **These documents are strategic, not tactical.** They improve your thinking, not just your talking. The goal is to understand seller's psychology AND your own response patterns.

2. **Frameworks are descriptive, not prescriptive.** They describe how negotiation actually works (emotional + rational). You decide how to apply them based on the specific situation.

3. **Personalization is required.** Don't use language word-for-word if it feels unnatural. Adapt to your voice, your style, your context. The core logic matters; the exact words can be personalized.

4. **Practice before deploying.** Read the scripts aloud. Use them in low-stakes conversations before the INA meeting. They'll feel natural in high-stakes if you've practiced in low-stakes.

5. **Psychological frameworks are ethically neutral.** You're not "manipulating" the seller by understanding his psychology. You're having a more honest conversation because you're addressing his actual motivations (stress relief, dignity, certainty) instead of just arguing about numbers.
