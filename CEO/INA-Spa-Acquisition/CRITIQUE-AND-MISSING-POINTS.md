- You inherit liability if equipment lien isn't cleared
- Deal math completely changes

- Equipment net value = €70K (your anchor price)
- Seller's equity = €70K, not the €120K you thought
- He'll refuse the appraisal, claim it's wrong, or demand you refinance the equipment loan
- Deal collapses or you're stuck renegotiating

Add "EQUIPMENT APPRAISAL PROTOCOL" section to Negotiation Brief:

```

2. Prepare your position in advance:

- If appraisal is outlier (>250K or <50K): Renegotiate with appraisal as new anchor

### CRITICAL: No Numi (Equipment Financing) Liability Protocol

**What's Missing:**
Briefing mentions "Numi legal issues unresolved" as a red line, but:
1. **You don't have a due diligence question for Act 1 to uncover this**
2. **You don't have a response script if seller admits Numi financing**
3. **You don't have a deal structure to handle it (refinancing, seller payoff, escrow hold)**
4. **You don't have a contingency if financing documents are missing or unclear**

**Why It Matters for THIS Deal:**
Spa equipment is financed frequently through Numi (a European equipment finance company). If INA has Numi financing:
- Lien on equipment = you can't take equipment free and clear
- Payoff balance may be undisclosed
- Maturity schedule may have prepayment penalties
- If financing is in default = creditor has priority, not seller

This is THE #1 liability that kills spa acquisitions in Southern Europe.

**Specific Risk Scenario:**
- Seller claims equipment is worth €200K, unencumbered
- You close the deal for €120K
- Post-close, you discover Numi lien for €150K on equipment
- You inherit the liability
- Equipment net value = €50K (your actual acquisition cost)
- You have no recourse; deal is done

**How to Address (BEFORE AND DURING Meeting):**

1. **Pre-Meeting Due Diligence Request (Send via solicitor BEFORE meeting):**
   > "Before we meet, please provide: (1) List of all equipment financing agreements; (2) Numi contract (if applicable); (3) Current payoff statement; (4) Any liens or UCC filings against equipment or business."

   This forces disclosure. If seller refuses or claims "nothing exists," you have red flag in writing.

2. **Act 1 Question to Add (Direct):**
   > "Walk me through your equipment financing. Do you have any loans against equipment, or is everything owned free and clear?"

   Listen for:
   - Direct answer (clean or financed)
   - Deflection ("My accountant handles that")
   - Defensiveness ("Why are you asking?")

   Any deflection = immediate red flag.

3. **Response Script (If He Admits Financing):**
   > "Thanks for being upfront. That's helpful. What's the Numi balance and maturity?"

   If he gives number:
   > "And what's the prepayment penalty? Can it be paid off at close?"

   If no penalty:
   > "Good. Prepayment protects us. Your proceeds = Equipment value - Numi payoff - Our purchase price."

   If penalty exists:
   > "We need to factor that penalty into deal math. Penalty reduces net proceeds to you. Let's see how that works out."

4. **Deal Structure Contingency (If Financing Exists):**
   Add new section to Ackermann sequence: "NUMI PAYOFF ADJUSTMENT"

   Instead of fixed moves (€72K, €96K, €114K), conditional moves become:
   - "Move 1: €72K if equipment is unencumbered. €65K if Numi financing with penalties."
   - "Move 2: Adjusted based on verified payoff balance and penalty."

   Example:
   - Equipment FMV: €150K
   - Numi balance: €100K
   - Numi payoff penalty: €5K
   - Equipment net to seller: €45K
   - Your revised offer: €65K (instead of €72K), reflecting lower equipment equity
   - Seller's total net: €110K (vs. €120K without financing)

5. **Contingency Protocol (If Financing Documents Missing):**
   > "If we can't locate the Numi documents, I need to escrow 20% of purchase price (€24K) until documents are provided and confirmed."

   This is deal protection, not punishment.

**Implementation Priority:** CRITICAL
**Timing:** Send due diligence request IMMEDIATELY, before meeting

### HIGH: No Emotional Trigger Diagnostic for Seller

**What's Missing:**
The briefing has psychological frameworks (mirror, label, loss aversion) but NO diagnostic to identify what specific emotional triggers will WORK with THIS seller.

Different sellers respond to different frames:
- **Pride-driven seller** (built from nothing): Appeals to legacy, reputation, "you built something real"
- **Cash-driven seller** (investor mentality): Appeals to certainty, cash in hand, tax optimization
- **Tired seller** (burnout, stress): Appeals to relief, putting business behind him, freedom
- **Protective seller** (mission-driven): Appeals to care for staff, preserving culture, reputation

If you don't diagnose which type THIS seller is, your psychological scripts will miss.

**Why It Matters:**
Loss aversion reframe works for tired sellers but alienates proud sellers ("Are you suggesting my business is a burden?"). Sunk cost redirect works for investors but wounds pride-driven sellers ("You're dismissing my €1M investment").

**Specific Risk Scenario:**
- Seller is pride-driven (built spa from nothing)
- You deploy loss aversion: "This business is bleeding you, let's end the stress"
- Seller gets angry: "I'm not trying to escape this business. I'm trying to get what it's worth!"
- Reframe backfires; he becomes defensive
- Deal stalls

**How to Address (BEFORE Meeting):**

Add new section: "SELLER ARCHETYPE DIAGNOSIS & MATCHING FRAMES"

```

Before the meeting, diagnose which seller archetype you're dealing with. Tailor your scripts accordingly.

ARCHETYPE 1: PRIDE-DRIVEN (Built from Nothing)
Indicators:
- €1M invested suggests his own capital, not borrowed
- 20+ years suggests personal commitment
- Describes business as "my creation," "my achievement"
- Mentions staff relationship or culture building
- Takes feedback about business personally

Emotional Triggers:
- Legacy ("This is something real you built")
- Recognition ("Your reputation precedes you")
- Stewardship ("You care about what happens next")

DO USE:
- "You built something remarkable. That matters."
- "I want to make sure it's run with the care you gave it."
- "Your reputation is part of what makes this valuable."

DON'T USE:
- "This business is stressing you out" (implies his creation is a burden; wounds pride)

SCRIPTS FOR THIS ARCHETYPE:

**How to De-Risk:**
Acknowledge the investment before reframing:
> "Your €1M investment is real and it matters. It created the business that generates €123K EBITDA today. That's valuable. The question is: what does that EBITDA value today? That's the market conversation. Not what it cost to build."

> "My offer is pending landlord approval AT CURRENT TERMS. If terms change, we recalibrate together. That's standard. Neither of us wants to inherit a worse lease."

- Seller shares revenue numbers
- You ask for verification documents
- Seller hears: "I don't believe you."

**How to De-Risk:**

> "I want to see the verification so I can show my accountant and explain the valuation to my investors. It's not about believing you—it's about having documentation."

This makes it process-driven, not doubt-driven.

**How to De-Risk:**
Explain your constraint, not your doubt:
> "I appreciate that. Earnouts can work. But my investor agreements typically require clean acquisitions. I literally don't have the flexibility to do earnouts. But let me see if there's another structure that works for both of us..."

This frames it as your constraint, not your distrust of him.
