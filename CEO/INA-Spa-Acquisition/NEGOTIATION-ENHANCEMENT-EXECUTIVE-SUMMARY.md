## THE THREE MOST POWERFUL ADDITIONS (Minimum Viable Enhancement)

### **1. LOSS AVERSION REFRAME (Your Strongest Script)**
**Deploy when:** He's tired, emotional, or price-stuck
**Exact language:**
> "Here's what I see. This business is bleeding your time and energy every month. That's a loss. What I'm offering is to stop that loss and give you certainty. €120K now, you're done. No more stress. You preserve the reputation you built. That matters more than another €10K either way."

**Why:** Behavioral research shows people are 2x more motivated to AVOID LOSS than capture equivalent gain. You're not selling low price; you're ending his pain.

---

### **2. MIRROR + LABEL (Emotional Calibration)**
**Deploy before your financial script (Act 2)**
**The technique:**
- When he anchors: You mirror it back ("€250K?") → He elaborates
- When he objects: You label emotion ("Sounds like this investment matters to you") → He opens up
- Then you deploy your reframe → Now he's ready to hear it

**Why:** Research shows emotional validation increases deal probability ~40%. Make him feel heard FIRST, then introduce logic.

---

### **3. SUNK COST REDIRECT (When He Mentions €1M)**
**Exact language:**
> "That €1M is already spent. Market prices based on what the business earns TODAY, not what was spent to build it. Your liquidation option gets you €100K. I'm offering €120K and taking the lease and liabilities. That's €20K better than your next option."

**Why:** Directly addresses his anchor (€1M invested) without defending your price. Shows market reality calmly.

## CRITICAL SUCCESS FACTORS

1. **Emotional validation comes BEFORE financial logic.** Mirror and label first, then explain numbers. If you reverse the order, defensiveness.

2. **Sunk cost is his anchor; market comps are your counter-anchor.** Without market data, he dwells on €1M spent. With comps, he thinks about market multiples.

3. **Loss aversion > gain motivation.** Stop talking about "what you're paying for." Start talking about "the stress you're ending."

4. **Seller BATNA determines his reservation price.** If his BATNA is weak (liquidation = €100K), your €120K is strong. If his BATNA is strong (other buyer at €200K), you may need to walk.

5. **Conversation breakdowns are recoverable.** STATE your path protocol prevents you from escalating a disagreement into a dead deal. Use it as insurance, not default.
