## SECTION 3: ACT 2 — EMOTIONAL CALIBRATION & REFRAME (20 minutes)

### 3.1 Act 2 Objective

**Your goal in Act 2:** Move seller from emotional anchor (€1M sunk cost, €250K demand) to rational anchor (market multiples, EBITDA-based valuation, €120K reality).

**Psychology phase:** Help him feel UNDERSTOOD first (emotions), then introduce MARKET REALITY second (logic).

**Success metric:** He's nodding along with your logic, not arguing every point. He's accepted your PREMISE (market prices on EBITDA, not capex) even if he hasn't accepted your PRICE yet.

**Failure signal:** He's still defending €250K as valuation basis, repeating "I invested €1M," or becoming more defensive. (If this happens, move to Act 3 and present Move 1 offer; sometimes sellers need to see numbers to shift anchors.)

---

### 3.2 Emotional Calibration (30 seconds before reframe)

**This is the most important 30 seconds of the meeting.** Before you launch into P&L walkthrough, deploy Voss techniques to disarm defensiveness.

#### TECHNIQUE 1: THE MIRROR
**When seller makes opening position, repeat his last 3 words back as question.**

Example Flow:
- Seller: "I need €250K. I've invested too much not to get that back."
- You: [pause, calm, reflective tone] "€250K."
- Seller: [elaborates, typically revealing emotion or constraint behind the number]

Why: Mirror buys time, shows listening, makes him elaborate. You gather intelligence; he feels heard.

---

#### TECHNIQUE 2: LABEL THE EMOTION
**Name the emotion you observe plainly. Not "Do you feel...?" but "It sounds like you..."**

Example Flow:
- Seller: [After anchor] "I built this from nothing. It's worth what I say it's worth."
- You: "It sounds like the business you created is important to you—not just financially, but personally. You want to make sure it's valued right."
- Seller: [Usually opens up] "Exactly. I put 20 years into this."
- You: "I can see that. That matters. Let me show you how current market reality affects that investment."

Why: Labeling disarms defensiveness. When emotions are named and validated, people stop defending them and become open to new information.

---

#### TECHNIQUE 3: ACCUSATION AUDIT
**State the worst-case interpretation of your position before he does. Removes his ability to blindside you.**

Example Flow:
- You: "I know it might sound like I'm lowballing you. Let me explain why market data points to a different range. You'll probably think I'm dismissing your capex—I'm not. Here's why that capex and market price are different things."
- Seller: [Nods, because you've already addressed his objection]

Why: Shows confidence. Removes his ability to attack with hidden objections. Flips power dynamic.

### 3.3 The Financial Story (5-7 minutes)

**Frame:** "Let me walk you through how market valuations work for spa acquisitions. It's not about right or wrong—it's about how buyers actually evaluate these deals."

#### STEP 1: ESTABLISH AGREED BASELINE NUMBERS
*Get him to confirm these numbers before you build on them.*

**"Let's start with what we know. Can we agree on these baseline numbers?"**

Ask: "Does that math roughly align with what you're seeing?"
- If yes: Continue.
- If no: "Help me understand where my OpEx assumptions are wrong." Adjust until you both agree on baseline EBITDA number.

**Why this matters:** You're not claiming he's wrong. You're asking him to agree on a number he presumably knows. Once he agrees, he's committed to the logic that flows from it.

---

#### STEP 2: INTRODUCE MARKET MULTIPLES (Anchor to EBITDA, Not Capex)

**"Market valuation works like this. Acquisitions are priced based on what the business EARNS today, not what was spent to build it. That €1M you invested was real and it created the €123K EBITDA we just agreed on. The question now is: what does that EBITDA value at?"**

**Share market multiples (use real or directional data):**

"Here's how spa/wellness acquisitions are priced in Europe right now:

| Business Type | Multiple |
|---|---|
| Distressed/liquidation | 0.5-0.7x EBITDA |
| Stable, single-location | 0.8-1.2x EBITDA |
| High-growth (clinic expanding) | 1.3-1.8x EBITDA |
| Premium (strong brand, sticky customers) | 1.5-2.0x EBITDA |

INA Spa is a stable, single-location business with good cash flow and customer base. So we're looking at 0.8-1.2x range.

At 1.0x, that's €123K.
At 1.2x (premium for quality), that's €148K.

I'm opening at 0.97x EBITDA, which is actually *below* market for a stable business like yours."

**Why this works:**
- Reestablishes YOUR anchor (€123K EBITDA) as reference point
- His €250K anchor fades; market multiples dominate
- You're not claiming he's wrong; you're showing how market works
- He can't argue with "that's how acquisitions work" because it IS

#### STEP 3: DEPLOY SUNK COST REDIRECT (If He Mentions €1M Investment)

**"I understand you invested €1M. That investment is real, and it matters. Here's the thing though—that €1M is already spent. It's in the past. The question for buyers today is: what does the business earn NOW? That's what market prices."**

#### STEP 4: DEPLOY LOSS AVERSION REFRAME (Most Powerful Psychological Frame)

**Use this ONLY if you sense he's tired, stressed, or emotionally anchored. Not if he's confident/strong.**

**"Here's what I'm actually seeing. This business is bleeding your time and energy every month. You're running it solo, managing staff, dealing with landlord issues, carrying operational stress. You preserve the business's reputation AND your own. That peace of mind is worth something."**

**Why this works:**
- Behavioral research: People are 2x more motivated to AVOID LOSS than capture equivalent gain
- You're not selling low price; you're ending his pain
- Frames you as savior, not acquirer
- Addresses his real emotional pain (stress, uncertainty)

**When to deploy:**
- If he mentions stress, fatigue, health concerns, or daily grind challenges
- If he seems emotionally stuck on €250K and logic isn't moving him
- NOT if he seems confident/strong (he'll see it as manipulation)
- Late in Act 2 or early in Act 3 if price is stalled

- His brain now thinks "multiples" not "what I spent"

**Success metric:** He's asking questions about TERMS (close timeline, landlord assignment, staff), not just price. He's saying "I need to think about this" or "What if X changes?" not "That's insulting, I need €250K."

**Failure signal:** He's still defending €250K, becoming angry, or saying deal is impossible. (If this happens, deploy STATE YOUR PATH recovery protocol below.)

**This is your walk-away price.** Don't go above it.
**If seller resists indemnification or close timeline:** "These terms protect both of us. Without them, we can't move forward."

**Then re-explain EBITDA multiples and market comps.**

**Then:** "Here's my question: Are you trying to recoup the €1M you invested (which is sunk cost), or are you pricing based on what it EARNS today? Those are two different conversations."

### 4.5 Act 3 Contingency: High-Stakes Moment Protocol

**If at any point in Act 3 the conversation breaks down (seller gets defensive, angry, or silent for >10 seconds):**

**STOP. Don't keep talking.**

**Step 1: PAUSE & ACKNOWLEDGE**
"Let me pause here. I notice we might be talking past each other."

**Step 2: STATE YOUR PATH (Re-establish dialogue)**
"Let me be transparent about where I'm coming from. I believe in this business. I respect what you've built. But I have responsibility to my investors. I evaluate acquisitions using market standards—current EBITDA multiples, not historical capex. Your current business earns €123K EBITDA. Market is paying 0.8-1.2x for stable spas. €120K is in that range. I'm opening at €72K to create negotiation room. I'm not trying to steal this. I'm trying to be clear about my logic. What's your thinking on valuation approach?"

**Step 3: LISTEN (Voss principle)**
After STATE your path, shut up. If he's still defensive:
- Mirror: "So you're saying my numbers are wrong?"
- Label: "It sounds frustrating that we value this differently"
- Ask again: "Help me understand your valuation method"

**Step 4: RESET FRAME (If dialogue resumes)**
"I think we both agree this business is valuable. We just disagree on price. How would you want to structure this so we both feel good about it?"

**RED LINE: If he says "I can't do business with you" or walks out:**
- DO NOT chase him or backpedal
- DO NOT offer last-minute concessions
- Say: "I understand. I respect that. If you change your mind, let me know. I'm interested in making this work."
- Then wait 24 hours. Send follow-up email per protocol (Section 6).
- DO NOT pursue if he doesn't respond in 5 days.

## SECTION 5: ACT 4 — CLOSE OR EXIT (10 minutes)

### 5.1 Act 4 Objective

**Your goal in Act 4:** Move seller from "I need to think about it" to "Yes, let's move forward" or "No, this doesn't work."

**Don't expect a yes in this first meeting.** Expect "I'll think about it" (normal) or a counteroffer that signals movement.

**Success metric:** Clear next step defined (second meeting in 7 days, due diligence documents requested, conditions understood).

**Failure signal:** He says "I need to think about it" and you have no follow-up protocol. (Momentum dies.)

---

### 5.2 Trial Close 1: Appeal to Loss Aversion

**"We're getting closer. Here's what I'd love to do: I want to put this behind you with a deal that gives you certainty and peace. That's worth more than another €10K either way. Can we work toward a number in the €115-125K range that gets us both comfortable?"**

**His likely response:**
- "I need to think about it" (EXPECTED)
- "I'm still looking for more like €150K" (GOOD—shows he's negotiating, not dismissing)
- Asks specific questions about terms (EXCELLENT—he's engaged)
- "I need to talk to my accountant" (EXPECTED—due diligence)

**"Before we wrap up, let me be clear on next steps. I'm ready to move on my schedule. Here's what I need from you:**

1. **Due diligence documents** (revenue verification, equipment financing, lease copy) — by end of week
2. **Equipment appraisal** — I'll hire independent appraiser, costs ~€1,500, split 50/50
3. **Landlord signal** — I'll contact your landlord to confirm assignment is possible
4. **Verification meeting** — Week 2, I bring appraisal results and verified data, we move to next offer tier

Is that timeline workable for you?"

**His response signals readiness:**
- "Yes, I'll get you those documents" (READY)

### 5.4 Trial Close 3: Anchoring Next Step

At that second meeting, I'll have appraisal results and verified numbers, and I'll move my offer to the next tier. Sound fair?"**

**Get agreement:** "Yep, that works" or "I have questions about X"

**Don't push. Don't lower offer to close.**

**Instead:**

**Why this works:**
