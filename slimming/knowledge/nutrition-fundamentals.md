## Protein: The Foundation

### Why Protein Comes First

Every main meal is anchored around a strong protein source to support:
- **Satiety** — You actually feel full, not just fed
- **Lean tissue retention** — You preserve muscle during weight loss
- **Metabolic health** — Protein costs energy to digest (thermic effect)
- **Stable appetite** — Reduces afternoon crashes and cravings
- **Nutrient density** — Protein-rich foods carry important nutrients

### Visual Rule (Easy to Remember)

**1 to 2 palm-sized portions of protein at each main meal**

What's a palm-sized portion?
- Your whole hand (open palm), thickness of your hand
- Roughly = 30-40g cooked protein
- Examples: chicken breast fist, fish fillet, steak, eggs, Greek yoghurt

### Protein Targets

**Clinical starting range:** 1.4–2.0g per kg of target body weight per day

**Example:**
- Target weight: 70kg → 98–140g protein daily
- Spread across meals: 30-40g lunch, 30-40g dinner, 20-40g snacks/breakfast

**Personalized by clinic based on:**
- Your appetite and how hungry you get
- Your body composition and training
- Your age and health status
- Any kidney or liver conditions

### Protein Sources

**Complete proteins (contain all amino acids):**
- Chicken, turkey, beef, pork, lamb
- Fish (salmon, white fish, tuna)
- Eggs
- Greek yogurt, cottage cheese
- Milk and cheese
- Plant-based: tofu, tempeh, legumes + grains together

**Plant-based proteins:**
- Legumes (lentils, chickpeas, beans)
- Nuts and seeds
- Soy products
- Whole grains (complement with legumes)

## The Points System: How We Track Without Stress

### The Rule and The One Exception

**Rule:** For most foods, **1 calorie = 1 point**

**Exception:** **Non-starchy vegetables = 0 points**

We treat vegetable calories as "free" because they:
- Help you feel full and satisfied
- Don't tighten your daily budget
- Provide fibre and nutrients
- Give you volume without restricting

**So yes, 1 calorie = 1 point... except we don't count vegetable points.**

### Why Points Instead of Calories?

Points feel less obsessive than calorie counting. It's a simple budget without the mental math on every item.

### Your Daily Points Budget

**Set by clinic based on:**
- Your current weight and target weight
- Your pace (how fast results come)
- Your activity level
- Your metabolism and hunger response

**Example ranges:**
- Conservative: 1200-1400 points daily
- Moderate: 1400-1600 points daily
- Less aggressive: 1600-1800 points daily

**Your clinic sets your specific target. Start with it. Adjust if constantly hungry or not losing weight.**

## What Counts as Points

These foods and extras count toward your daily points:

| Category | Examples | Status |
|----------|----------|--------|
| **Protein foods** | Meat, fish, eggs, dairy, yogurt, protein powder, legumes | ✅ COUNT |
| **Carbohydrates** | Bread, rice, pasta, cereal, potatoes, sweets, fruit | ✅ COUNT |
| **Fats** | Nuts, seeds, avocado, butter, cheese, oils | ✅ COUNT |
| **Sauces, dressings, spreads** | Olive oil, mayo, salad dressing, sauces | ✅ COUNT |
| **Drinks with sugar or milk** | Sweet coffee, lattes, juice, soft drinks, alcohol | ✅ COUNT |
| **Non-starchy vegetables** | Leafy greens, peppers, tomato, mushrooms, broccoli, courgette, aubergine | ❌ FREE |

## What Does NOT Count as Points

### Non-Starchy Vegetables (Free!)

**These are zero points:**
- Leafy greens (spinach, lettuce, kale, rocket)
- Peppers (red, green, yellow)
- Tomato, cucumber
- Mushrooms
- Broccoli, cauliflower, cabbage
- Courgette, aubergine
- Celery, radish
- Green beans, asparagus

**Why vegetables are powerful:**
- **Volume** — Makes your plate look and feel like "enough"
- **Fibre** — Supports appetite control and digestion
- **Satisfaction** — Makes the plan feel easier to follow
- **No budget impact** — Eat as much as you want

**Use vegetables to make meals bigger, calmer, and more filling.**

### Starchy Vegetables (They DO Count)

**These count as points:**
- Potatoes (any type)
- Corn
- Peas
- Large portions of legumes/beans/lentils (they're both protein and carbs)
- Sweet potatoes

**When in doubt: If it's starchy, count it as points.**

## The Liquid Points Trap

### The Problem

**Liquid points do not feel like food, but they still count.**

They can push you over your daily budget without giving you the fullness you expected.

**Common pattern:** "I didn't eat that much, but progress slowed." Reality: liquid points added up.

### Common Liquid Points That Quietly Add Up

- Sweet/flavored coffee (lattes, syrups, creamy drinks)
- Juices and smoothies (even "healthy" ones)
- Soft drinks with sugar
- Alcohol and mixers
- Milk added generously to coffee
- "Healthy" creamy drinks (look light, carry lots of points)
- Protein shakes with added milk

### Simple Swaps to Stay On Track

- **Water or sparkling water** ✅
- **Black coffee or plain tea** ✅
- **Coffee with small splash of milk** ✅ (measured, small)
- **Zero-sugar soft drinks** ✅ (occasionally, if it helps consistency)
- **Alcohol:** Simple, no sugary mixers, counted as part of dinner points ✅

**This isn't about being strict. It's about removing sneaky things that make you feel like the plan isn't working when the plan is actually fine.**

## Fibre Floor: The Missing Piece

### Why Fibre Matters

Protein is the anchor. **Fibre is what makes meals feel complete.**

Fibre:
- Slows down digestion (keeps meals satisfying longer)
- Supports digestion and gut health
- Reduces the "empty again" feeling after eating
- Helps you feel genuinely "fed," not just full

### The Fibre Target

**Aim for 25–30 grams per day**

This doesn't need to be obsessive. You just need to build it into your regular eating.

### Two Easy Ways to Hit Your Fibre Floor

**1. Build a vegetable base at lunch and dinner**
- Aim for 2 fists of vegetables at most main meals
- Cooked or raw both count
- This is the easiest fibre win

**2. Use fibre-smart carbs a few times per week**
- Beans and lentils (also protein)
- Chickpeas
- Oats
- Potatoes with the skin on
- Whole grains (when tolerated)

### If Fibre Causes Bloating

- Increase slowly (not all at once)
- Drink more water
- Your body adapts
- Your clinic can guide you based on tolerance

## Real-Life Examples

### Example 1: A Normal Lunch at Home

```
Grilled chicken breast (40g protein) — 200 points
Big salad (lettuce, tomato, cucumber, peppers) — 0 points
2 tbsp olive oil dressing — 180 points
Whole grain bread on the side — 100 points

TOTAL: 480 points
VEGETABLES: Free volume, makes the plate feel bigger
```

**Why this works:** Protein anchors, vegetables fill you up without budget impact, points are reasonable.

### Example 2: A Social Dinner (Maltese Context)

```
You're out for pasta. You want to enjoy it without stress.

Start with protein first (few bites of meat/fish) — counted
Add vegetables if there (salad starter) — 0 points
Enjoy the pasta as your main carb — counted
Avoid stacking extras mindlessly (bread basket, creamy sauces, dessert on autopilot)

DIFFERENCE: "I enjoyed it and stayed on plan" vs "I ruined it"
```

**Why this structure matters:** You're not depriving. You're being intentional. Huge difference psychologically.

### Example 3: Breakfast (Non-Fasting Track)

```
2 eggs scrambled — 150 points
1 slice whole grain toast — 100 points
Sliced tomato, mushrooms (free) — 0 points
Black coffee or tea — 0 points

TOTAL: 250 points
VEGETABLES: Free, adds volume and nutrition
```

**Why this works:** High protein keeps you full, vegetables add volume, reasonable points.

## Food Categories & Decision-Making

### Proteins (All Count)

**Animal-based:**
- Beef, pork, lamb, chicken, turkey
- Fish (salmon, tuna, white fish)
- Eggs
- Dairy (yogurt, cheese, milk)

**Plant-based:**
- Tofu, tempeh
- Legumes (lentils, chickpeas, beans)
- Nuts and seeds

**Processed:**
- Protein powder
- Deli meats (processed, but protein)
- Plant-based meat alternatives

### Carbohydrates (All Count)

**Whole grains:**
- Brown rice, oats, barley
- Whole grain bread
- Whole wheat pasta

**Refined carbs:**
- White bread, white rice
- Regular pasta
- Cereal, crackers

**Vegetables (starchy, count):**
- Potatoes (white, sweet, any type)
- Corn, peas

**Fruits:**
- Apples, bananas, berries
- Oranges, grapes
- All fruits count as points

**Sweets:**
- Sugar, honey, syrup
- Chocolate, candy, biscuits
- All desserts count

### Fats (All Count)

**Healthy fats:**
- Olive oil, avocado, nuts, seeds
- Fatty fish (omega-3 rich)

**All fats count the same:**
- Whether olive oil or butter, same points per gram
- Fats add flavour and satisfaction
- Keep them intentional, not accidental

**High-fat foods:**
- Nuts (high calorie density)
- Oils (very high density)
- Cheese (high density)
- Full-fat dairy

## Common Questions About Nutrition

### "Can I eat bread/pasta/rice?"
Yes. No foods are forbidden. You just count the points. Real life includes bread and pasta, especially in Malta. The question is how much and how often, adjusted to your points budget.

### "What if I don't like [vegetable]?"
Pick different ones. You need vegetables for volume and fibre, but not specific ones. If you hate broccoli, eat peppers. The goal is volume, not specific foods.

### "Do I have to track everything?"
Your clinic will tell you. Some people count every point; some estimate. Both work. Find what feels sustainable to you. Obsessive tracking often leads to quitting.

### "What about eating out?"
Estimate the points. Protein-first approach applies. You don't need to be perfect; you need to be consistent. One restaurant meal isn't failure.

### "Is there a list of approved/forbidden foods?"
No. This isn't a restrictive diet. Everything has points. Some foods are calorie-dense (small portions), some are calorie-light (bigger portions). Your budget works with foods you actually eat.

### "What if I go over my points budget?"
Normal. Once in a while, no problem. If it's constant, you need to adjust with clinic. Going over today doesn't break the system; returning to budget tomorrow does.

## When to Refer to Clinic

Nutrition questions requiring professional guidance:
- Personalized protein targets
- Kidney, liver, or other health conditions affecting intake
- Medications affecting metabolism
- Food allergies or intolerances
- Pregnancy or breastfeeding
- Vegan/vegetarian protein balancing
- Persistent hunger despite adjustments
- Progress plateau with unclear cause
- Sports/athletic nutrition requirements

**Don't personalize nutrition targets. Always refer to clinic for individual adjustments.**
