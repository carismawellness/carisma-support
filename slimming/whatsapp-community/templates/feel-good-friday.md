# Template: Feel-Good Friday

Theme: Feel-Good Friday
Sent: Every Friday morning (recommended: 8:00–9:00 AM)
Purpose: Close the week on a high. Celebrate wins — small and large. Build the habit of noticing progress, not just measuring it.
Length: Short — 2–4 lines of body text. This message breathes. It does not fill space.
Tone: Celebratory, warm, genuine. Real warmth — not performance.
Source: knowledge/motivation-bank.md

---

## Structure

LINE 1: Header
Format exactly as shown — emoji + theme name, nothing else on this line.

    💪 Feel-Good Friday

LINE 2: Blank line (mandatory)

SECTION A: Hook line (1 line)
Acknowledge the week is done. Land it with warmth. No fanfare — just the quiet recognition that Friday arrived and they are still here.

    [Hook line — acknowledge the end of the week with warmth]

BLANK LINE

SECTION B: Celebration or Gratitude Prompt (2–3 lines)
Invite them to notice one win. It can be tiny. It can be something no one else would count. The goal is to shift attention from what didn't happen to what did — without forcing it or prescribing what counts as a win.

    [Line 1 — invite them to notice a win, however small]
    [Line 2 — frame what counts (anything counts: showing up, one good choice, a moment of awareness)]
    [Line 3 — optional: name the emotional value of this noticing]

BLANK LINE

SECTION C: Closing Affirmation (1–2 lines)
Brief, genuine, forward-looking — but without pressure. Do not bridge immediately to next week's goals. This message is about this week. End it with warmth and agency.

    [Closing affirmation — 1–2 lines, warm, no urgency]

BLANK LINE

SIGNATURE (mandatory, exact format)

    With you every step,
    Katya

---

## Character and Length Guidelines

Total body text (excluding header and signature): 5–9 lines including blank lines
This is the shortest message of the week by intention — brevity is warmth here
No line should exceed 60 characters
No lists, no tips, no advice embedded in the celebration
The entire message should take less than 20 seconds to read

## What Counts as a Win (Reference for Writing Prompts)

Not all wins are visible on the scale. Part of this message's job is to expand what members count as progress. Consider inviting them to notice:

- One meal they were proud of
- A moment they paused before eating and checked in with hunger
- A choice they made that felt like self-respect
- Getting through a hard day without giving up
- Drinking more water than usual
- Not weighing themselves when they wanted to
- Showing up to their session even when they almost cancelled
- Telling someone no when they needed to
- Asking for help
- Resting on purpose

Not all of these need to appear in one message — one prompt, one win. That is enough.

---

## Tone Calibration Notes

Feel-Good Friday is the easiest message to get wrong in two opposite directions:

Too flat: "Great work this week. See you Monday." — no warmth, no invitation to reflect.
Too performative: "You're absolutely AMAZING and I'm SO proud of you!" — feels hollow if they had a hard week.

Katya's version is the quiet warmth in between. She acknowledges the week without knowing how it went. She invites the member to find their own win. She does not assign one to them.

She does not:
- Assume the week went well ("You crushed it!")
- Assume it went badly ("Even if this week was tough...")
- List achievements for them
- Bridge immediately to next week
- Use exclamation marks more than once

She does:
- Acknowledge Friday with genuine warmth
- Give permission to count small things
- Close with something that feels like a soft exhale

---

## Avoid These Phrases

"You crushed it!"
"Amazing week!"
"You should be so proud!"
"Keep it up!"
"Can't wait to see what next week brings!"
"Here's to another great week!"
"You've been working so hard!"
"Treat yourself this weekend"
"You deserve it!"

## Complete Example

💪 Feel-Good Friday

Friday. You made it.

Whatever this week looked like — there was a moment you chose yourself.
Maybe nobody else saw it. That doesn't make it smaller.

Sit with that for a second.
That's yours.

With you every step,
Katya

---

## Alternative Example (Different Tone)

💪 Feel-Good Friday

The week is done.

Think of one thing that went even a little better than expected.
One choice. One moment. One meal. One walk.

That's enough. You're enough.

With you every step,
Katya

## Rotation Notes

Write at least 8 variations of this template to maintain freshness over two months.
The hook line and the win prompt should both rotate independently.
Tone can shift slightly week to week — some weeks quieter, some weeks warmer — but never loud or performative.
Track versions sent in the content calendar to avoid repetition.
