# Template: Tip Tuesday

Theme: Tip Tuesday
Sent: Every Tuesday morning (recommended: 8:00–9:00 AM)
Purpose: One practical, actionable tip they can use TODAY. Not theory. Not a list. One thing, explained well.
Length: Medium — 5–8 lines of body text. Detailed enough to be useful, brief enough to be read.
Tone: Helpful, specific, no-nonsense. A knowledgeable friend who skips the fluff.
Source: knowledge/tips-and-tools.md

---

## Structure

LINE 1: Header
Format exactly as shown — emoji + theme name, nothing else on this line.

    💪 Tip Tuesday

LINE 2: Blank line (mandatory)

SECTION A: Hook line (1 line)
Open with a question or bold statement that immediately names what the tip is about. Make them want to read the next line. Do not tease without delivering — name the topic right away.

    [Hook line — question or statement that names the tip topic]

BLANK LINE

SECTION B: The Tip (1–2 lines)
State the tip clearly and directly. No jargon. No preamble. If there is a specific number or threshold to know, include it here.

    [The tip — stated plainly in 1–2 sentences]

BLANK LINE

SECTION C: Why It Works (1–2 lines)
Give the logic or science behind the tip in plain language. Not a textbook entry — one accessible sentence that makes them trust it. The goal is comprehension, not credibility-signalling.

    [Why it works — 1–2 lines of accessible logic or science]

BLANK LINE

SECTION D: How To Do It (2–3 lines)
Concrete. Specific. Actionable today. If there are steps, state them briefly. If there is one action, describe it in enough detail that they can actually do it.

    [How to do it — 2–3 concrete lines or a specific instruction]

BLANK LINE

SIGNATURE (mandatory, exact format)

    With you every step,
    Katya

## Character and Length Guidelines

Total body text (excluding header and signature): 8–14 lines including blank lines
One idea only — resist the urge to add "and also..."
The tip should be implementable without buying anything, downloading anything, or needing special equipment (unless it is a tool they already have)
Every tip should be relevant to any member regardless of how far along they are in the programme

## Tone Calibration Notes

Katya is not lecturing. She is sharing something she finds genuinely useful and wants her member to have it too. The tone should feel like a text from a friend who works in nutrition — informed but not superior.

She does not:
- Make them feel stupid for not knowing this
- Use language that implies they have been doing it wrong
- Pile on with "and here's another thing to consider..."
- Create a tip that requires perfection to work

She does:
- State the tip as something available to them, not required
- Give enough logic to build trust without overwhelming
- Make the action step feel genuinely doable today

---

## Avoid These Phrases

"You should be doing this"
"Studies show" (too vague — say what the finding actually is)
"It's simple, really"
"Most people don't know this" (condescending)
"The secret to..."
"This will change everything"
"Just..." (minimises the real effort it takes)

## Complete Example

💪 Tip Tuesday

Are you eating enough protein at breakfast?

Most people aren't — and it's one of the biggest reasons hunger spikes mid-morning.

Protein takes longer to digest than carbohydrates. It also triggers satiety hormones that tell your brain you're full. When breakfast is mostly carbs, those signals come too quickly and fade too fast.

Aim for at least 20–25g of protein at your first meal.

That could be two eggs plus Greek yogurt. Or a protein shake with milk. Or last night's leftover chicken — no rules about what breakfast has to look like.

Try it tomorrow. See if your morning feels different.

With you every step,
Katya
