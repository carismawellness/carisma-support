# Template: What's Cooking Wednesday

Theme: What's Cooking Wednesday
Sent: Every Wednesday morning (recommended: 8:00–9:00 AM)
Purpose: Share a quick, protein-first, points-friendly recipe that feels genuinely appealing. Real food. Fast food. Food that fits the week.
Length: Medium — 8–12 lines including ingredients and method. Full recipe format.
Tone: Fun, warm, practical. Like texting a friend who actually cooks and knows what fits the plan.
Source: knowledge/recipe-bank.md

## Structure

LINE 1: Header
Format exactly as shown — emoji + theme name, nothing else on this line.

    💪 What's Cooking Wednesday

LINE 2: Blank line (mandatory)

SECTION A: Hook line (1 line)
Sensory or outcome-led. How it tastes. How fast it is. How it feels to eat something this satisfying that still fits the plan. Make them want to make it.

    [Hook line — sensory, outcome-led, or both]

BLANK LINE

SECTION B: Recipe Name (1 line)
Simple, appealing, not clinical. Avoid "healthy", "clean", "guilt-free", "low-calorie" in the name. Just call it what it is.

    [Recipe name]

BLANK LINE

SECTION C: Ingredients (bullet list)
Use • for each item. Include quantities. Keep the list to 6–8 ingredients maximum — this is a quick recipe. Note any smart swaps or substitutions inline if helpful.

    • [Ingredient + quantity]
    • [Ingredient + quantity]
    • [Ingredient + quantity]
    • [Ingredient + quantity]
    • [Ingredient + quantity]
    (add more as needed, maximum 8)

BLANK LINE

SECTION D: Method (numbered steps)
Maximum 4–5 steps. Each step is 1 sentence. If it cannot be done in 5 steps, simplify the recipe — don't extend the method. Total cook + prep time should be under 20 minutes where possible.

    1. [Step one]
    2. [Step two]
    3. [Step three]
    4. [Step four]
    5. [Step five — optional, only if needed]

BLANK LINE

SECTION E: Points and Macro Info (1–2 lines)
Keep this factual and brief. Include approximate calories per serving and a note on protein content. If using a points system, include the points value. Do not moralize about the numbers.

    [Approximate calories or points per serving]
    [Protein note — e.g., "Packs about 35g of protein per bowl"]

BLANK LINE

SECTION F: Optional Swap (1 line, only if genuinely useful)
One easy substitution for a common dietary need or ingredient availability. Do not include if forced.

    [Optional: Swap [ingredient] for [alternative] if [reason]]

BLANK LINE

SIGNATURE (mandatory, exact format)

    With you every step,
    Katya

## Character and Length Guidelines

Total body text (excluding header and signature): 12–18 lines including blank lines
Ingredients: 5–8 items maximum
Method steps: 4–5 maximum
Total prep + cook time: ideally under 20 minutes, stated in the hook if a key selling point
Recipe should serve 1–2 people (individual portions, not batch cooking)
Batch cooking recipes can be noted as "makes 4 portions" but should still be simple

## Tone Calibration Notes

This is the most format-heavy message of the week. Even so, Katya's warmth should come through in the hook and closing. The recipe is the content. The voice is the container.

She does not:
- Use "guilt-free" or "clean" or "healthy" to describe the food
- Imply that eating this is virtuous and eating other things is not
- Overcomplicate a recipe in the name of nutrition
- Make the swap suggestion feel like a correction

She does:
- Make the food sound genuinely good
- Keep the method honest about time required
- Trust members to adapt to their own kitchen and preferences
- Choose recipes that feel satisfying, not depriving

## Language for Points and Macros

Say this: "Around 380 calories per serving"
Not this: "Only 380 calories!" (the word "only" moralises the number)

Say this: "Packs around 32g of protein"
Not this: "Packed with protein!" (vague)

Say this: "Roughly 6 Slimming World syns"
Not this: "A total bargain at just 6 syns!" (performative)

## Complete Example

💪 What's Cooking Wednesday

Ten minutes. One pan. Dinner done.

Halloumi and Chickpea Stir-Fry

• 100g halloumi, sliced
• 1 can chickpeas, drained and rinsed
• 1 red pepper, sliced
• 1 handful baby spinach
• 1 tbsp olive oil
• 1 tsp smoked paprika
• Salt and pepper to taste

1. Heat the oil in a pan over medium-high heat.
2. Add the halloumi and cook 2 minutes each side until golden.
3. Add chickpeas and pepper, toss with paprika, cook 4–5 minutes.
4. Remove from heat, stir through spinach until just wilted.
5. Season and serve straight from the pan.

Around 420 calories per serving.
Packs roughly 25g of protein — enough to keep you full into the evening.

Swap halloumi for firm tofu if you're going dairy-free.

With you every step,
Katya
