**Your Five Original Questions:**
1. Revenue breakdown (spa vs. aesthetics)?
2. Staffing costs and challenge areas?
3. Rent & landlord relationship?
4. Equipment financing or other liabilities?
5. Why now? (What changed?)

**NEW: Interest Diagnosis Questions (Ask naturally):**
6. "When would you ideally be completely done with this?"
   → Listen: Is timeline urgent? Leverage for you.
7. "Beyond the purchase price, what makes this a good outcome?"
   → Listen: Peace of mind? Reputation? Debt relief?

**What You're Really Doing:** Identifying his BATNA (liquidate, hold, hire operator, find another buyer). His BATNA determines his walk-away price.

**Take visible notes.** Shows you're serious.

## ACT 2: REFRAME VALUE (20-30 min)

**BEFORE YOUR P&L SCRIPT — Emotional Calibration (30-60 sec)**

**TECHNIQUE 1: MIRROR**
- He says: "I invested €1M."
- You say: "€1 million?"
- Result: He elaborates. You learn more. He feels heard.

**TECHNIQUE 2: LABEL**
- Observe emotion. Name it.
- "Sounds like you have a lot invested here—financially and personally."
- Result: Defensiveness drops. He opens up.

**TECHNIQUE 3: ACCUSATION AUDIT**
- State the hard thing he's thinking, on your terms first.
- "I know it might sound like I'm lowballing, but..."
- Result: You own the objection. He's less defensive.

---

**THEN DEPLOY YOUR REFRAME SCRIPTS**

**IF HE ANCHORS TO €1M INVESTED:**

> "That €1M is already spent. Markets price based on what the business earns TODAY, not what was spent. You could liquidate for €100K. I'm offering €120K and taking the lease and liabilities. That's €20K better than your next option."

**IF PRICE IS STUCK (Tired, Emotional, Circular):**

> "Here's what I see. This business is bleeding your time and energy every month. That's a loss. What I'm offering is to STOP that loss. €120K, we're done. No more stress. You preserve the reputation you built. That matters more than another €10K. Can we agree on that?"

**OPENING MOVE:**
"I want to make you an offer. Based on current business performance, I'm opening at €72,000 conditional on: (1) equipment appraisal, (2) landlord confirms lease assignment."

**RESPONSE PLAYBOOK (If He Objects):**

| If He Says... | You Say... |

## IF CONVERSATION BREAKS DOWN (State Your Path Protocol)

**Use only if:** Seller is defensive, angry, silent, or says "I don't think we can do business."

**STEP 1: PAUSE**
"Let me stop here. I think we might be talking past each other. I don't want to waste your time."

**STEP 2: STATE YOUR PATH (90 seconds, transparent)**
"Let me be direct. I came here because I believe in this business. But I have responsibility to my investors, so I use market standards for valuation—current earnings, not historical capex. I opened at €45K because that's fire-sale equipment value. You asked €250K. I moved to €120K because that's 1x EBITDA—actually a premium. I'm not trying to steal this. Here's my logic. What's your thinking on valuation?"

**STEP 3: LISTEN**
Don't interrupt. He'll elaborate, likely revealing new information or softening stance.

**STEP 4: RESET FRAME (If dialogue resumes)**
"I think we both agree this is valuable. We just disagree on price. How would you want to structure this so we both feel good about it?"

**If He Still Says He's Done:** Don't chase. Walk away calmly.
"I respect that. I think we see value differently. You have my number if you reconsider."

Wait 24-48 hours. Then brief email per Sheet 8 protocol.

## ACT 4: CLOSE OR EXIT (10-15 min)

**TRIAL CLOSE (Loss Aversion Frame):**

> "We're very close. I can feel it. Here's what I want: I want to put this behind you. €120K, we're done. Forty-five day close. I take the lease and liabilities. You have certainty. The stress stops. That's a good outcome. Can we agree on that?"

[**SHUT UP. Wait for his response. Don't fill silence.**]

**If He Says Yes:**
"Perfect. Let's have my solicitor contact you for due diligence."

**If He Hesitates:**
"What would need to happen for you to say yes to this?"

[Listen. Solve for the real objection—usually terms, not price.]

**If He Says No:**
"I respect that. I think we see value differently. I'll move on calmly. If you reconsider, you have my number."

[Then actually leave. Walk away with dignity.]

## WHAT NOT TO SAY

- ❌ "That's a sunk cost fallacy" (never use psychology jargon)
- ❌ "Every other seller accepted this" (lie)
- ❌ "I have another buyer waiting" (unless true, and verify)
- ❌ "Take it or leave it" (aggressive, triggers defensiveness)
- ❌ "€200K is my walk-away" (never disclose your ceiling)
- ❌ Long justifications (short, calm statements)
