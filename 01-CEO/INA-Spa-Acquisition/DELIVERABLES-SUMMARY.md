## KEY INSIGHTS ACROSS ALL DOCUMENTS

### **Insight 1: Emotion First, Logic Second**
Your current briefing leads with numbers. The frameworks show seller's €250K anchor is emotional (sunk cost fallacy, ego, fear of loss). Address emotion FIRST (mirror, label, validate), THEN introduce numbers. Effectiveness jumps from 10% to 90%.

### **Insight 2: Loss Aversion > Gain Motivation**
Seller isn't excited about getting €250K. He's stressed about LOSING €1M and facing uncertainty. Reframe your €120K as "ending the stress and giving certainty." That's more motivating than any logical argument.

### **Insight 3: Interests ≠ Positions**
His position: €250K. His interests: likely speed, certainty, relief from daily stress, dignity, reputation. Two new questions in Act 1 uncover these. Once you know his interests, you can reframe your offer to address them instead of just fighting over price.

### **Insight 4: His BATNA Determines Leverage**
If his best alternative is liquidation (€100K) or holding (€30K/year stress), your €120K is strong. If his best alternative is another buyer at €200K, you may need to walk. Diagnose in Act 1 which scenario he's in.

### **Insight 5: Conversation Breakdowns Are Recoverable**
When seller gets defensive, silent, or angry—don't escalate. Use STATE your path: transparent explanation of your framework. Disarms defensiveness. Re-establishes dialogue. Most deals that die do so here; this protocol saves them.
