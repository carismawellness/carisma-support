# BCG Senior Partner Advisory: Carisma Wellness Group

**Date:** 2026-03-24
**Revenue baseline:** €3.3M/yr (€275k/mo net, ex-VAT)
**Brands:** Carisma Spa & Wellness | Carisma Aesthetics | Carisma Slimming
**Locations:** 8 spas

---

## 1. Org Design: "Strategic Control" Model

Carisma should adopt the **Strategic Control** archetype — centralized strategy and brand standards with decentralized service delivery.

### Centralize (HQ-Controlled)
- Brand strategy and creative direction
- Marketing spend allocation and campaign management
- Pricing strategy (service menu, packages, promotions)
- CRM and lead management
- Procurement (leverage total group volume)
- Financial reporting and EBITDA calculation
- HR policies, contracts, compliance
- Technology stack decisions
- Hotel partner relationship management

### Decentralize (Location/Region-Controlled)
- Therapist scheduling and shift management
- Service delivery standards (within brand guidelines)
- Local upselling and cross-selling
- Day-to-day customer experience decisions
- Inventory ordering (within approved supplier list)
- Staff development and coaching

---

## 2. Key Design Principles

**Principle 1: CEO removal from the execution layer.** Mert's role must shift from "doing" to "deciding." CEO should spend 80% of time on: (a) hotel partner relationship management, (b) capital allocation across brands/locations, (c) AI/CI system development.

**Principle 2: Create the "Head of Growth" role.** Single most important hire. Owns full revenue funnel: marketing strategy, CRM performance, brand development, pricing. Budget: €3,500-4,500/month.

**Principle 3: Formalize Regional Manager structure.** Neli, Kristina, Melanie already functioning as mini-GMs. Give each: (a) clear P&L target, (b) authority over scheduling and minor cost decisions, (c) performance bonus tied to location EBITDA.

**Principle 4: Evolve business support.** Finance, HR, and AI/technology under "Head of Business Support" — initially fractional, maturing to full-time COO/CFO hybrid at €4.5M+ revenue.

## Final Observation

The binding constraint is not strategy, capital, or market opportunity. **It is organizational capacity.** The CEO has been the bottleneck precisely because the CEO was the best person to do everything — and that was true during the build phase. It is no longer true for the scale phase.

The business has built the engine. Now it needs to stop having the engineer also drive the car.
