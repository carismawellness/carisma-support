# Carisma Wellness Group: Strategic Revenue Analysis
## Prepared by BCG Strategy Practice | March 2026

---

# Executive Summary

Carisma Wellness Group has achieved remarkable revenue growth from EUR 673,694 in 2014 to EUR 3,300,050 in 2025 (through December projections), representing a **15.6% CAGR over 11 years** -- exceptional performance in hospitality wellness. However, this growth masks critical strategic vulnerabilities: over-concentration in a single property, an underdeveloped e-commerce channel, rapid portfolio expansion without clear unit economics, and a revenue reporting infrastructure that cannot support data-driven decision-making at the scale the business now demands.

The current revenue sheet is a liability, not an asset. It contains valuable 12-year longitudinal data buried under layers of inconsistent formatting, fragmented sections, and absent analytical frameworks. This document provides a comprehensive restructuring roadmap.

# 1. Strategic Assessment

## 1.1 Revenue Trajectory Analysis

### Total Group Revenue (EUR)

| Year | Total Revenue | YoY Growth | Active Hotels |
|------|-------------|------------|---------------|
| 2014 | 673,694 | -- | 3 (Inter, Seashells, Qawra) |
| 2015 | 768,122 | 14.0% | 3 |
| 2016 | 937,924 | 22.1% | 3 |
| 2017 | 1,069,485 | 14.0% | 4 (+Ramla) |
| 2018 | 1,280,256 | 19.7% | 4 |
| 2019 | 1,466,361 | 14.5% | 4 |
| 2020 | 590,662 | -59.7% | 6 (+Hugo's, Hyatt) -- COVID |
| 2021 | 1,031,241 | 74.6% | 7 (+Online, Spa Club) |
| 2022 | 1,447,978 | 40.4% | 5 (restructured) |
| 2023 | 1,980,938 | 36.8% | 8 (+Labranda, Sunny, Aesthetics) |
| 2024 | 2,549,050 | 28.7% | 8 (Riviera replaced Labranda) |
| 2025 | 3,300,050 | 29.5% | 10 (+Excelsior, Novotel) |

**Key Insight:** The business has nearly **5x'd revenue** over the full period. Excluding the COVID anomaly (2020), the compound growth rate is approximately 17-18%. Post-COVID recovery has been exceptionally strong, with the business reaching 2x its pre-COVID peak by 2024.

### Pre-COVID vs. Post-COVID Comparison

- **Pre-COVID peak (2019):** EUR 1,466,361 across 4 hotels = EUR 366,590/hotel
- **2024 actual:** EUR 2,549,050 across 8 revenue centers = EUR 318,631/center
- **2025 projected:** EUR 3,300,050 across 10+ revenue centers = EUR 300,005/center

**Critical Finding:** While total revenue has grown dramatically, **revenue per unit has actually declined by ~18%** from peak levels. Growth has been driven primarily by portfolio expansion (adding hotels), not by deepening revenue per location. This is a classic volume-over-value trap that must be addressed.

## 1.2 BCG Growth-Share Matrix: Hotel Portfolio Analysis

Using 2024 full-year data and available 2025 partial data for growth rates:

### Stars (High Growth, High Share)
- **InterContinental (5-star):** EUR 679,994 in 2024 (26.7% of total). Consistently the #1 property. 2025 trending at ~EUR 700K+. The flagship anchor of the portfolio.
- **Hugo's:** EUR 569,173 in 2024 (22.3%). Strong growth trajectory. Second-largest contributor. Has grown from zero (2020 launch) to the second most important property in 4 years.

### Cash Cows (Low Growth, High Share)
- **Hyatt:** EUR 363,260 in 2024 (14.2%). Stable performer, third-largest revenue contributor. Steady but growth has plateaued around the EUR 350-370K range.
- **Ramla:** EUR 299,863 in 2024 (11.8%). Entered portfolio in 2017. Reliable mid-tier performer with moderate but decelerating growth.

### Question Marks (High Growth, Low Share)
- **Excelsior:** New in 2025. Early data (Jan-Dec partial) suggests EUR 171,775 annualized. High strategic importance given the Excelsior hotel's market positioning. Needs 12-18 months to evaluate.
- **Aesthetics:** EUR 154,727 in 2024. This is not a hotel spa -- it is a new business line. Growing rapidly but still small. Could be the next major growth vector.
- **Riviera:** EUR 207,751 in 2024. First full year. Solid ramp. Need to monitor whether it can break EUR 250K.
- **Sunny Coast:** EUR 233,986 in 2024. Good ramp from first year. Trending above Riviera.

### Dogs (Low Growth, Low Share)
- **Seashells:** Was a top-3 property pre-2020 (peaked at EUR 326K in 2019). Dropped from portfolio or significantly de-emphasized post-COVID.
- **Qawra:** Similarly, peaked at EUR 167K in 2015 and declined to EUR 81K by 2019 before being dropped.
- **Novotel:** New in 2025 with very small revenue (EUR 32,979 in partial year data). Too early to judge.
- **Spa Club:** EUR 40,296 in 2024. Membership revenue is valuable for its recurring nature but the absolute amount is surprisingly low.

## 1.3 Revenue Concentration Risk

| Property | 2024 Share | Risk Level |
|----------|-----------|------------|
| InterContinental | 26.7% | HIGH -- single-property dependency |
| Hugo's | 22.3% | MODERATE -- strong #2 |
| Top 2 combined | 49.0% | CRITICAL -- half of revenue in 2 hotels |
| Top 3 combined (+ Hyatt) | 63.2% | VERY HIGH |

**Strategic Risk:** Nearly half the business depends on two hotel relationships. If the InterContinental contract were lost or renegotiated unfavorably, the group would lose over a quarter of its revenue.

**Mitigation Recommendation:** No single property should exceed 20% of group revenue by 2028.

# 2. Strategic Frameworks to Add to the Sheet

## 2.1 BCG Growth-Share Matrix (Quarterly Update)

- **X-axis:** Relative market share (property revenue as % of group total)
- **Y-axis:** Revenue growth rate (trailing 12-month YoY)
- **Bubble size:** Absolute revenue
- **Color coding:** Green (Star), Blue (Cash Cow), Yellow (Question Mark), Red (Dog)

## 2.2 Revenue Decomposition: Volume vs. Price vs. Mix

- **Volume effect:** Change in number of treatments/bookings delivered
- **Price effect:** Change in average transaction value (ATV)
- **Mix effect:** Shift between high-value and low-value services

## 2.3 Seasonality Index by Hotel

**Formula:** `Seasonality Index = (Month Revenue / Annual Revenue) * 12`
- Index > 1.0 = above-average month
- Index < 1.0 = below-average month

## 2.4 Hotel Lifecycle Stage Mapping

| Stage | Properties | Strategy |
|-------|-----------|----------|
| **Launch (Year 0-1)** | Excelsior, Novotel | Invest in awareness, build team, accept lower margins |
| **Growth (Year 1-3)** | Riviera, Sunny, Aesthetics | Scale rapidly, optimize operations, push for breakeven |
| **Maturity (Year 3-6)** | Hugo's, Hyatt | Maximize yield, focus on upselling and retail attachment |
| **Established (Year 6+)** | InterContinental, Ramla | Defend market share, innovate service mix, harvest cash |
| **Declined/Exited** | Seashells, Qawra, Labranda | Post-mortem analysis for learnings |

## 2.5 Revenue per Available Treatment Hour (RevPATH)

**Formula:** `RevPATH = Total Revenue / (Treatment Rooms * Operating Hours * Operating Days)`

# 8. Final Observations and Strategic Recommendations

## The Big Picture

Carisma has executed an impressive growth strategy (3 hotels/EUR 674K to 10+ locations/EUR 3.3M in 11 years). The post-COVID pivot was strategically sound.

The next phase of value creation must come from:
1. **Deepening revenue per property** (EUR 700K+ target for top 3)
2. **Scaling Aesthetics** (higher-margin, less dependent on hotel foot traffic)
3. **Building recurring revenue** (Spa Club from EUR 40K to EUR 200K+)
4. **Reviving e-commerce** (gift cards, product sales, online booking)
5. **Operational excellence** (utilization rates, therapist productivity, treatment-level economics)
