# Carisma Wellness Group -- Retail Product Sales Analysis

**Prepared:** 26 March 2026
**Purpose:** Establish retail performance baseline for Valmont distribution business case
**Data Sources:** Weekly KPIs Google Sheet (Jan 2024 -- Dec 2025), Accounting Master P&L (2024--2025)
**Period:** Full-year 2024 and full-year 2025

---

## Executive Summary

Carisma Wellness Group operates luxury spas inside 10 of Malta's premier hotels, serving an estimated **120,000--145,000 spa visits annually** (derived from approximately 2,300--2,800 weekly transactions across all locations). These guests represent the core addressable audience for in-spa retail.

In 2025, the group generated **EUR 124,922 in retail product revenue (ex VAT)** across its spa network -- representing **5.4% of total service sales** (EUR 2.32M). This is materially below the internal target of 12% set by the Spa Director. The gap to target represents an incremental **EUR 153,000--172,000** in annual retail revenue at current sales volumes -- a gap that a premium brand like Valmont could partially close through higher average transaction values.

Retail grew 18.5% year-over-year (from EUR 105,457 in 2024), roughly tracking total sales growth of 19.8%. The retail share remained flat at 5.4% in both years, indicating that retail has grown proportionally but has not yet structurally improved as a percentage of the business. The current product portfolio (The Purest Solutions at EUR 20--60 and Phytomer at EUR 30--80) appears to have reached its conversion ceiling.

The group operates **10 spa locations** plus **8 external partner salons** and an **online Shopify store** -- a distribution network of 19+ points of sale that creates a compelling infrastructure for exclusive brand distribution in Malta.

**Key takeaway for Valmont:** Three locations already achieve 7--9% retail share, proving the operational model supports higher conversion. The gap is not capability -- it is product-market fit at the luxury price tier, where Carisma has no offering today.

### Location Analysis

**Top 3 performers (Odycy, Labranda, Hyatt):**
- These three locations consistently hit 7--9% retail share, demonstrating that the 12% target is operationally achievable within the existing management structure
- Odycy improved from 6.8% to 8.8% -- the strongest trajectory in the network
- Hyatt improved from 6.8% to 7.4% YoY, the most stable high performer

**InterContinental -- the biggest opportunity:**

The flagship 5-star property experienced a 20.6% retail decline in 2025, dropping from 5.0% to 3.9% retail share (EUR -5,728 YoY). This decline is significant but, viewed strategically, it reinforces the case for Valmont: the current product portfolio -- mid-range brands priced at EUR 30--80 -- does not match the expectations of InterContinental's 5-star international clientele. These guests routinely purchase EUR 200+ skincare at home and at comparable hotel spas globally. The product-market mismatch is the most likely explanation for the retail decline.

As the highest-revenue location (EUR 568K in service sales), even a small retail percentage improvement at InterContinental has outsized impact:
- At 7.5% retail share (matching Labranda), InterContinental would generate **EUR 42,630** -- nearly double its current output
- This single location represents the highest-leverage target for a Valmont launch: 5-star clientele, highest traffic, most aligned with luxury positioning

**Hugo's -- volume leader with momentum:**
- Highest absolute retail revenue (EUR 27,820) despite only 5.0% retail share
- Grew 40.2% YoY, the fastest-improving legacy location
- At 7.5% target, Hugo's would generate EUR 41,836 -- an incremental EUR 14,016

**New locations (Excelsior, Novotel):**
- Both launched in H2 2025 and are still ramping
- Retail % of 3.2--3.4% reflects early operational focus on services over retail
- Represent incremental distribution points for Valmont with minimal cannibalization risk

## 8. Key Findings for the Valmont Business Case

1. **The retail infrastructure exists.** 10 spa locations, 8 partner salons, Shopify, and the Aesthetics clinic provide 22 distribution points -- more than sufficient for exclusive Malta distribution.

2. **The retail share is stuck at 5.4%.** Two years of data show no structural improvement despite 18.5% revenue growth. The current brand portfolio has hit its ceiling. A premium brand with treatment integration could break through this barrier.

3. **Three locations prove 7--9% is achievable.** Odycy (8.8%), Labranda (7.5%), and Hyatt (7.4%) demonstrate that higher retail conversion is operationally possible. The gap is product-market fit, not capability.

4. **InterContinental is the highest-leverage target.** The flagship 5-star property generates EUR 568K in service sales but only 3.9% retail conversion. Its retail decline (-20.6% YoY) reflects a product-market mismatch that Valmont is positioned to solve. At 7.5% retail, this single location would generate EUR 42K -- nearly double its current EUR 22K.

5. **The partner network is underutilized.** Only 4 of 8 salon partners generated any TPS sales. These dormant relationships represent ready-made wholesale doors, but activating them will require dedicated account management.

6. **Valmont's higher price point changes the math.** If a Valmont product averages EUR 200 (vs EUR 40 for current products), the same number of units sold generates 5x the retail revenue.

7. **Seasonality favours a luxury play.** The summer tourist season (Jun--Sep) and holiday gifting period (Nov) are already the strongest retail months. International hotel guests in 5-star properties during peak season are the ideal Valmont buyer.

8. **Annual guest volume of 120K--145K provides a large addressable base.** Even converting an additional 1--2% of spa visits to retail purchases -- at Valmont's price points -- would meaningfully move the needle.
