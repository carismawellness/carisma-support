## Design and Production Notes

### Visual Identity
- **Primary palette:** Neutral luxury -- deep charcoal, warm white, muted gold accents
- **Typography:** Clean serif for headlines (suggesting heritage), modern sans-serif for body
- **Photography:** High-resolution images of Carisma spa locations (InterContinental, Hyatt, Excelsior). Mediterranean light. Warm tones.
- **Charts:** Minimal, clean. No 3D effects. Use Valmont's brand colours (gold, deep navy) sparingly as accent if permitted

### Presentation Flow Logic
The deck follows a deliberate persuasion arc:

1. **Slides 1-3 (Opportunity + White Space):** Create desire. Show Valmont what they are missing. The audience should feel the gap before we offer to fill it.
2. **Slides 4-6 (The Partner):** Build credibility. Answer "who are these people?" before the audience has to ask. Move from company overview to infrastructure to proven track record.
3. **Slide 7 (Why Carisma Wins):** Eliminate alternatives. Make it clear that even if Valmont wanted to enter Malta, there is no better option.
4. **Slides 8-9 (The Plan):** Show execution clarity. This is not a vague pitch -- it is an operational plan with phases, targets, and timelines.
5. **Slides 10-11 (The Numbers):** Quantify the value. Lead with what Valmont earns, then show Carisma's investment to demonstrate conviction.
6. **Slide 12 (Risk):** Remove objections. Address grey market, brand dilution, and underperformance before the audience raises them.
7. **Slide 13 (The Ask):** Be specific. No ambiguity about what is being requested.
8. **Slide 14 (Close):** Paint the vision. The vignette makes it tangible. The replicable model makes it strategic. The "60 days" makes it actionable.

### Delivery Guidance
- **Pace:** 2-3 minutes per slide. Total presentation: 30-35 minutes, leaving 20-25 minutes for discussion.
- **Tone shift:** Slides 1-3 should feel like market intelligence briefing (data-driven, objective). Slides 4-7 shift to confident but grounded company narrative. Slides 8-14 become collaborative -- "here is what we build together."
- **Avoid:** Overselling, desperation, or any language that positions Carisma as the smaller party asking for a favour. This is a partnership proposal between professionals.
- **Emphasise:** Zero risk to Valmont. Proven infrastructure. Contractual commitments. Brand protection.
