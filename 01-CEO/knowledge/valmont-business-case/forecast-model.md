## 5. Cost Model

### 5.1 COGS Structure

COGS varies by revenue stream due to different margin structures:

| Revenue Stream | COGS % of Revenue | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Own-Spa Retail | 55% | Internal transfer at 40% off retail (cost is 55% of retail, sell at 60% of retail to own spas) |
| Professional Treatments | 15% | Only product usage cost (EUR 32 per treatment / EUR 210 avg price = 15%); therapist labor is existing Carisma cost |
| B2B Wholesale | 73% | Buy at 55% of retail, sell at 75% of retail; margin = (0.75-0.55)/0.75 = 27% gross |
| Online / E-commerce | 55% | Buy at 55% of retail, sell at 100% of retail; gross margin = 45% |
| Aesthetics Clinic | 55% | Same as own-spa retail (internal transfer pricing) |

**Blended COGS % by scenario (Year 3):**
- Conservative: 33.0% (treatments-heavy mix drives lower blended COGS)
- Base Case: 31.8%

## Appendix C: Market Size Cross-Check

### Bottom-Up vs. Top-Down Revenue Validation (Base Case Year 3)

**Bottom-up model output:** EUR 1,193,075

**Top-down cross-checks:**

1. **TAM penetration:** EUR 1.19M / EUR 2.25M (midpoint TAM) = **52.9% market share** -- Aggressive but plausible given exclusive distribution and 10+ captive spa locations. Valmont would be the only ultra-premium physical retail presence in Malta.

2. **Revenue per tourist:** EUR 1.19M / 4,300,000 (est. 2028 tourists) = **EUR 0.28 per tourist** -- Very modest. Even 0.5% of spa-going tourists purchasing one product would generate more.

3. **Valmont global revenue share:** EUR 1.19M / CHF 300M (~EUR 312M) = **0.38%** -- Malta would represent 0.38% of Valmont's global revenue. With ~1,800 global points of sale, Carisma's 22 points would represent 1.2% of Valmont's distribution network generating 0.38% of revenue -- a reasonable ratio for a small market.

4. **Revenue per Carisma spa employee:** With approximately 100+ spa therapists, EUR 1.19M represents roughly EUR 12K per therapist per year in Valmont-attributable revenue (treatments + retail). This is approximately 1 Valmont facial per therapist per week plus minimal retail -- very achievable.

All cross-checks validate that the base case is ambitious but achievable. The model does not require unrealistic assumptions about consumer behavior, market size, or operational execution.
