### 1.1 Bank Statement Reconciliation [CRITICAL]

**Q1.1a:** Provide complete bank statements for ALL accounts held by Well Being Services Limited from January 2024 through February 2026. Include operating accounts, savings accounts, merchant settlement accounts, and any other accounts in the company's name. We require PDF exports directly from the banking portal, not hand-prepared summaries.

> **WHY IT MATTERS:** Bank statements are the single most reliable verification document. Cash does not lie. Total deposits must reconcile with reported revenue within 3-5%. If the seller reports EUR 430K revenue but bank deposits total EUR 360K, EUR 70K of revenue is fictitious. If deposits total EUR 500K, there is undeclared income.
>
> **GOOD ANSWER:** Bank deposits sum to EUR 410-450K (within 5% of reported revenue), with timing differences explained by card settlement lags. Monthly deposit pattern matches the seasonal revenue data already provided (low in July, high in Q1/Q4).
>
> **RED FLAG:** Deposits materially below reported revenue (>10% gap). Unexplained large cash deposits. Transfers between Well Being Services Ltd and the director's personal accounts. Regular fixed outflows that do not appear in the P&L (indicating hidden costs). Refusal to provide statements, or "the accountant has them" delays lasting more than 7 days.

**Q1.1b:** Identify the bank(s) where the business accounts are held. Is it BOV, APS, HSBC Malta, or another institution? How many separate accounts does the business maintain?

> **WHY IT MATTERS:** Businesses sometimes operate multiple accounts to separate operating cash from savings or payroll. If only one account is provided but the business operates two, a revenue or cost stream is being hidden.
>
> **GOOD ANSWER:** One operating account (e.g., BOV Business Current Account) plus one merchant settlement account. Simple, standard setup.
>
> **RED FLAG:** Multiple accounts with unexplained intercompany transfers. An account in a different entity name. Accounts at non-Maltese banks without clear justification.

**Q1.1c:** Does the Spa Director receive any payments from business accounts? If so, what is the amount, frequency, and description on the bank statements?

> **WHY IT MATTERS:** The director's wage was excluded from the P&L. Bank statements will reveal whether the director was actually paid from the business (excluded from P&L = deliberate manipulation) or was truly unpaid (which raises the question: who runs the business for free?). This is the smoking gun question.
>
> **GOOD ANSWER:** Director receives no payments from business accounts. Director draws dividends from company profits at year-end (which means the cost was borne at the company level but not shown in the operating P&L -- a presentation issue, not fraud).
>
> **RED FLAG:** Regular monthly payments of EUR 2,200-3,000 to the director appearing in bank statements but absent from the P&L wages line. Payments to a company controlled by the director (a related-party cost absorption arrangement).

### 1.2 VAT Return Cross-Reference [CRITICAL]

**Q1.2a:** Provide copies of all VAT returns (Form VP) filed by Well Being Services Limited for the period January 2024 through December 2025 -- minimum 8 quarterly returns. We need the actual submissions, not summaries.

> **WHY IT MATTERS:** Revenue reported to the Commissioner for Revenue is the most reliable figure because filing false VAT returns is a criminal offence under Article 76 of the VAT Act (Cap. 406). The sum of quarterly Box 1 (taxable supplies at standard rate) should approximately equal gross revenue inclusive of VAT. At EUR 430K ex-VAT, we expect ~EUR 507K in gross supplies across the year.
>
> **GOOD ANSWER:** VAT returns show quarterly supplies that sum to EUR 500-515K inclusive of VAT (consistent with EUR 425-435K ex-VAT). No penalty notices. Clean filing history.
>
> **RED FLAG:** Quarterly figures that do not sum to reported annual revenue (>5% discrepancy). Penalty notices from the Commissioner (indicating prior compliance problems). VAT returns showing EUR 350K when the seller tells buyers EUR 430K -- this means the seller is either defrauding the tax authority or inflating revenue to buyers.

**Q1.2b:** Does Well Being Services Limited file VAT returns electronically through the CFR portal? Has there been any VAT assessment, audit, or inquiry from the Commissioner for Revenue in the last 3 years?

> **WHY IT MATTERS:** Electronic filings produce an immutable audit trail. Any pending assessment or audit could result in a liability that transfers to the buyer in a share deal or could indicate broader compliance problems.
>
> **GOOD ANSWER:** Electronic filing. No assessments, no audits, no inquiries.
>
> **RED FLAG:** Manual filing (harder to verify). Outstanding assessment. Pending audit. "We are in discussions with the Commissioner" = there is a tax dispute.

**Q1.2c:** What is the VAT treatment of services provided to hotel guests through the Barcelo partnership? Are these treated as B2B supplies to the hotel (possibly at reduced or zero rate) or as B2C supplies directly to the guest?

> **WHY IT MATTERS:** If 22% of revenue (EUR 93K) flows through the hotel partnership, the VAT treatment could differ. If the hotel is the contractual counterparty (B2B supply), there may be invoicing and VAT accounting differences. Misclassification could create a retrospective VAT liability.
>
> **GOOD ANSWER:** All services are invoiced directly to the end customer at 18% VAT, regardless of hotel referral. Simple, clean.
>
> **RED FLAG:** Complex invoicing arrangement where the hotel is the customer and pays INA net of commission. This could create VAT output tax discrepancies and suggests revenue recognized by INA may differ from cash received.

**Q1.3a:** What booking/POS system does INA Spa use? > **WHY IT MATTERS:** The POS system is an independent record of actual business activity that captures data neither bank statements nor VAT returns show -- appointment counts, average ticket values, treatment mix, therapist productivity, no-show rates, and refund patterns.

**Q1.3b:** What is the refund and cancellation rate? Provide a summary of all refunds processed in 2025 with amounts and reasons.

> **WHY IT MATTERS:** Refunds above 2% of revenue indicate service quality issues, pricing disputes, or gift voucher problems. A refund rate of 5% on EUR 430K means EUR 21.5K was given back to customers -- effectively reducing real revenue.

> **GOOD ANSWER:** Refund rate below 1.5%. Refunds are rare and individually documented with clear reasons (customer dissatisfaction, double booking, medical contraindication).

> **RED FLAG:** No-show rate above 15% (capacity management problem). No-show rate materially different between POS data and seller's verbal claims. No tracking of no-shows at all.

# 10. OPERATIONAL PERFORMANCE

*The spa underperforms industry benchmarks on every metric: revenue per room, revenue per employee, utilization, average transaction value, wages ratio. These questions quantify the performance gaps and determine whether they are fixable.*

---

### 10.1 Capacity and Utilization [HIGH]

**Q10.1a:** What is the spa's capacity utilization rate? Provide the calculation: total treatment hours delivered / total available treatment hours, by room, for each month of 2025.

> **WHY IT MATTERS:** Estimated utilization is 45-53%, against a benchmark of 65-75%. Every 5 percentage points of utilization improvement at EUR 57 per treatment equates to approximately EUR 30K in annual incremental revenue. But we need the real number, not an estimate.
>
> **GOOD ANSWER:** Utilization data available from the booking system. Rate is 50-55% overall, with peak-hours utilization of 75%+ and off-peak of 30-40%. Clear opportunity to fill off-peak slots.
>
> **RED FLAG:** Utilization below 40% (fundamental demand problem). Peak hours not filling (even when clients want to book, they cannot fill 7 rooms). No utilization tracking (the business does not measure its most important operational metric).

**Q10.1b:** What are the spa's operating hours? How many treatment rooms are operational at any given time? Are all 7 rooms in active use, or are some permanently or temporarily closed?

> **WHY IT MATTERS:** If only 5 of 7 rooms are in regular use, the effective capacity is lower, and the revenue per room calculation changes. If 2 rooms are idle, there is no incremental CAPEX needed to increase capacity -- just fill the empty rooms.
>
> **GOOD ANSWER:** All 7 rooms in regular use. Operating hours: 9am-7pm (10-hour day), 7 days/week.
>
> **RED FLAG:** 1-2 rooms regularly idle (especially the wet area rooms -- hammam, sauna). Operating hours have been reduced (staffing shortage forcing reduced hours). Weekend hours limited.

---

### 10.2 Pricing Analysis [IMPORTANT]

**Q10.2a:** Provide the current treatment price list. When was it last updated? How do prices compare to direct competitors in Sliema/St. Julian's?

> **WHY IT MATTERS:** At EUR 57 average revenue per appointment, INA is 31-53% below European premium spa benchmarks. If laser treatments are priced at EUR 80 when the market rate is EUR 150-200, there is a massive repricing opportunity. But we need the actual price list to confirm.
>
> **GOOD ANSWER:** Price list provided. Prices last reviewed in 2025. Competitive within the Sliema market but room for 10-20% uplift on premium services (laser, facials, packages).
>
> **RED FLAG:** Prices have not been updated since opening. No competitive benchmarking has been done. Heavy reliance on discounts and promotions to drive volume (indicates weak brand positioning).

**Q10.2b:** What percentage of transactions involve a discount, promotional offer, or package rate? What is the average effective discount?

> **WHY IT MATTERS:** If 30% of transactions are discounted by an average of 25%, the published price is meaningless -- effective pricing is EUR 43 per appointment, not EUR 57. This tells us whether the pricing problem is about list prices or discount discipline.
>
> **GOOD ANSWER:** Less than 15% of transactions discounted. Average discount of 10-15%.
>
> **RED FLAG:** Over 30% of transactions discounted. Aggressive promotional pricing. "Buy 5 get 1 free" packages that permanently reduce effective revenue per treatment.

### 10.3 Customer Acquisition [IMPORTANT]

**Q10.3a:** What is the customer acquisition cost (CAC)? How do new clients find INA Spa -- hotel referral, walk-in, social media, Google search, word of mouth, or other?

> **RED FLAG:** Low GBP visibility (<1,000 monthly views). Poor rating (<4.0 stars). No management response to reviews.
