## SECTION 3: ATTACKING THE 3x MULTIPLE

### 3.1 Why 3x Is Inappropriate

The buyer's model applies a 3x EBITDA multiple, producing valuations of EUR 126-228K. This multiple is aggressively low and inappropriate for the following reasons:

**3x is a distressed multiple.** In M&A practice, 3x EBITDA is applied to:
- Businesses in financial distress or facing insolvency
- Businesses with declining revenue and contracting margins
- Businesses with no intellectual property, no brand value, and no customer loyalty
- Businesses with short remaining lease terms (1-3 years)
- Businesses in commoditized industries with no competitive moat

INA Spa is NONE of these things:
- Revenue grew 33% year-over-year
- The lease has 10-13 years remaining
- The business is located in a prime Sliema seafront hotel
- 78% of clientele is local (recurring revenue)
- Equipment is modern, high-quality, and only 2 years old
- The spa has a functioning booking system, trained staff, and proven demand

**Comparable spa transaction multiples:**

| Transaction Type | Typical Multiple | Applicable? |
|-----------------|-----------------|-------------|
| Distressed spa / liquidation | 1-2x EBITDA | NO -- INA is not distressed |
| Breakeven single-location spa | 3-4x EBITDA | Marginally -- but ignores growth and lease |
| Growing spa with long lease | 4-6x EBITDA | YES -- this is INA's profile |
| Spa with clinic conversion potential | 5-7x EBITDA | YES -- buyer plans exactly this |
| Strategic acquisition (synergy buyer) | 5-8x EBITDA | YES -- Carisma is a strategic buyer |

The 10-13 year lease inside Barcelo Fortina Hotel in Sliema is, by itself, worth EUR 100-150K minimum. Here is why:

**Scarcity:** How many hotel spa leases are available in Malta right now? Zero. The buyer cannot go negotiate one from scratch because hotels either operate their own spas or have existing long-term concession agreements. This lease is unique.

**Location:** Barcelo Fortina is on the Sliema seafront -- Malta's premium wellness corridor. It has street-level access (enabling walk-in traffic from locals, which is why 78% of clients are local) and hotel guest flow (providing a captive secondary market).

**Duration:** 10-13 years remaining. That is an income-generating runway that extends to 2036-2039. Any business plan, any investment thesis, any DCF model benefits enormously from a guaranteed right to operate for that duration.

**Below-market cost:** The total rent (fixed + 5% turnover) is approximately EUR 72-82K per year. Comparable commercial space in Sliema at this quality and location would cost EUR 85-110K per year. The lease saves the operator EUR 13-38K annually. Over the remaining term, the NPV of that saving (at 6% discount rate) is EUR 115-337K.

The buyer will frame the 5% turnover fee as "punitive" and "escalating." This is misleading.

**Industry context:** Hotel spa concession agreements globally typically charge 8-15% of revenue plus a minimum annual guarantee. INA's 5% rate is at the LOW end of the market. The seller negotiated a favorable deal.

**INA's total rent as a percentage of revenue (19%) is within the industry range** and actually favorable compared to many comparable arrangements.

**The seller's script:**

The rent you see is not high -- it is favorable. And it comes with a 13-year guaranteed tenancy in a prime Sliema hotel. Name another commercial property in Malta where you get that combination."

The lease is not a liability -- it is the foundation of a decade-long business. The buyer's model treats the lease purely as a cost. The seller's framing should treat it as an asset:

The buyer will highlight that the remaining lease requires approximately EUR 867K-1M in cumulative rent. This sounds large.

## APPENDIX D: THE PSYCHOLOGICAL PLAYBOOK

### Power Dynamics

**Signal 1: Not desperate.** Arrive calmly. Do not lean forward. Take your time. Convey through body language that you have 13 years of lease and no urgency to transact.

**Signal 2: Knowledgeable.** Reference specific data points. Cite the wage discrepancy. Mention the depreciation manipulation. Show you understand the model as well as they do.

**Signal 3: Other options.** Without lying, convey that other parties are interested: "We have had productive conversations with other qualified groups." This is the single most effective negotiation tool.

**Signal 4: Emotional anchoring.** At the right moment, share the personal story: "My partners and I invested EUR 857K and two years of our lives building this. We hired people, trained them, won clients one by one. I understand market value is different from invested capital. But you should understand what you are asking me to accept."

**Signal 5: Genuine respect.** Acknowledge Carisma's capabilities: "I know you could do extraordinary things with this location. That is why I want us to find a deal. But it has to be a deal that respects what we built."

### The One Thing to Remember

The buyer's strongest weapon is their model. The seller's strongest weapon is the truth: **this business works, it is growing, and the buyer wants it badly enough to spend months analyzing it.** The model is a story. The truth is that EUR 857K was invested, 33% revenue growth was achieved, and there are 10-13 years of guaranteed tenancy remaining. No model can erase those facts.
