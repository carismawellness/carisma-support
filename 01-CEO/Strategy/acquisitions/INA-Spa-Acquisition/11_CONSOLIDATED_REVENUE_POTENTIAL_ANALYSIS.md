## 1. SUPPLY-SIDE ANALYSIS (Theoretical Maximum)

### What Was Asked
"What is the maximum revenue this spa could generate with 7 treatment rooms + 1 hammam?"

### Methodology
- Calculate total available treatment hours per year
- Apply maximum realistic throughput per room
- Price at market rates for each service category

### Capacity Calculation

| Parameter | Value | Basis |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| Treatment rooms | 7 | Fixed facility constraint |
| Hammam | 1 | Dual-use (group sessions + private) |
| Operating hours/day | 10 (9am–7pm) | Standard hotel spa hours |
| Operating days/year | 312 | 6 days/week |
| Turnaround between clients | 15 min | Industry standard |
| Effective treatments/room/day | 8 | 10 hrs ÷ (60min avg + 15min turnover) |
| **Total available treatment slots/year** | **17,472** | 7 rooms × 8 × 312 |
| Hammam sessions/day | 6 | 90min sessions, group + private |
| **Hammam slots/year** | **1,872** | 6 × 312 |

### Revenue at Different Utilization Rates

| Utilization | Treatment Slots Used | Hammam Sessions | ATR | Revenue (Rooms) | Revenue (Hammam) | **Total** |
|------------|---------------------|-----------------|-----|----------------|-----------------|-----------|
| 100% (theoretical max) | 17,472 | 1,872 | EUR 100 | EUR 1,747K | EUR 421K | **EUR 2,168K** |
| 70% (strong performance) | 12,230 | 1,310 | EUR 100 | EUR 1,223K | EUR 295K | **EUR 1,518K** |
| 50% (good performance) | 8,736 | 936 | EUR 100 | EUR 874K | EUR 211K | **EUR 1,085K** |
| 38% (INA current rate) | 6,639 | 711 | EUR 100 | EUR 664K | EUR 160K | **EUR 824K** |
| 38% at INA's current ATR | 6,639 | 711 | EUR 57.53 | EUR 382K | EUR 44K | **EUR 426K** |

### Key Insight
At current utilization (38%) but market-rate pricing (EUR 100 ATR), revenue nearly doubles from EUR 426K to EUR 824K. **Utilization is average. Pricing is the problem.**

### The Pricing Gap Is the Story

| Metric | INA Actual | Mid-Market Benchmark | Gap |
|--------|-----------|---------------------|-----|

This is either a deliberate pricing strategy to build volume, or a failure to position correctly — either way, it represents the single largest revenue opportunity.

  + 25K    Premium service additions (signature treatments)
  + 24K    Hammam programming (couples, group, evening sessions)

### Strategic Conclusions

1. **"This is a pricing story, not a growth story."** — The single most impactful lever is repricing from EUR 57 to EUR 85 ATR. This alone adds EUR 200K+ to revenue at constant utilization.

2. **Year 1 should be conservative.** Transition disruption (TUPE staff, ownership change, systems migration), seasonal troughs, and price elasticity mean Year 1 will not see dramatic growth. Budget EUR 450-530K.

3. **The EUR 700K+ threshold is a Year 3 target.** All four analysts initially treated EUR 700K as achievable in Year 1-2. The QC agent correctly identified this as aspirational — it requires 18-24 months of repricing, marketing investment, and operational stabilization.

4. **Price elasticity matters.** A 40% price increase (EUR 57 → EUR 80) will cause some client attrition (estimated 5-15%). The net revenue effect is still strongly positive, but budgeting for 10% volume loss during repricing is prudent.

5. **Hotel capture rate is a bonus, not a base case.** INA captures 1.9-2.3% of hotel guests. Industry average for hotel spas is 10-20%, but improving this depends on Barcelo's cooperation (not guaranteed). Budget 3-5% capture in Year 3, not 12-18%.

### QC-Adjusted Revenue Estimates

| Year | Original Analyst Range | QC-Adjusted Range | Key Adjustment |

| Year 1 | EUR 580K – 1,060K | **EUR 450K – 530K** | Transition disruption, TUPE constraints, cautious repricing |
| Year 2 | EUR 700K – 1,200K | **EUR 530K – 700K** | Repricing takes hold, marketing begins to deliver |
| Year 3 | EUR 850K – 1,500K | **EUR 650K – 820K** | Stabilized operations, full repricing implemented |

### Critical Challenges Raised

1. **EUR 103 ATR is aspirational, not actual.** No analyst provided evidence that a hotel spa in Malta charges EUR 100+ for standard massage/facial. Sliema market supports EUR 75-95 for premium treatments, not EUR 100+.

2. **Price elasticity not modeled.** A 40% price increase on a captive hotel spa clientele (who can walk to any of 7 competitors within 1km) will cause measurable client loss. Estimated elasticity: -0.8 to -1.4.

3. **Hotel capture rate of 12-18% contradicts actual data.** INA has been operating for 2 years and captures 1.9-2.3%. Improving this requires Barcelo to actively promote the spa — which they have no contractual obligation to do.

4. **Seasonal revenue pattern matters.** Malta spa revenue has a pronounced summer dip (tourists prefer beach/pool) and winter peak (residents seek wellness). Year 1 revenue depends heavily on which month the acquisition closes.

5. **TUPE constraints limit immediate restructuring.** Malta employment law (TUPE) requires maintaining existing staff terms post-acquisition. If current therapists are paid above market or have contractual benefits, these cannot be changed in Year 1. This limits both cost reduction and service repositioning.

## 7. FINAL VERDICT

**The revenue analysis confirms the acquisition thesis at EUR 200-220K.** The business does not need to achieve theoretical maximum (EUR 2.17M) or even mid-market benchmark (EUR 1M) to justify the purchase price. It needs to grow from EUR 426K to EUR 650K+ over 3 years through a pricing correction that every analyst independently identified as the primary opportunity.

The risk is not "can this business grow?" — it is "how fast can you reprice without losing clients?" The QC agent's conservative Year 1 estimate (EUR 450-530K) is the right planning assumption. EUR 700K+ is a Year 3 achievement, not a Day 1 reality.

**Bottom line:** You are buying a platform priced like a budget spa in a mid-market location. The revenue upside is real and primarily driven by a single, high-confidence lever (repricing). The deal works at EUR 220K with substantial margin for error.
