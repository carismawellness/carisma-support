# Clinic Expansion Business Plan
## INA Spa & Wellness — Medical Aesthetics Conversion
### Prepared by Carisma Wellness Group | March 2026

---

## 1. Executive Summary

### Investment Thesis

Carisma Wellness Group is acquiring INA Spa & Wellness, a 7-room spa located within the Barcelo/Numi Hotel (formerly Fortina) on the Tigne Seafront in Sliema, Malta. The facility generated EUR 426K in revenue from 7,474 appointments in 2025, with an average treatment value of EUR 57.

The core investment thesis is straightforward: **convert one to two treatment rooms into a medical aesthetics clinic to increase revenue per square meter by 3-5x.** A standard spa treatment room generates EUR 57 per appointment slot. A clinic room offering injectables, RF microneedling, or IV therapy generates EUR 150-350 per slot. This is not speculative — INA already operates a Candela laser and T-Shape body contouring device, with 23.6% of current treatments already technology-based. The infrastructure for a clinical pivot partially exists.

### Key Financial Highlights

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Total CAPEX required | EUR 80,000 |
| Revenue at maturity (Year 3+) | EUR 180,000 - 350,000 |
| Gross margin (clinic services) | 50 - 65% |
| Payback period on CAPEX | 6 - 12 months |
| Combined facility revenue (Year 3+) | EUR 600,000 - 750,000+ |

### Strategic Advantage

Carisma Wellness Group already operates three established brands in Malta — Carisma Spa & Wellness, Carisma Aesthetics ("Glow with Confidence"), and Carisma Slimming. Each brand maintains an active client database. This acquisition creates immediate cross-sell opportunity: INA's 78% local client base can be introduced to Carisma Aesthetics services, while Carisma's existing clients gain access to a premium Sliema waterfront location. No competitor in Malta has this level of integrated wellness infrastructure.

## 2. Market Analysis

### 2.1 The Malta Aesthetics Market

Malta's non-surgical aesthetics market exhibits characteristics that favour new entrants: small population (530,000), concentrated affluent areas, limited specialist competition, and high per-capita disposable income relative to the region. The Sliema-St Julian's-Gzira corridor represents the island's highest concentration of target demographics — professional women with disposable income and established beauty routines.

**Global context:** Non-surgical aesthetics is growing at 15-20% annually worldwide, driven by social media normalisation, declining treatment costs, and an expanding age range of consumers (now starting as young as 25). Malta is following this trajectory with a 2-3 year lag behind markets like the UK and Italy, meaning the growth curve is still early.

### 2.2 Target Demographic

| Attribute | Profile |
|-----------|---------|
| Gender | Primarily female (85-90% of addressable market) |
| Age range | 25-55 years |
| Location | Sliema, St Julian's, Gzira, Msida, Birkirkara (15-minute drive radius) |
| Income | Middle to upper-middle, EUR 30K+ household income |
| Behaviour | Already investing in beauty/wellness; open to "upgrading" from traditional spa to clinical results |
| Language | English, Maltese (bilingual market); some Italian, French, German (hotel guests) |

### 2.3 Competitive Landscape

Malta's aesthetics market is fragmented. Competitors range from solo-practitioner clinics to dermatology offices offering cosmetic services. No single operator dominates. Crucially, **Carisma already operates in this space** through Carisma Aesthetics and Carisma Slimming — the acquisition of INA adds a premium waterfront location and hotel traffic to an existing operational platform.

**Key competitive advantages of the INA location:**
- Premium hotel setting conveys clinical credibility and luxury positioning
- Walk-in foot traffic from hotel guests (22% of current spa clients)
- Established 78% local clientele with purchase history and contact data
- Existing technology base (Candela laser, T-Shape) reduces startup risk
- Carisma Group's shared procurement, training, and marketing infrastructure

### 2.4 Market Sizing (Addressable)

Conservative estimate of the primary catchment (Sliema-St Julian's-Gzira, women 25-55): approximately 15,000-20,000 potential clients. At a 3-5% penetration rate with an average annual spend of EUR 600-1,200 per active client, the addressable revenue is EUR 270K-1.2M annually — well above our projections.

## 4. Operations Plan

### 4.1 Room Allocation

Of the 7 existing treatment rooms, the plan converts **2 rooms** to clinical use:

- **Clinic Room 1 (Priority):** Dedicated to laser and energy-based devices (Candela, RF microneedling). Requires medical-grade finishes, enhanced ventilation, clinical lighting, and device storage. This room generates the highest revenue per hour.
- **Clinic Room 2:** Dedicated to injectables, IV therapy, and advanced skin treatments. Requires medical-grade surfaces, lockable pharmaceutical storage, clinical waste disposal, and a small consultation area.
- **Remaining 5 rooms:** Continue operating as traditional spa treatment rooms, maintaining the EUR 426K spa revenue base.

### 4.2 Staffing Model

**Existing staff (retrained):**
- 2-3 therapists upskilled for laser operation and advanced device protocols
- Reception staff trained on clinical booking, consent forms, and aftercare communication

**New hires:**
- 1 Senior Aesthetician / Clinic Manager (full-time) — experienced in RF microneedling, peels, device protocols. Salary: EUR 28,000-35,000/year
- 1 Medical Director (part-time / on-call) — licensed physician for injectable oversight. Retainer: EUR 12,000-18,000/year
- 1 additional therapist (hired Month 6 as demand grows). Salary: EUR 20,000-24,000/year

**Total incremental staffing cost Year 1:** EUR 45,000-60,000
**Total incremental staffing cost Year 2+:** EUR 60,000-77,000

### 4.3 Scheduling Optimisation

The clinic rooms operate on a structured time-block system:

| Time Block | Clinic Room 1 | Clinic Room 2 |
|------------|---------------|---------------|
| 09:00 - 12:00 | Laser treatments | Skin consultations + peels |
| 12:00 - 13:00 | Device maintenance / breaks | — |
| 13:00 - 17:00 | RF microneedling + body contouring | Injectables + IV therapy |
| 17:00 - 19:00 | Evening laser (high demand) | Evening skin boosters |

Target utilisation: 65% in Year 1, 80% in Year 2, 85%+ in Year 3.

### 4.4 Procurement and Consumables

- Consumables (tips, serums, injectables, IV fluids): 15-25% of treatment revenue
- Shared procurement with Carisma Group reduces unit costs by an estimated 10-15%
- Key supplier relationships: Candela (parts/warranty), skin booster distributors (Profhilo/Sunekos), pharmaceutical wholesalers for IV compounds
- Par stock levels maintained for all consumables with monthly inventory audits

### 4.5 Quality Control

- Standardised treatment protocols documented for every service
- Before/after photography for all clinical treatments (with consent)
- Client satisfaction surveys post-treatment (NPS target: 70+)
- Monthly clinical audit by Medical Director
- Annual equipment calibration and safety inspections

## 6. Marketing & Launch Strategy

### 6.1 Pre-Launch (Weeks 1-8 Before Opening)

**Database Marketing — Highest ROI Channel**
- Email/SMS campaign to INA's existing client database (78% local, repeat customers)
- Segment by treatment history: laser clients get advanced skin offers; massage clients get "upgrade your routine" positioning
- Cross-promotion to Carisma Aesthetics and Carisma Slimming databases
- Early-bird pricing: 25% off first clinic treatment for existing clients

**Digital Presence**
- Dedicated landing page: clinic services, pricing, before/afters, team bios
- Instagram content series: room transformation, equipment reveals, staff training footage

- VIP preview evening for top 50-100 clients (by spend and frequency)
- Live treatment demonstrations (LED therapy, Hydrafacial — non-invasive, visual)

- Influencer invitations (2-3 Malta-based lifestyle/beauty influencers)

## 7. Regulatory & Compliance Roadmap

### 7.1 Licensing Timeline

| Task | Timeline | Responsible | Status |
|------|----------|-------------|--------|
| Malta Medicines Authority — medical device registration | Weeks 1-6 | Legal / Compliance | Pre-acquisition |
| Professional indemnity insurance (clinic services) | Week 2 | Insurance broker | Pre-acquisition |
| Medical malpractice insurance (injectable services) | Weeks 4-8 | Insurance broker | Pre-launch |
| Operator certification — laser/IPL devices | Weeks 2-4 | Staff training | Pre-launch |
| Medical Director contract (part-time physician) | Weeks 4-8 | HR / Legal | Before Phase 3 |
| Pharmaceutical storage licence (for injectables) | Weeks 6-10 | Compliance | Before Phase 3 |
| Clinical waste disposal contract | Week 4 | Operations | Before Phase 2 |
| GDPR compliance — medical records processing | Weeks 2-6 | Data Protection Officer | Pre-launch |

### 7.2 Medical Oversight Arrangements

- Part-time Medical Director (licensed physician) retained for minimum 8 hours/week on-site
- Medical Director oversees: injectable protocols, treatment plans for medical-grade services, adverse event management, clinical audit
- On-call arrangements for evenings/weekends when injectable services are offered
- All injectable treatments require pre-treatment consultation signed off by Medical Director
- Emergency protocol and adverse reaction kit maintained on premises

### 7.3 Record-Keeping and GDPR

- All clinic treatments recorded in clinical management software (separate from spa booking system)
- Medical records retained for minimum 10 years (Malta regulatory requirement)
- Explicit consent obtained for: treatment, before/after photography, data processing
- Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA) completed before launch
- Patient records encrypted at rest and in transit

## 8. Risk Analysis

### 8.1 Risk Register

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|------------|
| **Regulatory delays** (licensing takes longer than planned) | Medium | Medium | Begin applications pre-acquisition. Phase 1 services (existing equipment) require minimal new licensing. |
| **Demand uncertainty** (clinic uptake slower than projected) | Medium | Medium | Conservative scenario still achieves payback within 18 months. Phase rollout limits committed spend. |
| **Cannibalization** of spa services | Low | Low | Data shows clinic clients are net-new or upgrading, not substituting. Spa rooms remain at 5 of 7. |
| **Staffing** (difficulty hiring qualified aestheticians) | Medium | High | Begin recruitment pre-acquisition. Offer above-market salary. Upskill existing therapists. Leverage Carisma brand reputation. |
| **Equipment failure / maintenance** | Low | Medium | Warranty and service contracts for all devices. Budget EUR 5K/year for maintenance. |
| **Medical adverse events** | Low | High | Medical Director oversight, emergency protocols, comprehensive insurance, documented consent process. |
| **Hotel relationship changes** | Low | High | Long-term lease agreement with Barcelo/Numi. Clinic enhances hotel offering (mutual benefit). |
| **Competitor response** | Medium | Low | First-mover advantage in hotel-integrated med spa. Carisma's multi-brand ecosystem is not easily replicated. |

## 11. Conclusion and Recommendation

The clinic expansion within INA Spa & Wellness represents a high-conviction, low-risk capital deployment for Carisma Wellness Group. The investment thesis is supported by four structural advantages:

1. **Existing equipment reduces CAPEX risk.** The Candela laser and T-Shape device are operational and generating revenue. The EUR 80K budget adds capability without starting from zero.

2. **Proven demand from the existing client base.** With 78% local clients and 23.6% of treatments already technology-based, the path from spa client to clinic client is a natural upgrade, not a cold start.

3. **Group synergies are immediate.** Cross-referral from Carisma Aesthetics and Carisma Slimming databases, shared procurement, unified booking, and brand credibility are available from Day 1.

4. **Downside is well-protected.** Even in the conservative scenario, CAPEX is recovered within 18 months, and the phased rollout ensures capital is only deployed against validated demand.

**Recommendation:** Proceed with the clinic conversion. Begin regulatory applications and recruitment immediately upon acquisition close, targeting Phase 1 launch by Month 3 and full service menu by Month 9.

The projected outcome at maturity (Year 3+) is a combined facility generating EUR 600,000-775,000 in annual revenue, with clinic services contributing EUR 180,000-300,000 at 40-50% EBITDA margins — transforming INA from a EUR 426K traditional spa into a diversified wellness and aesthetics destination.
