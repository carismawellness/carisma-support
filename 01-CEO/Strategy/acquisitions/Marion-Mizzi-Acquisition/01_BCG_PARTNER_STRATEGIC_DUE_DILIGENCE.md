## 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

### Deal Thesis

Marion Mizzi Wellbeing represents a once-in-a-generation opportunity to consolidate Malta's fragmented wellness market under a single, professionally managed platform. By acquiring Malta's most recognized slimming brand — with ~50 years of brand equity, 4,900+ clients served, and three established locations — Carisma Wellness Group can create a near-monopoly (estimated 90-95% combined market share) in the weight management and body transformation segment while simultaneously acquiring three spa locations that triple Carisma's physical footprint overnight. The critical strategic question is not whether this asset has value, but whether the value survives the twin disruptions of GLP-1 pharmaceuticals and personal-brand dependency at the price being discussed.

### Strategic Verdict: CONDITIONAL BUY

**Conditions for proceeding:**

1. Purchase price at or below EUR 2.0M (4.0x reported EBITDA) — not the EUR 2.25-2.5M implied by the accountant's 4.5-5.0x range
2. Three-year audited financials confirming normalized EBITDA of EUR 450K+ (after owner add-backs)
3. Hotel lease terms confirmed assignable at current rates with minimum 5-year remaining term
4. Petra (current owner) commits to 12-month transition period with active involvement
5. Deferred consideration structure: minimum 30% of purchase price tied to post-close performance milestones over 24 months
6. GLP-1 pivot strategy incorporated into integration plan from Day 1

**If all six conditions are met, this deal is strategically compelling at a probability-weighted expected IRR of 18-24%, with transformative upside if the GLP-1 pivot succeeds.**

**If any of conditions 1-4 fail, WALK AWAY.**

### 2.2 Growth Drivers and Headwinds

**Growth Drivers:**

1. **Malta population growth:** Malta's population has grown from 434K (2014) to approximately 542K (2025), driven by expatriate immigration. The foreign worker population now exceeds 80,000. This 2-3% annual population growth directly expands the addressable market.

2. **Tourism recovery and growth:** Malta welcomed approximately 2.8M+ tourists in 2024, recovering to near pre-pandemic levels. The Malta Tourism Authority projects 3-4% annual growth through 2030. Hotel-based spa locations (2 of 3 Marion Mizzi sites) benefit directly.

3. **Rising health consciousness:** Post-pandemic wellness spending has accelerated globally. The Global Wellness Institute estimates the global wellness economy at USD 5.6T in 2024, growing at 8-10% annually. Malta, as an affluent EU market, participates in this trend.

4. **Medical tourism potential:** Malta's English-speaking environment, EU regulatory framework, and competitive pricing versus UK/Northern Europe create medical tourism opportunity, particularly for body contouring and aesthetics.

5. **Aging population with disposable income:** Malta's median age is 42.6 years and rising. The 40-65 demographic represents the sweet spot for premium wellness services — they have both the health concerns and the financial means.

**Headwinds:**

1. **GLP-1 pharmaceutical disruption** (detailed analysis in Section 4): The single largest threat to traditional weight management businesses. Ozempic/Wegovy market projected at USD 100B+ globally by 2030-2035.

2. **Small market ceiling:** Malta's 530K population creates a hard cap on addressable clients. Even with 100% market share, growth is bounded by population and per-capita spending.

3. **Seasonality risk:** Hotel-based locations see 40-60% revenue variation between peak (June-September) and off-peak periods. Tourist-driven revenue is inherently volatile.

4. **Regulatory tightening:** EU advertising regulations for health claims are becoming stricter. Malta's MCCAA (Competition and Consumer Affairs Authority) actively enforces consumer protection in health services marketing.

5. **Labor market constraints:** Malta's tight labor market makes hiring qualified therapists, nutritionists, and medical professionals increasingly difficult and expensive. Payroll inflation is running 3-5% annually.

### 2.4 Market Concentration Analysis (HHI Index)

Pre-HHI = 35^2 + 20^2 + 10^2 + 8^2 + 7^2 + (20 small players at 1% each = 20)
= 1,225 + 400 + 100 + 64 + 49 + 20 = **1,858**

**Post-Acquisition HHI:**

Combined entity: 55%, Independent A 10%, Independent B 8%, Independent C 7%, Others 20%

Post-HHI = 55^2 + 10^2 + 8^2 + 7^2 + 20
= 3,025 + 100 + 64 + 49 + 20 = **3,258**

**Delta HHI: +1,400**

**Regulatory Interpretation:**

- HHI > 2,500 = "Highly concentrated market" (US DOJ/FTC guidelines)
- Delta HHI > 200 in a highly concentrated market = "Presumed likely to enhance market power"

**Competition Authority Risk Assessment:**

Malta's Office for Competition (part of MCCAA) has jurisdiction over merger review. However:

1. **Turnover threshold:** Malta's merger notification is triggered when combined turnover exceeds EUR 2.3M. Combined Carisma + Marion Mizzi at EUR 3-3.5M likely exceeds this threshold. **Notification is probably required.**

2. **Probability of challenge:** LOW-MODERATE (25-35%). Malta's competition authority has limited resources and has historically focused on larger transactions. The wellness/slimming market is not a regulated utility or essential service. However, the near-monopoly position could attract scrutiny if competitors or consumer advocacy groups raise concerns.

3. **Mitigation:** The market definition matters enormously. If the relevant market is defined as "wellness services" broadly (EUR 50-68M TAM), combined share is only 5-7% — well below concern thresholds. If narrowly defined as "professional weight management," the 85-95% share is problematic. Legal counsel should prepare to argue the broader market definition.

4. **Remedies if challenged:** Worst case, the authority could require divestiture of one location or behavioral commitments (e.g., no exclusive dealing arrangements with hotels). This is manageable.

**Recommendation:** Engage Malta competition law counsel before signing any binding agreement. Prepare a pre-notification submission arguing broad market definition. Budget EUR 15-25K for competition filing and legal costs.

## 4. GLP-1 DISRUPTION DEEP DIVE

### 4.1 Current State of GLP-1 Adoption in Europe

GLP-1 receptor agonists (semaglutide — branded as Ozempic for diabetes and Wegovy for weight management, plus tirzepatide/Mounjaro and emerging oral formulations) represent the most significant disruption to the weight management industry in decades.

**Global Market Trajectory:**

| Year | Global GLP-1 Market (est.) | Key Milestones |
|------|---------------------------|----------------|
| 2023 | USD ~21B | Ozempic shortages; off-label use explosion |
| 2024 | USD ~35-40B | Wegovy global rollout accelerates; generic compounds emerge |
| 2025 | USD ~50-60B | FDA approves oral semaglutide for weight loss; Super Bowl advertising |
| 2026 (current) | USD ~65-80B (projected) | European access expanding; price negotiations with EU health systems |
| 2030 | USD ~100-130B (projected) | Mainstream adoption; potential OTC versions; biosimilars enter market |

**European Adoption Timeline:**

Europe trails the US by 12-24 months in GLP-1 adoption, primarily due to:
- Public healthcare system gatekeeping (prescriptions require clinical justification)
- Price negotiations with national health agencies
- More conservative prescribing culture
- Supply constraints (Novo Nordisk prioritized US market)

**Malta-Specific Adoption Forecast:**

Malta's GLP-1 adoption curve will be shaped by several island-specific factors:

1. **Healthcare system:** Malta has a public healthcare system that covers most basic medications. However, GLP-1s for weight management (Wegovy) are not currently covered under the Malta government formulary. Patients must pay privately: EUR 200-400/month.

2. **Prescribing patterns:** Maltese physicians tend to be conservative prescribers. The small medical community means adoption follows word-of-mouth among a limited number of endocrinologists and GPs.

3. **Population size:** With only ~15-20 relevant specialists on the island, the rate of new prescriptions is inherently limited compared to a US city of similar population.

4. **Cost sensitivity:** EUR 200-400/month is significant for Maltese incomes (average salary ~EUR 20K-25K/year). This limits adoption to upper-middle-class and affluent segments — perhaps 10-20% of the target demographic.

5. **Cultural factors:** Malta has strong food culture and family-centered eating traditions. Medication-based weight loss may face cultural resistance more than in the US/UK.

**BCG Estimate — Malta GLP-1 Adoption Curve:**

| Timeframe | Est. GLP-1 Users in Malta | % of Weight-Seeking Population (~40K) |
|-----------|--------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| 2026 (now) | 500-1,000 | 1.3-2.5% |
| 2028 | 2,000-3,500 | 5-9% |
| 2030 | 4,000-6,000 | 10-15% |
| 2033 | 6,000-10,000 | 15-25% |
| 2035 | 8,000-15,000 | 20-38% |

### 4.3 The Pivot Opportunity: GLP-1 as ENABLER

**Why GLP-1 Creates More Demand Than It Destroys (With Active Pivot):**

**1. GLP-1 Management Clinics (The Biggest Opportunity)**

GLP-1 medications require ongoing medical supervision: monthly blood work, dosage titration, side-effect monitoring, nutritional guidance. Current Maltese healthcare infrastructure is not built for this volume of chronic medication management. A dedicated GLP-1 management clinic within the Marion Mizzi/Carisma network would:

- Charge EUR 100-200/month for monitoring packages
- Serve 200-500 patients within 3-5 years
- Generate EUR 240K-1.2M annual recurring revenue
- Create a captive audience for body contouring and aesthetics cross-sells
- Require relatively modest capex (EUR 30-50K for medical monitoring equipment)

**2. Post-GLP-1 Body Contouring Demand Surge**

GLP-1 medications cause rapid weight loss (15-20% of body weight in 12-18 months). This creates significant demand for:

- Skin tightening (VelaShape, radiofrequency) — loose skin after rapid weight loss
- Body sculpting (CoolSculpting, EMSculpt NEO) — refining body shape post-weight loss
- Cellulite reduction — exposed as subcutaneous fat decreases

Early data from US markets (where GLP-1 adoption is 24 months ahead) shows that body contouring clinics near GLP-1 prescribers are seeing 30-50% demand increases. Marion Mizzi's existing cryolipolysis, ultrasound, and radiofrequency equipment positions it perfectly for this demand.

**3. GLP-1 Transition Programs (Coming Off Medication)**

Research indicates that 66% of weight regain occurs within 12 months of stopping GLP-1 medications. This creates demand for structured transition programs combining:

- Nutritional counseling (sustaining habits without pharmacological appetite suppression)
- Exercise programming (gym facilities at Sliema and Mellieha are assets here)
- Behavioral coaching (relapse prevention)
- Body composition monitoring (Tanita analysis)

Marion Mizzi's existing 6-step/7-step slimming programs could be adapted as "GLP-1 Transition Protocols" with minimal development cost.

**4. Nutrition Programs for Active GLP-1 Users**

GLP-1 users face specific nutritional challenges:
- Muscle mass loss (up to 40% of total weight loss is lean mass without intervention)
- Nutritional deficiencies (reduced food intake)
- Gastrointestinal side effects requiring dietary adaptation

Marion Mizzi's existing nutrition counseling and supplement lines can be repackaged as "GLP-1 Companion Nutrition" programs.

### 4.4 Net Impact Assessment

**Verdict: GLP-1 is NET POSITIVE for this acquisition — but ONLY with active pivot.**

Without pivot: GLP-1 destroys EUR 300-450K of annual slimming revenue by Year 7-10, reducing Marion Mizzi's value by 30-45%. The acquisition becomes marginal.

With pivot: GLP-1 creates EUR 800K-1.33M in new annual revenue by Year 7-10, more than offsetting the EUR 300-450K traditional loss. Net positive impact: EUR 350-880K additional annual revenue above baseline.

**The pivot is not optional. It is a condition of the deal thesis.**

The integration plan must include a GLP-1 strategy from Day 1, with physician partnerships, monitoring equipment procurement, and staff retraining budgeted within the first 12 months.

**Integration Approach:** Do NOT attempt to impose Carisma's culture on Marion Mizzi in Year 1. Instead:

1. Respect the existing culture while introducing performance metrics gradually

4. Celebrate Marion Mizzi heritage publicly — it is an asset, not a liability

### 6.6 Staff Retention Strategy

Marion Mizzi staff are the operational backbone. Losing key therapists means losing client relationships — in a personal-service business, the therapist IS the product.

**Tier 1: Mission-Critical (3-5 people)**
- Lead therapists with personal client followings
- Retention bonus: EUR 5K each, paid 50% at Day 1 and 50% at 12-month anniversary
- Enhanced compensation: 10-15% salary increase + commission structure
- Career development: leadership roles in expanded organization

**Tier 2: Important (5-10 people)**
- Experienced therapists and client-facing staff
- Retention bonus: EUR 2-3K each, paid at 12-month anniversary
- Training in new services (Carisma protocols, new equipment)
- Clear career path communication

**Tier 3: Replaceable (remaining staff)**
- Standard employment continuity
- Performance review at 6 months
- No retention bonuses but genuine opportunity to grow

**Total retention budget: EUR 30-50K** — small relative to acquisition price, critical to integration success.

## 8. WHAT THE BUYER IS NOT SEEING (Blind Spots)

### Blind Spot 1: Personal Brand Dependency — The "Marion" Problem

**Risk:** Marion Mizzi Wellbeing IS Marion Mizzi. The brand is a personal name with 50 years of recognition. Marion is no longer involved. Petra (her daughter) is "checked out and fed up." The brand's soul — its founder — has left the building.

**What you're not seeing:** In personal-brand wellness businesses, client loyalty transfers to the PRACTITIONER, not the brand name. When clients discover that neither Marion nor Petra is present, they may feel deceived. The brand becomes an empty vessel.

**Probability:** 70% that this causes measurable client attrition (10-20%)
**Impact:** EUR 200-400K revenue loss over 3 years
**Mitigation:** Hire a charismatic, visible clinical director who becomes the new "face" of the brand. Introduce her via personal video to all existing clients within 30 days of acquisition. Make the transition story about "Marion's legacy continues with even more resources."

### Blind Spot 2: Unverified Financials — The EBITDA May Be Fiction

**Risk:** The EUR 500K EBITDA figure comes from "the accountant" — not from audited financials, not from bank statements, not from verified tax returns. In a family-operated business with 50 years of history, "reported EBITDA" may include:

- Owner salary not deducted (Petra draws EUR 60-100K/year)
- Family members on payroll who don't work
- Personal expenses run through the business (car, insurance, travel)
- Cash transactions not fully reported (common in Malta wellness businesses)
- One-time items inflating or deflating results

**What you're not seeing:** The INA Spa deal taught us that model assumptions are optimistic until field-verified. The INA accountant claimed EUR 72K EBITDA; field intelligence revealed the business was cash-negative. The same pattern could repeat here.

**Probability:** 65% that normalized EBITDA is 20-40% below reported EUR 500K
**Impact:** EUR 100-200K lower annual EBITDA (EUR 300-400K actual) = fair value drops to EUR 1.2-1.6M from EUR 2.0-2.5M
**Mitigation:** Non-negotiable: Three years of audited P&L, bank statements, and tax returns before any binding offer. Hire an independent forensic accountant to normalize EBITDA. Budget EUR 10-15K for this exercise.

### Blind Spot 3: Hotel Lease Concentration Risk

**Risk:** Two of three locations (67% of locations, estimated 50-60% of revenue) are inside hotels. The leases are with AX Hotels and Maritim Hotels. These leases are the business's operational lifeline. If either landlord refuses to assign the lease to a new operator, or demands significantly higher rent, the deal math breaks.

**What you're not seeing:** Hotel operators have significant leverage. They know you cannot move a spa — the buildout costs are sunk. They may view a change of ownership as an opportunity to renegotiate terms upward by 20-40%. Or worse, they may prefer to bring spa operations in-house (this is a growing trend among hotel groups — "Why pay an operator when we can do it ourselves?").

**Probability:** 30% that at least one hotel lease is problematic
**Impact:** Loss of one hotel location = EUR 600-800K revenue loss (30-40% of total)
**Mitigation:** Obtain written landlord consent to lease assignment as a condition precedent to closing. Visit both hotel GMs personally. Propose value-sharing arrangements that incentivize hotels to maintain the operator relationship.

### Blind Spot 4: Equipment Obsolescence — The Capex Time Bomb

**Risk:** Marion Mizzi has been operating for decades. Much of the equipment (particularly the slimming devices — cryolipolysis, ultrasound, radiofrequency) may be outdated, poorly maintained, or approaching end-of-life. The preliminary assessment estimates EUR 200-400K in modernization capex needed.

**What you're not seeing:** In the medical aesthetics industry, technology turns over every 5-7 years. Equipment that was state-of-the-art in 2018-2019 is already considered dated. Clients increasingly research equipment brands and models — using older devices positions the business as "behind the times" and creates clinical efficacy risk. Moreover, used medical equipment has near-zero resale value.

**Probability:** 75% that EUR 200K+ in equipment capex is needed within 24 months
**Impact:** EUR 200-400K unplanned capital expenditure, reducing effective returns
**Mitigation:** Commission independent equipment appraisal (EUR 3-5K cost). Reduce offer price by 50% of estimated required capex. Negotiate equipment escrow in purchase agreement.

### Blind Spot 5: The "Disengaged Owner" Revenue Trajectory

**Risk:** The memory file notes that Petra is "checked out, fed up, not interested." This describes an owner who has mentally exited the business. In personal-service businesses, owner disengagement manifests as:

- Declining service quality (no one enforcing standards)
- Staff complacency (no accountability)
- Marketing dormancy (spending stops, leads dry up)
- Client migration (to competitors or no treatment at all)
- Supplier relationship deterioration (late payments, reduced quality inputs)

**What you're not seeing:** The EUR 2M revenue and EUR 500K EBITDA may represent HISTORICAL performance from when the business was actively managed. Current run-rate could be 15-30% below reported figures. The business may be in active decline, masked by financial reporting lag.

**Probability:** 60% that current monthly revenue is declining
**Impact:** Actual acquisition-date revenue may be EUR 1.5-1.7M (not EUR 2.0M), reducing EBITDA to EUR 300-380K
**Mitigation:** Request trailing-12-month bank statements AND trailing-3-month bank statements. Compare. If recent months show a declining trend, use the lower run-rate as the valuation basis. This is the strongest negotiation lever available.

### Blind Spot 6: Biologique Recherche Exclusivity and Supply Risk

**Risk:** Marion Mizzi uses Biologique Recherche, a prestigious French skincare line with selective distribution. The brand relationship may be tied to the Marion Mizzi name, not transferable to a new owner without renegotiation. Biologique Recherche is known for strict distribution control — they approve their partners personally.

**What you're not seeing:** Losing the Biologique Recherche line would downgrade the spa offering from "premium" to "standard," impacting the high-end client segment. Clients who specifically come for Biologique Recherche facials may leave if the brand switches suppliers.

**Probability:** 20-30% that supplier relationship requires renegotiation
**Impact:** EUR 50-100K in spa revenue risk if Biologique Recherche partnership is lost
**Mitigation:** Contact Biologique Recherche pre-close to confirm partnership continuation under new ownership. Make this a due diligence item.

### Blind Spot 7: The Supplement Business — Regulatory and Margin Risk

**Risk:** Marion Mizzi sells proprietary supplements as part of its slimming programs (the "7-Step Diet" includes supplements). The regulatory environment for supplements in the EU is tightening, with increasing scrutiny on health claims, ingredient sourcing, and labeling compliance.

**What you're not seeing:** If the supplement line is a significant revenue contributor (EUR 100-200K estimated), any regulatory action — even a simple labeling compliance issue — could require pulling products from sale, reformulating, or rebranding. This is costly and time-consuming.

**Probability:** 25% of regulatory issue within 3 years
**Impact:** EUR 50-150K in revenue disruption + EUR 30-50K in reformulation costs
**Mitigation:** Conduct EU food supplement compliance audit pre-close (EUR 5-8K). Verify all health claims are authorized under EU Regulation 1924/2006.

## 9. FINAL RECOMMENDATION

### 9.1 Should Carisma Acquire Marion Mizzi?

**YES — CONDITIONAL BUY.**

The strategic logic of this acquisition is sound. Combining Malta's two dominant wellness brands creates a platform with:

- Near-monopoly market position in professional weight management
- Geographic coverage across the island (4 locations)
- Service line completeness from slimming to spa to aesthetics to fitness
- Sufficient scale to invest in the GLP-1 pivot that will define the industry's next decade
- Brand portfolio that serves the full 25-65 female demographic
- Estimated EUR 444K in annual synergies (risk-adjusted EUR 310K+)

However, the deal is NOT compelling at any price. It is compelling only under specific conditions.

### 9.2 At What Price?

**Recommended Pricing Strategy:**

| Level | Price (EUR) | Multiple (x EBITDA) | Rationale |
|-------|-----------|---------------------|-----------|
| **Opening offer** | EUR 1.4-1.5M | 2.8-3.0x | Anchored to GLP-1 risk, unverified financials, disengaged owner |
| **Target** | EUR 1.75-2.0M | 3.5-4.0x | Fair value if financials verify and conditions met |
| **Stretch** | EUR 2.0-2.15M | 4.0-4.3x | Maximum if EBITDA verifies at EUR 500K+ and all diligence clean |
| **Walk-away** | EUR 2.25M+ | 4.5x+ | Below this, deal clears hurdle rate; above this, returns are marginal |

**Price Justification:**

The accountant's suggested 4.5-5.0x EBITDA is based on "comparable transactions" — but these comparables do not adjust for:

1. **GLP-1 disruption risk** (haircut 0.5-1.0x from comparable multiple)
2. **Personal brand dependency** (haircut 0.3-0.5x — the "Marion" name has diminishing value without Marion)
3. **Unverified financials** (haircut 0.2-0.5x until audited)
4. **Aging equipment** (deduct EUR 200-400K from enterprise value)
5. **No competing bidders** (no auction premium warranted)
6. **Disengaged owner** (business may be declining, justifying below-market multiple)

**Adjusted fair multiple: 3.0-4.0x verified EBITDA.**

At EUR 500K EBITDA (if verified): EUR 1.5-2.0M fair value
At EUR 400K EBITDA (if normalized): EUR 1.2-1.6M fair value
At EUR 350K EBITDA (if declining): EUR 1.05-1.4M fair value

### 9.3 With What Conditions?

**Pre-Signing Conditions (Must Be Satisfied Before Binding Offer):**

1. Three years of audited P&L, balance sheet, and bank statements
2. Independent EBITDA normalization (forensic accountant review)
3. Trailing-3-month revenue verification (current run rate, not historical average)
4. Written landlord consent for lease assignment at both hotel locations
5. Equipment appraisal by independent valuation firm
6. Complete staff roster with compensation, contract terms, and tenure
7. Client database analysis: active vs. lapsed, retention rates, concentration risk
8. Confirmation of all regulatory licenses and permits (health authority, business permits)
9. Tax compliance verification (VAT, income tax, social security filings current)
10. No pending litigation, arbitration, or regulatory proceedings

**Deal Structure Conditions:**

1. **Deferred consideration:** Minimum 25-30% of purchase price deferred over 24 months, tied to revenue maintenance above 85% of trailing-12-month average
2. **Earnout component:** Additional EUR 100-200K payable only if EBITDA exceeds EUR 500K in either of the two post-close fiscal years
3. **Petra's transition:** 12-month consulting agreement (EUR 3-5K/month) with active involvement in client relationships and staff transition
4. **Non-compete:** 5-year non-compete across Malta for Petra and any Marion Mizzi family members in wellness/slimming/spa services
5. **Equipment warranty:** Seller represents all equipment is in working order; buyer holds EUR 75-100K in escrow for 12 months against equipment failure
6. **Employee retention:** Seller cooperates with buyer's retention program; provides client/therapist relationship mapping

### 9.4 Non-Negotiables

These five elements are absolute conditions. If any one fails, the deal should not proceed:

1. **Verified EBITDA >= EUR 400K (normalized).** If actual EBITDA is below EUR 400K, the business is not worth EUR 2.0M under any reasonable methodology. Either the price drops below EUR 1.6M or we walk.

2. **Both hotel leases transferable.** Loss of either hotel location destroys the geographic diversification thesis and removes 30-40% of revenue. This is a binary gate.

3. **Petra commits to 12-month transition.** Without the owner's cooperation during transition, client attrition could reach 20-30%. Her involvement is critical to bridge the brand-dependency gap.

4. **No undisclosed material liabilities.** Clean legal and tax diligence is non-negotiable. Any hidden debt, litigation, or tax liability above EUR 50K is a deal-breaker.

5. **Deferred consideration / earnout structure.** A 100% cash-at-close deal is too risky given the unverified financials and GLP-1 uncertainty. At least 25% of the purchase price must be tied to post-close performance.
