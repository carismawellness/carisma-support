# KKR Operating Partner Review: Carisma Wellness Group

**Date:** 2026-03-24
**Revenue baseline:** €2.65M/yr (Zoho Books, Mar 2025 – Feb 2026)
**Operating Profit:** €830k (31.3% margin)

---

## Executive Summary

1. **This is a solid small business masquerading as an unoptimized one.** A 31.3% operating margin on €2.65M revenue in a hotel spa model is respectable, but there is a clear path to 38-40% margins (~€1.0-1.06M EBITDA) through labor rationalization at three underperforming locations, elimination of €250k+ in questionable overhead, and revenue densification at high-potential sites. That is a €170-230k annual EBITDA uplift without opening a single new location.

2. **Three locations are destroying value.** Excelsior (45.8% HC%), Sunny Coast (42.1%), and Hyatt (41.5%) have labor costs that are structurally incompatible with profitability at their current revenue levels. Either revenue doubles at these sites or headcount gets cut by 30-40%. There is no third option.

3. **The overhead structure has a €200k problem hiding in plain sight.** €168k in "Consulting - Professional Services," €52k in Travel & Entertainment, and €31.5k in bank fees collectively represent 9.5% of revenue. For a business this size on a 30-minute island, these numbers are indefensible.

## 1. Revenue & Labor Productivity Analysis

### Revenue Per Location: The Power Law Problem

| Tier | Locations | Combined Revenue | Share | Combined Wages | Blended HC% |
|------|-----------|-----------------|-------|----------------|-------------|
| **Tier 1 (Core)** | InterContinental, Hugo's | €1,456k | 54.9% | €374k | 25.7% |
| **Tier 2 (Viable)** | Ramla, Labranda | €516k | 19.4% | €167k | 32.4% |
| **Tier 3 (Problem)** | Hyatt, Sunny Coast, Excelsior | €618k | 23.3% | €265k | 42.9% |
| **Tier 4 (Irrelevant)** | Novotel | €40k | 1.5% | €6k | 16.0% |

**The math is damning.** Tier 3 locations generate 23.3% of revenue but consume 32.6% of location wages. If you applied the Tier 1 HC% (25.7%) to Tier 3 revenue, wages would be €159k instead of €265k — a €106k annual saving that drops straight to EBITDA.

### Location-by-Location Recommendations

**InterContinental (€888k, 21.4% HC%) — INVEST**
- Crown jewel. 33.5% of revenue, lowest HC%, highest productivity.
- Every incremental euro of revenue at IC falls to a 78.6% contribution margin on labor.
- Can this location do €1M? If so, you just created €112k of incremental revenue at near-zero incremental labor cost.

**Hugo's (€568k, 32.6% HC%) — OPTIMIZE**
- Second largest but labor is 11.2pp worse than IC.
- Audit the schedule. Target: bring HC% to 27% — saving €32k annually.

**Ramla (€312k, 34.7% HC%) — RESTRUCTURE STAFFING**
- Revenue decent but labor eating too much. Reduce headcount by 1 FTE equivalent (save ~€18-22k).

**Hyatt (€229k, 41.5% HC%) — ULTIMATUM**
- €95k wages on €229k revenue. After rent, consumables, and overhead allocation, barely breaking even.
- Cut to skeleton crew (2 therapists max). Target HC% of 30% = €69k wages, saving €26k.
- If revenue cannot grow to €300k+ within 12 months, prepare exit.

**Sunny Coast (€209k, 42.1% HC%) — SAME AS HYATT**
- €88k wages on €209k revenue. Reduce to minimum viable staffing. Save €25-30k.

**Excelsior (€179k, 45.8% HC%) — ON PROBATION**
- Opened July 2025, partial pass for being new.
- 12-month gate: by July 2026, must be at €25k/mo (€300k annualized) with HC% below 35%. If it misses, close it.

**Novotel (€40k, 16.0% HC%) — CLARIFY OR EXIT**
- €40k annual revenue is a rounding error. If no contractual obligation, close it.

## 3. Operating Leverage Opportunities

### 3.1 Centralized Scheduling & Yield Management
- Central booking with yield management logic
- Dynamic pricing: peak hours/seasons at premium, off-peak at discount
- Cross-location therapist redeployment (30-min island advantage)
- Impact: 10% utilization improvement = ~€80k incremental revenue → ~€60k EBITDA

### 3.2 Floating Therapist Pool
- Replace 3 fixed FTEs at Tier 3 locations with 2 floating FTEs
- Net saving: 1 FTE (~€22k) plus better peak coverage

### 3.3 Procurement Centralization
- Consumables (€18.5k): 15-20% volume discount = €3-4k
- Laundry (€43.5k): single contract = 10-15% savings = €4-6.5k
- Combined: €7-10k annual savings

### 3.4 AI/Automation
- Conservative AI savings estimate: €40-60k/year within 18 months

## Final Assessment

The three things that matter most:

1. **Kill the consulting spend.** €168k is indefensible. Audit it this week.
2. **Fix the three problem locations.** Hyatt, Sunny Coast, Excelsior must hit €300k revenue with 30% HC% or get restructured/exited.
3. **Double down on InterContinental.** Best unit in the portfolio. Feed it disproportionately.

The business does not need more locations. It needs more profit per location.
