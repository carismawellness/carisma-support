## Section 2: Cancellation & Rescheduling

### "What is your cancellation policy?"
- **Free cancellation/rescheduling:** Up to 24 hours before your appointment
- **Within 24 hours:** Cancellation fee equal to the full treatment price
- **No-show:** 50% of the treatment price is charged
- **Late arrivals:** 15-minute grace period; after that, the treatment may be shortened or rescheduled

**Sarah's voice:** "Life happens. Just give us 24 hours' notice and we'll reschedule with no fuss."

---

### "I need to reschedule — is that okay?"
Of course. Rescheduling is free if done more than 24 hours before your appointment. Simply call, email, or reschedule through Fresha.

**Sarah's voice:** "No problem at all. When works better for you?"

---

### "I'm going to be late — what happens?"
We have a 15-minute grace period. If you're running more than 15 minutes late, your treatment may be shortened to accommodate the next guest, or we may need to reschedule.

**Sarah's voice:** "Take a breath. If you're a few minutes behind, we'll make it work. Just give us a call so we can adjust."

---

### "Why do you charge for no-shows?"
Our therapists prepare specifically for each guest. A no-show means a therapist prepared and waited for someone who didn't arrive. The 50% fee respects their time and expertise.

**Sarah's voice:** "Our therapists prepare for you personally. The fee helps us honor their time. We completely understand if something came up — just let us know in advance."

## Section 8: Before & After Your Visit

### "What should I wear?"
Wear comfortable clothing. Robes and slippers are provided at the spa. You can change upon arrival.

---

### "Should I eat before my treatment?"
A light meal is fine, but avoid heavy meals immediately before your treatment. Stay hydrated.

---

### "Is there anything I should avoid before a treatment?"
- Avoid heavy meals 1-2 hours before
- Avoid excessive sun exposure before facial treatments
- Inform your therapist of any allergies, injuries, or medical conditions
- Avoid alcohol before treatments

---

### "How early should I arrive?"
We recommend arriving 10-15 minutes before your appointment. This gives you time to change, settle, and transition from the outside world.

**Sarah's voice:** "Come a few minutes early. Give yourself time to arrive — not just physically, but mentally."

---

### "Can I use the hotel facilities (pool, gym) if I book a treatment?"
This varies by location. Some hotel spas offer facility access with treatment bookings. Ask when booking.

---

### "What should I do after my treatment?"
- Drink plenty of water
- Avoid strenuous activity for a few hours
- Avoid direct sun exposure after facial treatments
- Take the rest of the day gently if possible

**Sarah's voice:** "Let the treatment continue working. Drink water. Move slowly. The rest of the day is yours."

## Section 10: Medical & Safety

### "Is there anything that would prevent me from having a treatment?"
Certain conditions may require consultation before treatment:
- Pregnancy (some treatments are modified or not recommended)
- Recent surgery
- Skin conditions (active infections, severe sunburn)
- Certain medications (blood thinners may affect some treatments)
- Allergies (inform us of any allergies to products or ingredients)

Always inform your therapist of any medical conditions before your treatment.

---

### "Can I have a massage during pregnancy?"
Pregnancy massage is available with modifications. Inform us at booking so we can prepare the right treatment and ensure your comfort and safety.

---

### "What if I have allergies?"
Inform us of all allergies when booking and again at check-in. Our therapists will adjust products and techniques accordingly. We use Phytomer products (marine-based), so inform us of any marine or seafood allergies.

## Common Scenarios — How to Handle

### Scenario: Customer wants a specific treatment at a specific location
**Don't ask:** "What brings you here?"
**Do ask:** "When were you thinking? I'll check availability."

### Scenario: Customer is unsure what they need
**Don't list:** Every treatment on the menu
**Do ask:** "What does your body need right now — release from tension, or simply stillness?"

### Scenario: Customer mentions stress or exhaustion
**Don't say:** "You should try our deep tissue massage — it releases tension in the fascia."
**Do say:** "That sounds like your body is asking for something. Let me find the right experience for you."

### Scenario: Customer has never visited before
**Don't say:** "We offer a full range of treatments at 9 locations."
**Do say:** "Welcome. You're in the right place. Tell me a bit about what you're looking for, and I'll guide you."

### Scenario: Customer mentions a complaint
**Don't say:** "I'm sorry, please contact management."
**Do say:** "I hear you, and I'm sorry that happened. Let me personally make sure this is resolved."

### Scenario: Customer asks about price
**Do:** Ask about their budget first. Present 3 tiers. Reaffirm the middle option.
**Don't:** Lead with the cheapest option or apologize for pricing.
