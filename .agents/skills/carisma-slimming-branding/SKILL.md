# Carisma Slimming Branding

## Overview

Provide comprehensive brand voice, tone of voice, and messaging guidelines for Carisma Wellness Group's medically supervised weight loss programs targeting women 25+ in Malta. This skill ensures all copy aligns with Carisma's compassionate, evidence-based, shame-free brand identity and speaks authentically to the three core customer avatars.

## Core Voice Principles

Apply these five principles to all Carisma messaging:

### 1. Compassionate Truth Telling
Speak to women like a trusted friend who has receipts. Be warm and non-judgmental, but honest about what it really takes to change. Always acknowledge their struggle before offering a solution.

### 2. Gentle Structure, Not Punishment
Communicate that they are not broken—they just need a clear plan and support. Never glorify restriction or hustle. Frame structure as caring guidance, not discipline or punishment.

### 3. Evidence Led, Emotion Aware
Combine emotional understanding with grounded, real results. Lean on data, clear explanations, and proof, but always through a human, empathetic lens.

### 4. Safe, Shame Free Space
Never talk down to weight, age, or appearance. Normalize struggle, slip-ups, and starting again. Make them feel "I am not the only one" and "It is safe to try again here."

### 5. Future Focused, Present Centered
Help them envision their next chapter while anchoring them in tiny wins today. Focus less on "fix your body" and more on "build the life you want to live in your body."

## Language Guidelines

### ✅ DO:

**Validate their experience:**
- "You are not the only one who feels this way."
- "If you have started over more times than you can count, this is for you."

**Use calm, clear, concrete language:**
- "Lose 5 to 10 kilos in a medically supervised, step by step program."
- "We plan your meals, movement and check ins together."

**Normalize relapse and imperfection:**
- "You did not fail. The plan failed you."
- "We design something you can live with on a bad day, not just a perfect one."

**Emphasize control and choice:**
- "You choose what you eat. We show you how to make it work."
- "Your plan adapts to weekends, stress, and real life."

**Talk about how life feels, not just how the body looks:**
- "Climb stairs without thinking about it."
- "Wear the dress without checking angles in photos."

### ❌ DON'T:

- **Do not shame or blame:** Avoid "lack of discipline," "you let yourself go," "fix your body"
- **Do not use toxic positivity:** Avoid "Just love yourself and it will work out," "summer body in 2 weeks"
- **Do not over promise:** Avoid extreme claims like "drop 10kg in 10 days," "results for everyone, no effort"
- **Do not reduce them to a number:** Avoid only talking about kilos and sizes without mentioning energy, confidence, health
- **Do not use fear mongering:** Avoid "You will get sick if you do not join now," "your body is a ticking time bomb"

## Emotional Journey Design

Every piece of copy should guide the reader through this four-stage emotional progression:

### 1. Validation (The "Exhale")
Create the feeling: "Finally, someone understands that I'm trying hard but it's not working."

### 2. Relief (The "Safety")
Create the feeling: "Okay, this isn't just another starvation diet. There is a doctor/science involved."

### 3. Permission (The "Yes")
Create the feeling: "It is okay for me to spend this money on myself. I need this to function."

### 4. Excitement (The "Vision")
Create the feeling: "I can actually see myself wearing that dress/bikini."

## Usage

When creating copy for Carisma:

1. **Identify the avatar** - Determine if speaking primarily to Mariella, Grace, or Sarah
2. **Apply the emotional journey** - Ensure copy moves through Validation → Relief → Permission → Excitement
3. **Check language guidelines** - Verify all DO's are present and DON'Ts are absent
4. **Embody voice principles** - Ensure the five core principles are evident in tone and messaging
5. **Reference format examples** - Use the format-specific examples as templates
