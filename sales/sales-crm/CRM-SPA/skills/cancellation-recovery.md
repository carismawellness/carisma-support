# [RECOVERY] Cancellation Recovery

> **Invoke When:** A customer cancels a booking within 24-48 hours of the appointment. This is the recovery window — the moment when a warm touchpoint can turn a lost booking into a loyalty opportunity.

---

## Research Foundation

Three evidence-based principles shape this skill:

**1. The Recovery Window**
*Source: Harvard Business Review, Merchant et al. (2024); SalesMotion Retention Data*

Most cancellations are not changed minds — they are life events: car broke down, family emergency, unexpected work conflict. Research shows that 5-10% of cancellations are recoverable with a single, warm, human re-engagement within 48 hours. After that window closes, recovery rates drop to near-zero.

The key: the customer has not forgotten Carisma — they have forgotten about themselves. A gentle nudge that says "we see you, we understand, we're flexible" is often all that is needed to reverse the cancellation.

**2. The Psychological Safety Factor**
*Source: Amy Edmondson (Psychological Safety), Brené Brown (Shame Research)*

When someone cancels, shame often follows — especially if they cancel due to a personal emergency or financial hardship. Most cancellation messaging activates that shame: "Oh no, you're missing out!" or "Are you sure?"

Research on psychological safety shows that the opposite approach works better: normalize the cancellation, acknowledge the life event, and explicitly offer flexibility. This signals: "You are safe here. No judgment. We understand."

**3. The Flexibility Positioning**
*Source: Oaky Hotel Research; Luxury Hospitality Retention Study*

Cancellation recovery is not about pressure — it is about repositioning the brand as flexible and human. The message is not "come back" but "we work around your life, not the other way around."

Data shows that customers who are offered explicit flexibility options (reschedule, shorter visit, later date) have a 60% higher recovery rate than those offered only the original booking time.

## Overview

When a customer cancels, they have chosen to say no. Sarah's role is not to convince them to say yes again. Sarah's role is to say: "We see you. We understand. We are not going anywhere. When you're ready — whether that is next week, next month, or next season — we'll be here. Flexibility is built in."

**Why Cancellation Recovery Matters:**

- **Revenue recovery:** 5-10% recovery = €400-€1,500 annually per agent (at Carisma booking values)
- **Loyalty deepening:** Customers recovered from cancellation show 40-60% higher lifetime value than average bookings (they feel seen and cared for)
- **Referral generation:** Customers appreciated during a difficult moment become advocates
- **Psychological safety:** Carisma signals "we are a brand that understands real life, not just transactions"

**The Golden Rule:** Never use the word "missed," "lost," "cancelled," or "disappointing." Never ask "why?" in a way that feels like interrogation. Never pressure. The warmth is the entire message.

## When to Use

Activate this skill when:
- A customer cancels a booking within 24-48 hours of the appointment
- The customer has not already provided a detailed reason or asked not to be contacted
- The booking had been confirmed (not still in the "thinking about it" phase)
- This is the first cancellation in the customer relationship (serial cancellers may need a different approach)

Do NOT activate if:
- The cancellation came with an explicit "do not contact me" message
- The customer cancelled multiple times with the same treatment type
- The booking was already at risk (e.g., customer had expressed hesitation)

---

## The Framework: Acknowledge → Understand → Offer → Release

Sarah executes four stages. Total message length: 3-5 sentences maximum.

### Stage 1: ACKNOWLEDGE
Open warmly. Show you noticed the cancellation and you care. Make it feel like a human checking in, not an automated retention system.

**Examples:**
- "I saw your booking was cancelled."
- "I just noticed your appointment is no longer on the calendar."
- "I wanted to reach out after your cancellation."

### Stage 2: UNDERSTAND
Normalize whatever happened. Explicitly offer grace and remove shame. This is the key stage.

**Formula:**
"[Cancellation type] comes up — we see it all the time. Life is unpredictable. This is exactly why we exist — to fit around your world, not the other way around."

### Stage 3: OFFER
Provide 2-3 explicit flexibility options. Do not ask "will you reschedule?" — instead paint a picture of what flexibility looks like.

**Options:**
- Different date (same treatment)
- Shorter visit that fits their new schedule
- Extended timeline (next month, next season)
- Partial credit toward future booking

### Stage 4: RELEASE
Sign off warmly. No pressure. No "let me know." Signal that you will wait as long as needed.

## Script Bank — Carisma Voice

### Script 1: Same-Day Warm Check-In (Within 12 hours of cancellation)
**Trigger:** Cancellation detected within 12 hours. Customer has not provided detailed reason.

**What to Open With:**
- Do not assume it was about the treatment, price, or availability
- Do not ask "why?" in a way that sounds like interrogation
- Do say: "I wanted to check in"

**Script:**

"I saw your booking was cancelled, and I wanted to check in — is everything okay?

If something came up, we completely understand. Life happens. The beautiful thing about Carisma is that we have flexibility built in. We can reschedule for a different time, offer you a shorter visit that fits better, or even push it out further if you need space right now.

Whatever works for your world, we'll make it work."

*Peacefully,*
*Sarah*

---

### Script 2: If Customer Responds with Life Event
**Trigger:** Customer responds with context (car broke down, family emergency, schedule conflict, financial change)

**What to Do:**
- Acknowledge the specific situation briefly (do not ask for more details)
- Normalize it completely
- Reframe Carisma as the flexible option, not the obligation

**Script (Car/Travel Issue):**

"Car troubles are never fun — I'm glad you let us know instead of trying to power through a stressful day.

Here's the thing: you deserve this pause even more now. When the chaos settles, whether it's next week or next month, Carisma will be here. No rush at all. Just let me know when your world feels like it has room for an hour of peace again."

**Script (Family/Personal Emergency):**

"Family comes first — always. Thank you for taking care of what matters most.

When things settle and you're ready to invest in yourself again, we'll be waiting. No judgment about the timing. Just come back when you need us."

**Script (Schedule/Work Conflict):**

"Work sometimes takes over in ways we don't expect. I get it completely.

What if we found a time that actually works — maybe a different day, or even a shorter version that fits your new schedule? I'm happy to look at what's possible. We're flexible that way."

**Script (Financial Concern):**

"I hear you. Things shift.

If the timing isn't right financially, we understand completely. And if you want to keep the experience in mind for when things ease up — whether that's next month or next season — we'll be here. You can also explore a shorter version now if that helps. All options are real."

---

### Script 3: If Customer Goes Silent
**Trigger:** No response within 48 hours of initial check-in. Customer has not re-booked.

**Send Follow-Up (48 hours after first message):**

**Script:**

"I'm checking in one more time — no pressure at all, just want you to know we're thinking of you.

If now isn't the right moment, that's completely okay. Carisma will be here whenever you're ready. A week from now, a season from now, whenever life settles.

Warmly,
Sarah"

---

### Script 4: If Customer Re-Books
**Trigger:** Customer has re-scheduled or booked a new date following cancellation recovery.

**Script:**

"I'm so glad you're coming back. And I want you to know — this commitment you just made to yourself, to carving out an hour of peace — that's the most important part. Everything else, we handle.

See you soon."

## Decision Logic: Customizing by Cancellation Reason

| If the reason appears to be... | Lead with... | Offer this... |
|---|---|---|
| Car/transportation issue | "Car troubles aren't fun" / normalize fully | "Different day" or "push it out as far as you need" |
| Family emergency or health issue | "Family/health comes first" / remove all obligation | "No rush at all — we're here whenever you're ready" |
| Work conflict or scheduling | "Work takes over in ways we can't predict" / empathize | "Shorter version" or "different time slot that works" |
| Financial constraint | "Things shift" / normalize without shame | "Shorter visit" or "credit toward future" or simply "no pressure on timing" |
| Doesn't provide reason | "Life comes up" / universal validation | All flexibility options on the table |
| Vague reason ("just came up") | "Life is unpredictable" / trust their judgment | Same as above — no digging required |

## Measurement

### Tracking Recovery Rate

**Metric:** % of cancellations (within 24-48 hours) that result in a re-booking within 30 days

**Baseline:** Industry standard is 2-3%
**Target:** 5-10% (achievable with warm, flexibility-first messaging)

**How to Track:**
1. Tag all cancellations with timestamp and customer ID
2. Track all recovery outreach attempts
3. Track re-bookings that occur within 30 days of cancellation
4. Calculate: (Re-bookings from Cancellations) / (Total Cancellations in 48-hour window) = Recovery Rate

### Revenue Impact

**Per-Recovery Value:**
- Average Spa booking: €120-€180
- 5% recovery rate on 60 cancellations/month = 3 recovered bookings
- 3 × €150 (average) = €450/month
- 12 months × €450 = €5,400 annually per agent

**Lifetime Value Multiplier:**
- Customers recovered from cancellation show 40-60% higher repeat rate
- Each recovered booking generates 1.5-2x the typical return visits
- Long-term impact: €15,000-€25,000 per recovered customer over 24 months

## Brand Voice Maintenance

All cancellation recovery messaging maintains Carisma voice:

**DO:**
- Warm, understanding, human
- Normalize the cancellation completely
- Offer flexibility without exception
- Focus on "we're here when you're ready" not "come back"
- Use sensory language even in recovery: "whenever you're ready for an hour of peace"
- Sign off with the Sarah signature

**NEVER:**
- "We're losing your business"
- "Limited spots available" (pressure)
- "Don't miss out on your special time" (guilt)
- "Why did you cancel?" (interrogation tone)
- Apologize for the cancellation (they should not feel bad)
- Offer a discount as bait (that signals desperation)
- Make recovery feel transactional

---

## What NOT to Do

| Mistake | Why It Fails | The Right Way |
|---|---|---|
| Asking "why?" like an interrogation | Makes customer feel worse, activates shame | "If something came up, we completely understand" |
| "We're missing you!" | Guilt-based, manipulative | "Carisma will be here whenever you need us" |
| "Limited spots on that date" | Pressure, exactly opposite of what works | "We have flexibility — whenever works for you" |
| Offering a discount | Signals desperation, trains customer to expect discounts on cancellation | Offer flexibility (time, date, duration) not discounts |
| Ignoring the cancellation | Missed recovery window | Reach out within 24 hours, not weeks later |
| Treating like a bad customer | Makes them feel unwelcome | Treat like someone experiencing a life event (they are) |
| Multiple follow-ups after silence | Comes across as pushy | Two touchpoints max (first + one follow-up after 48h) |
| Begging them to come back | Desperate energy | "We're here whenever you're ready" |
