## Medical Positioning (Critical -- Reps Must Convey This)

### Doctor-Led Weight Loss as Standard
- This is not an add-on or marketing label. Medical evaluation is the foundation of every program.
- Every client begins with a full medical-grade assessment: body composition scan (Tanita), metabolic screening, blood tests where appropriate, intolerance screens, and doctor consultation.
- Lead doctor: Dr. Zaid Teebi (30+ years, Sports Medicine ACSM, Pain Management Harvard, Allergy Imperial College London).

### Medical-Grade Assessment First
- Body composition analysis (fat, muscle, visceral fat, water -- not just weight).
- Comprehensive medical history, medications, past diets, hormonal and metabolic concerns.
- Clinical measurements and day-to-day wellbeing assessment (fatigue, cravings, sleep, mood, digestion, joint pain).
- Blood tests, blood pressure checks, or food intolerance tests recommended before starting when appropriate.

### GLP-1 Only as Appropriate, Never Standalone
- GLP-1 medications (Ozempic, Mounjaro) are prescription-only, offered only after full medical assessment.
- Used to support, not replace, structured lifestyle change. Always paired with nutrition, movement, accountability, and treatment.
- "The biggest clinical mistake: prescribing GLP-1s without a system. Medication can quiet appetite, but it does not build muscle, teach eating, fix emotional drivers, or create long-term habits."
- GLP-1 is an option, not a requirement. If a client prefers not to use medication, the program focuses on lifestyle, movement, and treatments instead.

### Holistic Methodology
- Four integrated pillars: medical-grade assessment, diet and accountability, exercise and movement, targeted body treatments.
- One integrated program under one roof -- not separate disconnected services.
- "We are not another diet clinic."

## Core Philosophy

### The Central Belief
"People shouldn't be punished by their biology -- they should be understood, supported, and guided scientifically."

### Adherence Over Perfection
Real progress comes from what you can repeat, not what you can do once. The method is built for adherence, not perfection.

### Principles That Drive Us

1. **Medical First, Not Cosmetic First** -- Lead with medical evaluation and personalised assessment, ensuring safety and efficacy.
2. **Individualised, Not Template-Driven** -- Every plan is tailored to body, lifestyle, age, hormones, and metabolic history.
3. **Evidence-Based Tools & Technology** -- Proven interventions (nutrition strategies, activity prescriptions, clinically validated devices, and medications) -- not fads.
4. **Respect for What Works Long-Term** -- No crash diets, no starvation, no extreme restriction. Sustainable changes that protect muscle, metabolism, and daily life.
5. **Accountability with Compassion** -- Weekly check-ins, coaching support, measured results -- with encouragement every step of the way.
6. **Visible, Measurable Results** -- Inch loss and improved shape, not vague claims. Progress is verified, not hoped for.

### The Five Brand Pillars (Katya's Voice)

1. **Compassionate Truth-Telling** -- Warm, never judging, but honest about what it takes. Like a trusted friend with receipts.
2. **Gentle Structure, Not Punishment** -- Clear plan and support, no glorification of restriction.
3. **Evidence-Led, Emotion-Aware** -- Emotional understanding combined with grounded, real results. Science through a human lens.
4. **Safe, Shame-Free Space** -- Normalize slip-ups, starting again, hard weeks, and the messiness of real life.
5. **Future-Focused, Present-Centered** -- Help envision their next chapter while anchoring in tiny wins today.

### The Three Anchors (Reps Should Know These)

1. **Pace** -- Rhythm and consistency in eating times.
2. **Protein-first** -- Structure built around satiety, not restriction.
3. **Return at the next meal** -- Slip-ups are normal; returning is what matters.

### Key Principles to Remember
- **Adherence > Perfection** -- A plan you can repeat forever beats one you can't maintain.
- **Defaults > Decisions** -- Fewer choices = less mental energy = better adherence.
- **Structure > Willpower** -- Build the system so willpower isn't constantly tested.
- **Real Life > Fantasy** -- Design for how clients actually live, not how they wish they lived.

## Consultation Flow

### Step 1: Complimentary Consultation
"Every program starts with a full in-clinic consultation. This is where we decide together if our guided transformation protocol is the right fit for you. Because we stand behind your results, we are selective about who we can work with."

During consultation, the team:
- Goes through goals, reasons for changing, and timeline.
- Reviews health history, medications, and past diets.
- Looks at current capacity for food changes, movement, and clinic visits.
- Shares real before-and-after cases that match age, body type, and situation.
- Explains exactly how the guarantee works and what support is received.
- Tells the prospect honestly if they are a good candidate for transformation.

"If we do not believe we can deliver real, measurable change, we will not enrol you into the program."

### Step 2: Medical-Grade Assessment
- Full body composition analysis (Tanita scan: fat, muscle, visceral fat, water).
- Doctor consultation with Dr. Zaid Teebi.
- Medical history review, medications, hormonal/metabolic concerns.
- Blood tests, blood pressure checks, food intolerance tests as needed.
- Exploration of day-to-day wellbeing: fatigue, cravings, sleep, mood, digestion, joint pain.

### Step 3: Personalised Program Design
Based on assessment:
- Personalised nutrition plan using Mediterranean-style framework.
- Timing track selection (fasting vs non-fasting).
- Protein targets (1.4-2.0g per kg target body weight).
- Points budget set by clinic.
- GLP-1 evaluation (if clinically appropriate).
- Treatment plan: which body sculpting modalities suit their goals.
- Movement plan matched to fitness level.

### Step 4: Active Program
- Weekly check-ins with weigh-ins and measurements.
- One-to-one accountability with a dedicated coach.
- WhatsApp coaching and follow-up.
- Progress reports and small, clear adjustments.
- Treatment sessions as scheduled.
- Gym/movement access (Technogym facilities, group classes, PT).

### Step 5: Ongoing Support & Maintenance
- Regular progress body composition scans.
- Plateau management (clinic adjusts, not client restricts more).
- Extended Care Commitment if targets not met.
- Long-term maintenance plan for after initial fat loss.

### Who This Program Is For
- Adults 28-60 with 5-20 kg to lose.

- Wants a medical approach, not another fad diet.
- Wants to lose weight while still eating real food.
- Willing to follow a clear plan with weekly check-ins.
- Ready to invest time, energy, and budget into health.
- Committed to attend all scheduled appointments and sessions.

### Who This Program Is NOT For
- Pregnant or breastfeeding.
- Wants rapid, extreme "crash diet" solution.
- Not willing to follow a structured plan.
- Cannot commit to weekly check-ins or appointments.
- Wants results without changing routines, eating habits, or lifestyle.
- Currently dealing with unmanaged medical condition or medication requiring clearance (screened in consultation).

## Nutrition & Lifestyle Guidance

### The Carisma Slimming Method (4 Pillars)

#### Pillar 1: WHEN to Eat (Timing & Rhythm)
- **Default:** 4-hour delay from waking before first meal.
- **Finish dinner:** 3 hours before sleep when possible.
- **Two tracks:** Fasting (default) or Non-Fasting (for medical/personal reasons).
- **Morning tools:** Sparkling water, black coffee, plain tea. Nothing else.
- **Purpose:** Build rhythm, protect evenings (when willpower is lowest and social eating highest), reduce decision fatigue.
- **Maltese context:** "We don't fight culture -- we design around it." Family dinners, dinners out, late meals are expected.

**Who should NOT fast:**
- Pregnant or breastfeeding
- Type 1 diabetes or insulin-dependent
- History of disordered eating
- Regularly dizzy/faint without breakfast
- Under 18
- Doctor has advised against fasting
- Taking medications requiring food

**Safety rule:** "If you ever feel unwell, eat. Safety always comes first."

#### Pillar 2: WHAT to Eat (Nutrition)
- **Protein first** at every main meal (1-2 palm-sized portions).
- **Points system:** 1 calorie = 1 point. Non-starchy vegetables = 0 points (free).
- **Fibre floor:** 25-30g daily built into regular meals.
- **No forbidden foods** -- everything has points.
- **Watch liquid points:** Sweet coffees, juices, alcohol add up without providing fullness.
- **Protein targets:** Clinical starting range 1.4-2.0g per kg target body weight/day (personalised by clinic).

#### Pillar 3: HOW MUCH to Eat (Amounts)
- **Visual rule:** 1-2 palm portions of protein per main meal. 2 fists of vegetables per meal.
- **Points budget:** Set by clinic (typically 1200-1800 range depending on individual).
- **Adjust up if constantly hungry; adjust down if not losing.**

#### Pillar 4: WHICH ORDER to Eat (Plate Formula)
- **Build plate:** Protein first, vegetables next, carbs last, fats purposeful.
- **Eat sequence:** Protein and vegetables first, then carbs (supports blood sugar stability).
- **One main carb source per meal** (don't stack bread + pasta + rice).

### Key Nutrition Principles Reps Should Know

**The Points System:**
- Simple budgeting without obsessive calorie counting.
- Vegetables are free because they provide volume, fibre, and satisfaction without tightening the budget.
- Starchy vegetables (potatoes, corn, peas) DO count as points.

**Liquid Points Trap:**
- Sweet/flavoured coffees, juices, smoothies, alcohol, generous milk in coffee -- all count and add up fast.
- Most common reason clients think "the plan isn't working" when it actually is fine.

**The Plate Formula (Memorise This):**
1. Protein first (anchor of satiety)
2. Vegetables next (free volume)
3. Carbs last (flexible, structured)
4. Fats purposeful (intentional, not accidental)

### Common Questions and Answers

**"Can I eat bread/pasta/rice?"** -- Yes. No foods are forbidden. Count the points. Mediterranean-style framework includes these.

**"Do I have to follow a strict meal plan?"** -- Structure, not prison. Portions and timing so bread, pasta, wine, and dessert fit in the right way. Planned for weekends, events, and eating out.

**"What if I go over my points?"** -- Normal. Once in a while, no problem. Return to budget tomorrow. The system survives real life.

**"Can I exercise while doing this?"** -- Yes. Points budget already factors activity level. Don't count "exercise calories" on top.

**"Is this program different from a normal diet?"** -- "Most diets give you a meal plan and leave you alone. This brings everything together: medical assessment, personalised nutrition, movement plan, premium devices, and high-touch support."

### Meal Building Basics
- **Weekday lunch:** High protein, structured (chicken/fish/beef + 2 fists vegetables + one carb + measured fat).
- **Dinner:** Flexible, real life. Protein anchors the meal, rest accommodates social context.
- **Restaurant:** Order protein + vegetables + one carb. Don't stack carbs. Enjoy without guilt.
- **Family dinner:** Eat what's served, apply the formula as much as possible. One meal doesn't break the system.
- **Snacks:** Usually unnecessary if lunch protein is adequate. If needed: protein-based (Greek yogurt, nuts).

- "Call our clinic" / "Visit our website" / "Contact us at..."

## Red Flags in Conversations

### Unrealistic Promises to Watch For
- Promising specific kg loss in specific timeframe without "typical" qualifier.
- Saying treatments alone will cause weight loss (CoolSculpting/VelaShape/fat dissolving are for targeted contouring, NOT weight loss).
- Implying GLP-1 works without lifestyle change.
- Promising results without effort or commitment.
- Telling a client EMSculpt NEO replaces the gym.

### Medical Claims That Cross the Line
- Diagnosing medical conditions (diabetes, thyroid, hormonal issues). Always refer to clinic.
- Giving personalised protein/calorie targets. These come from the clinic only.
- Advising on medication interactions or adjustments.
- Telling pregnant/breastfeeding clients they can fast or restrict.
- Making claims about specific medical outcomes.

### Shame-Inducing Language
- Any language that implies the client is lazy, weak, or lacks willpower.
- Comparing their body negatively to others.
- Using "failure" language about past diet attempts (reframe as "the plans failed you").
- Judgmental tone about eating choices, slip-ups, or weight.
- "You should have..." / "If you had just..." / "Why can't you..."

### Diet Culture Language to Avoid
- "Cheat day" / "cheat meal" (use "social meal" or "flexible dinner").
- "Clean eating" (implies other eating is "dirty").
- "Guilty pleasure" (removes guilt from food entirely).
- "Detox" / "cleanse" (not evidence-based language for this program).
- "Earn your food" through exercise (old diet thinking).
- "Good" and "bad" food labels.
- "Starting over" / "back to day one" (use "returning to structure").

## Consultation Best Practices

### How to Discuss Sensitive Weight Topics
- Always validate before guiding. Acknowledge the struggle before offering the solution.
- Use "you" language, not "women" or "people" -- make it personal.
- Lead with emotion, support with logic. Start with how they feel, then explain why/how.
- Normalize struggle. "Everyone experiences these issues" -- not just them.
- Avoid reducing them to a number. Talk about energy, confidence, freedom, how life feels.

### Objection Handling for Slimming-Specific Concerns

**"I've tried everything and nothing works."**
Response framework: "That skepticism is earned. Most plans have failed you. This one's different because it's built for your real life. Give it 3 weeks of consistent structure. Results speak louder than belief."

**"That's more than I expected" (pricing).**
Reframe: "You're not paying for time, you're paying for the protocol that actually works. The 4-week Starter is a great way to try it first."

**"Do you have discounts?"**
Value response: "We offer payment plans so commitment feels easier -- not discounts. Better commitment = higher completion."

**"I don't believe this will work for me."**
"Past failures aren't your fault. This method is different: designed for real life, doesn't ask perfection. Try structure for 3 weeks. Your body will show you."

**"I'm scared of GLP-1 medication."**
"GLP-1 is an option, not a requirement. If you prefer not to use medication, we focus on lifestyle, movement, and treatments instead. Your choice."

**"I always give up after a few weeks."**
"That's not about you -- it's about the old plans being fragile. This system is designed to survive slip-ups. Return at the next meal. That's all we ask."

**"I had a bad week and ate everything."**
"One week doesn't erase your progress. We expected this and have a plan. Let's talk about what triggered it."

### Handling Relapse Conversations
- Slip-ups are information, not failure. What triggered it? That's data for adjustment.
- Return is the metric. How fast they come back matters more than how much they slipped.
- No punishment, no compensation. Just rhythm: return at next meal.
- Normalize: "Everyone struggles. That's being human."
- Structure over willpower: if constantly failing, structure needs tweaking, not the person.
