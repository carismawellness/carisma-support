# Retention — Hospitality Program Knowledge

> Source: Astra Culture Hospitality Program / Retention folder

## Overview

Retention is about creating an experience so exceptional that patients return, refer, and become brand advocates. It encompasses the full post-treatment journey, team accountability systems, and leadership frameworks that sustain service excellence.

## Steps of Service — Full 9-Step Framework

### Step 1: Initial Contact
- Answer within 3 rings with a warm tone
- Avoid giving pricing — redirect to consultation value
- Light discovery: identify needs, book the visit
- Use hospitality language (see Verbiage Standards)

### Step 2: Pre-Arrival
- Send confirmation messages with address and details
- Review patient notes and history
- Prepare intake forms and treatment room

### Step 3: Arrival and Check-In
- Greet by name, offer refreshments
- Welcome tour for first-time visitors
- 5ft/10ft Rule (eye contact at 10ft, verbal greeting at 5ft)
- Credential the provider before the consultation begins

### Step 4: Consultation
- Active listening first — let the patient tell their story
- Open-ended discovery questions
- Investment Frame for pricing
- Present Good/Better/Best program tiers
- Use PROOF visuals (before/after, scans, diagnostics)

### Step 5: Treatment Experience
- Comfort items offered proactively
- Continuous check-ins throughout treatment
- Luxury touches that elevate the experience
- Create shareable moments

### Step 6: Post-Treatment and Checkout
- Aftercare instructions (clear, written)
- Present VIP/membership options
- Schedule next visit before patient leaves
- Ask for reviews (Google, social media)

### Step 7: Follow-Up
- 24-hour post-treatment text check-in
- Referral incentive introduction
- Nurture sequences (email/text)
- Community event invitations

### Step 8: Emergency and Unexpected Situations
- **Late arrivals:** Accommodate graciously when possible, reschedule warmly when not
- **Cancellations:** Express understanding, rebook immediately
- **Dissatisfaction:** Listen fully, acknowledge, offer resolution before escalation

### Step 9: Managing Team Execution
- Standard Operating Procedures for every touchpoint
- Morning huddles to align the team daily
- Secret shopper audits for quality assurance
- KPI tracking (booking rates, retention rates, review counts)
- Gamification to keep standards high and morale up

## The Friendly Bust — Team Accountability System

Quick, respectful corrections to maintain standards in real time.

### What It Is
A brief, direct, non-punitive correction when a teammate misses a standard. Delivered in the moment, resolved in the moment.

### Common Scenarios and Corrections
- **Missed walk-out:** "Hey, Mrs. [Name] just left without scheduling her next visit — can you follow up before end of day?"
- **Room not reset:** "Room 2 still has the last patient's setup — can you flip it before the next one?"
- **Missed injection confirmation:** "Did we confirm the injection site with the patient before starting?"
- **Incomplete phone greeting:** "I noticed the brand name got dropped on that last call — let's tighten that up."
- **Treatment not charted:** "The chart for [patient] is still open — can you close it out?"
- **Payment step skipped:** "We forgot to collect the deposit before they left — let's call them."

### Expected Response Protocol
- "AOA — on it" (acknowledgment + action)
- "Thanks, I'll fix it now" (ownership)
- "Good catch" (gratitude, not defensiveness)

### Key Principles
- Never in front of patients
- Address the behavior, not the person
- Immediate — don't let it fester
- Two-way — anyone can bust anyone, regardless of seniority

## The CHEF Method — Transformational Leadership

### Coach
- Develop the person, not just the task
- Correct with clarity, not criticism
- Ask questions that lead to self-discovery: "What would you do differently?"
- Create growth moments from mistakes

### Host
- Set the tone for the entire clinic
- Model the presence you expect from the team
- Create emotional safety — team members should feel comfortable asking questions and admitting errors
- Be the energy barometer

### Execute
- Hold the standard — never let quality slide because it's a busy day
- Get hands dirty — leaders who only delegate lose respect
- Be early, be prepared, be specific
- Follow through on every commitment

### Follow Up
- Reinforce progress — acknowledge improvement publicly
- Acknowledge growth — "I noticed you handled that perfectly today"
- Address patterns — if the same issue recurs, it's a coaching conversation, not another bust

## The AOA — Clinic Communication System

### Core Concept
- **AOA (Audible Order Acknowledgement):** "I heard you, I'm on it"
- **No Hands:** "I'm currently unavailable, someone else needs to cover"

### Clinic Scenarios
- **Bathroom reset:** "Bathroom needs attention" → "AOA"
- **Phone coverage:** "Can someone grab the phone?" → "AOA — got it"
- **Patient walk-back:** "Ready to bring [patient] back" → "AOA — walking them now"
- **Room reset:** "Room 3 needs to be flipped" → "AOA — on it"
- **Vitals:** "[Patient] needs vitals before the doctor" → "AOA"
- **Checkout handoff:** "[Patient] is ready for checkout" → "AOA — I'll take them"
- **Supply restock:** "We're low on [item] in Room 1" → "AOA — restocking"
- **End-of-day close:** "Closing checklist — who has rooms?" → "AOA — I've got rooms 1-3"

### Why It Matters
- Eliminates ambiguity about who's handling what
- Prevents tasks from falling through the cracks
- Creates a culture of immediate accountability
- Keeps patient experience seamless
