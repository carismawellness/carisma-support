# Sales Feedback Agent -- Scoring Rubric

**Version:** 1.0
**Last Updated:** 2026-04-14
**Applies to:** All SDR conversations across Spa, Aesthetics, and Slimming brands
**Scored by:** AI sub-agents in the Sales Feedback Agent system

---

## Overview

Every SDR conversation is scored on 6 dimensions. Each dimension receives a score from 1 to 10. The composite score is a weighted average scaled to 100. Scoring agents must apply these criteria consistently regardless of brand, channel (phone, WhatsApp, DM, email), or SDR identity.

---

## Dimensions and Weights

| # | Dimension | Weight | What It Measures |
|---|-----------|--------|------------------|
| 1 | Script Compliance | 20% | Adherence to the brand's SDR script steps and sequence |
| 2 | Brand Voice and Positioning | 15% | Tone, persona consistency, forbidden language avoidance |
| 3 | Discovery and Needs Assessment | 20% | Quality of questions, listening, and concern identification |
| 4 | Objection Handling and Persuasion | 20% | Skill in addressing resistance and reframing value |
| 5 | Close and Next Steps | 15% | Booking attempt, deposit collection, confirmation process |
| 6 | Follow-Up and Re-engagement | 10% | Post-conversation nurture, referral asks, rebooking |

---

## Dimension 1: Script Compliance (20%)

Measures how closely the SDR follows the brand-specific call/chat script steps in the correct order.

### 9-10 (Elite)
- Every script step is executed in the correct sequence
- Greeting includes brand name, SDR's own name, and caller confirmation by name
- Treatment history check is asked naturally, not robotically
- Value explanation includes full package walkthrough: consultation, main treatment, bonuses
- Perceived value is stated before actual price (anchoring)
- Soft close ("How does this sound?") is used before moving to booking
- Deposit amount and payment options are presented correctly
- Appointment confirmed with day, time, and location
- Confirmation text/message promised and details collected
- Warm close with offer of further assistance
- CRM notes referenced or implied
- Conversation feels natural despite hitting every checkpoint

### 7-8 (Strong)
- Most script steps are completed (missing 1-2 non-critical steps)
- Greeting is correct but may be slightly informal or miss one element
- Value explanation covers the main points but may skip a bonus or the perceived value anchor
- Deposit and booking flow are handled correctly
- Sequence may deviate slightly (e.g., asking for details before confirming the time) but all steps are completed
- Minor omissions that do not materially impact the outcome (e.g., forgot to mention the number of spa locations)

### 5-6 (Developing)
- Several script steps are missed or reordered in a way that weakens the flow
- Greeting is present but missing brand name or caller confirmation
- Discovery question is asked but not the specific open-ended "why" question the script calls for
- Value explanation is partial: mentions the treatment but skips the full package walkthrough
- Price is stated without the perceived value anchor
- Deposit is mentioned but payment options are incomplete (only one option given)
- Appointment is booked but details are not confirmed back to the client
- Confirmation text is not explicitly promised

### 3-4 (Needs Improvement)
- More steps missed than hit
- Greeting is generic ("Hi, how can I help?") without brand name or name confirmation
- No treatment history check
- Value explanation is a single sentence rather than a structured walkthrough
- No soft close before moving to booking
- Deposit is either not mentioned or the wrong amount is stated
- Appointment details are incomplete or not confirmed
- No warm close, call ends abruptly

### 1-2 (Critical)
- Script is effectively ignored
- No greeting or a greeting that violates brand standards
- No discovery, no value explanation, no structured flow
- The SDR jumps directly to pricing without any context
- No booking attempt or deposit collection
- The conversation feels like an unstructured chat with no sales methodology
- CRM actions (notes, confirmations) are clearly not happening

## Dimension 2: Brand Voice and Positioning (15%)

Measures alignment with the brand persona, tone keywords, language rules, and forbidden phrases.

### 9-10 (Elite)
- Persona is consistent throughout (Sarah for Spa/Aesthetics, Katya for Slimming)
- Tone matches brand keywords exactly (peaceful/soothing for Spa, confident/warm for Aesthetics, compassionate/evidence-led for Slimming)
- Zero forbidden phrases or AI voice tells detected
- No em-dashes used anywhere
- Response length stays within brand limits (max 2-3 sentences, max 80 words per response for chat)
- One question maximum per message
- No bullet lists used for questions
- Factual questions answered immediately before any diagnostic
- Sensory/empowering/compassionate language used naturally (brand-appropriate)
- Pricing presented using 3-tier framework with Tier 2 reaffirmation
- Sign-off used only at natural conversation endpoints with the correct variant
- The conversation could be used as training material for new SDRs

### 7-8 (Strong)
- Persona is maintained throughout with minor inconsistencies
- Tone is on-brand with occasional neutral (neither good nor bad) phrasing
- No forbidden phrases, but language could be more distinctively brand-voiced
- No em-dashes
- Response length is mostly within limits (one response may be slightly long)
- Pricing uses the 3-tier framework but Tier 2 reaffirmation may be weak or absent
- Sign-off is correct when used

### 5-6 (Developing)
- Persona is identifiable but not consistently maintained
- Tone drifts between brand voice and generic professional tone
- 1-2 forbidden phrases or AI voice tells detected (e.g., "Certainly!", "I'd be happy to")
- Occasional em-dash usage
- Some responses exceed the word/sentence limit
- Multiple questions asked in a single message
- Pricing is presented but not using the 3-tier framework
- The conversation reads as competent but interchangeable with any clinic

### 3-4 (Needs Improvement)
- Persona is inconsistent or absent (wrong name, wrong signature)
- Tone does not match brand keywords
- 3+ forbidden phrases or AI voice tells
- Em-dashes used repeatedly
- Responses are routinely too long, too many questions per message
- Bullet lists used for questions or options
- Generic cliches present: "Pamper yourself", "Amazing!", "Fantastic!"
- Pricing is a flat number with no framework
- Brand heritage and positioning are never mentioned

### 1-2 (Critical)
- Wrong persona used (e.g., Sarah used for Slimming, Katya used for Spa)
- Tone actively contradicts brand voice (aggressive urgency for Spa, shame language for Slimming, "fix your flaws" for Aesthetics)
- Multiple forbidden phrases in a single response
- Referrals to external channels ("Call our team", "Visit our website", "Go to Fresha")
- Tourist-destination framing, corporate jargon, or clinical-only language
- Response reads like a chatbot template, not a human conversation
- Pricing errors or unauthorized discounts offered
- The conversation could damage brand reputation if shared publicly

## Dimension 3: Discovery and Needs Assessment (20%)

Measures the quality and depth of information gathering before presenting solutions.

### 9-10 (Elite)
- At least 2-3 open-ended discovery questions asked
- The "why" question is asked: what motivates them, what concerns them, what they hope to achieve
- Treatment/slimming history is explored to gauge experience level
- Emotional dimension is explored ("How does that make you feel?" / "What would change for you?")
- SDR paraphrases or acknowledges the client's concerns back to them before presenting solutions
- Client's awareness level is identified and language is matched accordingly
- Specific details are captured (concern area, timeline, previous attempts, budget range)
- Discovery feels conversational, not like an interrogation
- Information gathered directly informs the recommendation made

### 7-8 (Strong)
- 2+ discovery questions asked, including the core "why" question
- Treatment history is checked
- Concerns are acknowledged before presenting solutions
- Minor gaps in emotional exploration or timeline questioning
- Discovery is mostly conversational with occasional scripted-feeling moments
- Information gathered is used to shape the recommendation

### 5-6 (Developing)
- 1-2 discovery questions asked but they are surface-level or closed-ended
- Treatment history is either not asked or asked as an afterthought
- Concerns are heard but not paraphrased back or explicitly acknowledged
- SDR moves to the pitch too quickly after minimal discovery
- Some relevant information is gathered but key details are missing (e.g., budget, timeline, emotional drivers)
- Recommendation feels somewhat generic because discovery was insufficient

### 3-4 (Needs Improvement)
- Only 1 basic question asked ("What are you interested in?")
- No exploration of why, history, emotional dimension, or awareness level
- SDR does not acknowledge concerns before presenting solutions
- Discovery and pitch happen simultaneously, with the SDR leading toward a specific treatment from the start
- The recommendation does not connect to anything the client said
- Client's specific needs are assumed rather than uncovered

### 1-2 (Critical)
- No discovery questions asked at all
- SDR launches directly into a pitch, pricing, or booking
- Client's stated concerns are ignored or overridden
- The SDR tells the client what they need rather than asking
- No active listening demonstrated (no acknowledgment, no paraphrasing, no follow-ups)
- The interaction feels transactional rather than consultative

## Dimension 4: Objection Handling and Persuasion (20%)

Measures skill in addressing resistance, reframing value, and maintaining rapport under pressure.

### Special Handling: No Objection Arose

If no objection was raised during the conversation, score this dimension on the SDR's **persuasion quality** throughout the interaction:
- How effectively was value communicated?
- Was the Investment Frame used (perceived value before actual price)?
- Were proof elements used (social proof, patient stories, credentials)?
- Was hospitality language used for reframing (e.g., "investment" not "cost")?
- Were potential objections preemptively addressed?

A conversation with no objections and strong persuasion throughout can still score 9-10 on this dimension.

### 9-10 (Elite)
- Objection is acknowledged immediately without defensiveness
- Empathy is shown before redirecting ("I completely understand")
- The underlying concern is addressed, not just the surface objection
- Value is re-established using the Investment Frame
- Social proof or patient stories are used naturally ("Many of our patients felt the same way")
- Hospitality language is maintained throughout (Verbiage Swap standards)
- Negative language is reframed: "expensive" to "premium", "pain" to "sensation", "side effects" to "what to expect"
- The client's emotional state shifts from resistance to openness
- If price is the objection, a lower tier is offered without apologizing for the original price
- Rapport is maintained or strengthened after the objection is handled

### 7-8 (Strong)
- Objection is acknowledged and empathy is shown
- Redirect is effective but may lack specificity (generic social proof instead of a relevant story)
- Value is re-established but the Investment Frame may not be fully deployed
- Hospitality language is mostly maintained
- The client moves forward after the objection
- Minor missed opportunity to preemptively address a follow-up concern

### 5-6 (Developing)
- Objection is acknowledged but empathy is thin or formulaic
- The SDR addresses the surface objection but misses the underlying concern
- Value restatement is attempted but feels repetitive rather than reframed
- Some defensive or justifying language slips in
- Hospitality language is inconsistent
- The client moves forward but with lingering hesitation

### 3-4 (Needs Improvement)
- Objection is met with defensiveness or dismissiveness
- No empathy is shown before redirecting
- The SDR argues with the client or repeats the same pitch louder
- No value reframing, just restating the price
- Negative language is not reframed
- Unauthorized discounts are offered instead of reframing value
- The client becomes more resistant after the handling attempt

### 1-2 (Critical)
- Objection is ignored entirely
- The SDR becomes visibly frustrated, rude, or pushy
- Shame or pressure tactics are used ("You'll regret it", "This won't last")
- The SDR gives up immediately and offers a steep discount or says "I understand, no problem" and ends the conversation
- Brand-damaging language is used in the heat of the moment
- The interaction turns adversarial

## Dimension 5: Close and Next Steps (15%)

Measures the booking attempt, deposit handling, detail collection, and conversation conclusion.

### 9-10 (Elite)
- Clear, confident booking ask: "What day and time works best for you?"
- Two specific time options offered to simplify the decision
- Location and operating hours stated (brand-specific)
- Deposit/consultation fee explained with correct amount and correct payment method
- For Spa: EUR 50 deposit with card-over-phone or payment link options
- For Aesthetics: Fresha deposit process explained
- For Slimming: EUR 70 medical consultation fee via Fresha
- All personal details collected smoothly (email, DOB, address)
- Appointment confirmed back to the client (day, time, location)
- Confirmation text/message explicitly promised with appointment, address, and contact info
- Warm close with enthusiasm and reassurance
- Further assistance offered before ending
- CRM notes added (implied or explicit)

### 7-8 (Strong)
- Booking ask is clear and effective
- Time options are offered or preferred time is collected
- Location and hours are mentioned
- Deposit is handled correctly with minor omissions (e.g., only one payment option mentioned for Spa)
- Personal details are collected
- Appointment is confirmed
- Confirmation text is promised
- Close is warm but may lack the extra personal touch

### 5-6 (Developing)
- Booking is attempted but the ask is weak or buried ("So, would you maybe want to book?")
- Only one time option discussed, no alternatives offered
- Location or hours are mentioned but not both
- Deposit is mentioned but the process is unclear or the amount is vague
- Some personal details are collected but not all
- Appointment confirmation is partial (day but not time, or time but not location)
- Confirmation text is not explicitly promised
- Close is polite but not warm or enthusiastic

### 3-4 (Needs Improvement)
- No clear booking ask, the SDR waits for the client to initiate
- No time options offered
- Deposit is not mentioned or is handled incorrectly (wrong amount, wrong process)
- Personal details are not collected or the request feels abrupt
- Appointment details are not confirmed back
- No confirmation text promised
- Close is abrupt or procedural, no warmth
- The conversation just stops without a proper ending

### 1-2 (Critical)
- No booking attempt at all
- The conversation ends without any next step defined
- Deposit process is completely wrong or the SDR does not know the amount
- Client contact details are not collected
- No confirmation process
- The SDR ends the conversation mid-flow or says "Let me know" and disappears
- No CRM notes, no follow-up mechanism in place

## Dimension 6: Follow-Up and Re-engagement (10%)

Measures post-conversation nurture, referral asks, rebooking prompts, and re-engagement quality.

### Special Handling: N/A for Single Conversations

If the scored conversation is a first-contact interaction with no prior history and no follow-up has occurred yet, this dimension is scored as **N/A**. In that case, recalculate the composite using the remaining 5 dimensions reweighted proportionally (see formula below).

When a follow-up IS present in the conversation thread, score the full dimension.

### 9-10 (Elite)
- Follow-up occurs within 24 hours of previous interaction
- Message is personalized, referencing the specific treatment, concern, or conversation topic
- Genuine care is evident, not transactional (asks how they are feeling, not just pushing a rebook)
- Clear purpose: check-in, rebook prompt, referral ask, or review request
- Referral program is mentioned naturally with specific incentive (EUR 25 credit for both parties)
- Rebooking suggestion includes recommended timeframe based on treatment type
- Previous practitioner/doctor is mentioned by name for continuity
- No-show/cancellation recovery is handled with zero guilt and immediate rebooking options
- Soft urgency is used without pressure ("availability is limited this week")
- Low-pressure close that respects the client's autonomy

### 7-8 (Strong)
- Follow-up is timely and personalized
- Purpose is clear (check-in or rebook)
- Referral or review may not be mentioned but the core follow-up is strong
- Previous conversation details are referenced
- Tone is warm and non-transactional
- Minor missed opportunities (e.g., could have mentioned a specific timeframe for rebooking)

### 5-6 (Developing)
- Follow-up occurs but is not timely (48+ hours) or is somewhat generic
- References the client by name but not the specific treatment or conversation
- Purpose is vague or multi-purpose (trying to check in AND rebook AND ask for referral in one message)
- Tone is professional but lacks warmth
- No practitioner name mentioned
- Rebooking suggestion is generic ("Come back when you're ready")

### 3-4 (Needs Improvement)
- Follow-up is late (3+ days) or templated with no personalization
- No reference to previous conversation or treatment
- Message reads as a marketing blast, not a personal follow-up
- No-show recovery is guilt-laden or passive-aggressive
- No clear purpose or next step in the follow-up
- Tone is flat or overly corporate

### 1-2 (Critical)
- No follow-up at all when one was warranted
- Follow-up contains errors (wrong name, wrong treatment, wrong date)
- Guilt or shame language used in no-show recovery
- Aggressive re-engagement ("We haven't heard from you, are you still interested?")
- Follow-up violates brand voice standards
- Message could feel like spam to the recipient

## Composite Score Formula

### Standard (all 6 dimensions scored)

```
Composite = (D1 x 0.20 + D2 x 0.15 + D3 x 0.20 + D4 x 0.20 + D5 x 0.15 + D6 x 0.10) x 10
```

Where D1-D6 are each scored 1-10.

**Example:** D1=8, D2=7, D3=9, D4=7, D5=8, D6=6
Composite = (8x0.20 + 7x0.15 + 9x0.20 + 7x0.20 + 8x0.15 + 6x0.10) x 10
= (1.60 + 1.05 + 1.80 + 1.40 + 1.20 + 0.60) x 10
= 7.65 x 10
= **76.5 out of 100**

### Adjusted (Follow-Up is N/A)

When Dimension 6 is N/A, redistribute the 10% weight proportionally:

```
Adjusted weights: D1=22.2%, D2=16.7%, D3=22.2%, D4=22.2%, D5=16.7%
Composite = (D1 x 0.222 + D2 x 0.167 + D3 x 0.222 + D4 x 0.222 + D5 x 0.167) x 10
```

## Score Interpretation Table

| Score Range | Label | Description | Recommended Action |
|-------------|-------|-------------|--------------------|
| 90-100 | Elite | Textbook execution. This conversation is training material. | Share as a best-practice example. Recognize the SDR publicly. Use in onboarding. |
| 75-89 | Strong | Solid performance with minor improvements available. | Deliver 1-2 specific coaching tips. No urgent intervention needed. Track trends. |
| 60-74 | Developing | Missed key elements. Coaching opportunities present. | Schedule a 1:1 coaching session. Assign targeted practice scenarios. Review script steps. |
| 40-59 | Needs Improvement | Significant gaps. Likely lost the sale or damaged the lead. | Mandatory retraining. Shadow a top performer. Review fundamentals. Flag for manager. |
| 0-39 | Critical | Off-brand, off-script, or brand-damaging. | Immediate manager intervention. Remove from live conversations until retrained. Document incident. |

## Scoring Rules and Edge Cases

### Rounding
- Dimension scores are whole numbers only (1-10, no decimals)
- Composite score is rounded to one decimal place

### Tiebreakers
- When a conversation sits exactly between two score levels (e.g., between a 7 and an 8), lean toward the lower score unless the SDR demonstrated clear intent and self-correction

### Channel Adjustments
- **Phone calls:** All dimensions apply at full weight
- **WhatsApp/Text:** Script Compliance may score slightly higher for shorter interactions where not all steps apply (e.g., a follow-up message does not need a full greeting sequence)
- **Instagram DM:** Score against the DM-specific script (Scenario 11) rather than the call script
- **Email:** Score against the email-specific script (Scenario 12) rather than the call script

### Multi-Message Threads
- When scoring a thread of multiple messages, score the entire thread as one conversation
- The most recent messages carry slightly more weight (the SDR may have improved mid-thread)

### Incomplete Conversations
- If a conversation was cut short by the client (they stopped responding), score only the dimensions that were reached
- Mark unreached dimensions as N/A and reweight proportionally
- Do not penalize the SDR for steps they did not have the opportunity to perform

### Pricing Accuracy
- Pricing errors are scored within Dimension 1 (Script Compliance) and Dimension 2 (Brand Voice)
- Use the severity table from the Pricing Reference: Critical (more than 10% off), Major (wrong member/regular rate), Minor (imprecise rounding)
- A Critical pricing error caps Dimension 1 at a maximum score of 4
- A Major pricing error caps Dimension 1 at a maximum score of 6
