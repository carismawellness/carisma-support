## When a Customer Says: "That sounds nice, but how does that actually work?"

**Your Answer:**

"It is simple and it is real:

**Visit 1:** You tell us what brought you. During your treatment, we notice specific things — how your shoulders soften, what you say about your life. After you leave, we send you a message that references something we actually noticed about you. Not generic 'thank you for coming.' Specific: 'I noticed how you finally exhaled.'

**Visit 2:** You come back. We greet you by name. We mention something from last time. We suggest something we think will serve you even better this time, based on what we learned about you.

**Visit 3+:** By now, we genuinely know you. We know what brings you peace. We know if you prefer rhythm or silence, deep pressure or gentle. We know what you mentioned about your life and we ask about it when you arrive.

That is the whole system. No fancy technology. Just: we pay attention to you, we remember what we notice, and we let it inform how we care for you next time."

## When a Customer Says: "But how is that different from any other good spa?"

**Your Answer:**

"Most good spas will deliver a great treatment. We do that too. The difference is what happens after.

Most spas:
- Send a generic 'thank you for coming' email
- Ask 'what would you like today?' on your next visit
- Treat each visit independently

Carisma:
- Sends a message referencing something specific we noticed about you
- Knows what brings you peace before you have to say it
- Treats your visits as chapters in a relationship, not separate transactions

That might sound small. But it is the difference between 'I went to a spa' and 'Carisma is my place where I am known.' That difference is what drives people to come back for years, bring their friends, and feel genuinely seen."

Customers who are remembered by name and by what we actually notice about them come back. They bring friends. They tell us that Carisma is different from every other spa they have tried.

That difference is not in the treatment room. It is in the fact that we genuinely see you, remember you, and care about knowing you better each time you come back."

## DO & DON'T

**DO:**
- Use real examples from customers you actually remember
- Reference specific details (the way they softened, what they said)
- Emphasize that it is genuine, not marketing
- Show that you believe it because you have seen it work

**DON'T:**
- Oversell ("life-changing," "best spa in Malta")
- Make it sound complicated
- Compare yourself to other spas
- Use the word "exclusive" or "premium" (this is for everyone)
- Sound salesy (this is not a pitch, it is a philosophy)

---

## The Test

If a customer says, "That sounds wonderful, I am definitely coming back," you have explained it well.

If they say, "You actually seem to care," you have explained it perfectly.
