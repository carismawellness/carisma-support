## Overview

Pricing Psychology operates on three evidence-based principles from behavioral economics:

**1. The Anchor Effect** — The first price mentioned becomes the reference point for all subsequent prices. A low anchor makes higher prices seem reasonable by comparison.

**2. The Goldilocks Principle** — When presented with three options, 80% of customers choose the middle option (perceived as the "best value" without being the cheapest or most expensive).

**3. The Budget Conversation** — Asking about budget first (before presenting prices) increases perceived value by 23-40%. Customers who state their budget first are more likely to spend at that level.

**Goal:** Present pricing in a way that educates the customer, respects their decision-making process, and naturally guides them toward the option that delivers the best results — which is often the middle or premium tier.

---

## The 3-Tier Pricing Framework

Every pricing response follows this structure:

### Tier 1: The Anchor (Lowest Price)
- **Purpose:** Establish the entry point and set the psychological price floor
- **Positioning:** "If you are looking for a single, focused treatment..."
- **Psychology:** Allows budget-conscious customers to feel they have an accessible option, making higher tiers feel more reasonable by comparison
- **Example:** €150 for a single area Botox treatment or focused facial

### Tier 2: The Recommended (Middle Price) ⭐
- **Purpose:** Position this as the "Goldilocks" option — the best value, most transformative, and Sarah's actual recommendation
- **Positioning:** "For the results you described, this is the treatment that delivers..."
- **Psychology:** Customers subconsciously believe the middle option is the "smart choice." Mark this with confidence and specificity
- **Example:** €350 for comprehensive Botox + filler combination (most bookings land here)

### Tier 3: The Premium (Highest Price)
- **Purpose:** Expand the customer's perception of what's possible and increase average order value
- **Positioning:** "If you want to invest in a complete transformation..."
- **Psychology:** The presence of a premium option makes the middle tier feel more accessible. Customers who choose Tier 3 feel they are investing in something truly transformative. Customers who choose Tier 2 feel they are making a smart compromise
- **Example:** €650+ for comprehensive facial rejuvenation package with premium treatments

## The Budget Question — ALWAYS Ask First

Before presenting any pricing, establish the customer's budget context. This question should feel natural, not intrusive.

### When to Ask:
- Immediately after the diagnostic in `consult-and-pitch.md`
- When a customer asks "how much does this cost?"
- When recommending anything above the baseline treatment

### How to Ask (Choose One Based on Context):

**Budget Question 1 — Direct & Warm:**
"Before I share the options, is there a particular budget you had in mind? That helps me point you toward the treatment that gives you the best results for your investment."

**Budget Question 2 — Soft Entry:**
"I have options that work for what you described — ranging from €150 to €650+ depending on how comprehensive you want to go. Does that range work for you, or would you prefer something different?"

**Budget Question 3 — Results-Based:**
"Are you looking for subtle enhancement, or are you ready for a more noticeable transformation? That usually determines the investment level."

**Budget Question 4 — Timeline-Based:**
"Are you thinking one treatment, or are you open to a series of treatments for optimal results? Most transformations happen over time, and budgeting that out usually gives better value."

## Presenting the 3-Tier Framework

### Step 1: Validate the Goal
Acknowledge what the customer has shared, using the Named Concern Acknowledgment from `consult-and-pitch.md`.

**Example:**
"Those fine lines around your eyes are exactly what responds beautifully to micro-Botox — you can keep your natural expression while softening them."

### Step 2: Present the Tiers (After Budget Question)

Never dump all three prices at once. Present them in narrative order, with Tier 2 as the clear recommendation:

**Script:**

"Here is what I recommend based on what you described:

**The Focused Option** — €150 for targeted Botox in the area of concern (forehead, crow's feet, or frown lines). This gives you a noticeable softening while keeping your natural expression. Perfect if you want to start subtle.

**The Comprehensive Refresh** ⭐ (this is my recommendation) — €350 for a combination approach (Botox + strategic filler). This is where most patients see the transformation they are hoping for — the lines soften, the face regains some volume, and the results look completely natural. This is the investment that moves the needle most.

**The Complete Rejuvenation** — €650+ for a full facial rejuvenation package combining multiple treatments (Botox, advanced fillers, skin resurfacing, and a customized skincare protocol). This is the full step into transformation — most patients describe it as a confidence reset."

### Step 3: Reaffirm Your Recommendation

After presenting all three, immediately reaffirm the middle tier with confidence:

"For what you are describing, I would lean toward the comprehensive refresh at €350. It addresses both the dynamic lines and the volume loss, and the results look completely natural — like a really good night's sleep and some self-care."

## Average Order Value Optimization

### When to Introduce the Premium Tier

**Moment 1: When the customer's goals match premium-tier results**
- Customer: "I want to look like I did ten years ago"
- Response: "That level of transformation usually requires the full rejuvenation package. It is an investment, but most patients say it is life-changing."

**Moment 2: When they ask "what's the difference?"**
- Use this as an opening: "The €350 addresses the visible concerns now. The €650+ package is designed for long-term transformation — better skin quality, lasting volume, and results that compound over time."

**Moment 3: On milestone moments**
- Customer: "I want to look my best before my wedding"
- Response: "For a wedding, I would recommend our premium package. You want to look like the best version of yourself on that day — not just tweaked, but genuinely glowing."

### Words That Increase AOV

Use these phrases to make premium tiers feel like the obvious choice:

- "Most patients who see the initial results want to invest in..." (social proof)
- "The difference between these options is..." (specific benefit)
- "That level of transformation usually requires..." (authority)
- "If you want a real reset..." (aspiration)
- "This is where you see lasting change..." (outcome focus)

### Words That Undermine AOV

Never use these when presenting pricing:

- ❌ "If you're on a budget..."
- ❌ "The expensive option..."
- ❌ "You could do the cheaper version if you prefer..."
- ❌ "I know it seems like a lot..."

## Handling Budget Objections

### Objection 1: "That's More Than I Expected"

**Response (Reframe, Don't Discount):**

"I understand. Here's what is important: those results are from a combination of precision placement and premium products. The Botox alone is €150, the filler is €150–200, and then there is the expertise in blending them naturally. You are not paying for time — you are paying for the result.

The €150 focused option is a solid starting point if you want to begin there.

What feels right for you?"

*Note: Never drop price. Never apologize. Present the choice clearly and let her decide.*

### Objection 2: "Can I Start With Just Botox?"

**Response (Permission + Upgrade Path):**

"Of course. Botox alone gives you beautiful softening of the lines. Many patients start there and then add filler in future visits once they see how good it can look.

If you love the Botox results, the filler addition is something you can explore whenever you feel ready."

### Objection 3: "Do You Have Any Discounts or Package Deals?"

**Response (Value Over Discounting):**

"We don't run discounts on treatments because we want your results to be the focus — not the sale. What we do offer is a Results Commitment: if after two weeks you are not thrilled, we adjust at no extra charge.

For best value, the €350 comprehensive approach is where most patients see the transformation they were hoping for. Starting there usually saves money in the long run because you get the result you actually want, rather than multiple sessions trying different approaches."

## Multi-Treatment Bundling (Increase AOV Further)

Once the main treatment is selected, add-on treatments can increase AOV by 15–40%:

### Add-On Opportunities (Non-Pushy):

After recommending the treatment:

"This package includes [baseline treatments]. You could also add [specific treatment] to [specific benefit] — it completes the transformation. Would that be of interest?"

**Examples:**
- "Add premium filler for lips" (+€200)
- "Upgrade to advanced filler for cheekbones" (+€150)
- "Add skin resurfacing for texture" (+€300)
- "Include customized skincare protocol" (+€100)
- "Add preventative Botox maintenance plan" (+€50/month)

### Mention Once, Move On

If she does not take the add-on, do not mention it again. Pushing further is the opposite of luxury.

---

## Series Pricing (Increase Lifetime Value)

For treatments that improve with time (like skincare or maintenance):

"Most patients book a series of three treatments for optimal results. If you book all three upfront, we offer a 10% series discount. That brings the total to €945 instead of €1,050 — and you get locked in at today's pricing if rates increase."

*This increases commitment and lifetime value without feeling pushy.*

---

## When to Escalate (NOT Negotiate)

If a customer pushes back on pricing multiple times, or asks for a discount repeatedly:

**Response:**

"I appreciate you sharing that. Here is what I can tell you: our pricing reflects our expertise, our premium products, and the fact that you are investing in actual results — not just a treatment session.

I cannot negotiate on price, but I can absolutely help you find the option that works within your budget. The €150 focused option is a great starting point, or we can build a payment plan for the €350 option if that helps.

What works best for you?"

*If she still refuses, do not push further. A discounted customer is an unappreciative customer.*

## Quick Reference: When to Use Each Tier

| Customer Profile | Recommend | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Budget-conscious, first-time, unsure | Tier 1 (€150) | Entry point, low friction, upgrade path always available |
| Clear concern, open budget | Tier 2 (€350) ⭐ | Best value perception, highest satisfaction, best results |
| Specific milestone (wedding, event) | Tier 2 → Tier 3 | Occasion justifies premium; frame as investing in yourself |
| Wants "natural" enhancement | Tier 2 | Medium approach balances results with subtlety |
| Wants noticeable transformation | Tier 3 | Premium package delivers comprehensive results |
| Series treatment planned | Tier 2–3 + Series Discount | Multiple sessions = best results + locked pricing |
