### What Are GLP-1 Medications?
"GLP-1 medications (e.g., Ozempic & Mounjaro) are prescription-only treatments that support weight loss by improving appetite regulation and satiety. They can make consistency easier, but they work best inside a structured, medically supervised programme."

### What Medication Helps With
- Appetite regulation and feeling full sooner
- Craving reduction and less "food noise"
- Better control and adherence to your plan
- Better blood sugar control & metabolic support

### What Medication Does NOT Do
- Build muscle or protect your metabolism on its own
- Teach eating habits or create a repeatable routine
- Fix emotional drivers or stress eating
- Create long-term identity change and maintenance

"That's why we combine GLP-1 support (if clinically appropriate) with nutrition structure, strength training habits, tracking, and a maintenance plan."

### Sustainability Philosophy
"Weight gain isn't a failure of discipline -- it's a complex medical and behavioural challenge shaped by biology, stress, habits, and modern life."

At Carisma Slimming, we approach weight loss as a medical, lifestyle, and emotional process:
- Appetite regulation to quiet cravings and reduce food noise
- A repeatable eating structure you can follow on busy weeks
- Muscle protection to support metabolism as you lose fat
- Accountability and progression with tracking, check-ins, and adjustments
- A long-term plan for maintenance after the initial fat loss

### 4 Pillars of GLP-1 Programme
1. **Doctor-first, not drug-first** - clinical suitability first, not dosage. Reviews history, risks, goals.
2. **Appetite & Metabolic Support** - GLP-1 can quiet hunger and reduce food noise when qualified. Titrate carefully, support side effects, pair with eating structure.
3. **Body composition, not weight** - Track fat loss while protecting lean mass. Body composition trends, measurements, strength progression.
4. **Programme, not prescription** - Phases, milestones, defined maintenance plan. Structured start, progress targets, exit strategy.

### GLP-1 Eligibility Criteria

**Suitable for:**
- BMI >= 27
- Insulin resistance
- Emotional eating or long dieting history
- Menopause-related weight gain

**Unsuitable for:**
- Eating disorders
- Very lean patients
- Those unwilling to attend check-ins
- Currently pregnant or trying to conceive

### Safety, Side Effects & Management
Common side effects and how they reduce them:
- **Nausea:** slow titration, protein-first meals, hydration and electrolytes, injection timing guidance
- **Constipation:** tolerance-based fibre targets, fluids, daily movement, magnesium support when appropriate
- **Diarrhoea:** simple meal sequencing, trigger-food control, temporary dose stabilisation
- **Fatigue:** minimum calorie floors, protein and micronutrient monitoring, strength habits to protect energy
- **Reflux:** smaller structured portions, meal timing rules, behavioural guidance

"The biggest clinical mistake: prescribing GLP-1s without a system. Medication can quiet appetite, but it does not build muscle, teach eating, fix emotional drivers, or create long-term habits."

### GLP-1 Programme Steps
1. Initial Medical Consultation & Eligibility Review
2. Personalised GLP-1 Treatment Planning
3. Ongoing Medical Monitoring & Reviews
4. Nutrition & Lifestyle Support Integration

8. **Treatable areas?** Double chin, back fat, arm fat (bingo wing area), stomach fat and six-pack definition, flanks and waist, inner and outer thighs, under the buttocks, fat above knees.

## 14. CLINICAL EVIDENCE & RESEARCH BASIS

The website features extensive evidence-based sections supporting their methodology:

### Energy-Restricted Mediterranean Nutrition (HIGH evidence)
- Daily 500-750 kcal deficit, Mediterranean pattern
- Target 5-10% body-weight loss over first 3-6 months
- PREDIMED-Plus trial: 3.2 kg lost at 12 months vs 0.7 kg control

### Higher-Protein, Muscle-Protective Diet (HIGH evidence)
- Protein >= 1.2-1.5 g/kg ideal body weight
- Focus on lean meats, fish, dairy, legumes
- Meta-analysis of 47 trials: significantly reduced muscle mass loss

### Diet Plus Exercise (HIGH evidence)
- Combined produces additional 1.1 kg weight loss vs diet alone
- Technogym gym access, mix of resistance/cardio/mobility

### Cryolipolysis / CoolSculpting (Moderate-high evidence)
- 10-26% reduction in fat layer thickness
- 1-3 cycles per area, results visible from 8-12 weeks
- For stubborn pockets, not overall weight loss

### HIFEM + RF / Emsculpt NEO (Emerging high-quality evidence)
- About 25-30% muscle gain and 25-30% fat reduction in treated areas
- Typical course 4 sessions, about 1 week apart
- Visible changes from 4-12 weeks after final session

### RF, Infrared and Vacuum / VelaShape (Moderate evidence)
- Mean 0.8 inch (about 2 cm) thigh circumference reduction after 8 weeks
- 3-6 sessions per area
- Best for skin texture and mild contouring

### Lymphatic Drainage (Adjunct / Supportive)
- Improves edema and comfort when combined with compression and exercise
- Focus on comfort and fluid balance
- Not a fat-loss method by itself

### GLP-1 Receptor Agonists (Moderate-high evidence)
- Significantly greater and more sustained weight loss vs lifestyle changes alone
- Reduce activation in brain regions linked to food reward and impulsive eating
- Long-term follow-up: continued therapy maintained significantly more weight loss
- Most side effects mild to moderate and temporary when prescribed following clinical guidelines
