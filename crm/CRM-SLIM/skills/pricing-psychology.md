## Overview

Pricing Psychology operates on three evidence-based principles from behavioral economics:

**1. The Anchor Effect** — The first price mentioned becomes the reference point for all subsequent prices. A low anchor makes higher prices seem reasonable by comparison.

**2. The Goldilocks Principle** — When presented with three options, 80% of customers choose the middle option (perceived as the "best value" without being the cheapest or most expensive).

**3. The Budget Conversation** — Asking about budget first (before presenting prices) increases perceived value by 23-40%. Customers who state their budget first are more likely to commit.

**Goal:** Present pricing in a way that educates the customer, respects their decision-making process, and naturally guides them toward the program that delivers sustainable results — which is almost always the middle or premium tier.

## The 3-Tier Program Framework

Every pricing response follows this structure:

### Tier 1: The Anchor (Starter/Minimal Commitment)
- **Purpose:** Establish the entry point and set the psychological price floor
- **Positioning:** "If you want to understand how the program works..."
- **Psychology:** Allows budget-conscious customers to feel they have an accessible option, making higher tiers feel more reasonable by comparison
- **Example:** €199 for 4-week Starter Pack (weekly check-ins, meal plan, basic support)

### Tier 2: The Recommended (Core Program) ⭐
- **Purpose:** Position this as the "Goldilocks" option — the best value, highest success rate, and Katya's actual recommendation
- **Positioning:** "For sustainable change, this is the program that delivers..."
- **Psychology:** Customers subconsciously believe the middle option is the "smart choice." Mark this with confidence and specificity
- **Example:** €549 for 12-week Core Protocol (weekly sessions, personalized nutrition, behavioral coaching, accountability)

### Tier 3: The Premium (Complete Transformation)
- **Purpose:** Expand the customer's perception of what's possible and increase lifetime value
- **Positioning:** "If you want complete transformation with support every step..."
- **Psychology:** The presence of a premium option makes the middle tier feel more accessible. Customers who choose Tier 3 are committing to real change. Customers who choose Tier 2 feel they are making a smart compromise
- **Example:** €999 for 24-week Complete Reset (bi-weekly sessions, advanced protocols, lifestyle design, relapse prevention)

## The Budget Question — ALWAYS Ask First

Before presenting any pricing, establish the customer's budget context. This question should feel natural, not intrusive.

### When to Ask:
- Immediately after the diagnostic in `consult-and-pitch.md`
- When a customer asks "how much does this cost?"
- When they express hesitation about commitment

### How to Ask (Choose One Based on Context):

**Budget Question 1 — Direct & Compassionate:**
"Before I share what we offer, is there a particular budget range you had in mind? Being honest about what you can invest helps me point you toward what will actually work for you."

**Budget Question 2 — Investment Frame:**
"Most people think about this as an investment in their future health, not just a treatment cost. Are you looking for something modest to start, or are you ready to make a real commitment to change?"

**Budget Question 3 — Timeline-Based:**
"How much time are you willing to invest in this? Some programs work in 4 weeks, but real sustainable change usually happens over 12–24 weeks. That affects the investment."

**Budget Question 4 — Commitment-Based:**
"If I were to ask you to commit to 12 weeks of real work — both during our sessions and on your own — would that feel possible? Because that is usually where the breakthrough happens."

## Presenting the 3-Tier Framework

### Step 1: Validate the Struggle (Key for Weight Loss)
Acknowledge what the customer has shared with compassion, using the Validation Framework from `consult-and-pitch.md`.

**Example:**
"You haven't failed. Restriction-based diets fail people. That is exactly why this program is different — we expect relapse, and we plan for it."

### Step 2: Present the Tiers (After Budget Question)

Never dump all three prices at once. Present them in narrative order, with Tier 2 as the clear recommendation:

**Script:**

"Here is what I recommend based on what you described:

**The Starter Experience** — €199 for 4 weeks. This is perfect if you want to understand how the program works before committing fully. You get a personalized meal plan, weekly check-ins, and basic support. Most people use this to build confidence before moving to the Core Protocol.

**The Core Protocol** ⭐ (this is my recommendation) — €549 for 12 weeks. This is where real change happens. Twelve weeks is long enough for your body to reset and your mind to rewire. You get weekly one-on-one sessions, behavioral coaching, personalized protocols, and accountability. This is the program most people choose because it delivers results that stick.

**The Complete Reset** — €999 for 24 weeks. This is the full commitment — twice-monthly sessions, advanced protocols customized to your body, lifestyle design (not just diet), and intensive relapse prevention. This is for people who are ready for transformation, not just weight loss."

### Step 3: Reaffirm Your Recommendation

After presenting all three, immediately reaffirm the middle tier with confidence:

"For what you are describing, I would lean toward the 12-week Core Protocol. Four weeks is usually just enough to feel the momentum — the real magic happens weeks 5–12, when your mind catches up with your body. And by week 12, the habits have shifted from "I have to" to "I want to." That is when weight loss becomes sustainable."

## Average Order Value Optimization (Lifetime Value Focus)

### When to Introduce the Premium Tier

**Moment 1: When the customer has struggled with multiple failed attempts**
- Customer: "I've tried everything and it never sticks"
- Response: "That is exactly why 24 weeks is usually better than 12. You need time not just to lose weight, but to understand why your previous attempts failed and build new neural pathways. That is the transformation."

**Moment 2: When they mention life circumstances that demand support**
- Customer: "Work is insane right now"
- Response: "Actually, that is when the 24-week program becomes essential. Stress is usually what derails people. Having bi-weekly support means you have someone in your corner when things get hard."

**Moment 3: When they ask "what's the difference?"**
- Use this as an opening: "The 12-week program is excellent and most people see real results. The 24-week program is designed for people who want lasting transformation — not just this year, but for the rest of your life."

### Words That Increase Enrollment in Premium Tiers

Use these phrases to make premium programs feel like the obvious choice:

- "Most people who achieve lasting results invest in..." (social proof)
- "The difference between 12 and 24 weeks is..." (specific benefit)
- "That level of struggle usually requires..." (authority)
- "If you are ready for real transformation..." (aspiration)
- "This is where people actually change their relationship with..." (outcome focus)

### Words That Undermine Premium Enrollment

Never use these when presenting pricing:

- ❌ "If you're on a budget..."
- ❌ "The expensive program..."
- ❌ "You could do the 4-week trial if..."
- ❌ "I know 12 weeks is a long commitment..."

## Handling Budget/Hesitation Objections

### Objection 1: "That's More Than I Expected"

**Response (Reframe, Don't Discount):**

"I hear you. Here's what is important: you are not paying for time. You are paying for the protocol that actually works — the one that doesn't rely on willpower or restriction. You are paying for a system that expects relapse and plans for it. That is rare. And it is worth it.

If the 12-week program feels like too much right now, the 4-week Starter is a great way to experience how the program works. Many people start there and then move to the Core Protocol once they see the results.

What feels realistic for you?"

*Note: Never drop price. Never apologize. Present the choice clearly and let her decide.*

### Objection 2: "Can I Just Start With the 4-Week Program?"

**Response (Permission + Bridge Path):**

"Of course. The 4-week Starter gives you real momentum and a chance to experience how different this is from other programs.

Here's what usually happens: by week 4, people feel so different that they want to continue. If that happens for you, the 12-week Core Protocol picks up right where the Starter left off. Many people build their commitment that way."

*This honors her hesitation while gently making the case for the full program later.*

### Objection 3: "Can't I Just Do This Myself With a Food Plan?"

**Response (Compassionate Truth-Telling):**

"You could. And you have probably tried that already, which is why you are here.

The difference is: a food plan is what fails people after 6 weeks. This program is what keeps people on track when the food plan would normally collapse. It is not about the food. It is about your mind. And that is what takes real support to shift.

The 4-week Starter at €199 will show you that difference. Most people who try it realize it is not a luxury — it is essential."

### Objection 4: "Do You Have a Payment Plan?"

**Response (Structured Commitment):**

"Yes. For the 12-week Core Protocol, we offer a payment plan: €199 upfront, then 3 payments of €117. That breaks it into more manageable pieces while you are still fully committed.

The reason we do payment plans is not to discount — it is to make commitment easier. People who pay all upfront tend to have higher completion rates because they have skin in the game."

## Packaging for Maximum Lifetime Value

### Series Pricing (Different from Discounting)

Do NOT offer discounts. DO offer structured commitment:

"If you book the 12-week Core Protocol, the first month is €199, then €117/month for three months. That is the same total price (€549), just broken into pieces that feel easier."

### Upgrade Path (From Starter to Core)

"After your 4 weeks, if you want to continue into the Core Protocol, the remaining €350 applies toward it. So the 4-week Starter becomes the first month of your 12-week program."

*This removes the "starting over" feeling and increases progression to premium.*

### Add-Ons (Non-Pushy Upsells)

After the program is selected:

"The protocol includes the essentials. You can also add [specific service] for [specific benefit] — most people find it valuable. Would that interest you?"

**Examples:**
- "Add 24/7 text support" (+€50/month)
- "Add fitness integration with coaching" (+€75)
- "Add family nutrition coaching" (+€100)
- "Upgrade to bi-weekly sessions immediately" (+€75/month)

### Mention Once, Move On

If she does not take the add-on, do not mention it again. Pushing is the opposite of compassionate support.

## When to Escalate (NOT Negotiate)

If a customer pushes back on pricing multiple times, or asks for a discount repeatedly:

**Response:**

"I appreciate you being honest about the budget. Here is what I can tell you: I cannot discount the program because the price reflects the support you need to actually succeed. Cheap weight loss programs are why you are here — they did not work.

What I can do: the 4-week Starter at €199. That is the lowest entry point. Try it. See the difference. Then decide if the 12-week Core is worth it.

But I will not discount the full program. You deserve to have a system that is fully resourced to support your change — not a bargain version that will let you down like the others."

*This is compassionate but firm. It honors her financial reality while protecting the integrity of the program.*
