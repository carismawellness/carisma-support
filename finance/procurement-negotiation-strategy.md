## Executive Summary

The clinic's single supplier has raised prices between 3% and 43% across different product lines since the owner's original 2023 negotiation. Some increases are defensible (inflation, logistics), but several are clearly opportunistic -- particularly the Saypha line, where prices jumped 37-43% in a period of ~9% cumulative inflation. The clinic has grown substantially since 2023, giving it significantly more leverage than when the original deal was struck. This document lays out the complete negotiation playbook: leverage points, target prices, commitment structures, and fallback positions.

**Bottom line:** The clinic should target an overall COGS reduction of 15-25% versus the supplier's current quote, landing close to or below 2023 prices on high-volume products. The total annual spend at stake is estimated at EUR 25,000-45,000+, making this a material negotiation for both sides.

### 2.2 What the Profhilo Price Drop Reveals

Profhilo dropped 10% while everything else went up. This is the single most important data point in the entire analysis. It tells us:

1. **The supplier's cost basis has NOT uniformly increased.** If logistics, shipping, storage, and euro depreciation were the justification for increases, Profhilo would have gone up too. It didn't.
2. **Profhilo has competitive pressure.** IBSA (the Profhilo manufacturer) likely has multiple distributors in Malta/EU, creating price competition. The supplier had to stay competitive on Profhilo but inflated prices on products where they face less scrutiny.
3. **The Saypha increases are margin plays, not cost pass-throughs.** If the supplier's underlying costs justified 37-43% increases on Saypha, then Profhilo should not have dropped. The inconsistency proves selective price inflation.
4. **This is your negotiation trump card.** Use it: "I noticed Profhilo actually came down in price. If your costs across the board went up due to inflation and logistics, how did Profhilo drop 10%? The increases on Saypha Filler and Volume don't add up."

### 2.3 Suspicious Pricing Pattern on Saypha (Croma) Products

The Saypha line stands out as the worst offender:

| Product | Old Price | New Price | Increase |
|---------|-----------|-----------|----------|
| Saypha Filler | EUR 37.00 | EUR 53.00 | +43% |
| Saypha Volume | EUR 39.50 | EUR 54.00 | +37% |
| Saypha Volume Plus | EUR 54.00 | EUR 60.00 | +11% |
| Saypha Rich | EUR 36.49 | EUR 40.00 | +10% |

The lower-cost SKUs (Filler and Volume) got hit hardest. This is classic distributor behavior: inflate the high-frequency, low-scrutiny items because buyers focus their attention on the big-ticket items like Sculptra or Juvederm.

**Negotiation angle:** "Saypha Filler went from EUR 37 to EUR 53. That is a 43% increase in three years. What specifically changed about this product to justify that? Because the EUR 37 price already included your margin, delivery, and storage costs in 2023."

## 3. Volume Commitment Strategy

### 3.1 Three Commitment Scenarios

**Scenario A: Monthly Standing Order**

| Commitment | Pro | Con |
|-----------|-----|-----|
| Fixed monthly quantities per product | Supplier gets guaranteed cash flow and can plan inventory | Clinic locked into quantities even in slow months |
| **Best for:** High-use products (Radiesse, Profhilo) | Supplier reduces their risk | Less flexibility |

**Scenario B: Quarterly Volume Commitment**

| Commitment | Pro | Con |
|-----------|-----|-----|
| Agree to minimum quarterly quantities | More flexibility than monthly | Slightly weaker from supplier's perspective |
| **Best for:** Medium-use products (Saypha, NCTF, Lemon Bottle) | Clinic adjusts to seasonal demand | Requires quarterly review |

**Scenario C: Annual Volume Guarantee (RECOMMENDED)**

| Commitment | Pro | Con |
|-----------|-----|-----|
| Guarantee minimum annual spend (e.g., EUR 120,000+) | Maximum leverage for lowest pricing | Commitment feels large (but clinic is likely already spending this) |
| **Best for:** Cross-portfolio negotiation | Supplier locks in a major account for 12 months | Need to track against target |

### 3.2 Recommended Approach: Tiered Annual Commitment

Propose an annual framework:

- **Tier 1 (Base):** EUR 100,000 annual spend -- unlocks 10% below current list on ALL products
- **Tier 2 (Target):** EUR 130,000 annual spend -- unlocks 15% below current list on ALL products
- **Tier 3 (Stretch):** EUR 160,000+ annual spend -- unlocks 20% below current list, plus priority delivery and first access to new products

**Why this works:** It is product-agnostic. The clinic can shift mix between products without renegotiating each line. The supplier gets a guaranteed revenue commitment. Both sides win.

### 3.3 Walk-Away Value

If the supplier refuses to negotiate, this is what they lose:

- **Immediate:** EUR 120,000-150,000+ in annual revenue
- **Growth:** The clinic is growing, so this number would have increased year-over-year
- **Referrals:** The clinic likely refers other practitioners or clinics to the same supplier
- **Market position:** Losing the largest account in a small market is a reputational hit

**Make this explicit (politely):** "We want to stay with you. But I need prices that reflect the volume we bring. If we cannot get there, I have to consider splitting our purchasing across multiple distributors."

### 5.2 Multi-Supplier Strategy (Contingency)

If the current supplier does not reach acceptable terms on all products, the clinic can split:

| Supplier | Products | Rationale |
|----------|----------|-----------|
| Current supplier (retain) | Products where pricing is acceptable (Profhilo, NCTF, Radiesse if negotiated) | Maintain relationship for reasonable products |
| Alternative 1 | Saypha/Croma line | Move the most overpriced products to a competitor |
| Alternative 2 | Juvederm/Allergan line | Galderma and Allergan have strong distribution; easy to source elsewhere |

**Important:** Even if you do NOT intend to switch, having quotes from alternatives dramatically strengthens your position. Get at least 2 alternative quotes before the negotiation meeting.

### 5.3 Risks of Switching

| Risk | Severity | Mitigation |
|------|----------|------------|
| Delivery delays during transition | MODERATE | Phase the switch; start with one product line |
| Product authenticity concerns | LOW (if using licensed distributors) | Only use authorized distributors; verify batch numbers |
| Loss of relationship benefits | LOW-MODERATE | The relationship has not prevented 43% price increases; its value is limited |
| Administrative burden | LOW | One-time setup cost |
| Minimum order quantities with new suppliers | MODERATE | May need to commit to larger initial orders |

### 5.4 Pre-Negotiation Actions (Critical)

Before the negotiation meeting/email:

1. **Get 2-3 alternative quotes** for Saypha Filler, Saypha Volume, Radiesse, and Sculptra
2. **Calculate exact annual spend** from purchase records (not estimates)
3. **Document growth** -- show the supplier your volume trajectory with specific numbers (e.g., "We purchased X units in 2023, Y units in 2024, Z units in 2025")
4. **Identify your top 5 products by spend** -- these are the negotiation priorities

## 6. Recommended Deal Structure

### 6.1 The Proposed Agreement

**Framework: 12-Month Partnership Agreement with Fixed Pricing**

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Term** | 12 months, with option to renew for 12 more at same terms |
| **Volume commitment** | Minimum EUR 120,000 annual spend (the clinic likely already exceeds this) |
| **Pricing** | Fixed prices for the full 12 months (no mid-contract increases) |
| **Price review** | Prices reviewed annually. Any increase capped at 3% (matching Malta CPI) |
| **Payment terms** | Net 30 days (or whatever current terms are -- do not give ground here) |
| **Delivery** | Within 5 business days of order. Priority delivery for urgent orders. |
| **New products** | First access to new products/brands the supplier onboards, at negotiated volume pricing |
| **Exclusivity trade** | Clinic commits to purchasing 80%+ of total product needs from this supplier in exchange for partnership pricing |

### 6.2 Negotiation Sequence (How to Structure the Conversation)

**Step 1: Establish context (non-threatening)**
"I'm back managing procurement directly. I want to review our pricing arrangement because our volume has grown significantly."

**Step 2: Present the data (factual, not emotional)**
"Looking at the numbers, I see some products have increased 37-43% since 2023. Overall inflation in Malta was about 9% over that period. I'd like to understand the gap."

**Step 3: Use the Profhilo trump card**
"I noticed Profhilo actually dropped 10%. If costs across the board went up, how did Profhilo come down? That tells me the Saypha increases aren't purely cost-driven."

**Step 4: Present the opportunity (carrot)**
"Here's what I'm proposing: a 12-month partnership. We commit to EUR 120,000+ in annual spend -- all from you. In return, we need pricing that reflects our volume. I'm not asking for charity; I'm asking for prices that make sense for an account our size."

**Step 5: Present specific targets**
"Here is what I need to make this work." (Present the TARGET column from Section 4.3, not the ASK column -- save the ASK column for opening if they push back.)

**Step 6: Have the BATNA ready (stick, used only if needed)**
"I want to stay with one supplier. But I've received quotes from [Alternative 1] and [Alternative 2] that are competitive on the Saypha and Juvederm lines. I'd prefer not to split our purchasing, but I need the numbers to justify staying consolidated."

### 6.3 Concessions the Clinic Can Offer

These are things the supplier values that cost the clinic little:

| Concession | Value to Supplier | Cost to Clinic |
|-----------|-------------------|----------------|
| 12-month commitment | HIGH -- guaranteed revenue | LOW -- already buying this much |
| 80% exclusivity | HIGH -- locks out competitors | LOW -- already single-sourcing |
| Faster payment (Net 15 instead of Net 30) | MODERATE -- improves cash flow | LOW-MODERATE -- depends on clinic cash position |
| Larger batch orders (quarterly instead of monthly) | MODERATE -- reduces delivery costs | LOW -- requires storage but reduces admin |
| Case study / testimonial | LOW-MODERATE -- marketing value | LOW -- minimal effort |
| Referral to other clinics | MODERATE -- new business | LOW -- if clinic is comfortable |

### 6.4 Non-Negotiable Items (Do Not Concede)

- **Price increases during the contract term** -- prices are fixed for 12 months
- **Minimum order quantities that force overstocking** -- the clinic buys what it needs
- **Payment-in-advance or deposits** -- maintain current payment terms at minimum
- **Bundled products the clinic does not use** -- no forced product tie-ins

## 7. Talking Points Summary (Quick Reference)

### The Five Key Messages

1. **"We are one of your largest accounts."** -- Frame the relationship as strategically important.

2. **"Our volume has grown, but our pricing has gone the wrong direction."** -- The more the clinic buys, the less it should pay per unit. The opposite has happened.

3. **"A 43% increase on Saypha Filler cannot be justified by inflation."** -- Be specific. Name the number. Do not let them hide behind vague cost explanations.

4. **"Profhilo dropped 10%. That tells me some of these increases are discretionary."** -- This is the factual anchor that undermines the "everything went up" defense.

5. **"I want to consolidate with you long-term, but I need pricing that makes that possible."** -- Always frame as partnership, not threat. The stick (alternative suppliers) stays in the background.

### Phrases to Use

- "Let's find something that works for both of us"
- "Our business has grown, and I'd like our pricing to reflect that"
- "I'm looking at the data, and a few things don't add up"
- "I've done my homework on market rates"
- "I want to keep things simple with one supplier"

### Phrases to Avoid

- "This is outrageous" (too emotional)
- "Take it or leave it" (too aggressive)
- "I don't have other options" (never reveal a weak position)
- "We'll buy more if you give us better prices" (commit to specific numbers instead)
