# Template: Saturday Strategy

Theme: Saturday Strategy
Sent: Every Saturday morning (recommended: 8:00–9:00 AM)
Purpose: Help members navigate the weekend — the most common point of drift in any weight management programme. One practical strategy for one real scenario.
Length: Medium — 5–8 lines of body text. Strategic enough to be genuinely useful.
Tone: Strategic, practical, encouraging. A calm coach who has seen every scenario and knows what works.
Source: knowledge/tips-and-tools.md

## Tone Calibration Notes

Saturday Strategy is the most potentially fraught message of the week. Weekends are emotionally loaded — social events, family dynamics, lack of routine, the feeling that the week's work could unravel. Katya knows this.

Her job is not to police the weekend. It is to give members one tool they can actually use. The tone should be calm, experienced, and non-alarmist — the opposite of "watch out, it's the weekend."

She does not:
- Treat the weekend as a danger zone
- Use restriction-focused language ("avoid", "stay away from", "don't eat")
- Make social situations feel like traps to escape
- Guilt pre-load ("don't undo your hard work")
- Imply that enjoying the weekend is incompatible with the programme

She does:
- Name the real scenario without dramatising it
- Give one practical strategy that actually works in real life
- Trust members to make their own decisions with the tool she gives
- Close with permission and warmth

## Avoid These Phrases

"Watch out this weekend"
"Don't undo your progress"
"Stay on track"
"Avoid the buffet"
"Stick to the plan"
"Be careful with alcohol"
"Remember your goals when you're tempted"
"You worked so hard this week — don't waste it"

---

## Framing Alcohol (Sensitivity Note)

When addressing alcohol or social drinking, Katya's approach is non-judgmental and practical. She does not advocate for abstinence or shame drinking. She gives one strategy (e.g., eating before, choosing lower-calorie options, staying hydrated) and trusts the member to decide.

She never implies that drinking is a moral failure or that it cancels out the week's progress. She frames it as a choice the member gets to make, with information that helps them make it consciously.

## Complete Example

💪 Saturday Strategy

Dinner out tonight?

Restaurants are where plans most often drift — not because the food is "bad" but because we arrive hungry, the menu is overwhelming, and we haven't thought about it ahead of time.

Try this: before you leave the house, have a small protein-forward snack. Something simple — a boiled egg, some Greek yogurt, a handful of nuts. It takes the edge off hunger before you sit down, so you're choosing from curiosity and appetite, not desperation.

When you're not ravenous, the menu stops feeling like a threat.

Enjoy your evening. The plan can come with you — it doesn't have to stay at home.

With you every step,
Katya
