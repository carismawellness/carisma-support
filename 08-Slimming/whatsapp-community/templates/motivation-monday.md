# Template: Motivation Monday

Theme: Motivation Monday
Sent: Every Monday morning (recommended: 8:00–9:00 AM)
Purpose: Reset mindset for the week. Validate last week. Set one grounded intention.
Length: Short — 2–4 lines of body text. Breathing room is the point.
Tone: Warm, grounding, empowering. Therapist energy, not cheerleader energy.
Source: knowledge/motivation-bank.md

---

## Structure

LINE 1: Header
Format exactly as shown — emoji + theme name, nothing else on this line.

    💪 Monday Reset

LINE 2: Blank line (mandatory — creates visual breathing room)

SECTION A: Hook line (1 line)
Land the emotional reality of Monday. Meet them where they actually are — tired, hopeful, uncertain, starting again. Do not open with energy or enthusiasm. Open with truth.

    [Hook line — 1 sentence that names where Monday finds most people]

BLANK LINE

SECTION B: Validation (2–3 short lines)
Acknowledge last week without knowing what happened. Whatever happened — it counts. Short sentences. Each thought on its own line if it helps readability.

    [Line 1 — acknowledge last week wasn't perfect, and that's normal]
    [Line 2 — name what they did do: showed up, made choices, kept going]
    [Line 3 — optional: the reframe (not failure — data / not behind — choosing)]

BLANK LINE

SECTION C: Intention (2–3 lines)
One small, concrete thing. Not a goal list. Not a plan. One thing. Keep it vague enough to be personal to each reader, specific enough to be actionable.

    [Line 1 — invite them to pick just one thing this week]
    [Line 2 — reinforce: small is enough]
    [Line 3 — optional closing thought: warmth, agency]

BLANK LINE

SIGNATURE (mandatory, exact format — no variations)

    With you every step,
    Katya

---

## Character and Length Guidelines

Total body text (excluding header and signature): 6–10 lines including blank lines
No line should exceed 60 characters — short lines read better on mobile
No paragraph should exceed 3 lines before a blank line break
The message should feel spacious when read on a phone screen

## Variable Slots

[HOOK LINE] — Changes weekly. Drawn from motivation-bank.md hook collection.
[VALIDATION LINES] — 2–3 lines. Rotate each week to avoid repetition.
[INTENTION PROMPT] — The one small focus for this week. Keep it universal.
[CLOSING THOUGHT] — Optional warmth line before signature.

## Tone Calibration Notes

This message is the emotional anchor for the week. Members read it Monday morning when they are often at their most self-critical — comparing themselves to who they "should" be. Katya does not add to that pressure.

Her job here is to:
1. Name the reality (Monday is hard, last week happened)
2. Make it okay (nothing to fix, just data)
3. Point forward (one small thing, not a complete overhaul)

What she does NOT do:
- Generate enthusiasm they don't feel yet ("New week, new YOU!")
- List multiple intentions or goals
- Imply last week was a failure that needs correcting
- Rush them toward productivity

The message should read like a text from someone who genuinely knows how Monday morning feels.

---

## Avoid These Phrases

"New week, new you"
"Fresh start!"
"You've got this week!"
"Make this week count"
"Let's crush it"
"Don't give up"
"Every day is a new opportunity"
"Push yourself"

## Complete Example

💪 Monday Reset

Monday again.

You didn't get through last week perfectly.
Nobody does.

But you showed up. You made choices.
Some worked. Some didn't.

That's not failure — that's data.

This week, pick one small thing.
Just one.
And do it every single day.

You've got this. I've got you.

With you every step,
Katya

## Rotation Notes

Write at least 8 variations of this template to avoid repetition across 2 months.
The hook line, validation lines, and intention prompt should all rotate independently.
The emotional arc (Seen → Relieved → Capable → Moving forward) stays constant.
Track which versions have been sent in the content calendar.
