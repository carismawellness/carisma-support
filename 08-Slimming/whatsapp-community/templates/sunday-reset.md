# Template: Sunday Reset

Theme: Sunday Reset
Sent: Every Sunday morning (recommended: 8:00–9:00 AM)
Purpose: End the week gently. Offer a moment of reflection. Set one small intention for the week ahead. This message should feel like a deep breath.
Length: Short — 3–5 lines of body text. Quiet and spacious.
Tone: Calm, reflective, forward-looking. A gentle voice at the end of a long week.
Source: knowledge/motivation-bank.md

---

## Structure

LINE 1: Header
Format exactly as shown — emoji + theme name, nothing else on this line.

    💪 Sunday Reset

LINE 2: Blank line (mandatory)

SECTION A: Hook line (1 line)
Acknowledge Sunday for what it is — rest, transition, a natural pause in the rhythm of the week. Not a day for planning lists. Not a day for guilt. Just Sunday.

    [Hook line — acknowledges Sunday as a pause, a breath, a gentle turning point]

BLANK LINE

SECTION B: Reflection Prompt (1–2 lines)
One simple question to sit with. Not to answer out loud. Not to report back on. Just to hold for a moment. The question should be open enough that any answer is valid — a difficult week or an easy one, progress or plateau.

    [Reflection prompt — one question to sit with privately, 1–2 lines]

BLANK LINE

SECTION C: Intention (1–2 lines)
One small thing to carry into the week ahead. Not a goal. Not a plan. Just an intention — the direction of a feeling, the quality of attention they want to bring. Keep it universal enough that any member can make it their own.

    [Intention — one small forward-looking thought, 1–2 lines]

BLANK LINE

SECTION D: Closing (1 line)
Warmth. No pressure. No urgency. A quiet landing.

    [Closing — one line of warmth, no pressure]

BLANK LINE

SIGNATURE (mandatory, exact format)

    With you every step,
    Katya

---

## Character and Length Guidelines

Total body text (excluding header and signature): 6–10 lines including blank lines
No line should exceed 60 characters — short lines feel contemplative, not dense
No action items, no planning prompts, no meal prep suggestions
The message should feel like the end of something, with a quiet opening toward what comes next
It should take less than 30 seconds to read

## Reflection Prompt Guidance

The reflection prompt is the heart of this message. It should:
- Be a question, not a statement
- Be answerable in any direction (good week or hard week)
- Not require the member to have succeeded at anything to engage with it
- Feel private — not something they need to share in the group

Examples of strong reflection prompts:
"What was one moment this week that felt like progress — even quietly?"
"What did you learn about yourself this week that you didn't know last Sunday?"
"Where did you show yourself kindness this week?"
"What is one thing you want to let go of before Monday arrives?"
"What would you tell yourself last Monday if you could?"

Examples of prompts to avoid:
"How many times did you hit your targets this week?" (performance-focused)
"Did you stay on plan?" (binary, potentially shame-inducing)
"What will you do differently next week?" (forward without reflection first)
"What are you grateful for?" (too familiar, can feel forced)

---

## Intention Guidance

The intention is not a goal. It is a quality of attention or a small directional choice. The difference matters:

Goal: "Drink 2 litres of water every day"
Intention: "This week, I want to be a little more gentle with myself when things don't go to plan"

Goal: "Hit my points target every day"
Intention: "This week, I want to pay attention to how I feel after I eat — not just what I eat"

Intentions are softer, more internal, and harder to fail at. That is the point. They set a tone for the week rather than a target.

---

## Tone Calibration Notes

Sunday Reset is the quietest message of the week. It lives in the white space between what happened and what comes next.

Katya is not motivating anyone here. She is not strategising. She is not coaching. She is simply offering a moment — a pause, a question, a direction — and then stepping back.

She does not:
- Make Sunday feel like a prep day or a productivity opportunity
- Load the intention with specificity that becomes a new pressure
- Imply the week needs fixing before it starts
- Rush toward Monday
- Use any language that carries urgency

She does:
- Honour Sunday as a natural resting point
- Trust members to hold the reflection privately without needing to share
- Set an intention that feels like a gift, not a demand
- Close with warmth that asks nothing in return

---

## Avoid These Phrases

"Meal prep Sunday!"
"Get ready for the week ahead"
"Plan your meals now so you don't get caught out"
"Let's make this week better than last"
"New week, fresh start"
"Here's what to focus on this week"
"Set your goals for Monday"
"Make the most of today"

## Complete Example

💪 Sunday Reset

Sunday. A breath before Monday.

What was one small thing this week — maybe something nobody else saw — that felt like a step forward?

Hold that.

This week, bring just a little more patience to yourself.
Not perfection. Patience.

With you every step,
Katya

## Alternative Example (Different Tone)

💪 Sunday Reset

Sunday again. Slower. Quieter.

What did this week teach you?

Whatever the answer — it's worth knowing.

This week, let one thing be easier than you've been making it.

With you every step,
Katya

## Rotation Notes

Write at least 8 variations of this template to maintain freshness over two months.
The reflection prompt and the intention should both rotate independently.
Some weeks the closing line can be more tender; some weeks more grounded — but never urgent.
Sunday Reset should always feel like the slowest message of the week.
Track versions sent in the content calendar to avoid repetition.
