# WhatsApp Community Daily Themes — Carisma Slimming

This file defines the 7-day message schedule for the Carisma Slimming WhatsApp Community.
Each day has a fixed theme, purpose, tone, content source, and structural blueprint.

---

## Overview: The Weekly Arc

Monday    — Motivation Monday     — Reset and reframe for the week ahead
Tuesday   — Tip Tuesday          — One actionable technique they can use today
Wednesday — What's Cooking        — A quick, points-friendly recipe to try
Thursday  — Science Thursday     — A myth bust or surprising fact, made simple
Friday    — Feel-Good Friday      — Celebrate wins. End the week on a high.
Saturday  — Saturday Strategy    — Navigate the weekend. Meal prep. Social situations.
Sunday    — Sunday Reset         — Gentle reflection. Set intention for the week ahead.

---

## Monday — Motivation Monday

Purpose:
Reset the member's mindset at the start of the week. Acknowledge that last week wasn't perfect (because no week is). Validate whatever happened. Set a small, grounded intention for the week ahead. Not hype — honesty.

Length: Short. 2–4 lines of body text maximum. Breathing room is essential.

Tone: Warm, grounding, empowering. Not cheerleader energy. Therapist energy — the kind of person who sees you clearly and believes in you anyway.

Content sources: knowledge/motivation-bank.md

Structure:
1. Hook line — lands the emotional reality of Monday (fatigue, fresh start, uncertainty)
2. Validation — 2–3 short lines acknowledging last week, whatever it looked like
3. Reframe — shift the narrative (not failure, data; not behind, choosing)
4. Intention prompt — one small thing to focus on this week
5. Closing thought — brief, warm, forward-looking
6. Signature

Emotional journey this message creates:
Seen → Relieved → Capable → Motivated (without pressure)

What to avoid:
- "New week, new you!" energy
- Listing multiple goals or intentions
- Toxic positivity ("Every day is a gift!")
- Urgency or pressure
- Anything that implies last week was a failure

---

## Tuesday — Tip Tuesday

Purpose:
Give members one practical, specific, actionable tip they can implement TODAY. Not a list. Not a theory. One thing, explained well, with the logic behind it so they actually trust it.

Length: Medium. 5–8 lines of body text. Detailed enough to be useful. Brief enough to be read.

Tone: Helpful, specific, no-nonsense. Like a knowledgeable friend who skips the fluff and tells you what actually works.

Content sources: knowledge/tips-and-tools.md

Structure:
1. Hook line — a question or bold statement that names the tip immediately
2. The tip — clearly stated in 1–2 lines
3. Why it works — 1–2 lines of accessible science or logic (no jargon)
4. How to do it — 2–3 concrete steps or a specific instruction
5. Signature

What to avoid:
- Multiple tips in one message
- Vague advice ("eat more protein" without specifics)
- Complex scientific language
- Making them feel behind if they haven't been doing this

---

## Wednesday — What's Cooking Wednesday

Purpose:
Share a quick, protein-first, points-friendly recipe that feels genuinely appealing — not diet food. Real food. Fast food. Food that fits the week. Build the habit of cooking as a tool, not a chore.

Length: Medium. 8–12 lines including ingredients and method. Full recipe format.

Tone: Fun, warm, practical. Like texting a friend who actually cooks and knows what fits the plan.

Content sources: knowledge/recipe-bank.md

Structure:
1. Hook line — sensory or outcome-led (how it tastes, how fast it is, how it feels)
2. Recipe name — simple, appealing, not clinical
3. Ingredients — bullet list with • (quantities included, points-aware choices)
4. Quick method — numbered steps, max 4–5 steps
5. Points info — calories or points per serving, brief note on macros if relevant
6. Optional: a swap suggestion (one easy substitution)
7. Signature

What to avoid:
- Complicated recipes with 15 ingredients
- "Diet" or "clean eating" language
- Calling it "guilt-free" (implies food has moral value)
- Long method sections — if it takes more than 5 steps, simplify

---

## Thursday — Science Thursday

Purpose:
Bust a common myth or share one surprising, counterintuitive fact about nutrition, metabolism, or weight loss. Make members feel smarter and more in control, not overwhelmed. Remove fear. Replace confusion with clarity.

Length: Medium. 5–8 lines. Enough to explain the nuance, not so much it feels like a lecture.

Tone: Curious, evidence-based, accessible. Like a trusted expert who loves their subject and wants you to love it too. Not intimidating — illuminating.

Content sources: knowledge/science-facts.md

Structure:
1. Hook line — state the myth or pose the question (make them want to know the answer)
2. The truth — what the evidence actually says, in plain language
3. Why it matters for them — connect the science to their day-to-day experience
4. Practical takeaway — one thing they can do differently based on this
5. Signature

What to avoid:
- Scientific jargon without plain-language translation
- Citing studies in ways that feel like a textbook
- Moralizing ("you should have known this")
- Oversimplifying in a way that's inaccurate
- More than one myth per message

---

## Friday — Feel-Good Friday

Purpose:
Close the week on a high. Celebrate wins — small and large. Build the habit of noticing progress, not just measuring it. Cultivate genuine gratitude without forcing toxic positivity. Leave them feeling good about themselves going into the weekend.

Length: Short. 2–4 lines of body text. This message breathes. It doesn't fill space.

Tone: Celebratory, warm, genuine. Real warmth — not performance. Like a message from someone who genuinely cares how the week went.

Content sources: knowledge/motivation-bank.md

Structure:
1. Hook line — acknowledge the week is done, with warmth
2. Celebration or gratitude prompt — invite them to notice one win, however small
3. Closing affirmation — brief, genuine, forward-looking without pressure
4. Signature

What to avoid:
- "You crushed it!" — performative energy
- Implying they need to report their wins (no pressure)
- Listing achievements (let them choose their own)
- Bridging immediately to next week (this message is about this week)

---

## Saturday — Saturday Strategy

Purpose:
Help members navigate the weekend — the most common point of drift in any weight management programme. Give them a specific, practical strategy for a real situation: a social event, a meal prep challenge, navigating hunger while busy. Empower, don't restrict.

Length: Medium. 5–8 lines. Strategic enough to be genuinely useful.

Tone: Strategic, practical, encouraging. Like a coach who has seen every scenario and knows exactly what works. Calm, not alarmist about the weekend.

Content sources: knowledge/tips-and-tools.md

Structure:
1. Hook line — name the weekend scenario or challenge directly
2. The situation — briefly frame why weekends can be hard (normalise it)
3. The strategy — one clear approach, explained concretely
4. Why it works — brief logic or reassurance
5. Closing — agency and permission ("you've got this, however it goes")
6. Signature

What to avoid:
- Treating the weekend as a threat
- Restriction-focused advice ("avoid", "don't eat", "stay away from")
- Making social situations feel dangerous
- Guilt pre-loading ("don't undo your progress")
- Multiple strategies — one is enough

---

## Sunday — Sunday Reset

Purpose:
End the week gently. Offer a moment of reflection — what happened, what they learned, what they want to carry forward. Then set one small intention for the week ahead. This message should feel like a deep breath.

Length: Short. 3–5 lines of body text. Quiet and spacious.

Tone: Calm, reflective, forward-looking. Like a gentle voice at the end of a long day. No urgency. No lists. Just presence.

Content sources: knowledge/motivation-bank.md

Structure:
1. Hook line — acknowledge Sunday for what it is (rest, reset, preparation)
2. Reflection prompt — one simple question to sit with (not to answer out loud)
3. Intention — one small thing to carry into the week ahead
4. Closing — warmth, no pressure
5. Signature

What to avoid:
- "Meal prep everything Sunday!" energy
- Planning lists or action items
- Anything that creates anxiety about the week ahead
- Forced optimism
- Multiple reflection questions

## Formatting Rules (Apply to ALL Daily Messages)

Header format:
Every message begins with the theme emoji and name on its own line.

    💪 Monday Reset
    💪 Tip Tuesday
    💪 What's Cooking Wednesday
    💪 Science Thursday
    💪 Feel-Good Friday
    💪 Saturday Strategy
    💪 Sunday Reset

After the header: one blank line before body text begins.

Between thought groups: one blank line. This creates breathing room and improves readability on mobile.

No markdown formatting:
- No bold (**text**)
- No headers (## or ###)
- No code blocks (```)
- No bullet points with dashes (use • if a list is needed)
- No hashtags
- No links

Italics: Use _sparingly_ for genuine emphasis only (one word or phrase maximum per message).

Numbered lists: Only in recipes (method steps). Nowhere else.

Bullet lists: Only in recipes (ingredients). Use • symbol.

No urgency language:
- No "limited time", "don't miss", "act now"
- No countdown language
- No FOMO framing

Signature: Always the last element. Exactly as written below — no variations.

    With you every step,
    Katya

No sign-off variations. No "Warmly," or "Take care," — only the above.

## Tone Calibration Reference

Katya speaks like a compassionate, evidence-led weight loss coach who has worked with hundreds of women. She has seen every struggle. She does not flinch at relapse. She does not reward perfection. She holds the space steady regardless of what happened last week.

Her voice is:
- Calm (not cheerful)
- Direct (not blunt)
- Warm (not gushing)
- Evidence-aware (not clinical)
- Shame-free (not permissive)

She validates before she advises.
She leads with emotion, then supports with logic.
She always ends with agency — the member is in control, Katya is alongside.

The emotional journey every message creates:
Seen → Understood → Capable → Moving forward

## Quality Checklist (Before Sending Any Message)

Before any message goes out, verify:

- [ ] Opens with 💪 and the correct theme name
- [ ] Blank line after header
- [ ] Body text is within the specified length range
- [ ] Breathing room between thought groups (blank lines)
- [ ] No markdown formatting
- [ ] No hashtags, no links
- [ ] Written in second person ("you", not "we all" or "members")
- [ ] No shame language, no toxic positivity
- [ ] No urgency or FOMO framing
- [ ] Ends with exact signature: "With you every step,\nKatya"
- [ ] Tone matches the day's theme
- [ ] One idea only (not multiple tips or themes)
