```
EGG & FETA BREAKFAST WRAP
Prep: 10 mins | Serves: 2
Protein: 24g per serving | Points: ~340 per serving

Ingredients:
• 4 large eggs
• 50g feta cheese, crumbled
• 1 large tomato, diced
• Handful of baby spinach
• 2 wholemeal wraps
• 1 tsp olive oil
• Salt, pepper, dried oregano

Method:
1. Whisk eggs with a pinch of salt, pepper, and oregano.
2. Heat olive oil in a non-stick pan over medium heat.
3. Add spinach and tomato — cook 1 minute until wilted.
4. Pour in eggs and scramble gently for 2–3 minutes until just set.
5. Stir in crumbled feta off the heat.
6. Spoon into wraps, fold, and serve immediately.

Katya's tip: Make the egg filling the night before and store in the fridge — in the morning just warm it up and wrap. Perfect for school-run mornings.
```

```
GREEK YOGURT PROTEIN PARFAIT
Prep: 5 mins | Serves: 1
Protein: 28g per serving | Points: ~280 per serving

Ingredients:
• 200g full-fat Greek yogurt (Chobani or Total work well)
• 1 tbsp honey
• 30g mixed nuts (almonds, walnuts)
• 1 handful of fresh berries or sliced banana
• 1 tbsp chia seeds

Method:
1. Spoon Greek yogurt into a glass or bowl.
2. Layer berries on top.
3. Sprinkle chia seeds and nuts over the fruit.
4. Drizzle with honey.
5. Eat immediately or refrigerate overnight.

Katya's tip: This works as a breakfast or a 4pm snack when you're craving something sweet. The protein from the yogurt keeps you full for hours — it's not a treat, it's a strategy.
```

```
KATYA'S QUICK COTTAGE CHEESE PANCAKES
Prep: 15 mins | Serves: 2
Protein: 22g per serving | Points: ~310 per serving

Ingredients:
• 250g cottage cheese (low-fat)
• 2 large eggs
• 4 tbsp rolled oats (blended to flour, or use oat flour)
• 1 tsp vanilla extract
• Pinch of salt
• 1 tsp coconut oil or butter for cooking
• Fresh strawberries and a drizzle of honey to serve

Method:
1. Blend cottage cheese, eggs, oat flour, vanilla, and salt until smooth (or mix well by hand — small lumps are fine).
2. Heat a non-stick pan over medium-low heat with a tiny bit of coconut oil.
3. Pour small rounds of batter (about 2 tbsp each) into the pan.
4. Cook 2–3 minutes until bubbles form on the surface, then flip carefully.
5. Cook another 1–2 minutes until golden.
6. Serve with fresh strawberries and a light honey drizzle.

Katya's tip: These taste like real pancakes — your kids will eat them too. Batch-make and refrigerate for 3 days. Reheat in 30 seconds in the microwave.
```

```
TURKISH-STYLE BAKED EGGS IN TOMATO SAUCE
Prep: 20 mins | Serves: 2
Protein: 18g per serving | Points: ~260 per serving

Ingredients:
• 4 large eggs
• 1 tin (400g) chopped tomatoes
• 1 small onion, finely diced
• 2 garlic cloves, minced
• 1 tsp olive oil
• 1 tsp cumin
• 1 tsp paprika (smoked or sweet)
• Salt, pepper, fresh parsley
• Optional: 1 tbsp labneh or Greek yogurt on the side

Method:
1. Heat olive oil in an oven-safe pan over medium heat.
2. Sauté onion for 3–4 minutes until soft. Add garlic, cumin, and paprika — cook 1 minute.
3. Pour in chopped tomatoes, season with salt and pepper, simmer 5 minutes.
4. Make 4 wells in the sauce. Crack an egg into each well.
5. Cover with a lid and cook on low for 5–7 minutes until whites are set but yolks still runny.
6. Scatter fresh parsley on top and serve straight from the pan.

Katya's tip: This is a full breakfast that feels special enough for Sunday but quick enough for Tuesday. Serve with a small piece of sourdough to scoop — or skip the bread entirely and just enjoy the eggs and sauce.
```

```
Katya's tip: No cooking beyond boiling the eggs. This takes less time than queuing at a café and keeps you full until 2pm. Pre-boil eggs in a batch on Sunday for the whole week.
```

```
15-MINUTE LEMON HERB CHICKEN BOWL
Prep: 15 mins | Serves: 2
Protein: 38g per serving | Points: ~420 per serving

Ingredients:
• 2 chicken breasts (about 300g total), sliced thin
• Juice of 1 lemon
• 2 garlic cloves, minced
• 1 tsp dried oregano
• 1 tbsp olive oil
• 1 cup mixed greens or rocket
• 1 large cucumber, diced
• 1 large tomato, diced
• 50g feta, crumbled
• ½ cup cooked bulgur or quinoa (optional, for extra points)

Method:
1. Toss chicken slices with lemon juice, garlic, oregano, salt, and pepper.
2. Heat olive oil in a pan over high heat. Cook chicken 3–4 minutes per side until cooked through and golden. Set aside.
3. Build your bowl: greens at the base, cucumber and tomato on top, chicken slices over that.
4. Scatter feta. Add bulgur if using.
5. Squeeze any remaining lemon over the bowl. Serve.

Katya's tip: Make double the chicken at dinner and use the leftovers for this bowl the next day. The chicken slices reheat beautifully in 90 seconds in the microwave.
```

```
TUNA & WHITE BEAN MEDITERRANEAN SALAD
Prep: 10 mins | Serves: 2
Protein: 32g per serving | Points: ~380 per serving

Ingredients:
• 2 tins (160g each) good-quality tuna in olive oil, drained
• 1 tin (400g) white beans (cannellini), rinsed and drained
• 1 large tomato, diced
• ½ red onion, thinly sliced
• Handful of flat-leaf parsley, chopped
• Juice of 1 lemon
• 1 tbsp olive oil
• Salt, pepper, dried oregano

Method:
1. Combine tuna and white beans in a large bowl, breaking tuna into chunks.
2. Add tomato, red onion, and parsley.
3. Dress with lemon juice, olive oil, salt, pepper, and oregano.
4. Toss gently and serve. Works immediately or even better after 30 minutes in the fridge.

Katya's tip: This travels brilliantly in a container — perfect for lunch at work or on the go. White beans add fibre and protein so you stay satisfied. Use the best tuna you can find; it makes a real difference.
```

```
TURKEY & VEGETABLE LETTUCE WRAPS
Prep: 15 mins | Serves: 2
Protein: 30g per serving | Points: ~290 per serving

Ingredients:
• 400g lean turkey mince
• 1 garlic clove, minced
• 1 tsp ginger (fresh or powdered)
• 1 tbsp soy sauce (low-sodium)
• 1 tbsp sesame oil
• 1 carrot, julienned or grated
• 1 cup bean sprouts (or thinly sliced cabbage)
• Spring onions, sliced
• 1 head of butter lettuce or iceberg (leaves separated)
• Squeeze of lime

Method:
1. Heat sesame oil in a pan over medium-high heat.
2. Cook turkey mince, breaking it up, until browned — about 5 minutes.
3. Add garlic, ginger, and soy sauce. Stir and cook 2 more minutes.
4. Remove from heat. Stir in carrot and bean sprouts.
5. Spoon into lettuce cups. Top with spring onions and lime.

Katya's tip: Lay out all the components in the centre of the table and let everyone build their own wraps. Kids love this. Low points, high fun, zero diet-food energy.
```

```
QUICK LENTIL & VEGETABLE SOUP
Prep: 25 mins | Serves: 4
Protein: 18g per serving | Points: ~220 per serving

Ingredients:
• 250g red lentils, rinsed
• 1 onion, diced
• 2 garlic cloves, minced
• 2 medium carrots, diced
• 2 celery stalks, sliced
• 1 tin (400g) chopped tomatoes
• 1 litre vegetable or chicken stock
• 1 tsp cumin
• 1 tsp turmeric
• 1 tbsp olive oil
• Salt, pepper, squeeze of lemon to serve

Method:
1. Heat olive oil in a large pot. Sauté onion and garlic for 3 minutes.
2. Add carrots and celery — cook 3 more minutes.
3. Stir in cumin and turmeric. Add lentils, tomatoes, and stock.
4. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer 15–20 minutes until lentils are soft.
5. Season with salt and pepper. Blend partially if you prefer a thicker texture.
6. Serve with a squeeze of lemon.

Katya's tip: This makes 4 portions — refrigerate for 4 days or freeze. Having ready-made soup means you always have a warm, protein-rich lunch waiting. Pair with a small piece of crusty bread if you need the extra energy.
```

```
GRILLED CHICKEN & HALLOUMI SALAD
Prep: 20 mins | Serves: 2
Protein: 42g per serving | Points: ~450 per serving

Ingredients:
• 2 chicken breasts (300g total)
• 100g halloumi, sliced into strips
• 2 large handfuls of rocket or mixed leaves
• 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
• 1 cucumber, sliced
• 1 tbsp olive oil
• Juice of 1 lemon
• 1 tsp dried mint
• Salt, pepper

Method:
1. Season chicken with salt, pepper, lemon juice, and dried mint.
2. Grill or pan-fry chicken over medium-high heat — 4–5 minutes per side. Rest 2 minutes then slice.
3. In the same pan, grill halloumi strips 1–2 minutes per side until golden.
4. Build salad: leaves, tomatoes, cucumber. Lay chicken and halloumi on top.
5. Drizzle with olive oil and a final squeeze of lemon.

Katya's tip: Halloumi is a Maltese staple — and it's genuinely high in protein. This salad feels indulgent and satisfying, not like diet food at all. Great for Sunday lunch with the family too.
```

```
EGG FRIED CAULIFLOWER RICE WITH PRAWNS
Prep: 20 mins | Serves: 2
Protein: 29g per serving | Points: ~310 per serving

Ingredients:
• 300g raw prawns (peeled and deveined)
• 1 small head of cauliflower, grated (or 1 bag pre-riced cauliflower)
• 3 eggs, beaten
• 1 cup frozen peas
• 2 garlic cloves, minced
• 1 tbsp low-sodium soy sauce
• 1 tsp sesame oil
• 1 tbsp olive oil
• Spring onions to garnish
• Pinch of chilli flakes (optional)

Method:
1. Heat olive oil in a large pan or wok over high heat.
2. Add prawns — cook 2 minutes until pink. Remove and set aside.
3. In the same pan, add garlic and cauliflower rice. Stir-fry 4–5 minutes.
4. Push to the side. Scramble eggs in the empty space until just cooked, then mix through.
5. Add peas, soy sauce, sesame oil, and prawns. Toss everything together.
6. Garnish with spring onions and chilli flakes.

Katya's tip: Cauliflower rice is free (zero points) so this entire dish is filling, flavourful, and budget-friendly. If your family wants regular rice on the side, make that separately — no pressure to convert everyone at once.
```

```
ONE-PAN MEDITERRANEAN BAKED FISH
Prep: 25 mins | Serves: 4
Protein: 34g per serving | Points: ~350 per serving

Ingredients:
• 4 white fish fillets (sea bass, bream, or cod — available at most Malta fishmongers)
• 400g cherry tomatoes
• 1 courgette, sliced into rounds
• 1 red pepper, sliced
• 4 garlic cloves, halved
• 1 lemon, sliced into rounds
• 2 tbsp olive oil
• 1 tsp dried oregano
• 1 tsp dried thyme
• Salt, pepper
• Fresh parsley to finish

Method:
1. Preheat oven to 200°C.
2. Scatter cherry tomatoes, courgette, pepper, and garlic in a large baking dish. Drizzle with 1 tbsp olive oil, season well.
3. Nestle fish fillets on top of the vegetables. Drizzle remaining olive oil over the fish.
4. Season fish with oregano, thyme, salt, and pepper. Lay lemon slices on top.
5. Roast for 18–22 minutes until fish flakes easily.
6. Scatter fresh parsley and serve straight from the dish.

Katya's tip: Everything goes in one pan — minimal washing up. Serve with a small portion of crusty bread or roasted potatoes for anyone who needs more carbs. The vegetables are free, the fish is the star.
```

```
TURKEY MEATBALLS IN TOMATO SAUCE
Prep: 30 mins | Serves: 4
Protein: 36g per serving | Points: ~380 per serving

Ingredients:
• 600g lean turkey mince
• 1 egg
• 3 tbsp breadcrumbs
• 2 garlic cloves, minced
• 1 tsp dried oregano
• Salt, pepper

For the sauce:
• 2 tins (400g each) chopped tomatoes
• 1 onion, diced
• 2 garlic cloves, minced
• 1 tbsp olive oil
• 1 tsp sugar
• Fresh basil to finish

Method:
1. Mix mince, egg, breadcrumbs, garlic, oregano, salt, and pepper. Roll into golf ball-sized meatballs.
2. Brown meatballs in a little olive oil over medium-high heat — 2 minutes per side. Set aside.
3. In the same pan, sauté onion and garlic for 3 minutes. Add tomatoes and sugar. Simmer 5 minutes.
4. Return meatballs to the sauce. Cover and simmer 15 minutes.
5. Finish with fresh basil. Serve over pasta (for the family) or courgetti / extra vegetables (for you).

Katya's tip: These freeze perfectly. Double the batch, freeze half the meatballs in sauce, and thank yourself in two weeks. Turkey meatballs are leaner than beef but just as satisfying in a good tomato sauce.
```

```
SPICED CHICKEN THIGHS WITH ROASTED VEGETABLES
Prep: 30 mins | Serves: 4
Protein: 40g per serving | Points: ~420 per serving

Ingredients:
• 8 chicken thighs (bone-in, skin-on for flavour — or boneless skinless to reduce points)
• 2 tsp paprika
• 1 tsp cumin
• 1 tsp garlic powder
• ½ tsp turmeric
• 1 tbsp olive oil
• Salt, pepper

For the vegetables:
• 2 red peppers, cut into chunks
• 1 large courgette, chunked
• 1 red onion, cut into wedges
• 200g cherry tomatoes
• 1 tbsp olive oil

Method:
1. Preheat oven to 210°C.
2. Mix paprika, cumin, garlic powder, turmeric, olive oil, salt, and pepper into a paste. Rub all over chicken.
3. Toss vegetables with olive oil and salt. Spread in a large roasting tin.
4. Place chicken thighs on top of the vegetables, skin side up.
5. Roast 30–35 minutes until chicken skin is golden and juices run clear.
6. Rest 5 minutes before serving.

Katya's tip: The vegetables underneath catch all the chicken juices and become incredibly flavourful. Serve with a small portion of rice for the family — the chicken and vegetables alone are a complete meal for your programme.
```

```
PASTA E FAGIOLI (PASTA WITH BEANS)
Prep: 25 mins | Serves: 4
Protein: 22g per serving | Points: ~480 per serving

Ingredients:
• 200g small pasta (ditalini or elbow macaroni)
• 2 tins (400g each) borlotti or cannellini beans, rinsed
• 1 tin (400g) chopped tomatoes
• 1 onion, diced
• 3 garlic cloves, minced
• 2 celery stalks, sliced
• 1 tbsp olive oil
• 1 tsp dried rosemary
• 1 litre chicken or vegetable stock
• Parmesan rind (optional — adds depth)
• Salt, pepper, fresh parsley

Method:
1. Heat olive oil in a large pot. Sauté onion, garlic, and celery for 4–5 minutes.
2. Add rosemary and tomatoes — cook 3 minutes. Add beans, stock, and parmesan rind if using.
3. Simmer 10 minutes. Remove 2 cups of the soup and blend until smooth, then return to pot (this thickens it naturally).
4. Add pasta and cook according to packet instructions in the soup.
5. Season with salt and pepper. Finish with fresh parsley and a drizzle of good olive oil.

Katya's tip: This is traditional Italian peasant food — deeply nourishing, genuinely filling, and naturally high in protein and fibre. It is family food, not diet food. The beans are the protein here. Eat this with a salad first.
```

```
SEARED TUNA STEAKS WITH CAPONATA
Prep: 25 mins | Serves: 2
Protein: 44g per serving | Points: ~390 per serving

Ingredients:
• 2 fresh tuna steaks (200g each) — Malta has excellent fresh tuna
• Salt, pepper, olive oil

For the caponata:
• 1 medium aubergine, diced
• 2 tomatoes, diced
• 1 celery stalk, sliced
• 2 tbsp capers
• 1 small onion, diced
• 1 tbsp red wine vinegar
• 1 tsp sugar
• 1 tbsp olive oil
• Fresh basil

Method:
1. Heat olive oil in a pan. Fry aubergine with a pinch of salt until golden — 5–6 minutes. Remove and set aside.
2. In the same pan, sauté onion and celery 3 minutes. Add tomatoes, capers, vinegar, and sugar.
3. Return aubergine to the pan. Simmer 8–10 minutes until flavours meld. Add basil.
4. Season tuna steaks generously with salt and pepper. Heat a dry pan until very hot.
5. Sear tuna 90 seconds per side for medium-rare (or longer if preferred). Slice and serve over caponata.

Katya's tip: Fresh tuna from the Maltese market is a real luxury that's also incredibly good for you. Sear it fast and keep the centre pink — overcooking tuna makes it dry. This dish feels like a restaurant meal.
```

```
QUICK BEEF STIR-FRY WITH BROCCOLI
Prep: 20 mins | Serves: 2
Protein: 38g per serving | Points: ~380 per serving

Ingredients:
• 300g lean beef strips (sirloin or flank, sliced thin)
• 1 large head of broccoli, cut into florets
• 1 red pepper, sliced
• 3 garlic cloves, minced
• 1 tsp fresh ginger (or ½ tsp ground)
• 2 tbsp low-sodium soy sauce
• 1 tbsp oyster sauce
• 1 tsp sesame oil
• 1 tbsp olive oil
• Pinch of chilli flakes
• Spring onions to garnish

Method:
1. Mix soy sauce, oyster sauce, sesame oil, and chilli flakes in a small bowl. Set aside.
2. Heat olive oil in a wok or large pan over very high heat.
3. Sear beef strips in one layer — 1 minute, don't move them. Toss once, cook 30 more seconds. Remove and set aside.
4. In the same pan, stir-fry broccoli and pepper for 3–4 minutes.
5. Add garlic and ginger — cook 30 seconds.
6. Return beef to the pan. Pour sauce over everything. Toss quickly for 1 minute.
7. Serve immediately, garnished with spring onions.

Katya's tip: The key to a good stir-fry is high heat and not crowding the pan. Serve over rice for the family or eat as-is — broccoli is free, beef is your protein anchor. Takes under 20 minutes start to finish.
```

```
COTTAGE CHEESE & HERB DIP WITH VEGETABLE STICKS
Prep: 5 mins | Serves: 2
Protein: 18g per serving | Points: ~120 per serving

Ingredients:
• 250g low-fat cottage cheese
• 1 garlic clove, minced (or ¼ tsp garlic powder)
• 2 tbsp fresh chives or spring onions, chopped

• Squeeze of lemon
• Salt, pepper

To dip:
• Carrot sticks
• Cucumber sticks

• Bell pepper strips (all free — eat as much as you like)

Method:
1. Blend cottage cheese until smooth (or leave chunky if you prefer texture).
2. Stir in garlic, chives, dill, lemon, salt, and pepper.
3. Taste and adjust seasoning.
4. Serve with a large plate of vegetable sticks.

Katya's tip: Make a big batch on Sunday. Store in a jar in the fridge for up to 4 days. When you want a snack, just grab your jar and cut some vegetables. This is what planned snacking looks like — no willpower needed.
```

```
SPICED ROASTED CHICKPEAS
Prep: 5 mins prep + 25 mins oven | Serves: 4
Protein: 10g per serving | Points: ~180 per serving

Ingredients:
• 2 tins (400g each) chickpeas, rinsed, drained, and thoroughly dried
• 1 tbsp olive oil
• 1 tsp smoked paprika
• 1 tsp cumin
• ½ tsp garlic powder
• ½ tsp salt
• Pinch of cayenne (optional)

Method:
1. Preheat oven to 200°C.
2. Pat chickpeas very dry with a kitchen towel — the drier they are, the crispier they get.
3. Toss with olive oil, paprika, cumin, garlic powder, salt, and cayenne.
4. Spread in a single layer on a baking tray.
5. Roast 25–30 minutes, shaking the tray once halfway through, until crispy and golden.
6. Cool completely before storing (they crisp up more as they cool).

Katya's tip: These replace crisps completely for snacking. Store in an airtight jar for up to 3 days. They lose crunch in the fridge, so keep at room temperature. Grab a small handful when you need something crunchy and satisfying.
```

```
HARD-BOILED EGGS WITH ZA'ATAR & OLIVE OIL
Prep: 12 mins | Serves: 2
Protein: 12g per serving | Points: ~160 per serving

Ingredients:
• 4 large eggs
• 1 tbsp za'atar (widely available in Malta, or mix dried thyme, oregano, sesame seeds)
• 1 tsp good olive oil
• Flaky sea salt

Method:
1. Place eggs in a pot of cold water. Bring to a boil.
2. Reduce to a gentle simmer and cook exactly 9 minutes for jammy yolks (11 minutes for fully set).
3. Transfer immediately to a bowl of iced water. Cool 5 minutes then peel.
4. Halve the eggs. Drizzle with olive oil, sprinkle with za'atar and flaky salt.
5. Eat immediately or refrigerate (unpeeled) for up to 5 days.

Katya's tip: Keep a batch of unpeeled boiled eggs in the fridge at all times. They are the fastest protein snack that exists. Za'atar transforms a plain boiled egg into something you actually want to eat.
```

## Wednesday Posting Notes

**When sharing a recipe, Katya's intro should:**
- Name the day warmly ("Happy Wednesday!")
- Give one sentence on why this recipe works for the programme
- Share the recipe
- End with a gentle call to action ("Try it this week and let me know what you think")

**Sample intro text (adapt weekly):**
> "Happy Wednesday! Here's this week's recipe — and before you ask, yes, the whole family can eat this. Protein first, always. Give it a try and let me know how it goes."

**Rotation suggestion:** Cycle through all 20 recipes across 20 weeks (5 months), then restart. By the time a recipe repeats, members will likely be ready to try it again — and some will have become favourites.
