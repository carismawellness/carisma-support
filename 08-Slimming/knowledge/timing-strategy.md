# Carisma Slimming — Timing Strategy (WHEN TO EAT)

**Last Updated:** 2026-02-22 | **Reference:** Slimming_Guide_vFINAL.md

---

## Core Principle

"When the day has rhythm, hunger loses its urgency."

Timing isn't about discipline. It's about reducing decision fatigue and protecting the hours when you're most likely to struggle.

---

## The Two Tracks

### TRACK 1: Morning Fasting (Default)

**Timing Pattern:**
- Wake up → 4-hour delay before first meal
- Example: Wake 8am → First meal 12pm (noon)
- Finish dinner → 3 hours before sleep
- Example: Sleep 11pm → Finish dinner by 8pm

**Morning (clean morning):**
- Water, sparkling water, black coffee, or plain tea
- Nothing else

**Lunch (structured, high protein):**
- Your main eating window begins
- Protein-first approach
- This protects your afternoon and evening

**Dinner (flexible, with room):**
- Eat when hungry
- Flexible points for real-life socializing
- Finish 3 hours before sleep when possible

**Why this works:**
- Mornings are naturally easier (low appetite for most people once habit sets)
- Protects your evening when willpower is lower and social eating is higher
- Aligns with Maltese culture (family dinners, dinners out, late meals)
- Reduces decision fatigue (fewer meals = fewer decisions)

### TRACK 2: Non-Fasting (Alternative)

**For people who:**
- Are pregnant or breastfeeding
- Have type 1 diabetes or insulin dependency
- Use medications requiring food
- Have history of disordered eating
- Feel dizzy/faint without breakfast
- Are under 18
- Have been advised not to fast by their doctor
- Simply feel unwell without breakfast

**Timing pattern:**
- Still has structure (not a free-for-all)
- Three balanced meals across the day
- Same protein-first approach
- Same points budget
- Same evening flexibility

**Same results possible:**
Both tracks follow the same method. Only timing changes.

## Specific Timing Rules

### When to START Eating (First Meal)

**Default rule: 4-hour delay after waking**

**Examples:**
- Wake 7am → First meal 11am
- Wake 8am → First meal 12pm
- Wake 9am → First meal 1pm

**Why consistent timing matters:**
Your body learns rhythm. Rhythm makes hunger calmer. Try to keep first meal time consistent most days.

**What if you wake up hungry?**
1. Sparkling water + wait 10 minutes (hunger comes in waves)
2. If still hungry: eat a proper meal, not snacks
3. If always hungry: adjust timing earlier or switch to non-fasting track
4. Tell your clinic: constantly hungry = plan needs tweaking

---

### When to STOP Eating (Dinner Cutoff)

**Default rule: Finish dinner 3 hours before sleep**

**Examples:**
- Sleep 10:30pm → Finish dinner by 7:30pm
- Sleep 11:30pm → Finish dinner by 8:30pm
- Sleep 12:30am → Finish dinner by 9:30pm

**Why this matters:**
- Eating too close to sleep keeps glucose higher overnight
- Increases digestion work when your body should rest
- Lighter, less refreshing sleep = more hunger and cravings next day
- Disrupted sleep = higher stress = harder to stick to plan

**If dinner is late (Maltese context):**
- No panic. This happens.
- Keep it simpler: eat slowly, start with protein, avoid grazing after
- Return to normal rhythm tomorrow
- Goal is rhythm, not perfection

---

### Late Dinners and Real Life

**Common in Malta:**
Family dinners, eating out, social events often mean late meals (10pm, 11pm, even midnight).

**The approach:**
- You're not fighting culture
- Eat your dinner when life calls for it
- Next morning: Return to your normal structure
- That's it. No compensation. No guilt.

**Example:**
- Friday: Dinner at 11pm
- Saturday morning: Return to normal timing. If fasting, break fast at your usual lunch time.
- This one meal doesn't reset the system.

## Morning Fasting — How to Make It Easier

### The Four Tools

**1. Sparkling Water (The Secret Weapon)**
- Most effective tool for easing morning transition
- Creates feeling of fullness
- Reduces food urgency
- Simple pattern: one glass after waking, one mid-morning, one when hunger appears

**2. Black Coffee**
- Reduces hunger and improves focus
- Avoid milk/sugar in morning (triggers more hunger)
- Keep it simple

**3. Plain Tea**
- Gentle alternative to coffee
- Chamomile, peppermint, green tea all work
- Creates ritual without calories

**4. The "Same Time" Rule**
- Pick your first-meal time and aim to keep it consistent
- Your body learns when to expect food
- Makes fasting feel automatic, not forced

## What to Expect Week-by-Week

### Days 1-3
- Hunger waves (not constant, but come in cycles)
- More thoughts about food (habit, not real hunger)
- Habit cravings ("time to eat" thoughts)
- Your brain says "this feels wrong" — that's just habit

### Days 4-7
- Mornings feel calmer
- Hunger becomes less urgent
- Sparkling water + waiting works better
- Pattern starts to feel more normal

### Week 2
- Rhythm feels normal
- Hunger is quieter
- Mornings feel easy
- Evening points budget starts to feel manageable

### Week 3-4
- Fasting feels automatic
- No longer thinking about it
- Cravings quieter
- Energy steadier

## Troubleshooting Morning Fasting

### "I feel dizzy/shaky mid-morning"

**Solution:**
1. Eat. Safety first.
2. Adjust timing earlier (3-hour delay instead of 4)
3. Increase sparkling water intake
4. Tell your clinic
5. If persistent: Switch to non-fasting track (same method, different timing)

### "Hunger is unbearable"

**Check:**
- Are you actually fasting, or just delaying breakfast? Confirm you're not eating before lunch.
- Is lunch high-protein? Low protein = hunger persists.
- Are you drinking enough water/sparkling water?
- How many days in? (Week 1 is hardest; gets easier)

**Solutions:**
- Sparkling water + wait 10 minutes + move around
- If still intolerable: Switch to non-fasting track
- See clinic for personalized adjustment

### "I ate breakfast on day 3 because I was too hungry"

**This is fine.** You're learning about your body.

**Next steps:**
1. Try again tomorrow (don't reset, just continue)
2. Use sparkling water more aggressively
3. Make lunch even higher protein
4. If it keeps happening: Non-fasting track might be better for you
5. Clinic can personalize — structure matters more than fasting

### "I feel fine for 3 days, then quit on day 4-5"

**Common pattern.** Week 1 is hardest.

**What helps:**
- Sparkling water throughout week
- Plan your first-meal time (make it something you look forward to)
- Tell yourself: "Days 4-7 get easier"
- After 7 days, it usually feels normal
- Most people adapt in 7-14 days if it's safe

### "Coffee makes me jittery"

**Solution:**
- Switch to tea (black, green, herbal)
- Or decaf coffee
- Keep mornings calm
- Still use sparkling water as your main tool

### "I worked late and ate at 9pm, now I'm panicking"

**You didn't fail.**
- This happens in real life
- Next morning: Return to your normal rhythm
- Keep morning clean and break fast at usual lunch time if you feel okay
- That one meal doesn't unravel the system
- Goal is consistency, not perfection

## Meal Timing Structure (Full Day)

### Fasting Track Example

```
7:00am — Wake up
7:00-11:00am — Clean period
  - Sparkling water
  - Black coffee
  - Plain tea
  - Water
  - NOTHING ELSE

11:00am — LUNCH (First meal, structured, high protein)
  - Protein first: 1-2 palm portions
  - Vegetables: 2 fists
  - Carbs: one source
  - Drink: water or sparkling water

4:00pm — Optional small snack OR just water/tea
  - If hungry: small protein-based snack
  - If not hungry: water/sparkling water fine

7:30pm — DINNER (Flexible, real-life)
  - Protein: anchors the meal
  - Vegetables: add volume
  - Carbs: flexible
  - Social context: restaurants, family, friends all okay

10:30pm — STOP EATING (3 hours before 1:30am sleep target)

1:30am — Sleep
```

## Safety Guidelines (CRITICAL)

### Who Should NOT Fast

❌ Do not use fasting track if:
- Pregnant or breastfeeding
- Type 1 diabetes
- Insulin-dependent
- History of disordered eating or eating disorders
- Regularly dizzy/faint without breakfast
- Under 18 years old
- Your doctor has advised against fasting
- You have advanced kidney or liver disease
- Taking medications that require food

### If You Ever Feel Unwell

**EAT. Safety first.**

Then tell your clinic.

Symptoms that require immediate stop:
- Dizziness/fainting
- Severe shakiness
- Heart palpitations
- Anxiety or panic
- Extreme fatigue

Adjust to non-fasting track or modified timing.

## The Non-Fasting Track (Full Protocol)

If fasting isn't safe or right for you, use this approach:

**Three balanced meals, same structure:**

### Breakfast (Within 1-2 hours of waking)
- Protein: 1 palm portion
- Vegetables: if possible
- Carbs: whole grain bread, oatmeal, etc.
- Example: Scrambled eggs + whole wheat toast + tomatoes

### Lunch (Structured, high protein)
- Same as fasting track
- 1-2 palm portions protein
- 2 fists vegetables
- One carb source
- Points counted

### Dinner (Flexible)
- Same as fasting track
- Protein-based
- Real-life flexible
- Points counted

**Same daily points budget. Same structure. Same results possible.**

## Timing in Real-Life Scenarios

### Restaurant/Dinner Out (Maltese Context)

**Fasting track:**
- Stick to normal lunch time earlier if possible
- Or eat at the restaurant as your "first meal" (later than normal is okay once)
- Eat protein-first
- Enjoy your meal without guilt
- Return to normal rhythm tomorrow

**Non-fasting track:**
- Same flexibility
- Just eat when the meal happens
- Structure remains (protein-first approach still applies)

### Weekend Disruption

**Fasting track:**
- Saturday sleeping in? Push first meal back by 1-2 hours
- Keep spark ling water available
- Still aim for 3-hour dinner cutoff
- If not possible: no stress, return to normal Sunday

**Non-fasting track:**
- Similar flexibility
- Meal structure remains
- One disrupted day doesn't break the system

### Shift Work or Irregular Schedule

**Work with your clinic to:**
- Adjust fasting window to match your schedule
- Find "first meal" time that works for your shift
- Use non-fasting track if shifts are too irregular
- Consistency matters more than exact timing

## Myths vs Truth

| Myth | Truth |
|------|-------|
| "Breakfast starts your metabolism" | You burn energy the same way. Timing doesn't change calorie burn. |
| "Skipping breakfast always causes binge eating" | Sometimes. Structure and protein prevent it. |
| "Fasting means suffering" | If it feels awful or unsafe, we adjust. Safety first. |
| "I've always eaten breakfast, I can't change" | Most people adapt in 7-14 days if it's safe. |
| "It only works if you fast for very long" | A consistent, doable window (like 4 hours) works best. |
| "Late dinner ruins everything" | One late meal doesn't reset the system. Return at next meal. |
| "Missing breakfast slows your metabolism" | No. Metabolic rate is determined by overall intake and activity, not meal timing. |
